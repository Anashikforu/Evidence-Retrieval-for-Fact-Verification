She got international fame as an actress in musicals|musical and dramatic roles, as a album|recording artist, and on the concert Stage (theatre)|stage. She got a Juvenile Academy Award and won a Golden Globe Award. She also won Grammy Awards and a Special Tony Award. She was wikt:nominate|nominated for the Academy Award for "Best Actress" for her role in the remake of ''A Star is Born''. She also got a nomination for "Best Supporting Actress" for her performance in the 1961 movie, ''Judgment at Nuremberg''. She was the youngest person to be given the Cecil B. DeMille Award for lifetime achievement in the movie industry.

Garland was in vaudeville with her two older sisters. She was signed to MGM|Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer as a teenager. She made more than two dozen MGM movies. Nine of these movies co-starred Mickey Rooney. Her most famous MGM movie was ''The Wizard of Oz (1939 movie)|The Wizard of Oz'' of 1939. She worked with MGM for 15 years. She went on to make record-breaking concert appearances.

Garland had personal problems throughout her life. She did not like the way she looked. Her feelings were made worse by movie executives who told her she did not look good. They changed the way she looked Human body|physically in movies. She also had finance|financial problems. She often owed hundreds of thousands of dollars in unpaid taxes. She marriage|married five times. Her first four marriages ended in divorce. She had a long struggle with alcoholism|alcohol and illegal drugs|drug use during most of her career. She died of an accidental drug overdose at the age of 47. She had three children. They are Liza Minnelli, Lorna Luft, and Joey Luft.

In 1997, Garland was given a Grammy Lifetime Achievement Award years after her death. Several of her recordings have been sent to the Grammy Hall of Fame. In 1999, the American Film Institute placed her among the ten greatest female stars in the history of American movies.
