
 is a former Japanese Association football|football player and manager. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team.

Biography
Ueki was born in Kawasaki, Kanagawa|Kawasaki on September 13, 1954. After graduating from Nihon University, he joined Japan Soccer League club Shonan Bellmare|Fujita Industries in 1977. The club won the league champions in 1977, 1979 and 1981. The club also won 1977 and 1979 Emperor's Cup. He retired in 1988. He played 144 games and scored 16 goals in the league.

On July 13, 1979, Ueki debuted for the Japan national football team|Japan national team against Singapore national football team|Singapore and Japan won the match.

After retirement, Ueki started coaching career at Shonan Bellmare|Fujita Industries (later ''Bellmare Hiratsuka''). In November 1995, he became a manager as Mitsuru Komaeda successor and managed at 1995 Emperor's Cup and 1995 Asian Cup Winners' Cup. The club won the champions at Asian Cup Winners' Cup. In September 1996, he became a manager for Bellmare again as Toninho Moura successor and managed until 1998. In 1999, he moved to J2 League club Montedio Yamagata and managed until 2000. In 2002, he moved to Japan Football League club Thespakusatsu Gunma|Thespa Kusatsu. In 2004, he managed 1 season and promoted the club to J2 League from 2005. In 2006, he became a manager for Thespa again and managed until 2008.
