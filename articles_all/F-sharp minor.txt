
'''F-sharp minor''' is a minor scale based on F sharp. Its key signature has three sharps. 

Its relative key|relative major is A major.

Many people think it is a wikt:melancholy|melancholy and :en:wikt:gloomy|gloomy key.
Harry Farjeon says that F sharp minor is "light red", and that it is the key Mendelssohn uses when he is being passionate.
Johann Mattheson wrote that 'F minor, although it leads to great wikt:distress|distress, wikt:nevertheless|nevertheless is more :en:wikt:languid|languid and love-sick than wikt:lethal|lethal. wikt:moreover|Moreover, it has something wikt:abandon|abandoned, wikt:singular|singular, and :en:wikt:misanthropic|misanthropic about it.'

Famous music in this key
Very few symphonies are written in this key. Joseph Haydn|Haydn's ''Symphony No. 45 (Haydn)|Farewell Symphony'' is one famous example. George Frederick Bristow and Dora Pejačević also wrote symphonies in this key.

Many piano pieces have been written in F-sharp minor.
Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart|Mozart's only composition in this key is the second movement to his Piano Concerto No. 23 (Mozart)|Piano Concerto No. 23 in A major. 2 of Chopin's 52 mazurkas, and 1 of his nocturnes are in this key, though he seems to have liked the related key C-sharp minor better. Scriabin's Piano Sonata No. 3 (Scriabin)|Piano Sonata No. 3 is also in this key, and the 3rd movement of Beethoven's "Hammerklavier" Piano Sonata is too.

Both Max Reger|Reger's and Michael Tippett|Tippett's second string quartets are in this key, and so is Shostakovich's seventh string quartet.
