
'''Thomas John Cook''' (May 7, 1907 – October 2, 1961) was a Canadians|Canadian professional ice hockey forward (ice hockey)|forward who played 348 games in the National Hockey League. Born in Fort William, Ontario|Fort William, Ontario, he played for the Montreal Maroons and Chicago Blackhawks|Chicago Black Hawks. He won the Stanley Cup in 1933–34 NHL season|1934 with the Chicago Blackhawks|Chicago Black Hawks.
