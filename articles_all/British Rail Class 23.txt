
'''British Rail Class 23''', originally called English Electric List of British Rail power classifications|Type 2, was a class of ten Diesel locomotive|diesel-electric locomotives built by the English Electric Company in 1959. They were numbered from D5900 to D5909.  The power unit was a Napier Deltic T9-29 9 cylinder engine of 1,100&nbsp;bhp. This drove an EE generator, which powered the four traction motors. The Class 23 was nicknamed "Baby Deltic" because of its similarity to the British Rail Class 55|Class 55.
