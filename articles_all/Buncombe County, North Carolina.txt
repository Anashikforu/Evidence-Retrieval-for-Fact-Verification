
'''Buncombe County''' is a county in the U.S. state of North Carolina.  In 2006, 222,174 people lived there. Its county seat is Asheville, North Carolina|Asheville.

 History 
The county was made in 1791 from parts of Burke County, North Carolina|Burke County and Rutherford County, North Carolina|Rutherford County.

In 1808 the western part of Buncombe County was made into Haywood County, North Carolina|Haywood County.  In 1833 parts of Burke County and Buncombe County were combined to make Yancey County, North Carolina|Yancey County, and in 1838 the southern part of Buncombe County was made into Henderson County, North Carolina|Henderson County.  In 1851 parts of Buncombe County and Yancey County were combined to make Madison County, North Carolina|Madison County.  And, in 1925 a small part of McDowell County, North Carolina|McDowell County was made into a part of Buncombe County.
