
'''John Gerald Davey''' (September 5, 1914 – February 12, 1977) is a former ice hockey player who played in the English National League (ENL). He also played for the Great Britain national ice hockey team which won the gold medal at the 1936 Winter Olympics (see Ice hockey at the 1936 Winter Olympics). He is a member of the British Ice Hockey Hall of Fame.

Career
Although born in Barking, England, Davey learned to play ice hockey in Port Arthur, Ontario with the Elmwood Midgets after his family emigrated there when he was a child.

=Club=
When he was 16-years-old, Davey's mother returned with him to England in 1931. He got a place with the Princes Ice Hockey Club|Princes club playing in the English League (ice hockey)|English League with the help of a London newspaper. After a short time with ZSC Lions in Switzerland, he returned to the UK to play with Streatham Redskins|Streatham in 1933 before eventually moving on to play with the Falkirk Lions in the Scottish National League (1932 - 1954)|Scottish National League between 1938 and 1940, whom he also Coach (sport)|coached in 1938–39.

During World War II, Davey joined the Royal Canadian Navy continuing to play ice hockey in the Toronto Services League. After the war in 1946, Davey returned to Streatham where he took up playing in Defenceman (ice hockey)|defence having previously played Winger (ice hockey)|right wing. Davey then moved the Wembley Lions the following year for the 1947–48 season. Davey then turned his hand to Official (ice hockey)|refereeing before returning to playing for a brief spell during the 1949–50 season with the Streatham Royals in the Intermediate League.

=International=




After arriving in England, Davey made an immediate impact with the Princes club and was selected to play for the GB national team in the 1932 Ice Hockey World Championships|European Championships held in Berlin. The team finished the tournament in seventh position with Davey scoring seven of the team's eleven goals.

Davey went on to play for the GB national team at all the international tournaments between 1932 and 1939, most notably the team which took part in the 1936 Winter Olympics. Having fallen ill, Davey came back from his sick bed to score 40 seconds into the game against Canada national men's ice hockey team|Canada – which GB won 2–1 – helping the team along the way to securing the gold medal.

Davey also helped the GB team to a bronze medal in the 1935 Ice Hockey World Championships|World Championships, which also earned a silver medal in the European Championships,

Davey scored a total of forty-three goals for the GB national team, a record which still stands.
