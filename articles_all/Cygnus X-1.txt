'''Cygnus&nbsp;X-1''' (abbreviated '''Cyg&nbsp;X-1''') is a well-known galactic X-ray source. It is thought to be a black hole in the constellation Cygnus (constellation)|Cygnus. 

It was discovered in 1964 during a rocket flight, and is one of the strongest X-ray sources seen from Earth. Cygnus&nbsp;X-1 was the first X-ray source accepted as a black hole. It is among the most studied astronomical objects in its class. The compact object is estimated to have a mass about 14.8 times the Solar mass|mass of the Sun.Orosz, Jerome ''et al'' 2011.
