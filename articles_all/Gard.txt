
'''Gard''' is a France|French Departments of France|department in southern France in the Occitanie Regions of France|region and named after the Gardon river. Its Prefectures in France|prefecture is Nîmes.

History
Gard is one of the 83 French departments made during the French revolution, on 4 March 1790. It was divided in 8 districts: Alais, Beaucaire, Nîmes, Pont-Saint-Esprit, Saint-Hippolyte, Sommières, Uzès and Le Vigan. The Capital city|capital was Nîmes alternating with Alais and Uzès. In 1794, Nîmes became the only capital.

With the creation of the ''arrondissements'', the 8 districts became four ''arrondissements'': Nîmes, Alais, Uzès and Le Vigan. In 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Uzès was eliminated.

The highest point in the department is ''Mont Aigoual'', ( high; it is in the ''Cévennes'' mountains, on the border with the Lozère department.

The main river of Gard is the Gardon (or ''Gard''), a right tributary of the Rhone. The Rhone flows through the eastern border of the department.

Administration
Gard is part of the region of Occitanie. The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Gard'' in Nîmes.

Demographics
The inhabitants of Gard are known, in French language|French, as ''Gardois'' (women: ''Gardoises'').

Gard has a population, in 2014, of 736,029, for a population density of  inhabitants/km2. The ''arrondissement'' of Nîmes, with 547,598 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Alès and Le Vigan, have respectively 151,823 and 36,608 inhabitants.

The city with more people living in it is the Capital city|capital, Nîmes (151,075). The subprefectures of Alès and Le Vigan, Gard|Le Vigan have, respectively, 39,993 and 3,933 inhabitants.
