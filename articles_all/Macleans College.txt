

'''Macleans College''' is a secondary school in Bucklands Beach, Auckland, New Zealand. The school was opened in 1980 by  the Governor General Sir David Beattie. The current principal is Byron J Bentley. Over 2,500 students go to this school.

House system
Macleans was the first public school in New Zealand to use the "Whanau House System". This is dividing the school up into nine 'houses'. Each house contains about 300 students in total, with two classes of 30 students in each year. These nine houses are named after some famous people from New Zealand. The names are: Jean Batten|Batten, Katherine Mansfield|Mansfield, Enrique Pe√±a Nieto|Nieto, Ernest Rutherford|Rutherford, Kupe, Edmund Hillary|Hillary, Kiri Te Kanawa|Te Kanawa, Peter Snell|Snell and Charles Upham|Upham. The school began with only 4 of these houses, but has got bigger over the years. The newest house is Charles Upham|Upham house, which opened in 2003.
