

The '''Potter Park Zoo''' is a zoo in Lansing, Michigan.  Potter Park Zoo is the oldest public zoo in Michigan.  The zoo is  in size.  The zoo has more than 160 species of animals.  The zoo is owned by the City of Lansing, and operated by Ingham County, Michigan|Ingham County.  The zoo is a member of the Association of Zoos and Aquariums (AZA).

History
Potter Park Zoo was dedicated on July 5, 1915.  The zoo officially opened in 1920.#HileK|Hile, p. 6

The Bird and Reptile building opened in 1929.  In 1930 the Lion building opened.  The bighorn sheep exhibit (originally called Monkey Island) was constructed in 1936.  The aviary was constructed in 1941.  The barnyard and petting zoo opened in 1949.  In the 1950s, Potter Park Zoo had 138 mammals and 267 birds.#HileK|Hile, pp. 21–23

Image:Magellanic Penguins at Lansing Potter Park Zoo.jpg|thumb|right|175px|Magellanic Penguins at Potter Park Zoo
In the late 1960s, the zoo began a decline from municipal neglect.  In 1969 a columnist of the ''Lansing State Journal'' encouraged residents to raise money for the zoo.#ManassahS|Manassah, pp. 99–100

A Magellanic Penguin|magellanic penguin exhibit opened in 1985.  In 1986 the zoo became a member of the American Association of Zoological Parks and Aquariums|American Association of Zoos and Aquariums (AZA).#HileK|Hile, p. 101  In 1989 the zoo's Lion House was renovated and renamed the Felidae|Feline and Primate Building.

Image:Siberian-Amur Tiger at Lansing Potter Park Zoo.jpg|thumb|right|175px|Siberian tiger|Amur (Siberian) Tiger at the zoo
In 2005 endangered tiger cubs were born at the zoo. They were featured on NBC's Today (NBC program)|Today Show.  In 2005 the ''River Otter'' and ''Arctic Fox'' exhibits opened.  In November 2006 Lansing transferred the operation and maintenance of the zoo to Ingham County.

Image:Snow Leopard 001.jpg|thumb|right|175px|Snow Leopard at the zoo
In January 2009 the zoo announced plans to expand the Eastern Black Rhinoceros|eastern black rhinoceros exhibit.  In 2009 the ''Wings from Down Under'' aviary opened with more than 600 Australian birds.  In March 2010 Potter Park Zoo artificial insemination|artificially inseminated a female snow leopard.  It is believed to be the first successful procedure without surgery or anesthesia.  A larger Eurasian Eagle-owl exhibit opened in September 2010.  

Animals and exhibits
Potter Park Zoo's '''Feline and Primate Building''' has two Siberian tiger|Amur tigers, Lion|African lions, snow leopards, mandrills, Pallas' Cat|Pallas' cats, cotton-top tamarins, Spider monkey|black-headed spider monkeys,Golden Lion Tamarin|golden lion tamarins, Lemur|mongoose, Lemur|red-ruffed, and ring-tailed lemurs.
 
The '''Bird and Reptile House''' has rare flying creatures like Myna|Bali starlings, Macaw|chestnut-fronted macaws, Owl|eastern screech owls, Owl|Eurasian eagle owls, green aracaries, Dove|Jambu fruit doves. There are scaly species like chukwallas, Bearded dragon|inland bearded dragons, Gecko|leopard geckos, Skink|blue-tongued skinks, Gila monsters, water dragons, boa constrictors, green tree pythons, and more.     

The '''Coral Reef Room''', showcases exotic fish, crustaceans, eels, and coral.

The '''Farmyard EdVentures''' is where children can pet and feed animals at the barnyard and petting zoo. The petting zoo has goats, burros, reindeer, yaks, toy pigs, tortoises, and rabbits.

The '''Wing From Down Under''' aviary has grass parakeets, cockatiels, and eastern rosellas.

Gardens and a pond have ducks, geese, swans, and pelicans.  Other animals at the zoo are kangaroos, penguins, North American River Otter|river otters, bongo (antelope)|bongos, bald eagles, eastern timberwolf|eastern timberwolves, red pandas, arctic foxes, and porcupines.
