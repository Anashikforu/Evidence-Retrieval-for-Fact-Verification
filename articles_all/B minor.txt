
'''B minor''' is a minor scale based on B.  The harmonic minor raises the A to A.  Its key signature has two sharps.

Its relative key|relative major is D major, and its parallel key|parallel major is B major.


In Baroque times, people thought that B minor was the key of wikt:passive|passive wikt:suffering|suffering.The chord was famously used as the first chord in Pink Floyd's 1979 hit "Comfortably Numb". The second movement of Joaquín Rodrigo|Rodrigo's Concierto de Aranjuez, one of the most famous compositions for the classical guitar, is in B minor.

It is a common key used in rock, folk, country and other guitarist-based styles because a guitar is naturally tuned so that all the open strings are notes in B minor.
