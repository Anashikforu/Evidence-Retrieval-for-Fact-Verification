


'''Alexander Ramsey''' (September 8, 1815â€“ April 22, 1903) was an Americans|American politician. He was born near Harrisburg, Pennsylvania.

Ramsey was elected from Pennsylvania as a United States Whig Party|Whig to the United States House of Representatives|U.S. House of Representatives and served in the 28th United States Congress|28th and 29th United States Congress|29th congresses from March 4, 1843 to March 3, 1847. He served as the first Territorial Governor of Minnesota June 1, 1849 to May 15, 1853 as a member of the Whig Party.


In 1855, he became the mayor of St. Paul, Minnesota. Ramsey was elected the second Governor of Minnesota after statehood and served from January 2, 1860 to July 10, 1863. Ramsey is credited with being the first Union governor to commit troops during the American Civil War (he happened to be in Washington, D.C. when fighting broke out). He resigned the governorship to become a United States Senate|U.S. Senator, having been elected to that post in 1863 as a United States Republican Party|Republican. He was re-elected in 1869 and held the office until March 3, 1875, serving in the 38th United States Congress|38th, 39th United States Congress|39th, 40th United States Congress|40th, 41st United States Congress|41st, 42nd United States Congress|42nd, and 43rd United States Congress|43rd congresses.

Ramsey served as United States Secretary of War|Secretary of War from 1879 to 1881, under President of the United States|President Rutherford B. Hayes.

 Legacy 
The Minnesota Historical Society preserves his home as a museum. It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1969. Alexander Ramsey Park, located in Redwood Falls, Minnesota, is the largest municipal park in Minnesota.
