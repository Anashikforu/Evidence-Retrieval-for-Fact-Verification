
'''Lomé''' is the Capital (city)|capital city of Togo. Lomé is the Administration|administrative and industry|industrial center of the country. The city is also the main harbor|port of Togo. The city exports coffee, cocoa, copra, and palm tree|palm kernels. The population of the city was estimated as around 700,000 people in 1998. It is estimated that more than 1 million people live in Lomé area at that time.

Lomé is on the Gulf of Guinea. It is in the south west of Togo, on the border between Togo and Ghana. Things to see in the city include Lomé Grand Market, the Togo National Museum in the Palais de Congrés, a fetish (voodoo) market, Lomé Cathedral, beaches and the former wharf.

 History 
The city was :wikt:found|founded in the 18th century|eighteenth century by the Ewe people. In 1882, the village, known then as '''Bey Beach''', became a major trade|trading center for the United Kingdom|British.

Bey Beach became the capital of Togo when the Germany|German rulers moved the capital from Aneho in 1897. The city then grew quickly until it was taken by the france|French Army in 1914 during World War I.
