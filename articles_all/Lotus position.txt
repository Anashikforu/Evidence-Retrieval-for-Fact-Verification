The '''lotus position''' (Sanskrit: ''Padmasanam'') is a cross-legged sitting position which  is common in Meditation|meditative practices of Hinduism. It is a part of the Hindu Yoga tradition. The position is said to resemble a Nelumbo|lotus plant and to encourage proper breathing and to be helpful for physical stability.
