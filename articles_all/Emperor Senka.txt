 Traditional history 
According to ''Kojiki'', Senka was a son of Emperor Keitai.  

When Emperor Ankan died childless, the throne passed to his brother Senka.

 Events of Senka's life 
Very little is known about the events of Senka's life and reign.  Only limited information is wikt:available|available for study prior to the reign of the 29th monarch, Emperor Kimmei.Titsingh, pp. 34-36; Brown, pp. 261-262; Varley, pp. 123-124. 

During this reign, Soga no Iname became the first verifiable "Great Minister" or ''Omi'' (also identified as ''Ō-omi'').Titsingh, p. 33.

The reign of Emperor Senka wikt:lasted|lasted for three years. 

= After his death =
