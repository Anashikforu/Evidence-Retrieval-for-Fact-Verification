Since the beginning of the English Premier League|Premier League, 58 football stadiums have been used to host matches, with one more stadium set to host its first match in the 2016–17 season. The first round of Premier League matches took place on 15 August 1992. 11 clubs hosted the opening fixtures.

When Fulham F.C.|Fulham came up from Football League Championship|Division 1 in the 2000–2001 season, terraces returned for a short while. This gave them more time to complete the changes.

Burnley F.C.|Burnley's Turf Moor stadium became the 50th Premier League stadium on 19 August 2009, where they faced Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United. A.F.C. Bournemouth|Bournemouth's Dean Court is the most recent stadium to host a Premier League match, when they played their first ever home Premier League match against Aston Villa F.C.|Aston Villa on 8 August 2015. The next stadium to host its first Premier League match will be Olympic Stadium (London)|London Stadium, new home to West Ham United F.C.|West Ham United, when West Ham hosts Bournemouth on 21 August 2016.
