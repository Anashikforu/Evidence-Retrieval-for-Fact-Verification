
The '''province of L'Aquila''' () is a Provinces of Italy|province in the Abruzzo region of Southern Italy. Its Capital city|capital is the city of L'Aquila.

It is the largest province of the region but the one with fewest people living in it; it is a landlocked province, that is, surrounded by other provinces by all sides.

Geography

L'Aquila is the largest province in the Abruzzo region with almost half of the area of the region; it has area of . The province is bordered to the north with Tuscany, to the northeast by the province of Teramo, to the east by the Province of Pescara|Pescara and Province of Chieti provinces, to the south and southeast with the Molise region (province of Isernia), and to the west and southwest by the Lazio region (province of Frosinone, Metropolitan City of Rome Capital|Rome and province of Rieti).



The main river in the province is the Aterno-Pescara river, known here as ''Aterno''; it starts in the ''Monti della Laga'' and flows through the province.

L'Aquila is the province of the Abruzzo region with more mountains; there are three mountain ranges that go from the northwest to the southeast, and that are Parallel (geometry)|parallels to the coastline: ''Monti della Gaga'', ''Gran Sasso'' and ''Majella massif''. The highest mountain in the province is ''Monte Amaro'',( high and is the highest mountain of three provinces: Chieti, L'Aquila and Pescara of the Abruzzo.
