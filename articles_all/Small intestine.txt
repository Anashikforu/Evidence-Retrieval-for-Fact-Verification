The '''small intestine''' is between the stomach and the large intestine, and is where most of the digestion and absorption happen.The main function of the small intestine is the absorption of nutrients and minerals from food. 
The small intestine has three regions: the duodenum, the jejunum and the ileum. 

The duodenum: receives bile and juice from the pancreas, and is where most digestion in the small intestine takes place. Secretion|Secretions limit, or Base (chemistry)|neutralise, the stomach acidity, and enzymes help to digest the food.

The jejenum: the products of digestion (Sugar|sugars, amino acids, and fatty acids) are Absorption|absorbed into the Circulatory system|bloodstream here.

The ileum: absorbs vitamin B12, bile salts, and any other products of digestion that were not absorbed by the jejunum.
