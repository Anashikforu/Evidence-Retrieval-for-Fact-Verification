
'''Mazar-e-Quaid''' () or the '''National Mausoleum''' refers to the tomb of the founder of Pakistan, Muhammad Ali Jinnah. It is an iconic symbol of Karachi throughout the world. The mausoleum was completed in the 1960s. It is situated at the heart of the city. It is the last rest place of Muhammad Ali Jinnah. Near the tomb there is a park that has strong lights that show the tomb with lights in the night. Here  with Quaid-e-Azam, Liaquat Ali Khan and Fatima Jinnah are also buried.
