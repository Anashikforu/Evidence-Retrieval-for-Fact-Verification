


'''Helmut Haller''' (; 21 July 1939 – 11 October 2012) was a German association football|footballer who played as a Forward (association football)|forward. At international level, he represented Germany national football team|West Germany at three Football World Cup|World Cups. At club level, he played in both Germany and Italy, and won Serie A|Italian league titles with Bologna F.C. 1909|Bologna and Juventus. He suffered of dementia and Parkinson's disease.

 Style of play 


Both a playmaker and goalscorer, Haller was noted for his exceptional technique and finesse as a Striker (association football)|striker, yet also for his weight problems, which troubled him throughout his career.

 Personal life 
On Boxing Day 2006, Haller suffered a serious heart attack and was reported to be recovering well after treatment. Later he also suffered from dementia and Parkinson's disease. Haller died on 11 October 2012. He was survived by his third wife, the native Cuban Noraimy Rodriguez Guiterrez, whom he married in 2003 when she was aged just 21, two sons and a daughter. Haller was also an uncle of Christian Hochstätter, a long-serving Borussia Mönchengladbach player and twice West German international.
