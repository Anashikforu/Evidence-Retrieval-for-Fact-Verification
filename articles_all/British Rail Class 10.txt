
The '''British Rail Class 10''' diesel locomotive was a variation on the British Rail Class 08|Class 08 diesel-electric Switcher|shunter. A Blackstone & Co|Blackstone diesel engine was fitted instead of one made by the English Electric company.  Traction motors were by either the General Electric Company plc (GEC) or British Thomson-Houston (BTH). 

The locomotives were built at the BR Works in Darlington railway works|Darlington and Doncaster railway works|Doncaster over the period 1953-62.  Early batches were classified British Rail Class D3/4|D3/4 (those with GEC motors) and British Rail Class D3/5|D3/5  (those with BTH motors).
