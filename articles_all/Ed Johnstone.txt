
'''Edward Lavern "Eddie" Johnstone''' (born March 2, 1954) is a Canadians|Canadian retired professional ice hockey player. He played for the New York Rangers and Detroit Red Wings of the National Hockey League (NHL).

Playing career
Johnstone played junior hockey|major junior with the Medicine Hat Tigers of the Western Hockey League|Western Canada Hockey League from 1971–72 WCHL season|1971 to 1973–74 WCHL season|1974. After two 100 point seasons in a row with Medicine Hat, Johnstone was drafted 104th overall by the New York Rangers in the 1974 NHL Amateur Draft, and 78th overall by the Michigan Stags in the 1974 WHA Amateur Draft. He played 23 games for the Stags in 1974–75 WHA season|1974–75 and played in the American Hockey League for the Providence Reds. Johnstone started his National Hockey League career in 1975–76 NHL season|1975–76, scoring 3 points in 10 games. Johnstone played for the Rangers until the end of the 1982–83 NHL season|1982–83 season, before being traded to the Detroit Red Wings along with Ron Duguay and Eddie Mio for Mike Blaisdell, Willie Huber, and Mark Osborne (ice hockey)|Mark Osborne. He played for the Red Wings and their AHL minor league affiliate, the Adirondack Red Wings before retiring in 1986–87 NHL season|1987.
