


A '''slaughterhouse''', also called by the French language|French word '''abattoir''', is a building where farm animals are killed and turned into meat. The animals most often slaughtered for food are cattle|cows (for beef and veal), sheep (for lamb (food)|lamb and Lamb (food)|mutton), pigs (for pork), fowl (including chicken and waterfowl such as ducks and goose|geese), and horses (for horsemeat).
