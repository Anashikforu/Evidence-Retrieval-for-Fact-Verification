The '''decimal separator''' is a symbol used to mark the border between the integer|integral and the fraction (mathematics)|fractional parts of a decimal numeral. This symbol can be a period (punctuation)|period ("."), as is common in United States and other English-speaking countries, or a comma (","), as in continental Europe. '''Decimal point''' and '''decimal comma''' are also common names for the decimal separator. For example, 9.5 means nine and one half in English language|English speaking countries, while in many European countries, the same number might be written as 9,5.
