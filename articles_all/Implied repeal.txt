
'''Implied repeal''' is a theory on the Constitution of the United Kingdom|British constitution that laws can be amended or repealed by an Act of Parliament. If the Parliament of the United Kingdom|British Parliament passes two laws about the same subject that state different things, the more recently enacted law states normally what must be done.

The doctrine is expressed in the Latin phrase ''leges posteriores priores contrarias abrogant'' ("more recent laws overwrite earlier laws").
