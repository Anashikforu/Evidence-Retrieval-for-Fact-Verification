


'''AnnaSophia Robb''' (born December 8, 1993) is an American actress and model. The films in which she starred include ''Because of Winn-Dixie (film)|Because of Winn-Dixie'' (2005), ''Charlie and the Chocolate Factory (film)|Charlie and the Chocolate Factory'' (2005), ''Bridge to Terabithia (2007 film)|Bridge to Terabithia'' (2007), ''Race to Witch Mountain'' (2009), ''Soul Surfer (film)|Soul Surfer'' (2011), and ''The Way, Way Back'' (2013). She also played the lead role of Carrie Bradshaw on The CW's series ''The Carrie Diaries (TV series)|The Carrie Diaries'' from 2013 to 2014.

Early life
Robb was born in Denver, Colorado, the daughter of Janet, an interior designer, and David Robb, an architect.

Career
At age 9, she was scouted by an agent, and she and her mother drove down to Los Angeles. After several auditions, she was given the opportunity to appear in a commercial, advertising Bratz dolls that would be aired nationally. After appearing in a commercial for McDonald's, Robb had a small role in the episode, "Number One Fan" of the television series ''Drake & Josh''. Her first major role was the Samantha Parkington|title character in the television special, ''Samantha: An American Girl Holiday''. She wore a long brown wig for the role.

Robb's two big-screen appearances in 2005 were both adaptations of popular children's books. She starred as Opal in ''Because of Winn-Dixie (film)|Because of Winn-Dixie'' and as the competitive and rude Violet Beauregarde in Tim Burton's remake of ''Charlie and the Chocolate Factory (film)|Charlie and the Chocolate Factory''. The latter was a major box office success worldwide, and helped escalate Robb's popularity among pre-teen audiences.

In 2005, Robb was the face of Trad Clothing, helping to design and model a fashion line for girls. In 2006, she had a guest role on the cartoon show, ''Danny Phantom'' as the voice of Dani Phantom|Danielle "Dani" Fenton. Robb played Leslie Burke in ''Bridge to Terabithia (2007 film)|Bridge to Terabithia'' with Josh Hutcherson, which opened in U.S. theaters on February 16, 2007. She recorded a song for the soundtrack titled, "Keep Your Mind Wide Open", and the accompanying video received Rotation (music)|rotation on the Disney Channel. Robb was a fan of the book before being cast in the role, saying that it "touched me in a way I hadn't been touched by a book before".

Robb went on to appear in ''The Reaping'', ''Have Dreams, Will Travel'', ''Jumper (2008 film)|Jumper'', and ''Spy School''. Despite negative reviews of the film overall, Robb's performance in ''Sleepwalking (film)|Sleepwalking'' garnered praise. ''Time magazine|Time'' film critic Richard Schickel said: "There is a wonderful range to Robb's work...this is extraordinarily mature acting from someone this young and she wins our sympathy without once begging for it."

In May 2011, Robb was announced to star as Peter and Wendy|Wendy in a new and dark take on the classic story about Peter Pan, tentatively titled, ''Pan (2015 film)|Pan'', but later dropped out of the project.Zakia Udin (May 11, 2011). 
In October 2013, Robb started filming for ''A Conspiracy on Jekyll Island|Jekyll Island'', starring alongside Frank Grillo, Ed Westwick, Dianna Agron, John Leguizamo, Minnie Driver, and Maggie Q. The film is directed by Aram Rappaport and produced by Hilary Shor, Atit Shah and Aaron Becker. ''Jekyll Island''  is set to release in 2017.

Robb played the role of Alice Green in the ''PBS'' Civil War historical drama series ''Mercy Street (TV series)|Mercy Street'' from 2016 to 2017.

Personal life
In 2014, Robb was enrolled at New York University.

Robb was one of the demonstrators at the 2017 Women's March held on January 21, 2017 in Washington, D.C..

Awards and nominations
On March 30, 2008, Robb won her first career award when she was named Leading Young Actress at the Young Artist Awards for her role in ''Bridge To Terabithia''. The film itself won a Young Cast award. On April 24, 2009, she received the ''Horizon Award'' at the 14th Annual Palm Beach International Film Festival. She was awarded the Rising Star Award at the Denver Film Festival on November 12, 2009.
