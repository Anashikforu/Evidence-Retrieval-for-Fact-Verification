
'''Puy-de-Dôme''' is a France|French Departments of France|department in the centre of France in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes Regions of France|region and named after the dormant volcano the '''Puy de Dôme'''. Its Prefectures in France|prefecture is the city of Clermont-Ferrand.

History
Puy-de-Dôme was created in 1790 during the French Revolution, when the whole of France was divided into departments, replacing the old provinces. Puy-de-Dôme was formed with part of the old province of Auvergne.

It was divided in eight districts: Ambert, Besse, Billom, Clermont-Ferrand, Issoire, Montaigu, Riom and Thiers, with Clermont-Ferrand as is Capital (city)|capital.

In 1800, with the creation of the Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' in France, the eight districts were changed into five ''arrondissements'': Ambert, Clermont-Ferrand, Issoire, Riom and Thiers. On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of  Ambert was eliminated but, in 1942 Ambert became again an ''arrondissement'' of the department.

Administration
Puy-de-Dôme is part of the region of Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes. The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Puy-de-Dôme'' in Clermont-Ferrand.

=Administrative divisions=
There are 5 Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' (districts), 31 Cantons in France|''cantons'' and 467 Communes in France|''communes'' (Municipality|municipalities) in Puy-de-Dôme.
