Traditional history
Itoku is almost certainly a wikt:legend|legend. The ''Kojiki'' records only his name and genealogy. The  ''Nihonshoki'' includes Itoku as the third of .Aston, pp. 142-143.

The ''Gukanshō'' records that Itoku was the second or third son of Emperor Annei,Brown, p. 251. but the surviving documents provide no basis for making guesses about why the elder brother or brothers were passed over.Ponsonby-Fane, p. 29; Varley, p. 89.

During reign of Emperor Itoku, the capital of Japan was at Karu, Yamato.Ponsonby-Fane, Richard. (1915). ''The Imperial Family of Japan'', pp. 2-3.
