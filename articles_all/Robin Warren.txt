

'''John Robin Warren''' Order of Australia|AC (born 11 June 1937 in Adelaide) is an Australian pathology|pathologist, Nobel Prize|Nobel winner and researcher. In 1979, he discovered the bacterium ''Helicobacter pylori'', together with Barry Marshall.

 Life and career 

He received his Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery|MBBS degree from the University of Adelaide, having completed his high school education at St Peter's College, Adelaide.  In 1967 he was became a member the Royal College of Pathologists of Australia and became a senior pathologist at the Royal Perth, Western Australia|Perth Hospital where he spent most of his career.  At the University of Western Australia with Barry Marshall|Barry J. Marshall, Warren proved that the bacterium is the cause of stomach ulcers.Warren JR, Marshall BJ. ''Unidentified curved bacilli in the stomach patients with gastritis and peptic ulceration.'' Lancet 1984;1(8390):1311–1315. PMID 6145023  Warren helped develop an easy diagnostic test (Urea breath test|14C-urea breath-test) for finding ''H. pylori'' in ulcer patients.Surveyor I, Goodwin CS, Mullan BP, Geelhoed E, Warren JR, Murray RN, Waters TE, Sanderson CR. ''The 14C-urea breath-test for the detection of gastric ''Campylobacter pylori'' infection.'' Med J Aust. 1989; 151(8):435–439. PMID 2593958.  In 2005, Warren and Marshall were awarded the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine|Nobel Prize in Medicine.

He married Winifred Theresa Warren and together they had five children. Winifred Warren is a psychiatrist.

An Australian documentary was made in 2006 about Warren and Marshall's road to the Nobel Prize, called "The Winner's Guide to the Nobel Prize".  He was appointed a Order of Australia|Companion of the Order of Australia in 2007.
