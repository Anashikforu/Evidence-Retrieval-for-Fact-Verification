A '''county seat''' is the location of a County (United States)|county government, and is usually the largest, oldest, or most central town or city within a county. In the United Kingdom and Ireland, the name county town is used rather than county seat. 

In the United States|U.S. state of Louisiana, counties are called parishes and have '''parish seats''' rather than county seats; while in Alaska, counties are called Borough|boroughs and have '''borough seats'''.
