'''Eurydome''' or '''

Eurydome is about 3&nbsp;kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 23,231,000&nbsp;km in 723.359 days, at an inclination of 149° to the ecliptic (147° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.3770.

It was named in August 2003 after Eurydome in Greek mythology, who is sometimes described as the mother of the Charites|Graces by Zeus (Jupiter).
Eurydome belongs to the Pasiphaë group, non-Sphere|spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at distances ranging between 22,800,000 and 24,100,000&nbsp;km, and with inclinations ranging between 144.5° and 158.3°.
