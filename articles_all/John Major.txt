'''Sir John Major'''  (born 29 March 1943) is a British politician who was Prime Minister of the United Kingdom and Leader of the Conservative Party (UK)|Leader of the Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party from 1990 to 1997. He served as a member of the Cabinet of the United Kingdom|Cabinet under Margaret Thatcher as Chief Secretary to the Treasury (1987&ndash;1989), Foreign Secretary (1989) and Chancellor of the Exchequer (1989&ndash;1990).

 Early life and career 
Major was born at St Helier Hospital and Queen Mary's Hospital for Children in St Helier, London, in England and left school at the age of 16. He became a Conservative councillor in 1968. At the 1979 United Kingdom general election|1979 general election, Major was elected Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament (MP) for Huntingdonshire (UK Parliament constituency)|Huntingdonshire (Huntingdon (UK Parliament constituency)|Huntingdon after 1983).

Prime Minister
After holding several cabinet positions, Major was elected to replace Margaret Thatcher as Prime Minister and party leader. On taking office, he saw British action in the Gulf War (1991) and his party's narrow re-election in the 1992 United Kingdom general election|1992 general election. 

After this, the Conservatives under Major became unpopular because of the "Black Wednesday" stock market crash in late 1992 and how they became divided over the issue of European Union. Beaten at the 1997 United Kingdom general election|1997 general election, Major stood down as Prime Minister for Tony Blair of the Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party, and was replaced as Conservative leader by William Hague. He stood down as an MP at the 2001 United Kingdom general election|2001 general election.

Personal life
Major married Norma Johnson (now (Dame) Norma Major ) on 3 October 1970. She was a teacher, and a member of the Young Conservatives. They became engaged after only ten days.

He is a keen follower of cricket, motor racing, and also a supporter of Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea Football Club.

Major has so far declined a life peerage on standing down from British House of Commons|Parliament.
