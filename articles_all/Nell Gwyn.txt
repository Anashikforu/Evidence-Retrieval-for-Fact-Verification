
'''Eleanor "Nell" Gwyn''' (or '''Gwynn''' or '''Gwynne''') (1650 - 14 November 1687), was one of the earliest England|English actresses to receive prominent recognition, and a long-time mistress (lover)|mistress of King Charles II of England|Charles II.

Called "pretty, witty Nell" by Samuel Pepys, she has been called a living embodiment of the spirit of English Restoration|Restoration England and has come to be considered a folk heroine, with a story echoing the rags-to-royalty tale of Cinderella.
