The '''Musée d'Orsay''' is an art museum in Paris. The building was built in 1900, and first used as a railway station. In 1986, it became the museum, and it is still very famous today. 

The museum exhibits artworks of the 19th century including Impressionism|Impressionist paintings. The Impressionist paintings include works by Monet, Degas, Renoir, Cézanne, Manet, and Vincent van Gogh|Van Gogh. That's why the Orsay is called the 'Impressionism museum'. 

The museum holds mainly French art dating from 1848 to 1915, including paintings, sculptures, furniture, and photography. Its extensive collection of impressionist and post-Impressionist|post-impressionist masterpieces is the largest in the world. It features work by such painters such as Monet, Manet, Degas, Renoir, Cézanne, Seurat, Alfred Sisley|Sisley, Gauguin, Van Gogh and many others.
