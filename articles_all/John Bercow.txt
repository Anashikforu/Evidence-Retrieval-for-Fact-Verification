'''John Simon Bercow''' (surname pronounce ''BER-COE''; born 19 January 1963) is a British people|British politician who was the Speaker of the House of Commons|Speaker of the House of Commons of the United Kingdom from 2009 through 2019. He was the Member of Parliament (MP) for the constituency of Buckingham.

 Biography 
John Bercow was active in the Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party from a young age and was a Conservative councillor, for Lambeth, from 1986 to 1990. He stood as a candidate to become an MP at the United Kingdom general election, 1987|1987 and United Kingdom general election, 1992|1992 General Elections but was not successful. At the United Kingdom general election, 1997|1997 General Election, Bercow was elected the Conservative MP for Buckingham. He served in the shadow cabinets of Iain Duncan Smith and Michael Howard.
Bercow is the first British Jews|Jewish Speaker.
But, along with tradition, Bercow does now display his coat of arms at Speaker's House.
On 9 September 2019, Bercow told the House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons that he would stand down on 31 October, or at the next general election, whichever occurs first. He officially left the office on 4 November 2019.
In 2021, Bercow became a member of the Labour Party, saying that Boris Johnson's leadership was making his old party "reactionary, Right-wing populist|populist, British nationalism|nationalistic and sometimes even xenophobic".
