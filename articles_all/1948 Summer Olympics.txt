
 

The '''1948 Summer Olympics''', officially known as the '''Games of the XIV Olympiad''', were held at London in the United Kingdom.Sports Reference.com (SR/Olympics), 
These were the first Summer Olympics to be held since the 1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin, because no Games were held during World War II.

A total of 59 nations sent athletes to compete at the London Games.Taking part in the games for the first time were British Guiana (now Guyana), Burma (now Myanmar), Ceylon (now Sri Lanka), Iran, Iraq, Jamaica, South Korea|Korea, Lebanon, Pakistan, Puerto Rico, Singapore, Syria, Trinidad and Tobago, and Venezuela.
