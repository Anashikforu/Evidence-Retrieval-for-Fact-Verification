

The '''Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs''' (known as the '''Foreign Secretary''') is the Minister (government)|Minister in charge of the United Kingdom's Foreign and Commonwealth Office. In many countries this job is called Foreign minister.

Until the 1960s, the Foreign Secretary only handled relations with foreign countries and international organisations. There was a separate Commonwealth of Nations|Commonwealth Secretary and a Colonial Secretary, but all three are now joined into one.

Dominic Raab has been Foreign Secretary since 24 July 2019.

The Foreign Secretary's is one of the four Great Offices of State.
