

'''Harlan Jay Ellison''' (May 27, 1934 &ndash; June 27, 2018) was an award-winning United States|American writer of short stories, novellas, teleplays, essays, and criticism. His most famous work is in the science fiction area, where he won many Hugo Award|Hugo and Nebula Award|Nebula awards.

Ellison died from cardiac arrest on June 27, 2018 at his Sherman Oaks, Los Angeles home, aged 84.
Works
 ''Web of the City'' (1958) (originally published as ''Rumble'')
 ''Spider Kiss'' (1961) (originally published as ''Rockabilly'')
 "A Boy and his Dog"
 ''The Starlost #1: Phoenix Without Ashes'' (1975)
