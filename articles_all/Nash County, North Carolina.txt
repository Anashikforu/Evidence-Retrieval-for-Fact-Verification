
'''Nash County''' is a county in the U.S. state of North Carolina.  As of 2000, 87,420 people lived there. Its county seat is Nashville, North Carolina|Nashville.

 History 
The county was made in 1777 from Edgecombe County, North Carolina|Edgecombe County. It was named for Brigadier General Francis Nash, who had been wounded that year at the Battle of Germantown.

In 1855 parts of Nash County, Edgecombe County, North Carolina|Edgecombe County, Johnston County, North Carolina|Johnston County, and Wayne County, North Carolina|Wayne County were combined to make Wilson County, North Carolina|Wilson County.

 Government 
Nash County is part of the regional Upper Coastal Plain Council of Governments.
