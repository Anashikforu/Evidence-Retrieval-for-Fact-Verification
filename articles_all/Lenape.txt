
The '''Lenape''', '''Lenappe''', '''Lenapi''' or '''Lenni Lenape''' (meaning "''the people''" or "''true people''") are a group of several bands of Native American people who share culture|cultural and linguistics|linguistic wikt:trait|traits. They are also known as the '''Delaware Indians'''. They spoke two similar languages known as the Delaware languages: ''Unami'' and ''Munsee''. 

The Lenape lived in the area called '''Lenapehoking''', roughly the area around and between the Delaware River|Delaware and lower Hudson Rivers. These areas are known today as the U.S. states of New Jersey, eastern Pennsylvania around the Delaware and Lehigh valleys. They also lived on the north shore of Delaware and much of southeastern New York, mostly the lower Hudson Valley and Upper New York Bay. Nowadays most live in Oklahoma, Wisconsin and Ontario.
