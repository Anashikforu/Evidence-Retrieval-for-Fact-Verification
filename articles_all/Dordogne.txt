

'''Dordogne''' () is a departments of France|department in the southwest of France. It is named after the Dordogne (river)|Dordogne river that runs through the department.

The department is in the Regions of France|region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine. It is between the valley of the Loire River|Loire river and the Pyrenees. Its Prefectures in France|prefecture (Capital city|capital) is Périgueux.

History
Dordogne was formed during the French Revolution on 4 March 1790 with parts of the old province of Périgord. It was divided into nine districts: Belvès, Bergerac, Excideuil, Montignac, Montpon, Nontron, Périgueux, Ribérac and Sarlat.

Bergerac, Dordogne|Bergerac, Périgueux and Sarlat-la-Canéda were alternatively the Capital (city)|capital of the department but later it was decided that only Périgueux would be the capital.

The main river in the department is the Dordogne (river)|Dordogne that gives its name to the department. It runs from east to west through the department. Other rivers are the Isle (river)|Isle and the Vézère, both Tributary|tributaries of the Dordogne.

The highest point in the department is the southwestern summit of the ''Forêt de Vieillecour'' (.

Administration
Dordogne is part of the region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine. It is managed by the ''Departamental Council of Dordogne'' in Périgueux.

=Administrative divisions=
There are 4 Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' (districts), 25 Cantons in France|''cantons'' and 520 Communes in France|''communes'' (Municipality|municipalities) in Dordogne.

Demographics
The inhabitants of Dordogne are known, in French language|French, as ''Dordognais'' (women: ''Dordognaises''). They are also known as ''Périgordins'' or ''Périgoudins''.

in 2014, the department of Dordogne had a population of 416,350, for a population density of  inhabitants/km2. The ''arrondissement'' of Périgueux, with 189,301 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other three, Bergerac, Nontron and Sarlat-la-Canéda, have respectively 111,875, 40,508 and 74,666 inhabitants.
