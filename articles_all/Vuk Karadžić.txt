
'''Vuk Stefanović Karadžić''' (Serbian language|Serbian alphabet: Вук Стефановић Караџић; November 7, 1787 – February 7, 1864) was a Serbs|Serbian linguistics|language mistakes fixer and pioneer. He first made Serbian language as the Serbian that people talk today. Vuk had largely changed Serbian language of that time, he collected folk songs from Serbian villagers and he was the author of the first Serbian dictionary.
