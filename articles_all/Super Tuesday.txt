In the United States, '''Super Tuesday''' commonly refers to the Tuesday in early February or March of a U.S. presidential election|presidential election year when the greatest number of states hold United States presidential primary|primary elections to select delegates to United States presidential nominating convention|national conventions. Each Political party|party's President of the United States|presidential candidates are officially nominated. 

More delegates can be won on Super Tuesday than on any other single day of the primary calendar, and accordingly, candidates seeking the presidency traditionally must do well on this day to secure their party's nomination. 

In 2008, Super Tuesday was February 5; 24 states held primaries or caucuses on this date, with 52 percent of all pledged Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party delegates and 41 percent of the total United States Republican Party|Republican Party delegates at stake.
