

'''Denis Diderot''' (; 5 October 1713 – 31 July 1784) was a French people|French philosopher, art critic and writer. He was a known figure during the Age of Enlightenment|Enlightenment. He is best known for serving as co-founder, chief editor and contributor to the ''Encyclopédie'' along with Jean le Rond d'Alembert.Jacques Smietanski, "Le Réalisme dans Jacques le Fataliste" (Paris: Nizet, 1965). 

Diderot died from pulmonary thrombosis in Paris, aged 70.
