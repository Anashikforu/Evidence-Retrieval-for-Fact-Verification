


'''Merseyside''' is a metropolitan county in North West England, with a population of 1,365,900. Taking its name from the River Mersey, the title "Merseyside" came into existence as a metropolitan county in 1974.

Merseyside is divided into two parts by the River Mersey|Mersey estuary: the Metropolitan Borough of Wirral|Wirral is on the west side of the estuary upon the The Wirral Peninsula|Wirral Peninsula; the rest of the county is on the east side. The northern part of Merseyside borders onto Lancashire to the north, Greater Manchester to the east, both parts border Cheshire to the south.

Local government
=Metropolitan boroughs=
Merseyside contains the metropolitan boroughs of Liverpool, England|Liverpool, Knowsley (borough)|Knowsley, Sefton, Metropolitan Borough of St Helens|St Helens and the Wirral (borough)|Wirral.

Settlements
Merseyside is divided into two parts by the River Mersey|Mersey estuary, the Metropolitan Borough of Wirral|Wirral is on the west side of the estuary, upon the The Wirral Peninsula|Wirral Peninsula and the rest of the county is on the east side of the estuary.

The two parts are linked by two road tunnels, a railway tunnel, and the famous Mersey Ferry. Other districts that are part of the urban area (but not part of Merseyside) are Ellesmere Port and Neston and Halton (borough)|Halton. The designation "Greater Merseyside" has been adopted for the area that includes Merseyside and Halton, the term "Liverpool City-Region" is less well-defined. Most of the region was once served by the now scraped MTL Trust Holdings Ltd transport company.
