

'''Afghanistan''' is in the middle of Asia. The country is landlocked and mountainous, and has most of the Hindu Kush mountains. There are four major rivers in the country: the Amu Darya, the Hari River, Afghanistan|Hari River, the Kabul River and the Helmand River. The country also contains a number of smaller rivers, lakes, and streams.

Borders
Afghanistan has 5529&nbsp;km of borders. They are with neighbouring countries:
#Pakistan: 2,430&nbsp;km, known as the Durand Line. This is in the south and southeast of Afghanistan. 
#Iran, to the west of Afghanistan. The border is 936&nbsp;km long 
#Tajikistan has a border of 1,206&nbsp;km with Afghanistan to the north 
#Turkmenistan has a border of 744&nbsp;km 
#Uzbekistan has a border of 137&nbsp;km
#Afghanistan also has a disputed border with China. It is 76&nbsp;km in length and is in the far northeast of the country. This is disputed by Pakistan and Tajikistan which both claim the area.

The borders are called "porous". That means that in many places people can cross over with not much official control.

Mountain systems

The Hindu Kush reaches a height of 7485&nbsp;m. / 24,557&nbsp;ft. at Nowshak, Afghanistan's highest peak. Of the ranges extending southwestward from the Hindu Kush, the Koh-i-Baba reaches the greatest height (Shah Fuladi, 5,142&nbsp;m /16,870&nbsp;ft). The Safed Koh range, which includes the Tora Bora area, dominates the border area southeast of Kabul.

The area towards the Khyber Pass across the Safed Koh are in eastern Afghanistan; at the top the height is 1070&nbsp;m. / 3,509&nbsp;ft. at Landi Kotal. This area is near to Pakistan, the town of Torkham is five kilometres from Pakistan.
