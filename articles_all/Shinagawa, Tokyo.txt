As of 2008, the ward has an estimated population of 344,461 and a population density|density of 15,740 persons per km². The total area is 22.72&nbsp;km².

History

The ward was founded on March 15, 1947 by combining Ebara Ward with the older Shinagawa Ward.
 Both Ebara Ward and Shinagawa Ward had been created in 1932 when Tokyo City expanded after the 1923 Great Kanto earthquake|Great Kantō earthquake.

Shinagawa is a transport hub with the busy Shinagawa Station nearby in Minato, Tokyo|Minato Ward.

Politics and government

Shinagawa is run by a city assembly of 40 elected members.
