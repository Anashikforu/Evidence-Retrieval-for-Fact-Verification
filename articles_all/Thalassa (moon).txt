


'''Thalassa''' or '''Neptune&nbsp;IV''', is the second closest moon to Neptune (planet)|Neptune. It was named after a daughter of Aether and Hemera from Greek mythology.
"Thalassa" is also the Greek language|Greek word for "sea".

Thalassa was discovered sometime before mid-September, 1989 from the images taken by the ''Voyager 2'' space probe|probe. It was given the designation '''S/1989&nbsp;N&nbsp;5'''. The discovery was said (IAUC 4867) on September 29, 1989, but the text only talks of "25 frames taken over 11 days", giving a discovery date of sometime before September 18. The name was given on 16 September 1991.

Thalassa is not a sphere and shows no sign of any geological changes. Unusually for a non-spherical moon, it appears to be oddly shaped not able to be explained.
