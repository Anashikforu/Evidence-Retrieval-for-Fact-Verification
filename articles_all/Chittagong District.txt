

'''Chittagong District''' () is a district in the south-eastern region of  Bangladesh. It is a part of the Chittagong Division. The Harbour|port city of Chittagong, the second largest city in Bangladesh, is in this district.

History
Because of its harbour, Chittagong had been an important place for trade, bringing Arab traders as early as the 9th century CE. The region fell under the rule of kings from Arakan in the 16th century|16th and 17th century|17th centuries, but later, the Mughal Empire|Mughal army under Islam Khan conquered Chittagong. During the 17th century, the region also faced a lot of attacks by Portugal|Portuguese pirates. The Mughals established Chittagong as a district in 1666. The Chittagong Hill Tracts were separated from Chittagong in 1860. Cox's Bazar was separated into a district in 1984.
People
