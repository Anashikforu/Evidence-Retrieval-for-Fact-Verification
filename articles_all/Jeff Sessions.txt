
'''Jefferson Beauregard''' "'''Jeff'''" '''Sessions III''' (born December 24, 1946) is an American politician. He was the 84th United States Attorney General serving from February 9, 2017 to November 7, 2018.

Before being Attorney General, he was the Seniority in the United States Senate|junior United States Senate|United States Senator from Alabama. He is a member of the Republican Party (United States)|Republican Party. At the time of his senate career, he ranked Seniority in the United States Senate|15th in seniority in the United States Senate. He was the most senior junior Senator upon the retirement of Barbara Boxer in January 2017 to February 2017.

In November 2019, Sessions announced that he would run for his old United States Senate|Senate seat in 2020. He lost the Republican nomination to Tommy Tuberville.

Early life
Sessions was born in Selma, Alabama on December 24, 1946. He was raised in Camden, Alabama. Sessions earned Bachelor of Arts|B.A. Degree from Huntingdon College and a Juris Doctor|J.D. Degree from the University of Alabama.

Early career
From 1981 to 1993, he was United States District Court for the Southern District of Alabama|U.S. Attorney for the Southern District of Alabama. Sessions was elected Attorney General of Alabama in 1994.

United States senator (1997&ndash;2017)
Sessions was elected to the U.S. Senate in United States Senate election in Alabama, 1996|1996 and re-elected in United States Senate election in Alabama, 2002|2002, United States Senate election in Alabama, 2008|2008, and United States Senate election in Alabama, 2014|2014. Sessions was considered one of the most conservative members of the U.S. Senate.

As a senator, he is known for being against illegal immigration and for reducing legal immigration. He supported the major legislative efforts of the George W. Bush administration, including the Economic Growth and Tax Relief Reconciliation Act of 2001|2001 and Jobs and Growth Tax Relief Reconciliation Act of 2003|2003 tax cut packages, the Iraq War, and a proposed Federal Marriage Amendment|national amendment to ban same-sex marriage.

He opposed the Emergency Economic Stabilization Act of 2008|establishment of the Troubled Asset Relief Program, the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009|2009 stimulus bill, the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act, and the Don't Ask, Don't Tell Repeal Act of 2010|''Don't Ask, Don't Tell'' Repeal Act. As the ranking Republican on the United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Senate Judiciary Committee, he opposed all three of President Barack Obama's nominees for the United States Supreme Court|Supreme Court.

An early supporter of Donald Trump presidential campaign, 2016|Donald Trump's 2016 presidential campaign, Sessions was considered as a possible Vice President of the United States|Vice Presidential nominee, but Trump finally chose Governor of Indiana|Indiana governor Mike Pence.

Sessions resigned from the senate to become the United States Attorney General on February 8, 2017.

United States Attorney General (2017&ndash;2018)
On November 18, 2016, it was announced that President-elect of the United States|President-elect Donald Trump planned to nominate Sessions for United States Attorney General.

On January 10, 2017, the United States Senate Committee on the Judiciary|Senate Judiciary Committee hearings on his nomination began. The senate narrowly confirmed his nomination on February 8, 2017. He was sworn-in by Vice President Mike Pence on February 9. On May 7, 2018 he announced a new morally questionable policy that separated children from their mothers at the border.

As U.S. Attorney General, Sessions overturned a memo delivered by Eric Holder to reduce mass incarceration by avoiding mandatory sentencing,.

On November 7, 2018, President Trump fired Sessions as Attorney General in a tweet.
2020 United States Senate race
In October 2019, Sessions began exploring a potential candidacy for his old Senate seat in the 2020 United States Senate election in Alabama|2020 election. He announced his Senate run on November 7, 2019.
Sessions lost the Alabama Senate Primary to Tommy Tuberville on July 14, 2020.

Personal life
Sessions and his wife Mary have three children and six grandchildren.
