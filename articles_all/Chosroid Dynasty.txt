The '''Chosroids''' (Khosroviani, Georgian: ხოსროვიანი) were a dynasty of the monarch|kings and later of the ruling princes of the early Georgia (country)|Georgian state of Kingdom of Iberia|Iberia, natively known as Kartli, from the fourth to the ninth centuries. Presumably of Iranian origin and a branch of the Mihranid House, the family accepted Christianity as their official religion around the year 337, and maneuvered between the Byzantine Empire and Sassanid, Iran to retain a degree of independence.
