
 was a Japanese Association football|football player. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team.

Biography
Shigematsu was born in Hiroshima on April 2, 1930. When he played for Keio University, he won 1952 Emperor's Cup as a member of All Keio. After graduating from Keio University, he joined his local club Sanfrecce Hiroshima|Toyo Industries in 1954. He won the 2nd place at 1954 and 1957 Emperor's Cup. At 1954 Emperor's Cup, it was first Emperor's Cup finalist as a works team.

In May 1958, Shigematsu was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for Football at the 1958 Asian Games|1958 Asian Games. At this competition, on May 28, he debuted against Hong Kong national football team|Hong Kong.

After retirement, in 1974 Shigematsu became a president of his local baseball club Hiroshima Toyo Carp. In 1981, he moved to Shonan Bellmare|Fujita Industries (later ''Bellmare Hiratsuka'') and became a president of the club in 1997. In 1999, he left the club.

In 2018, Shigematsu died at the age of 88.
