

The '''Mausoleum at Halicarnassus''', or '''Tomb of Mausolus''',, modern Turkish language|Turkish: Halikarnas Mozolesi. "''Mausoleion''"  meant "''building dedicated to Mausolus''". was a tomb built between 353 and 350&nbsp;BC at Halicarnassus (present Bodrum, Turkey). It was built   for Mausolus, a satrap in the Achaemenid Empire|Persian Empire, and Artemisia II of Caria, who was both his wife and his sister.

The building was designed by the Ancient Greece|Greek architects Satyros and Pythius of Priene.
 It was such an accomplishment that Antipater of Sidon|Antipater of Sidon said it was one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The word ''mausoleum'' has come to be used generically for any grand tomb.
