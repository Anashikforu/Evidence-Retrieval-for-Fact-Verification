The '''Orthodox Autocephalous Church of Albania''' is one of the Eastern Orthodox Church|Eastern Orthodox churches. It is very new. It was created in 1922 by the fathers of the Albanian Orthodoxy Fan Noli, Visarion Xhuvani, and others. The Albanian Orthodox Church is autocephaly|autocephalous, meaning its bishop does not have to answer before any superior clergy|clerical authority.

Since its beginning it has had a difficult time. The church suffered during the Second World War, and in the communism|communist period that came after. This became worse after 1967 when Albania was declared an atheism|atheist state, and no public or private expression of religion was allowed. After religious freedom returned in 1991, the church has become very active again. More than 250 churches have been created or repaired, and more than 100 clergy being ordained. 

The Church currently has four dioceses, Tirana, Durrës and Elbasan; Berat and Kanina; Gjirokastër; and Korçë.
