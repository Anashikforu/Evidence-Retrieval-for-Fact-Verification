


The '''aphelion''' is the point in the orbit of an object where it is farthest from the Sun. The point in orbit where an object is nearest to the sun is called the perihelion. The word ''aphelion'' derives from the Greek language|Greek words, ''apo'' meaning ''away, off, apart'' and ''Helios''. 

The Earth's orbit has its aphelion around July the third , at which it is approximately 152.1 million kilometers or 94.5 million miles from the Sun.
