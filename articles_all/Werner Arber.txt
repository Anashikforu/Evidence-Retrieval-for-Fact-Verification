

'''Werner Arber''' (born 3 June 1929)
 is a Switzerland|Swiss microbiology|microbiologist and genetics|geneticist. He won the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1978 for his discovery of restriction endonucleases which he shared with Daniel Nathans and Hamilton O. Smith.
