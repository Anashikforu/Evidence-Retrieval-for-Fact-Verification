 

'''Stone Temple Pilots''' (often called '''STP''') is an United States|American rock music|rock band that played in the 1990s and early 2000s. They broke up in 2003 but reformed in 2008. The band has Scott Weiland (lead vocals|vocals), brothers Robert DeLeo|Robert (bass guitar, backing vocals|vocals) and Dean DeLeo (guitar), and Eric Kretz (drumkit|drums, percussion). The band released the greatest-hits album ''Thank You (Stone Temple Pilots album)| Thank You'' in 2003 in music|2003. The band then fell apart. Since the band's end, Weiland moved on to become the frontman of Velvet Revolver with former members of Guns N' Roses. The DeLeo brothers started the band Army of Anyone with Richard Patrick from the band Filter (band)|Filter. Army of Anyone released its first album in 2006, which was also called ''Army of Anyone''. Kretz started Bomb Shelter Studios  in Los Angeles. 

In 2008, Stone Temple Pilots reformed. They went on a concert tour that began on May 1 and ended on October 30. In a short time, they will return to the studio to record a seventh album.

History
Scott Weiland and Robert DeLeo met during a concert. They formed a band with two of Scott's friends from Edison High School, Huntington Beach|Edison High School, Corey Hicock (guitar) and David Allen (drums). During the recording, they got a call from their lawyer who told them that there was a Blues music|blues man who had already claimed the name Mighty Joe Young (musician)|Mighty Joe Young. They changed the band's name to Shirley Temple's Pussy and performed under this name for a short time, but then changed the name again due to record label pressure.
