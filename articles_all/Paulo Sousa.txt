
'''Paulo Manuel Carvalho de Sousa''', :en:Order_of_Prince_Henry|CavIH (; born 30 August 1970) is a Portuguese :en:Association_football|football :en:Manager_(association_football)|manager and former professional player. He is the head coach of the :en:Poland_national_football_team|Poland national team.

He played for S.L. Benfica|Benfica and Sporting CP|Sporting in Portugal. He then went to Juventus F.C.|Juventus and after to Borussia Dortmund. He won the UEFA Champions League in both of these clubs.

Sousa was a member of Portugal's ":en:Golden_generation#Portugal_(2000â€“2006)|Golden Generation". and appeared with the :en:Portugal_national_football_team|national team at the :en:2002_FIFA_World_Cup|2002 World Cup and two :en:UEFA_European_Championship|European Championships.

He started coaching in the late 2000s, managing clubs in several countries and winning national championships with :en:Maccabi_Tel_Aviv_F.C.|Maccabi Tel Aviv and :en:FC_Basel|Basel.

He currently is the manager of the Polish national team.
