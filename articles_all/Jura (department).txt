

'''Jura''' is a departments of France|department in the east of France in the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté Regions of France|region. It is named after the Jura mountains.

The largest city in the department is Dole, Jura|Dole but its Prefectures in France|prefecture (Capital city|capital) is the city of Lons-le-Saunier.

 History 
Historically, Jura belonged to the County of Burgundy|Free County of Burgundy, known in French as the Franche-Comté. Dole, Jura|Dole was the capital until the region was conquered by Louis XIV of France|Louis XIV. Louis XIV moved the capital to Besançon and Dole is now a ''Prefectures in France|sous-préfecture'' of Jura.

As early as the 13th century, inhabitants of the southern 2/3 of Jura spoke a dialect of Arpitan language. It continued to be spoken in rural areas into the 20th century.

Jura is one of the original 83 departments created during the French Revolution on March 4, 1790. It was created from part of the former province of Free County. The department was created with six districts: Dole, Lons-le-Saunier, Orgelet, Poligny, Saint-Claude and Salins; the capital was Lons-le-Saunier alternating with Dole, Poligny and Salins but from 1794 only Lons-le-Saunier remained as the capital.

In 1800, with the creation of the Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' in France, the six districts were changed into four ''arrondissements'': Lons-le-Saunier, Dole, Poligny and Saint-Claude. On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Poligny was eliminated.

The highest point in the department is Crêt Pela ( high; it is also the highest mountain of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region. The ''Crêt Pela'' mountain is in the ''Forêt du Massacre'', Jura Mountains|Jura mountain range.

Many lakes can be found throughout the Jura department. The largest natural lake in the department is the Lac De Chalain. It is  wide. Lac de Vouglans was formed after the building of a hydro-electric dam; it is one of the largest man-made lakes in France.

The Ain (river)|Ain river starts in the Jura department but it is the main river of the Ain department. The Doubs (river)|Doubs also flows through a part of the department. Many Tributary|tributaries of both rivers flow, partially or completely, in the Jura department.

= Climate =
The climate of the Jura varies greatly by elevation. The lower valleys are temperate and pleasant, but the high mountain valleys have bitterly cold winters.

Administration
Jura is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Jura'' in Lons-le-Saunier. The department is part of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region.

 Economy 
Jura is a wine-growing region. The Jura wines are very distinctive and unusual wines, such as vin jaune, which is made by a similar process to sherry. It develops under a flor of yeast. This is made from the local Savagnin grape variety. Other grape varieties include Poulsard, Bastardo grape|Trousseau, and Chardonnay.

 Tourism 
The Jura mountains provide ample opportunities for hiking, skiing, mountain biking and other winter sports.
