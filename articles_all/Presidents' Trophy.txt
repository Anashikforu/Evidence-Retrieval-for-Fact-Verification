

The '''Presidents' Trophy''' is an award presented by the National Hockey League (NHL) to the team that finishes with the most points in the league during the regular season. If two teams wikt:tie|tie for the most points, then the trophy goes to the team with the most wins. The winning team is also awarded Canadian dollar|$350,000 in cash bonuses. The Presidents' Trophy has been awarded 27 times to 15 different teams since first being awarded in 1985. The most recent winner is the Tampa Bay Lightning for the  NHL season.

 History 
The trophy was wikt:introduction|introduced at the start of the 1985-86 NHL season|1985–86 NHL season by the league's Board of Governors. Before the 1985–86 NHL season, the best team in the league during the regular season was allowed to hang a banner stating "NHL League Champions". provided the team goes that far, so it remains the most likely position to produce the cup winner.

From 1937 to 1968, the same rules now used for winning the Presidents' Trophy were used to award the Prince of Wales Trophy. 

The only teams to have won the Presidents' Trophy more than twice are the Detroit Red Wings with six and the Washington Capitals with three. However, the Montreal Canadiens have finished first overall 21 times, the most times in league history, although they have yet to win the Presidents' Trophy. Detroit is second with 18 first-overall finishes.
