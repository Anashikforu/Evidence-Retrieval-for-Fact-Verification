
The '''890s''' was the decade that started on January 1, 890 and ended on December 31, 899.

Events
 891  &ndash; Battle of Leuven (891) between the Franks and the Vikings
 893 December 28 &ndash; An 893 Dvin earthquake|earthquake destroys the city of Dvin (ancient city)|Dvin in Armenia.
 Byzantine–Bulgarian war of 894–896
 896 April &ndash; Pope Boniface VI succeeds Pope Formosus as the 112th pope.
 896 May &ndash; Pope Stephen VI (also referred to as Stephen VII) succeeds Pope Boniface VI as the 113th pope.
 897 August &ndash; Pope Romanus succeeds Pope Stephen VI as the 114th pope.
 897 November &ndash; Pope Romanus is deposed.
 897 December &ndash; Pope Theodore II succeeds Pope Romanus as the 115th pope, but dies twenty days later.
 897 &ndash; Emperor Uda of Japan is succeeded by Emperor Daigo.
 898 January &ndash; Pope John IX succeeds Pope Theodore II|Theodore II as the 116th pope.
 899 - Edward the Elder becomes King of Wessex.
