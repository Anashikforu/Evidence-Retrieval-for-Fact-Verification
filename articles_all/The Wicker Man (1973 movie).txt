

'''''The Wicker Man''''' is a cult movie|cult 1973 in movies|1973 United Kingdom|British horror movie, made and set in Scotland. The movie was movie director|directed by Robin Hardy and screenwriter|written by Anthony Shaffer. It stars Edward Woodward, Christopher Lee, Diane Cilento, Ingrid Pitt and Britt Ekland. Paul Giovanni composer|composed the soundtrack.

A police sergeant, Neil Howie goes to a remote island in the Hebrides to investigate the disappearance of a young girl, Rowan. He finds out that the island is run by a pagan cult who are hostile of outsiders, including him. They practice human sacrifice. He believes that Rowan will be used as their human sacrifice. The islanders force Neil into a wicker man and burn him to death.

''The Wicker Man'' is generally very highly regarded by critics. Movie magazine ''Cinefantastique'' described it as "The ''Citizen Kane'' of Horror Movies", and in 2004 the magazine ''Total Movie'' named ''The Wicker Man'' the sixth greatest British movie of all time. It also won the 1978 Saturn Award for Saturn Award for Best Horror Film|Best Horror movie. A scene from this movie was #45 on Bravo (television network)|Bravo's ''100 Scariest Movie Moments''.
