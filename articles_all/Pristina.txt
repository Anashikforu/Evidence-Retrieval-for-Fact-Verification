


'''Pristina''', It is in the north-eastern part of Kosovo, close to the Goljak|Goljak mountains.

 History 

In ancient history, the Vinča culture lived in the area.
In 1912, Albanian rebels led by Hasan Pristina took the city. 

On 17 April 1941, Yugoslavia surrendered to the Axis Powers. On 29 June, Benito Mussolini said that there would be a Greater Albania, and that Kosovo would be a part of it. After that, Italian and Albanian Fascism|fascists killed lots of Serbs, and tens of thousands left Kosovo.Sabrina P. Ramet 
After World War II, Yugoslavia became a communist country. Large parts of the town were destroyed to try to make room for new buildings, including most of the town center. This included a lot of buildings that had been built by the Ottomans. Pristina got a lot of money from the Yugoslav government because it was the capital of the Autonomous Province of Kosovo and Metohija, so it developed quickly. The University of Pristina (1969–1999)|University of Pristina was founded, lots of high-rise apartment blocks were built, and a new industrial zone near the city attracted a lot of people from other parts of Kosovo. Pristina's population increased very quickly, from 69,514 in 1971 to 109,208 in 1981. But this period of quick growth was soon ended by an economic and social crisis. After Josip Broz Tito|Tito died, the many different ethnic groups in Yugoslavia started having troubles with each other. This included the Serbs and Albanians.Reuters 1986-05-27, "Kosovo Province Revives Yugoslavia's Ethnic Nightmare"Christian Science Monitor 1986-07-28, "Tensions among ethnic groups in Yugoslavia begin to boil over"
