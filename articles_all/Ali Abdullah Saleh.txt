
'''Ali Abdullah Saleh''' (; born 21 March 1942 – 4 December 2017) was a Yemeni politician. He was the first President of Yemen|President of the Republic of Yemen. Before that time, Saleh served as the President of North Yemen|President of the Yemen Arab Republic (North Yemen) from 1978 until 1990. In 1990, he assumed the office of chairman of the Presidential Council of the Republic of Yemen (North & South Yemen). He was the longest-serving president of Yemen, ruling since 1978.

In December 2017, Saleh said he would not support the Houthi movement|Houthis and instead sided with his former enemies – Saudi Arabian-led intervention in Yemen|Saudi Arabia and president Hadi.
