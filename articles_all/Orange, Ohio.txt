


'''Orange''' is a village of Cuyahoga County, Ohio|Cuyahoga County, Ohio, United States.   It is a suburb of the big city of Cleveland, Ohio|Cleveland.  The population was 3,236 people as of 2000.

Orange was first settled in 1815, and was founded as a township in 1820.  In 1831, Orange became the birthplace of United States president James Garfield|James A. Garfield.

Orange was made into a village in 1929.
