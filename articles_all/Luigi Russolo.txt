
'''Luigi Russolo''' (30 April 1883 – 4 February 1947)  was a painter and musician from Italy. He was part of the Futurism movement and built his own musical instruments. His instruments were destroyed in World War II in Paris, because the Nazi Germany|Germans bombed the city, others have simply disappeared.Barclay Brown, "The Noise Instruments of Luigi Russolo", ''Perspectives of New Music'' 20, nos. 1 & 2 (Fall-Winer 1981, Spring-Summer 1982): 31–48; citation on 36
