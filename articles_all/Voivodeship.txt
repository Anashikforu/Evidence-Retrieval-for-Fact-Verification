A '''voivodeship''', also spelled '''voivodship''', '''voivodina''' or '''vojvodina''' (Romanian language|Romanian: ''voievodat'', Polish language|Polish: ''województwo'', Serbian language|Serbian: ''vojvodina'' (војводина), ''vojvodstvo'' (војводство) or ''vojvodovina'' (војводовина), Hungarian language|Hungarian: ''vajdaság'', Lithuanian language|Lithuanian: ''vaivadija'', Latin ''Palatinatus'' in Poland), is a geography|geographical administrative division dating to medieval Romania, Hungary, Poland, Lithuania, Latvia, Russia and Serbia (see Vojvodina), ruled by a voivode|voivod (''voivode'', ''wojewoda''). The voivod (literal translation: "the one who leads the warriors", is the same as to Dux Exercituum / Herzog) was originally the military commander next to the ruler.
