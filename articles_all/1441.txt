

 Events of 1441 
 King's College, Cambridge|King's College, University of Cambridge, is founded by King Henry VI.
 Ouagadougou becomes the capital of the Mossi|Mossi Empire.
 Two Ethiopians attend an ecclesiastical council at Florence, as part of the negotiations concerning a possible union of Coptic Christianity|Coptic and Roman Catholicism|Roman Catholic Christianity. This is the earliest recorded contact of the Ethiopian Church with Europe.
 Revolt in Yucatán (state)|Yucatán capital Mayapan; Maya civilization falls into warring city-states.
 Christopher of Bavaria succeeds Charles VIII of Sweden|Karl Knutsson Borde as king of Sweden.
 Alfonso V of Aragon captures Naples after a five-month siege.
 With the help of the Grand Duchy of Lithuania, governor Haci I Giray|Haci Giray declares his province independent of the Golden Horde and establishes the Crimean Khanate.
 The Republic of Venice annexes the  seigniory of Ravenna, ending the Polentani|Da Polenta dynasty.
