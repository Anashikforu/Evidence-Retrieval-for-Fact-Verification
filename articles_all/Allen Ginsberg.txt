He was a friend of writers Jack Kerouac and William S. Burroughs, and later made friends with musicians Bob Dylan, Donovan, John Lennon and Yoko Ono, and many other celebrity|celebrities.

Ginsberg's most famous work was called ''Howl.'' It was a long poem about the social conditions of the United States in the 1950s. It began with the words "I saw the best minds of my generation destroyed by mental illness|madness." It went on to describe the frustration felt by many Americans, young people and :wikt:minority|minorities in particular. There were problems in the country, such as prejudice and wikt:tolerance|intolerance, that needed to be spoken about. Ginsberg :wikt:publish|published the poem, and also performed it at public poetry readings.

A few people did not like ''Howl'', or the wikt:graphic|graphic language Ginsberg used. They tried to have ''Howl'' :wikt:ban|banned, and they wanted Ginsberg to be wikt:prosecute|prosecuted (given a court trial, then either fined or put in jail). Ginsberg went to trial, and the judge decided ''Howl'' was an important work, and told the truth about America at the time. The judge rejected the ban and cleared Ginsberg, who could then continue to publish and perform the poem.

Ginsberg became involved in social causes through his work, and through the famous people he met. He grew up in the Judaism|Jewish faith, but later became a Buddhism|Buddhist.
Death
Ginsberg died in 1997 of liver cancer.
