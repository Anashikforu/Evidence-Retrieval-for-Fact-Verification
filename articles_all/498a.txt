'''Section 498-A''' is an offence under the Indian Penal Code, 1860. It was brought into the '''IPC''' in 1983 to stop cruelty to married women because of dowry. This often led to their deaths. It makes it a crime to be cruel to your wife or to your relative's wife. The law explains that "cruelty" means anything that is done on purpose, and either causes harm to the woman or makes the woman want to kill herself. It also makes the Law|laws against dowry or payment made by the bride's family to the husband's family stronger. It makes it a crime to Harassment|harass a woman to try to make her family pay a dowry.

If someone breaks this law he can be put in prison for up to 3 years with fine.

The law is criticized. The critics of the law say it allows a woman to ask the police to arrest her husband without any Evidence (law)|evidence or investigation, and that women have Abuse|abused the law in domestic disputes.
