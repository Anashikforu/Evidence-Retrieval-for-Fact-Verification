
 is a Japanese castle which is in Himeji, Hyōgo|Himeji in Hyōgo Prefecture. It includes 83 buildings made of wood. It is sometimes known as ''Hakurojō'' or ''Shirasagijō'' ("heron|White Heron Castle") because of its brilliant white exterior.

It is a Japanese National Cultural Treasure.  It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.UNESCO, 
Along with Matsumoto Castle and Kumamoto Castle, it is one of Japan's "Three Famous Castles", and is the most visited castle in Japan.

Several movies have been filmed at the castle, like Akira Kurosawa's ''Kagemusha'' and ''Ran (movie)|Ran''; ''The Last Samurai''  starring Tom Cruise; and the ''Shōgun (TV miniseries)|Shogun'' 1980 TV-miniseries/movie starring Richard Chamberlain.

In 2009, Himeiji Castle was wikt:recognize|recognized as one of the 100 Landscapes of Japan (Heisei period)|100 Landscapes of Japan which best show wikt:contemporary|contemporary Japan and its culture in the Heisei period.
