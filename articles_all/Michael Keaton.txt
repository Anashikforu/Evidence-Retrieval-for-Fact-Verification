
'''Michael John Douglas''' (born September 5, 1951), better known by the artistic name '''Michael Keaton''', is an American actor who became popular for his early comedic film roles, most notably his performance as the title character of Tim Burton's ''Beetlejuice (movie)|Beetlejuice'', and later gained international fame for his dramatic portrayal of Batman|Bruce Wayne / Batman in Tim Burton's ''Batman (1989 movie)|Batman'' (1989), ''Batman Returns'' (1992) and will play the role again in ''Batgirl (movie)|Batgirl'' (2022) and ''The Flash (movie)|The Flash'' (2023). He won a Golden Globe Award and was nominated for an Academy Award in 2015 for his role in ''Birdman (movie)|Birdman''. In 2016, he played Ray Kroc in ''The Founder''.

Early life
Keaton was born in Kennedy Township, Allegheny County, Pennsylvania|Kennedy Township, Pennsylvania. Keaton was raised in a Catholic family. He is of Irish and Scots-Irish ancestry. He attended Montour High School in Pennsylvania. Douglas studied speech for two years at Kent State University|Kent State, before dropping out and moving to Pittsburgh.

Politics
Keaton supported Senator Bernie Sanders for President of the United States|President in the United States presidential election, 2016|2016 U.S. presidential election.
