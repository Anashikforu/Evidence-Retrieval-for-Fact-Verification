
'''11001''' was one of the first British Railways diesel locomotives. It was built in 1949 at British Railways' Ashford railway works|Ashford Works. It was designed by Oliver Bulleid|O. V. S. Bulleid when he was Chief Mechanical Engineer of the Southern Railway (Great Britain)|Southern Railway. It was powered by a Paxman (engines)|Paxman RPH Series 1 engine, capable of delivering 500&nbsp;Horsepower|bhp at 1,250&nbsp;rpm. It was driven via a Vulcan Sinclair fluid coupling to an SSS (Synchro-Self-Shifting) Powerflow gearbox. The gearbox provided three forward and reverse gears in either high or low range. It gave a top speed ranging from 5&nbsp;Miles per hour|mph (8&nbsp;Kilometre per hour|km/h) in 1st gear, low range up to 36&nbsp;mph (58&nbsp;km/h). It had an 0-6-0 wheel formation.
