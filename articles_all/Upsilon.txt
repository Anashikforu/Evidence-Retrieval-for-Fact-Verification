

'''Upsilon''' (uppercase/lowercase '''Υ υ''') is the 20th letter of the Greek alphabet. It is used to represent the "u" sound in Ancient Greek and "i" in Modern Greek language|Modern Greek. In the system of Greek numerals, it has a value of 400. Letters that came from it include the Roman U, V and Y, and the Cyrillic У.

In mathematics, the uppercase \Upsilon is used to represent the upsilon function (a variant of Riemann zeta function), while the lowercase \upsilon is used as a general variable.
