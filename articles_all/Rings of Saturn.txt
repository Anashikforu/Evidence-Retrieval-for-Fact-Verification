
The '''rings of Saturn''' are the planetary rings around the planet Saturn (planet)|Saturn, which can be seen with a small telescope. They consist of many small particles, ranging in size from microscopic to the size of a bus (or bigger) that form clumps that orbit|move around Saturn. The particles in the rings are made almost entirely of ice|water ice, The ''Cassini Division'', discovered by Giovanni Domenico Cassini, is a gap between the A ring and the B ring. It is a product of orbital resonance.
