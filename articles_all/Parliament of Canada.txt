The '''Parliament of Canada ''' () is the Canadian federalism|federal Legislature|legislative branch of Canada, seated at Parliament Hill in the national capital, Ottawa. 
Parts
The parliament has three parts.  
# Queen Elizabeth II as Monarchy of Canada|Canadian monarch, represented by her Governor General of Canada|governor general
# The Senate of Canada|Senate, or "upper house" and 
# The House of Commons of Canada|House of Commons often called the "lower house"
Members
The governor general summons and appoints each of the 105 members of the upper house on the Advice (constitutional)|advice of the Prime Minister of Canada, while the 308 members of the lower house are directly elected by Canadian voters, with each Member of Parliament representing a single electoral district (Canada)|electoral district.
