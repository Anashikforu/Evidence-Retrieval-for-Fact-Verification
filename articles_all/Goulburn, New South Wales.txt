
'''Goulburn''' is a city in the Southern Tablelands of New South Wales, Australia. It is 190 kilometre|km south-west of Sydney, Australia|Sydney on the Hume Highway. It is 690 metres above sea-level. In 2006 there were 20,127 people living in Goulburn.{{Census 2006 AUS

 | accessdate = 2007-06-27}} Goulburn calls itself as "Australia's first inland city". The city of Bathurst, New South Wales|Bathurst also claims to be the first inland city in Australia. 

Goulburn was named by James Meehan after Henry Goulburn, a British politician. The Governor Lachlan Macquarie approved the choice of name. The Indigenous Australian|Aboriginal name for Goulburn is Burbong. This is a Murring/Wiradhuri language|Wiradjuri word meaning a special place.

Goulburn has a railway station on the Main Southern railway line. It is a service centre for the many farms in the area. It also provides a rest stop for those travelling on the Hume Highway. It has a central park and many historic buildings, including old houses near the railway station on Sloane Street. It also has two 19th-century cathedrals. It is home to the ''Big Merino'', the world's largest cement sheep.
