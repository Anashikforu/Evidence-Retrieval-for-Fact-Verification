

'''Emil Gustav Friedrich Martin Niemöller''' (14 January 1892 – 6 March 1984) was a Germans|German pastor who opposed the Nazi Germany|Nazis. At first he supported Hitler, but when the Nazis tried to control the churches he helped to form the Confessing Church, an association of anti-Nazi churches. Because of his beliefs, he was imprisoned in concentration camps, and almost got execution|executed. Niemöller is best known because he wrote the poem ''First they came...''.

He was born in Lippstadt. He died in Wiesbaden, aged 92.
