

'''Saburo Sakai''' (, ''Sakai Saburō'', August 25, 1916 – September 22, 2000) was a Japanese navy|naval pilot and fighter ace (''"Gekitsui-O"'') of the Imperial Japanese Navy during World War II. He was the 4th best Japanese pilot by number of enemy aircraft he shot down. He survived the war, and became a famous figure for his criticism of the War and the government of the Japanese Empire. He became a pacifism|pacifist and a Buddhism|Buddhist and promised that he would never again kill another living thing, even a mosquito. Only months before his death, Sakai told reporters that he still prayer|prayed for the souls of the Chinese, American, Australian and Dutch pilots he had killed. He also helped Microsoft design the popular computer game ''Combat Flight Simulator 2''.

Sakai claims he shot down 64 enemy airplanes. He shot down a B-32 Dominator on the last day of the war.
