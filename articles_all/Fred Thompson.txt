
'''Fred Dalton Thompson''' ('''Freddie Dalton Thompson'''; September 19, 1942 &ndash; November 1, 2015) was an Americans|American politician, actor, attorney, and lobbyist. 

Thompson became famous during the 1970s during the Watergate scandal|Watergate Scandal because he discovered the Watergate tapes which would lead to President Richard Nixon's resignation in 1973.



He starred on ''Law & Order (TV series)|Law & Order'' as Arthur Branch, the District Attorney of New York. He quit the show to run as the Republican Party (United States)|Republican candidate for the United States President of the United States|presidency in 2008, but he did not become the candidate. He was also the host of ''The Fred Thompson Show'', one of Westwood One's most popular talk shows.

On November 1, 2015, Thompson died from non-Hodgkin's lymphoma (NHL), a form of cancer, at the age of 73 in Nashville, Tennessee.
