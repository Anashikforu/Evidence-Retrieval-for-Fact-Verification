
'''Drôme''' () is a France|French Departments of France|department in southeastern France in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes Regions of France|region and named after the Drôme (river)|Drôme river. Its Prefectures in France|prefecture is the city of Valence, Drôme|Valence.

History
Drôme is one of the 83 French departments made during the French revolution, on 4 March 1790; it was made with part of the old French province of Dauphiné. It was divided in 6 districts: Romans, Valence, Die, Buis, Montélimar,and Crest. The Capital city|capital was Chabeuil but in the same year the capital was moved to Valence, Drôme|Valence.

With the creation of the ''arrondissements'', the 6 districts became four ''arrondissements'': Valence, Die, Montélimar and Nyons. In 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Montélimar was eliminated.

Demographics
The inhabitants of Drôme are known, in French language|French, as ''Drômois'' (women: ''Drômoises'').

Drôme has a population, in 2014, of 499,159, for a population density of  inhabitants/km2. The ''arrondissement'' of Valence, with 314,233 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Nyons and Die, have respectively 141,971 and 42,955 inhabitants.

The city with more people living in it is the Capital city|capital, Valence, Drôme|Valence (62,150 inhabitants). The subprefectures of Nyons and Die, Drôme|Die have, respectively, 6,641 and 4,516 inhabitants.
