


The '''City of Lancaster''' (2002 population: 133,914) is a Non-metropolitan district|local government district in Lancashire, England. Its main town is Lancaster, Lancashire|Lancaster, from which it obtained its city status. Other towns in the district include Morecambe, Heysham, Slyne, and Carnforth.

 History 
The district was made on April 1 1974, under the Local Government Act 1972, when the boroughs of Lancaster, Lancashire|Lancaster and Morecambe and Heysham, along with Carnforth urban district, Lancaster Rural District and Lunesdale Rural District joined together.
