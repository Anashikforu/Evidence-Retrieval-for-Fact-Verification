=January=

The Eurasian Economic Union comes into effect, creating a political union|political and economic union between Russia, Belarus, Armenia, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan.
Lithuania officially Lithuania and the euro|adopts the euro as its currency, replacing the Lithuanian litas|litas, and Enlargement of the eurozone|becomes the nineteenth Eurozone country.
January 7:
A Charlie Hebdo shooting|terror attack by Islamic extremists in Paris, France results in the deaths of 12 people at a French newspaper (''Charlie Hebdo'') building.
A 2015 Sana'a bombing|car bomb explodes in Yemen, killing at least 38 people, standing near the entrance of a police officers' academy in central Sana'a.

 11th President of Italy, Giorgio Napolitano, resigns from his office due to old age. Italian senate president, Pietro Grasso, succeeds him as president.

=February=

 February 12 - Leaders from Russia, Ukraine, Germany and France reach an agreement on the conflict in War in Donbass|eastern Ukraine that includes a ceasefire and withdrawal of heavy weapons.
 February 14 - A 2015 Copenhagen attacks|spree-shooting occurs in Copenhagen, Denmark at a local cultural center and synagogue. 3 people are killed including the suspected shooter.
 February 16 - The Egyptian Egyptian Armed Forces|military begins conducting airstrikes against a branch of the Islamic militant group Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|ISIL in Libya in retaliation for the group's beheading of over a dozen Egyptian Christians.
