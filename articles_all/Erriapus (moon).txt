'''Erriapus''' or '''Saturn&nbsp;XXVIII''', is non-Sphere|spherical moon of Saturn (planet)|Saturn. It was found by Brett Gladman, John J. Kavelaars, et al. in 2000, and given the designation '''S/2000&nbsp;S&nbsp;10'''. It was named in August 2003 after Erriapo, a giant in Celtic mythology#Remnants of Gaulish and other mythology|Gaulish mythology. Later, the spelling was corrected to ''Erriapus''.

Erriapus is about 10 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Saturn at an average distance of 17,300,000&nbsp;km in 871 days.

Erriapus is a member of the Saturn's Gallic group of satellites|Gallic group of non-Sphere|spherical, sharing a similar orbit and displaying a similar ''light-red'' colour, Erriapus is thought to have been formed in a break-up of a bigger body.
