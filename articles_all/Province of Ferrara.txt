
The '''province of Ferrara''' () is a Provinces of Italy|province in the Emilia-Romagna region in Northeast Italy, on the Adriatic Sea. Its Capital city|capital is the city of Ferrara.

Geography

The province of Ferrara is the most northerly province in the Emilia-Romagna region. It has an area of  northeast of Bologna, the capital of the region.

The province is bordered to the north by the province of Rovigo in the Veneto region, to the northwest by the province of Mantua in Lombardy, to the west by the Province of Modena|Modena and Province of Bologna|Bologna provinces, to the south by the province of Ravenna and to the east by the Adriatic Sea.



The province is completely flat, with an altitude that goes from 0 m to less than  of average altitude; the ''comune'' with a higher average altitude is Sant'Agostino, Emilia–Romagna|Sant'Agostino.

The main rivers in the province are the Po River|Po and the Reno (river)|Reno. The Po forms the north border of the province with the province of Rovigo. In Ferrara, the river has three main branches: the ''Po di Volano'', that flows through the city of Ferrara, the ''Po di Primaro'' and the ''Po di Goro''.

The Reno river is the longest of the region without being a tributary of the Po.

The main lakes in the province are the ''Lago delle Nazioni'' ("Nation's Lake), an artificial lake, and the ''Lago del Quaternario''.
