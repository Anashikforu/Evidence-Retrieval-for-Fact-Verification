'''Anne Rice''' (born '''Howard Allen O'Brien''', October 4, 1941 – December 11, 2021) was an Americans|American author of horror/fantasy novels. ''Interview with the Vampire'' and ''The Queen of the Damned'' are among her best-known works. She wrote four series, including ''The Vampire Chronicles'' and ''Christ the Lord''.

Rice was born in New Orleans, Louisiana to Irish people|Irish Roman Catholic parents. One of her sisters was novelist Alice Borchardt (1939–2007). Rice was marriage|married to poet Stan Rice (1942–2002) from 1961 his death. They had a daughter, Michele (1966–1972) and a son, novelist Christopher Rice|Christopher (born 1978).

Rice died on December 11, 2021, of complications from a stroke. She was 80.
