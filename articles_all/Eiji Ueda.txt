Biography
Ueda was born in Tateyama, Chiba|Tateyama on December 22, 1953. After graduating from Aoyama Gakuin University, he joined Japan Soccer League club Shonan Bellmare|Fujita Industries. He played many matches from 1978. The club won the league champions in 1977, 1979 and 1981. He retired end of the 1982 season.

After the retirement, Ueda became an assistant coach for Shonan Bellmare|Fujita Industries (later, ''Bellmare Hiratsuka'', ''Shonan Bellmare''). In 1999, he became a manager for Shonan Bellmare|Bellmare Hiratsuka. But he resigned in July. From 2000, he became a manager for the Macau national football team|Macau national team and managed until May 2002. In August 2002, he became a manager for the Japan women's national football team|Japan women's national team.
