
'''Orenthal James "O. J." Simpson''' (born July 9, 1947), also known by his nickname, '''The Juice''', is a retired American football player and movie actor. He originally attained stardom as a running back at the college football|collegiate and professional levels, and was the first National Football League|NFL player to rush (American football)|rush for more than 2,000 yards in a season. He later worked as an actor, spokesperson|spokesman, and broadcaster.  O. J. Simpson is famous for being accused of murdering his ex-wife Nicole Brown Simpson and her friend Ronald Goldman in 1994.

Murder of Nicole Simpson and Ronald Goldman

O. J. Simpson was put on trial for the murder of his ex-wife Nicole Brown Simpson and her friend Ronald Goldman in 1994. The court case took many months, and the newspapers and television news followed it closely.  The criminal court in Los Angeles, California found that Simpson was not guilty of those murders in 1995.  However, Goldman's family sued Simpson for wrongful death in civil law (common law)|civil court.  In 1997, the civil court said Simpson was liable for their wrongful deaths, but to date he has paid little of the $33.5 million he was ordered to pay.

Conviction
In September 2007, Simpson faced more lawsuits, as he was arrested He was imprisoned at the Lovelock Correctional Center in Lovelock, Nevada.

=Parole and release=
On July 20, 2017, Simpson was granted parole. He was released from prison on October 1, 2017.

Career
Simpson played T.D. Parker in the TV series ''1st & Ten: The Championship'' from 1986 to 1991. In 1995 he won the Razzie Award for Worst Supporting Actor.
