
The '''2010 Copiapó mining accident''' was a mining accident that happened on August 5, 2010, when the copper-gold mining|mine of San José, near Copiapó, Chile, collapsed. It left 33 miners trapped around 700 meters under the ground.

The Mine of San José (Mina San José) is about  from the entrance of the mine. The mine has had accidents in the past, including one death.
 There has been controversy over why the mine was not closed down earlier.

Some people, including lawyers representing the mining company, think that the owners of the mine will declare bankruptcy after the miners are rescued.

On October 13, 2010, Florencio Ávalos reached the surface. He was the first miner to be rescued.
