Biography
Sano was born in Shizuoka Prefecture on September 21, 1912. He played for Waseda WMW which was consisted of his alma mater Waseda University players and graduates.

In 1936, when Sano was a Waseda University student, he was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for Football at the 1936 Summer Olympics|1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin. At this competition, on August 4, he debuted against Sweden national football team|Sweden. Japan completed a come-from-behind victory against Sweden. The first victory in Olympics for the Japan and the historic victory over one of the powerhouses became later known as "Miracle of Berlin" (:ja:ベルリンの奇跡|ベルリンの奇跡) in Japan. In 2016, this team was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame. On August 7, he also played against Italy national football team|Italy. He played 2 games for Japan in 1936.

Sano died on March 26, 1992 at the age of 79.
