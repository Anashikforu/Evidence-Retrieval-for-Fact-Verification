'''Rosalind''' is a closer moon to Uranus. It was found from the images taken by ''Voyager 2'' on January 13, 1986, and was given the designation '''S/1986&nbsp;U&nbsp;4'''.

Rosalind belongs to Portia Group of moons, which also includes Bianca (moon)|Bianca, Cressida (moon)|Cressida, Desdemona (moon)|Desdemona, portia (moon)|Portia, Juliet (moon)|Juliet, Cupid (moon)|Cupid, Belinda (moon)|Belinda and Perdita (moon)|Perdita. These moons have similar orbits and photometric properties. Unfortunately, other than its orbit, radius of  36&nbsp;km and geometric albedo of 0.08 almost nothing is known about it.

At the Voyager 2 images Rosalind appears as an almost Sphere|spherical object. The ratio of axises of the Rosalind's prolate spheroid is 1.0-0.8. Its surface is grey in color.
