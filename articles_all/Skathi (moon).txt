'''Skathi''' or '''Saturn&nbsp;XXVII''', is a Satellite (natural)|moon of Saturn (planet)|Saturn. It was found by Brett Gladman, John J. Kavelaars|Kavelaars, et al. in 2000, and given the designation '''S/2000&nbsp;S&nbsp;8'''.

Skathi is about 6.4 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Saturn at an average distance of 15,576,000&nbsp;km in 725.784 days, at an inclination of 149° to the ecliptic (150° to Saturn's equator) with an eccentricity of 0.246.

Skathi may have been formed from debris knocked off of Phoebe (moon)|Phoebe by big impacts at some point in the solar system's history.

Its name comes from Norse mythology, where Skaði is a giantess (female giant) who is the wife of the Vanir god Njord.
