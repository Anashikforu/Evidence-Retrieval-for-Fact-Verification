

'''WrestleMania XX''' was the twentieth WrestleMania professional wrestling pay-per-view produced by World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE). It took place on March 14, 2004 at Madison Square Garden in New York City|New York.

The card for the event has two main events. The main match for the Raw brand was a Professional wrestling match types#Triple Threat match|Triple Threat match for the World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)|World Heavyweight Championship between Champion Triple H, Shawn Michaels and Chris Benoit. Benoit won the match, doing the Professional wrestling holds#Crossface|Crippler Crossface, making Triple H let him win after that.

Background
Image:Triple H-WorldHeavyweight-Champ@Commons.jpg|thumb|left|125px|Triple H as World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)|World Heavyweight Champion.
The main Feud (professional wrestling)|feud at the start of the pay-per-view was on the WWE Raw|Raw WWE Brand Extension|brand, with Triple H, Shawn Michaels and Chris Benoit feuding over the World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)|World Heavyweight Championship. Triple H was the champion going into the event. At the Royal Rumble (2004)|Royal Rumble event two months before, Triple H and Michaels had fought each other with the World Heavyweight Championship, they were both trying to win the Last Man Standing match. The match ended in a Tie (draw)|draw after neither of them could answer a ten-count when Michaels did the Professional wrestling attacks#Superkick|Superkick. As a result, Triple H kept the title.

Event
Image:John Cena as United States Champion 2.jpg|right|thumb|125px|John Cena, who faced off against Paul Wight|The Big Show.
The event began with the Boys Choir of Harlem singing ''America the Beautiful''. This match was the first time that the United States Championship had been defended at a WrestleMania.

The next match was a Professional wrestling match types#Basic non-elimination matches|Fatal Four-Way tag match between Rob Van Dam and Booker T (wrestler)|Booker T, Lance Cade|Garrison Cade and Mark Jindrak, The Dudley Boyz (Mark LoMonaco|Bubba Ray and Devon Hughes|D-Von) and La Résistance (René Goguen|René Duprée and Rob Conway) for the World Tag Team Championship. The match ended when Conway was pinned by Van Dam after Booker T used his Scissor kick (strike)|Scissor kick maneuver on Conway. That was followed by Van Dam's Professional wrestling aerial techniques#Frog splash|Five-Star Frog Splash. Booker T and Rob Van Dam kept the titles as a result.

The third match was between Christian (wrestler)|Christian and Chris Jericho. Christian won the match after Trish Stratus hit Jericho on the face with her elbow. Next up was a Professional wrestling match types#Handicap match|Handicap match with Evolution (professional wrestling)|Evolution (Randy Orton, Dave Batista|Batista and Ric Flair) and The Rock 'n' Sock Connection (Dwayne Johnson|The Rock and Mick Foley). Evolution won the match when Orton pinned Foley after successfully executing an Cutter (professional wrestling)#Jumping cutter|RKO.

"Mean" Gene Okerlund then introduced the WWE Hall of Fame Class of WWE Hall of Fame#2004|2004. This was followed by a Playboy Professional wrestling match types#Bra and panties match|Evening Gown Match with Torrie Wilson and Sable (wrestler)|Sable against Stacy Keibler and Jackie Gayda|Miss Jackie. Wilson pinned Jackie for the win. The next match was a Professional wrestling match types#Gauntlet match|Cruiserweight Open for the WWE Cruiserweight Championship (1991–2007)|WWE Cruiserweight Championship. Cruiserweight fighter stood outside the ring, while two fighters wrestled in the ring. As one wrestler lost, another one e went in the ring. Último Dragón pinned Shannon Moore, but was then pinned by Jamie Noble. The next person to lose was Shoichi Funaki|Funaki, who was pinned by Noble. James Maritato|Nunzio then lost by count out when he was unable to return to the ring after doing a dive through the ropes onto Noble. Billy Kidman then entered the match, pinning and eliminating Noble. Kidman was then pinned and eliminated by Rey Mysterio, Jr.|Rey Mysterio. Yoshihiro Tajiri|Tajiri was next to fight, but was pinned by Mysterio. James Yun|Akio was the next scheduled fighter, but was not able to compete because he was inadvertently attacked by Tajiri when Mysterio ducked. The Crusierweight Champion, Chavo Guerrero, Jr.|Chavo Guerrero was the final entrant and pinned Mysterio to win with the assistance of his father Chavo Guerrero|Chavo Guerrero, Sr., thus keeping the WWE Cruiserweight Championship.

The seventh match had Brock Lesnar and Bill Goldberg|Goldberg with special guest referee, Stone Cold Steve Austin|Steve Austin. The beginning of the match began with both men staring each other down, making much heat (professional wrestling)|heat from the fans throughout the match. Goldberg then took Lesnar down with a Professional wrestling attacks#Spear|Spear, that would only draw a two count after a pin attempt. Lesnar then did the Facebuster#F-5|F-5 maneuver on Goldberg, but also resulted in a two-count pin attempt. Afterwards, Lesnar missed an attempt to knock Goldberg down, allowing Goldberg to execute another spear and a successful Professional wrestling throws#Jackhammer|Jackhammer maneuver that led to a three count for Goldberg, thus winning the match. After the match, Steve Austin did a Stunner (professional wrestling)|Stone Cold Stunner on both fighters to the crowd's delight and celebrated with beer in the ring.

The eight match was the encounter of Lisa Maria Varon|Victoria and Molly Holly for the WWE Women's Championship (1956–2010)|WWE Women's Championship, where Holly would have to be shaved bald if she lost. Victoria would counter a Neckbreaker#Gory neckbreaker|Widow's peak maneuver attempt by Holly into a Pin (professional wrestling)#Backslide|Backslide pin, drawing a three count, thus winning the match and retaining the WWE Women's Championship. Holly tried running towards the backstage area to escape the match's stipulation, but was eventually stopped by Victoria who would knock her out and place her onto the barber's chair, shaving her bald.

Image:Chris Benoit in the Ring.jpg|150px|thumb|right|Chris Benoit, one of the challengers in the main event
The next match had Eddie Guerrero and Kurt Angle fighting over the WWE Championship. Guerrero used a DDT (professional wrestling)|DDT and Professional wrestling aerial techniques#Frog splash|Frog Splash maneuver on Angle that would result in a two-count pin attempt. Angle then secured Guerrero with an Professional wrestling holds#Ankle lock|Ankle lock hold. However, Guerrero escaped the hold by rolling through it and throwing Angle out of the ring. As Angle was outside, Guerrero loosened his ring boot to relieve the pain. As Angle returned to the ring, he applied another Ankle lock on Guerrero that would end up being unsuccessful with Guerrero escaping the hold by pushing Angle off with his other foot leaving Angle holding the Guerrero's loosened ring boot. With Angle confused, Guerrero did a Pin#Roll-up|Roll-up pin maneuver leading to a successful pinfall, thus winning the match and retaining the WWE Championship.

The tenth match of the event was the encounter of The Undertaker and Kane (wrestler)|Kane, which would be Undertaker's first match since he was Professional wrestling match types#Buried Alive match|buried alive at Survivor Series (2003)|Survivor Series 2003. The Undertaker made his return under his Personas of The Undertaker#The Phenom (Original Deadman)|"Deadman" gimmick, as he was accompanied to the ring by Paul Bearer. The two wrestlers brawled in the beginning of the match, until Kane used a Chokeslam maneuver on Undertaker, which made no effect as Undertaker sat up immediately afterwards. The Undertaker then retaliated by Chokeslamming Kane and executing a Piledriver (professional wrestling)#Tombstone piledriver|Tombstone piledriver into a successful pinfall, thus winning the match and remaining undefeated at WrestleMania with a 12-0 record.

The main event was a Professional wrestling match types#Triple Threat match|Triple threat match over the World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)|World Heavyweight Championship between Triple H, Chris Benoit, and Shawn Michaels. This was the seventh title defense of the night, the most in WrestleMania history. Benoit gained control over Michaels when he did the Professional wrestling holds#Crossface|Crossface hold. As Michaels was about to submit, Triple H would save the match. Triple H and Michaels teamed up to Professional wrestling double-team maneuvers#Double suplex|suplex Benoit through the announcer's table. Michaels then attempted to hit Benoit with a Professional wrestling attacks#Superkick|Sweet Chin Music maneuver, but Benoit countered it, throwing Michaels out of the ring. As this occurred, Triple H attempted to Facebuster#Double underhook facebuster|Pedigree Benoit. However, Benoit also countered it and held Triple H with Crossface hold that would force Triple H to submit. As a result, Benoit won the match and the World Heavyweight Championship.

Aftermath

Even though WWE tried hard to promote the Brock Lesnar versus Bill Goldberg|Goldberg contest, some critics were not happy with the match. Many in Madison Square Garden booed the fighters, and only cheered for Stone Cold Steve Austin|Steve Austin because both of the fighters were leaving the WWE after the event.

At Backlash (2004)|Backlash, the original main event match was between Chris Benoit and Shawn Michaels for the World Heavyweight Championship (WWE)|World Heavyweight Championship.

The feud between Kurt Angle and Eddie Guerrero continued. On the March 25 edition of ''SmackDown!'', Angle was made the Kayfabe|on-screen List of authority figures in professional wrestling#SmackDown! brand authorities|General Manager of SmackDown!,
