

'''Edward Giacomin''' (born June 6, 1939 in Greater Sudbury|Sudbury, Ontario) was a Canadians|Canadian professional ice hockey goaltender who played for the New York Rangers and Detroit Red Wings in the National Hockey League, as well as for the Providence Reds of the American Hockey League. Giacomin's sweater number, #1, was the second one retired by the Rangers, after Rod Gilbert.
Early life
Giacomin grew up in Gatchell, a small town near Sudbury. When Giacomin was a teenager, a kitchen stove explosion|exploded and heavily burned him. The doctors advised him to stop hoping to play hockey professionally.
