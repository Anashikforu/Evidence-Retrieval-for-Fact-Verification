

'''B minor''' or '''B-flat minor''' is a minor scale based on B-flat. Its key signature has five flats.

Its Relative key|relative major is D-flat major, and its parallel key|parallel major is B-flat major. Its enharmonic equivalent is A-sharp minor.

B-flat minor is often seen as a "dark" key."Wilfred Mellers, "Round and About in Górecki's Symphony No. 3" ''Tempo'' '''168''' 3 (1989): 23 Pyotr Ilyich Tchaikovsky|Tchaikovsky said that the oboe Solo (music)|solo in B-flat minor in the second movement of his Symphony No. 4 (Tchaikovsky)|Symphony No. 4 was "the feeling that you get when you are all alone".

The old valveless Horn (musical instrument)|horn could not play easily in B-flat minor. In fact, the only example found in 18th century music is a modulation (music)|modulation in the first minuet of Franz Krommer's Concertino in D major, Opus 80.J. Murray Barbour, ''Trumpets, Horns, and Music'' (1964), p. 163
