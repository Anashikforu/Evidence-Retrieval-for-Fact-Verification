A '''birth name''' is the family name someone has when he or she is birth|born, or at least from the time he or she is a child. This term can be used by a man or a woman who changes a name later in life for any reason.

A ''maiden name'' is the family name—usually the birth name—a woman has before she marriage|marries.

A ''married name'' is the family name taken by a married person at the time of the marriage. The most common example is when a woman takes the family name of her spouse at the time of marriage. But sometimes a man changes his name at marriage. Also, sometimes both people change their names, such as to a wikt:hyphenation|hyphenated or other mixed name.

Sometimes the words "né" (for a man) or "née" (for a woman) are used to show a birth name or maiden name. These words mean "born", and are wikt:borrow#Verb|borrowed''v.2'' from French language|French.
