


'''Murray Bookchin''' (January 14, 1921 – July 30, 2006)Small, Mike. ''Murray Bookchin'' '''The Guardian''' August 8, 2006 was an Americans|American Libertarian socialism|libertarian socialist, political and social philosopher, speaker and writer. For much of his life he called himself an anarchist, although as early as 1995 he privately renounced his identification with the anarchist movement.
Bookchin was a radical anti-capitalism|anti-capitalist and always favored the decentralisation of society. His writings on libertarian municipalism, a theory of face-to-face, grassroots democracy, had an influence on the Green movement|Green Movement and anti-capitalist direct action groups such as Reclaim the Streets. His criticisms of "new age" Greens such as Charlene Spretnak contributed to the divisions in the American Green movement in the 1990s.

Although an anti-capitalist, Murray Bookchin often met with libertarian thinker Murray Rothbard and occasionally attended Libertarian Party (United States) conventions, endorsing their presidential candidate Roger MacBride in 1976.

Bookchin has influenced the thinking of captured Kurdish militant Abdullah Öcalan, who has renounced violence and now wants a peaceful solution to the question of Kurdish autonomy.

Libertarian municipalism
Bookchin was the first to use the term "Libertarian municipalism", to describe a system in which libertarian socialism|libertarian institutions of directly democracy|democratic assemblies would oppose and replace the State with a confederation of free municipalities.
