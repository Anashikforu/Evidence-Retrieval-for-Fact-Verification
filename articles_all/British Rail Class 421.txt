The British Rail '''Class 421''' (or British Rail unit designations|4Cig) electrical multiple units were built at BR York Works from 1964-1972.  Units were built in two batches, and were initially introduced on services on the Brighton Main Line. Later units were introduced on services to Portsmouth. These units replaced older Southern Railway (UK)|Southern Railway-designed units, such as the British Rail Class 403|5Bel "Brighton Belle" units, and British Rail Class 404|4Cor units. These trains were known commonly as "slam-doors".
