'''Adams''' is a town in Green County, Wisconsin|Green County, Wisconsin, United States. There were 464 people living in Adams as of the United States Census, 2000|2000 Census.  The town has an area of 36.2&nbsp;square miles, thanks to the United States Census Bureau.

History
The first settlers were Cornwall|Cornish lead mining|miners. They were followed by settlers from New England, Ireland, Switzerland, and Norway. The town has been the home of a Lutheranism|Lutheran and a Catholicism|Catholic parish. Saint Francis Catholic Church was made by Catholic immigrants from Ireland. Later, the parish joined with Saint Joseph Catholic Church in Argyle, Wisconsin. The church was later torn down. Only the cemetery is still there.
