
'''Diane Keaton''' (born '''Diane Hall''', January 5, 1946) is an Americans|American movie actress, director, producer, and screenwriter.

Early life
Keaton was born on January 5, 1946 in Los Angeles, California. Keaton was raised a Free Methodist by her mother.Stated in ''Then Again'', by Diane Keaton, 2011 Her mother won the "Mrs. Los Angeles" pageant for homemakers; Keaton has said that the theatricality of the event inspired her first impulse to be an actress, and led to her wanting to work on stage.
Personal life
Keaton had bulimia nervosa in the early 1970s. She has never married. She has two adoption|adopted children: a daughter Dexter Keaton (born 1995) and a son Duke Keaton (born 2000).

She starred in ''Annie Hall'', ''Looking for Mr. Goodbar (movie)|Looking for Mr. Goodbar'', ''Manhattan Murder Mystery'', ''Marvin's Room'', ''Baby Boom (movie)|Baby Boom'', ''Father of the Bride (1991 movie)|Father of the Bride'', ''Father of the Bride Part II'', ''The First Wives Club'' and ''Something's Gotta Give (movie)|Something's Gotta Give''.
