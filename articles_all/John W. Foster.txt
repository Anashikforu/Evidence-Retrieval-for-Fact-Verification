After that, he was the U.S. Minister to Mexico (1873-1880), Russia (1880-1881) and Spain (1883-1885). He was U.S. Secretary of State under President of the United States|President Benjamin Harrison from 1892-1893. In 1895, he helped the Qing Dynasty in wikt:drafting|drafting the Treaty of Shimonoseki of 1895. He worked for the Chinese as a legal wikt:consult|consultant and wikt:commission|commissioner.

His grandchildren included John Foster Dulles, who also became a U.S. Secretary of State, Allen Welsh Dulles, a Director of Central Intelligence, and Eleanor Lansing Dulles, economist and diplomat.
