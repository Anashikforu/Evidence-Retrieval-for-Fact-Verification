

'''F-sharp major''' is a major scale based on '''F-sharp'''. Its key signature has six sharps.

Its relative key|relative minor is D-sharp minor|D major.

Use of this key
This key is not used often in orchestral music except to modulation (music)|modulate. It is used more often in piano music, like Scriabin's sonatas. When piano music in this key needs to be arrangement (music)|arranged for orchestra, sometimes it is better to change it to F or G major. If F-sharp major needs to be used, transposing instruments in B flat should have their music written in A-flat major, not G-sharp major.

The Presentation of the Rose scene in Act Two of Richard Strauss's opera Der Rosenkavalier is written in F-sharp major.
F-sharp major is the key of Ludwig van Beethoven|Beethoven's Piano Sonata No. 24 (Beethoven)|Piano Sonata, Op. 78, of Frédéric Chopin|Chopin's Barcarolle (Chopin)|Barcarolle, of Liszt's Hungarian Rhapsody No. 2, of Gustav Mahler|Mahler's unfinished Symphony No. 10 (Mahler)|Tenth Symphony, Erich Wolfgang Korngold|Erich Korngold's Symphony in F sharp major (Korngold)|Symphony Op. 40, and Scriabin's Sonata No. 4 (Scriabin)|Fourth Sonata.

In a few scores, the F-sharp major key signature in the bass clef is written with the sharp for the A on the top line.
