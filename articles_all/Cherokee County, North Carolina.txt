
'''Cherokee County''' is a county in the U.S. state of North Carolina.  In 2000, 24,298 people lived there.  Its county seat is Murphy, North Carolina|Murphy.

 History 
The county was made in 1839 from the western part of Macon County, North Carolina|Macon County.  In 1861 the southeastern part of Cherokee County became Clay County, North Carolina|Clay County and in 1872 its northeastern part became Graham County, North Carolina|Graham County.

 Government 
Cherokee County is part of the local Southwestern Commission.

 Connected counties 
These counties are connected to Cherokee County:
