

'''Michael Andrew Fox'''  (born June 9, 1961), known professionally as '''Michael J. Fox''', is a retired Canadian-American actor, author, film producer and activist with a film and television career spanning from the 1970s to 2020. He played Marty McFly in ''Back to the Future (franchise)|Back to the Future'' trilogy. On television, he played Alex P. Keaton in the NBC sitcom ''Family Ties'' and Mike Flaherty (Deputy Mayor)|Mike Flaherty on the American Broadcasting Company|ABC sitcom ''Spin City''. He has won five Primetime Emmy Awards, four Golden Globe Awards, a Grammy Award and two Screen Actors Guild Awards.

Fox was diagnosed with Parkinson's disease in 1991, and told the public about it in 1998. He semi-retired from acting in 2000 as the symptoms of the disease worsened. He has since become an advocate for research toward finding a cure, and founded the The Michael J. Fox Foundation|Michael J. Fox Foundation. Sweden's Karolinska Institute gave him an Honorary degree|honorary doctorate on March 5, 2010 for his work advocating a cure for Parkinson's disease.

Fox has also worked as a voice-over actor on films, such as ''Stuart Little (movie)|Stuart Little'' and  ''Atlantis: The Lost Empire''. On the CBS television show ''The Good Wife'', he earned Emmy nominations for three consecutive years for his recurring role as crafty attorney Louis Canning. He has also taken recurring guest roles and cameo appearances in ''Boston Legal'', ''Scrubs (TV series)|Scrubs'', ''Curb Your Enthusiasm'', ''Rescue Me (U.S. TV series)|Rescue Me'', and ''Designated Survivor (TV series)|Designated Survivor''. He has written four books. Fox was appointed an Officer of the Order of Canada in 2010 and was also inducted into Canada's Walk of Fame in 2000.
