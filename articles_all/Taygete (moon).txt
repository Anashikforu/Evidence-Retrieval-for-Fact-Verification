'''Taygete''' or ''''''.
Taygete is about 5 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 22,439,000&nbsp;km in 686.675 days, at an inclination of 165° to the ecliptic (163° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.3678.

 History 
It was named in October 2002
It belongs to the Carme group, made up of non-spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23,000,000 and 24,000,000&nbsp;km and at an inclination of about 165°.
