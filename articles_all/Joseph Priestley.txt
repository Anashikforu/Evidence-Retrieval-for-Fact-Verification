
'''Joseph Priestley''' (13 March 1733 – 8 February 1804) was an England|English chemistry|chemist, Philosophy|philosopher, Christianity|clergyman, and teacher.  He is known as the discoverer of oxygen.
Antoine Lavoisier and Carl Wilhelm Scheele Carl Wilhelm Scheele also have a claim to the discovery of oxygen. ''See'' Kuhn, Thomas. (1996). ''The Structure of Scientific Revolutions'', pp. 53–60. He was a Unitarian.
