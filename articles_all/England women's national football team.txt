


The '''England women's national football team''' plays for England in international women's football (soccer)|women's football. The side has been quite successful of late. The team has qualified for five FIFA Women's World Cup|World Cups: FIFA Women's World Cup 1995|1995, FIFA Women's World Cup 2007|2007, 2011 FIFA Women's World Cup|2011, 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup|2015 and 9FIFA Women's World Cup|2019.

In May 2009, it was announced that a number of the England Women's Team would be awarded central contracts, to help the players concentrate on full-time training and playing, without having to fit it around full-time employment. Seventeen players initially signed contracts.

In 2009, the team reached the European Championships Final, losing 6–2 to Germany. In 2015, the side had their most impressive achievement to date by finishing in third place in the 2015 FIFA Women's World Cup|2015 World Cup.
