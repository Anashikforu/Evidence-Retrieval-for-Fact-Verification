'''Tissue culture''' or  '''cell culture''' is a way to grow cells in a laboratory. Cells are taken, and put into in a flask or Petri dish. They can be grown on a growth medium, sometimes an agar plate. The cells cell division|divide, and can be treated in various ways.  This is done for a number of purposes, especially for the use of scientific research: 

Growing bacteria or fungi for experiments or for identification. An example would be growing bacteria to see what antibiotics they are antibiotic resistance|resistant to.
Growing cells of plants or animals for various experimental purposes. 
Plant cuttings can be grown: roots grow out of a sprig. 
IVF when eggs are taken and fertilized with sperm in the dish.
