
The '''University of Notre Dame''' (or simply '''Notre Dame''') is a private Catholic Church|Catholic university near South Bend, Indiana. It was founded in 1842 by Father Edward Sorin of the Congregation of Holy Cross. Sorin was also the school's first president. In the beginning, it was an all-male school. In 1972 females were admitted. The university has five colleges and one professional school. Notre Dame offers master's degrees and doctoral degrees. The school's athletic teams are known as the Fighting Irish.
