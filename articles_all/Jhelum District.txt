
'''Jhelum District''' () is in the Punjab (Pakistan)|Punjab province of Pakistan. According the 1998 census, the Districts of Pakistan|district had 936,957 people, of which 31.48% were urban. Jhelum is known for providing many soldiers to the United Kingdom|British and later to the Pakistani military|Pakistan armed forces due to which it is also known as '''city of soldiers''' or '''land of martyrs and warriors'''.
Administration

The district of Jhelum, which covers an area of 3,587&nbsp;km2, It also contains the city of Jhelum City|Jhelum.

Demographics
According to census 2017 total population of District Jhelum is 1222650 . In Total population males are 601964 , female are 620636 and Transgender are 50 . Average annual growth rate is 1.41 from 1998 to 2017.

According to the 1998 census of Pakistan, 936,957 people lived in Jhelum District. There were 261 people per square kilometre.1998 Census of Pakistan According the Punjab Education Department's annual literacy statistics for 2006, Jhelum had a literacy rate of 79% which is among the highest in Pakistan.
Language

People of Jhelum District speak Punjabi. The written language is Urdu. Many also speak Pothwari language|Pothwari.


Climate
The climate of the tract is extreme. In winter it is very cold and summer is very hot. The average rainfall varies from 48 to 69&nbsp;mm each year.
