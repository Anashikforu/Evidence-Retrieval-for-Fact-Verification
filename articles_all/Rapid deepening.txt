'''Rapid deepening''' is a condition that happens when the minimum sea-level atmospheric pressure of a tropical cyclone goes down at lot very quickly. The National Weather Service describes rapid deepening as a decrease of 42&nbsp;bar (unit)|millibars in less than 24&nbsp;hours.  However, this phrase is usually applied to most storms that are quickly becoming stronger.
