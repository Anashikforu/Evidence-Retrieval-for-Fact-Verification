'''Cyllene''' or ''''''.
Cyllene is about 2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 23,396,000&nbsp;km in 731.099 days, at an inclination of 140° to the ecliptic (140° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.4116.

It was named in March 2005 after Cyllene (mythology)|Cyllene, a Naiads|naiad (stream nymph) or oread (mountain nymph) associated with Mount Kyllini, Greece.
It belongs to the Pasiphaë group, non-Sphere|spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at distances ranging between 22,800,000 and 24,100,000&nbsp;km, and with inclinations ranging between 144.5° and 158.3°.
