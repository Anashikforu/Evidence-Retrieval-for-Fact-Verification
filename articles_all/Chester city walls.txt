The '''Chester city walls''' are big intact defensive walls around an important England|English city of Chester. The city has been defended with walls since the foundation of the Deva Victrix Roman fort|fort on the site in 79.

History
The first walls were built to help the Roman Empire|Roman fort in 79, although these were replaced by stronger stone walls between the 1st century|1st and 3rd century. The walls enclosed a far smaller area than the modern walls, with the south-western corner much farther from the River Dee, Wales|River Dee.
