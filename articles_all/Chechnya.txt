
'''Chechnya''' (), is a Federal subjects of Russia|federal subject in Russia. It is located in the Caucasus region. The capital is Grozny.

Most people in Chechnya are Islam|Muslims and are of ancient Hurrians|Hurrian roots who spoke a Caucasian languages|Caucasian language. Most Chechens belong to the Shafi`i school of Sunni Islam.

Chechens speak their own language, not related to the Russian language. Chechen is part of the Northeast Caucasian languages|Northeast Caucasian, or ''Vainakh'', family, while Russian is a Slavic languages|Slavic language.

During Soviet Union|Soviet control, Chechnya was unified with Ingushetia. After the Dissolution of the Soviet Union|fall of the Soviet Union, Chechnya broke away from Ingushetia to form its own republic. The Chechens wanted independence. After the First Chechen War, Chechnya was de facto independent as the Chechen Republic of Ichkeria. After the Second Chechen War, Russia regained control of Chechnya.

Russia has claimed Chechnya as part of its country since the Russians invasion|invaded the Caucasus in the 18th century.

The current leader of the Chechen Republic is Ramzan Kadyrov. He is also the son of the 1st Chechen President, Akhmad Kadyrov.
