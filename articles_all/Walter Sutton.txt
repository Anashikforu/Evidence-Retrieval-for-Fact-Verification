'''Walter Stanborough Sutton''' (5 April 1877 – 10 November 1916) was an United States|American biologist and surgeon. Sutton was born in Utica, New York and raised in Russell, Kansas. He got Bachelors and Masters degres from the University of Kansas. Sutton then went to Columbia University and got his Doctorate degree|doctorate in medicine in 1907.

In 1903 Sutton suggested that chromosomes, which segregate in a Gregor Mendel|Mendelian fashion, are hereditary units. Edmund B. Wilson (1856–1939), Sutton's teacher, and the author of one of the most famous text-books in biology,Wilson E.B. 1896; 1900; 1925. ''The cell''. Macmillan, London. The third edition ran to 1232 pages. called this the ''Sutton–Boveri hypothesis''.
