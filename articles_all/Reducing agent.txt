

A '''reducing agent''' is a chemical that gives away electrons to another chemical compound, the oxidizing agent. For example, zinc is a reducing agent. When it wikt:react|reacts with an oxidation|oxidizing agent, it gives up two electrons, changing its oxidation state from 0 to +2. All chemical elements have an oxidation state of 0.

Examples
Tin(II) chloride
Zinc
Aluminum
Alkali metal
Sulfur
Hydrogen
