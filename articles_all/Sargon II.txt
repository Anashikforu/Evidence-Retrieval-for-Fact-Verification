'''Sargon II''' (, "legitimate king", reigned 722 – 705 BC) was an Neo-Assyrian Empire|Assyrian king. Sargon II became co-regent with Shalmaneser V in 722 BC.  Shalmaneser V died in 722 BC. From then on, Sargon II was the only ruler of Assyria.  It is not clear if he was the son of Tiglath-Pileser III or a usurper unrelated to the royal family. In his inscriptions, he styles himself as a new man and rarely talks about his predecessors. He nevertheless took the name ''Sharru-kinu'' ("true king"), after Sargon of Akkad — who had founded the first Akkadian Empire|Semitic Empire in the region about 16 centuries earlier.Another "Sargon", a predecessor of Shamshi-Adad of the 18th century BC. ''Sargon'' is the Biblical form of the name.
