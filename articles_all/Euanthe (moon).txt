Euanthe is about 3 kilometres in diameter.  It orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 20,465,000 kilometer|km in 598.093 days.  This orbit is at an inclination of 143° to the ecliptic (142° to Jupiter's equator) with an orbital eccentricity of 0.2001.

It was named in August 2003 after Euanthe, who was the mother of the Charites|Graces, according to some Ancient Greece|Greek writers.
Euanthe is in the Ananke group, retrograde non-spherical moons which orbit Jupiter between 19,,300,000 and 22,700,000&nbsp;km, at inclinations of about 150°.
