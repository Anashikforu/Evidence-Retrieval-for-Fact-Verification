'''Galatea''' or '''Neptune&nbsp;VI''', is the fourth closest List of Neptune's moons|moon to Neptune. It is named after Galatea (mythology)|Galatea, one of the Nereids of Greek Mythology|Greek legend.

Galatea was found in late July 1989 from the images taken by the ''Voyager 2'' space probe|probe. It was given the designation '''S/1989&nbsp;N&nbsp;4'''. The discovery was said (IAUC 4824) on August 2, 1989, but the text only talks of "10 frames taken over 5 days", giving a discovery date of sometime before July 28. The name was given on 16 September 1991.

It is not a sphere and shows no sign of any geological changes.
