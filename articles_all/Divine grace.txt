In Christianity '''Divine Grace''' means a favour of God for humankind. God favours every person, without looking at what they did or how much they are worth. This wikt:grace|grace of God to humans may happen in the gifts that God gives him, such as wikt:creation|creation or Salvation (Christianity)|salvation.

Types of Grace

=Common Grace=
Common grace refers to blessings which God gives to everyone.  Jesus said that God "sends the rain upon the Morality|just and the unjust. Gospel of Matthew|Matthew 5:45 This idea is found in Greek philosophy.  Plato spoke of God's "gifts". 
=Prevenient Grace=
Prevenient grace (or enabling grace) is a Christian teaching in Jacob Arminius|Arminian theology. It appeared earlier in Roman Catholic|Catholic and early Anabaptist teaching. It is divine grace that goes before human decision. In other words, God will start showing special love to a person at a certain time of his life.

Prevenient grace is taught by Christians who are influenced by the theology of Jacob Arminius or John Wesley. Wesleyan Arminians believe God helps people to accept the gift of salvation. 

=Saving Grace=
This refers to the good things that God gives to those who believe in Jesus.  The gifts include forgiveness of sin, Immortality|eternal life, and the power to live a good and holy life.

In Popular Culture

"Grace" is a common girl's name in many countries.

"Amazing Grace" is a song that became very popular.  It is played at funerals, sometimes on a Bagpipes|bagpipe. This song was written by John Newton who was an evil slave trader.  His life was changed when he became a Christian.  He later worked to stop the practice of slavery. His story is told in a 2006 Amazing Grace (2006 movie)|movie.
