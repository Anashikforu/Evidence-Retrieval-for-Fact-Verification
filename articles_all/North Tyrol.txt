'''North Tyrol''', or '''North Tirol''' (German language|German and Austro-Bavarian language|Austro-Bavarian: '''''Nordtirol''''') is the main part of the Austrian state of Tyrol (state)|Tyrol, located in the western part of the country. The other part of the state is East Tyrol, which also belongs to Austria, but does not share a border with North Tyrol. 

Besides those two regions, the historical region of Tyrol (region)|Tyrol also included the areas today known as Province of Bolzano-Bozen|Bolzano-Bozen and Province of Trento|Trento for many centuries. They were annexed by Italy after World War I which made North Tyrol and East Tyrol effectively cut off from each other.
