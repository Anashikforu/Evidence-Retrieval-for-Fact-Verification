'''Austrobaileyales''' is the botanical name for an order (biology)|order of flowering plants. There are about 100 species of woody plants, perhaps the most famous of which is the spice star anise, from ''Illicium verum''. 

The Angiosperm Phylogeny Group|APG system, of 1998, did not recognize such an order. However, the APG II system, of 2003, did accept this order and placed it among the basal angiosperms.
