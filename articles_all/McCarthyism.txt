'''McCarthyism''' is the term describing a period of intense anti-Communist :wikt:suspicion|suspicion in the United States which began during the start of the Cold War, that lasted roughly from the late 1940s to the mid to late 1950s. The term gets its name from United States Senate|U.S. Senator Joseph McCarthy, a United States Republican Party|Republican of Wisconsin. The period of McCarthyism is also referred to as the '''Second Red Scare''' (The '''First Red Scare''' happened after the Bolshevik October Revolution of 1917, that lasted from the late 1910s to the late 1920s). It happened at the same times as increased fears of Communist influence on American institutions, espionage by Soviet Union|Soviet agents such as the Julius and Ethel Rosenberg|Rosenbergs, heightened tension from Eastern Bloc|Soviet control over Central Europe|Central and Eastern Europe, the success of the Chinese Civil War|Chinese Communist Revolution (1949) and the Korean War (1950-1953). There is a play called 'The Crucible' which is about the beginning of the Salem Witch Trials, but also about McCarthyism.
