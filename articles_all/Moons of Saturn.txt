The planet Saturn (planet)|Saturn has 53 named Satellite (natural)|moons, and another 29 which are still being studied. Many of the moons are named after Titan (mythology)|Titans, Giant|Giants, or minor Ancient Greece|Greek or Ancient Rome|Roman gods. Some of Saturn's moons are very large; the moon Titan (moon)|Titan is bigger than the planet Mercury (planet)|Mercury.

Hypothetical moons
Two moons were claimed to be discovered by different astronomers but never seen again. Both moons were said to orbit between Titan (moon)|Titan and Hyperion (moon)|Hyperion.
 Chiron (hypothetical moon)|Chiron which was supposedly sighted by Hermann Goldschmidt in 1861, but never observed by anyone else.
 Themis (hypothetical moon)|Themis was allegedly discovered in 1905 by astronomer William Henry Pickering|William Pickering, but never seen again. Nevertheless, it was included in numerous almanacs and astronomy books until the 1960s.
