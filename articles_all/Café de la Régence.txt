

The '''Café de la Régence''' was a meeting place for chess players in Paris, France. It was the most famous chess meeting-place for over a century. The building was a coffeehouse in the centre of Paris, founded in 1688 and named in 1718. At first, it was under the patronage of the Duc d'Orleans, and attracted a literature|literary crowd.Chicco, A. & Porreca, G. 1971. ''Dizionario enciclopedico degli scacchi''. Mursia, Milano.p417 

Chess players moved there en masse around 1740. The clientele included amateurs, such as Voltaire, Rousseau, Robespierre, Benjamin Franklin and Napoleon. The leading professionals, Deschapelles, La Bourdonnais, Verdoni and Philidor, were there every day when they were in Paris.Hooper D. and Whyld K. ''The Oxford companion to chess''. 2nd ed, Oxford.p65 Howard Staunton|Staunton played his match against Pierre Saint-Amant|Saint-Amant there; Paul Morphy played there in 1858, including his match with Adolf Anderssen|Anderssen. The café was moved in 1855. The chess room was finally closed in 1916.Cornil, Etienne. ''Le Café de la Régence''. Les Cahiers du CREB. A 268-page booklet available on-line.
