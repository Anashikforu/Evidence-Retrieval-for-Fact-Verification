'''Meze''' or '''mezze'''  'to taste',Oxford English Dictionary, Draft Revision Dec. 2008Merriam-Webster Online - 
In Levant|Levantine cuisines and in the Caucasus region (especially in Armenia and Georgia (country)|Georgia), these dishes are served as a part of any large-scale meal.Alan Davidson, ''The Oxford Companion to Food'', Oxford University Press, 1999, pp. 500-501. Not accompanied by alcohol, meze is known in the Arab world as ''muqabbilat'' (Arabic language|Arabic for "starters").
