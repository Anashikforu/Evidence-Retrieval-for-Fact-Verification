'''Kallichore''' or ''''''.
Kallichore is about 2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 23,112,000&nbsp;km in 717.806 days, at an inclination of 165° to the ecliptic (164° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.2042.

It was named in March 2005 after the nymph Kallichore (mythology)|Kallichore.
Kallichore belongs to the Carme group, made up of non-Sphere|spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23,000,000 and 24,000,000&nbsp;km and at an inclination of about 165°.
