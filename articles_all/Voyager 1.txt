'''Voyager 1''' is a space probe used by NASA to explore Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter and Saturn (planet)|Saturn. It is identical in form to its sister spacecraft, ''Voyager 2''.

''Voyager 1'' was launched on September 5, 1977, to study the outer planets of the Solar System. It is now in an extended mission. Its encounter with Saturn and Titan sent it on a hyperbolic trajectory out of the solar system, traveling at  which is much faster than escape velocity.

''Voyager 1'' from Earth.

Both ''Voyager 1'' and ''Voyager 2'' include the Voyager Golden Record, which is a recording of sounds and images of life on Earth.  It was designed by a team headed by Carl Sagan to communicate with extraterrestrial life.
