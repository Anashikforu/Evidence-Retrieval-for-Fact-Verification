
'''East Flanders''' (Dutch language|Dutch: ''Oost-Vlaanderen'', , German language|German: ''Ostflandern'') is a province of Flemish Region|Flanders, one of the three regions of Belgium.

Its Capital (city)|capital, and also the largest city of the province, is Ghent ().

Name
''East'' in ''East Flanders'' means that it is in eastern part of Flanders, a region that forms part of the present Belgium. The word ''Flanders'' refers to the historic county of Flanders and appears for the first time in the year 358 under the name ''pagus Flandrensis'' (''pagus'' is a Latin language|Latin word meaning a very small town, with only few houses).

The ''pagus Flandrensis'' region was a coastal area with large Tide|tidal channels and green marshes where sheep farmers lived.

The modern province of East Flanders was the old Escaut (department)|Escaut Departments of France|department, created in 1795 during the First French Empire in the eastern part of the CountyIn this case "county" () means "land under a count (the United Kingdom|British earl)." of Flanders.

In 1815, during the creation of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, the department became the province of East Flanders.
