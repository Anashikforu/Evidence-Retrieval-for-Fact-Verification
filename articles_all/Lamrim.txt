'''Lamrim''' (Tibetan: "stages of the path") is a Tibetan Buddhism|Tibetan Buddhist text which presents the stages in the complete path to Enlightenment in Buddhism|enlightenment as taught by Gautama Buddha|Buddha. In Tibetan Buddhist history there have been many different versions of ''lamrim'', presented by different teachers of the Nyingma, Kagyu and Gelug schools.The Sakya school, too, has a somewhat similar textual form, the ''lamdré''. However, all versions of the ''lamrim'' are elaborations of Atisha|Atiśa's 11th Century root text ''A Lamp for the Path to Enlightenment''.
There is a project to expand the Lamrim Teachings with several translated texts.
