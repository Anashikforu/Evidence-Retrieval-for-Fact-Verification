
The '''Empire State Building''' is a skyscraper in Midtown Manhattan, New York City.  It is   it is one of the most famous landmarks in the US. When the Empire State Building opened on May 1, 1931, it was the world's tallest building.

History
Designed by the architects named Shreve, Lamb & Harmon Associates, it was built at a time when many people were trying to make the list of tallest buildings in the world|world's tallest building, but the Empire State Building won. In the 1930s, the Empire State Building was called the "Empty State Building" because not many people worked in the building.See:




The Empire State Building became the tallest building in New York City and the world until the first World Trade Center (1973â€“2001)|World Trade Center towers passed it in 1970. When these towers were brought down in the September 11, 2001 attacks, the Empire State Building was again the tallest building in New York. 

On B-25 Empire State Building crash|Saturday, July 28, 1945, a plane crashed into the building due to heavy fog and started a fire, but the building was only damaged a little.  14 people (3 in the plane) were killed in the accident.

 Building design 
The Empire State Building is at 350 Fifth Avenue. The building has its own ZIP Code|ZIP code, 10118.

The 80th, 86th, and 102nd floors have places where people can look at the city from high above.
