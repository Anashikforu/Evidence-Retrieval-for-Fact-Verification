
'''Teddy Randazzo''' (May 13, 1935 - November 21, 2003) was an United States|American singer, songwriter and record producer, best known for his work in the 1950s and 1960s. One of the groups he worked with was Little Anthony and the Imperials.

Randazzo appeared as himself in a 1962 movie, ''Don't Knock the Twist'', which was about the Twist (dance)|Twist craze of the early 1960s.
