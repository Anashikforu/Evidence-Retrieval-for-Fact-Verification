'''Gata Kamsky''' Tatar Ğata Kamskiy, Ğataulla Rөstəm uğlı Sabirov; Гата Камский, Гатаулла Рөстәм улы Сабиров; ) (born June 2, 1974) is a Soviet-born United States|American chess grandmaster. Kamsky won the 2010 Grenke Open World Rapid Chess Championship.
Kamsky was a prodigy who reached the final of the FIDE World Chess Championship 1996 at the age of 22, and reached a ranking of fourth in the world in 1996. He played almost no FIDE-rated games between 1997 and late 2004. He was studying medicine at the time.Dylan Loeb McClain: A chess master returns older, and maybe wiser. ''New York Times'', January 27, 2008:  
Kamsky won the Chess World Cup 2007. This earned him a match against Veselin Topalov for the right to challenge for the World Chess Championship 2010, which he lost. World Chess Challenge 2009 Kamsky also played in the Candidates Tournament to determine the challenger for the World Chess Championship 2012. He advanced to the semifinals before losing to Boris Gelfand.
