

The '''Arno''' is a river in the Tuscany region of Italy. It is one of the important rivers of central Italy. 

 Source and route 

The river begins on Mount Falterona in the Casentino area of the Apennine Mountains|Apennines. It first takes a curve to the south. The river turns to the west near Arezzo. It passes through Florence, Empoli and Pisa, then flows into the Tyrrhenian Sea at the Marina di Pisa. At a length of , it is the longest river in the region.

A 1966 Flood of the Arno River|flood on November 4, 1966 collapsed the embankment in Florence. This killed at least 40 people. It also damaged or destroyed millions of works of art and rare books. 40 years later, hundreds of works have still not been restoration|restored.
