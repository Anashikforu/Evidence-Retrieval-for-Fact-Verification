
'''Granville County''' is a county in the U.S. state of North Carolina.  As of 2000, 48,498 people lived there.  Its county seat is Oxford, North Carolina|Oxford.

 History 
The county was made in 1746 from Edgecombe County, North Carolina|Edgecombe County.

In 1752 parts of Granville County, Bladen County, North Carolina|Bladen County, and Johnston County, North Carolina|Johnston County were combined to form Orange County, North Carolina|Orange County.  In 1764 the eastern part of Granville County became Bute County, North Carolina|Bute County.  And in 1881 parts of Granville County, Franklin County, North Carolina|Franklin County, and Warren County, North Carolina|Warren County were combined to form Vance County, North Carolina|Vance County.

 Government 
Granville County is part of the Kerr-Tar Regional Council of Governments.
