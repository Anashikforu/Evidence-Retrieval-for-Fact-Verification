'''Phycology''' (or '''algology'''), a part of botany, is the study of algae. Algae are important as primary production|primary producers in aquatic ecosystems. Most algae are eukaryote|eukaryotic, photosynthesis|photosynthetic organisms that live in a wet environment. They are different from the higher plants by a lack of "normal" roots, stems or leaf|leaves. Many types have one cell and are very small or microscopic (including phytoplankton|plankton and other microalgae); many others multicellular|have many cells; some of these are very big, like seaweeds such as kelp and ''Sargassum''.

Phycology includes the study of prokaryote|prokaryotic forms known as blue-green algae or cyanobacteria. Some microscopic algae are also in lichens.
