

'''Charles Julius Guiteau''' (June 30, 1882) was an Americans|American writer and lawyer. He is best known as the peoson who Assassination of James A. Garfield|assassinated president of the United States|US President James A. Garfield  on July 2, 1881. Guiteau shot Garfield after being angry with him about not receiving credit for Garfield's victory in 1880. Guiteau wrote a speech about Garfield but never made the speech.

Guiteau was born in Freeport, Illinois. He was a preacher, writer and lawyer. He was found guilty in a court of law. He was executed by hanging in Washington, D.C.
