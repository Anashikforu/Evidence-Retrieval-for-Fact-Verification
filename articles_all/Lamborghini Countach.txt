
The '''Lamborghini Countach''' is a sports car. Lamborghini built it from from 1974 to 1990. The Countach came after the Lamborghini Miura|Miura. Gruppo Bertone designed the Countach. The car used several different V12 engines by the time Lamborghini stopped making it in 1990.

History
Lamborghini first showed the Countach to the public at the 1971 Geneva Motor Show. The car was known as the Lamborghini LP500 back then. The Countach was put into wikt:production|production in 1974. The first Countach model was called the Countach LP400.

=Production models=


Lamborghini made several different Countach models between 1974 and 1990. 
LP400

LP400 S
Lamborghini started making the Countach LP400 S in 1978. This model replaced the Countach LP400. The LP400 S’s engine was a little less powerful than the LP400’s. The LP400 S made . There were some changes made to the wikt:exterior|exterior. These included fender flares and a rear wing. These changes stayed until Lamborghini stopped making the Countach in 1990.

LP500 S and LP5000 QV
Lamborghini made the Countach LP500 S in 1982. The LP500 S had a larger () V12 engine than the Countach LP400 and LP400 S. Lamborghini built 321 Countach LP500 S cars.

U.S. version

Lamborghini made a version of the Countach for the United States.
