

'''Christian Dannemann Eriksen''' (born 14 February 1992) is a Danish Association football|footballer who plays for the Italian club F.C. Internazionale Milano|Inter Milan and the Denmark national football team|Denmark national team as an midfielder. He made his debut for the Denmark national football team|Denmark national team in March 2010, and was the youngest player of the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa. 

In 2011, Eriksen was named Danish Football Player of the Year, Dutch Footballer of the Year#Dutch Football Talent of the Year (Young player)|Dutch Football Talent of the Year, List of AFC Ajax records#AFC Ajax prize winners|Ajax Talent of the Year (Marco van Basten Award), and made the 2011 UEFA European Under-21 Football Championship#Team of the Tournament|UEFA Euro U-21 Team of the Tournament. He won the Eredivisie with Ajax in 2010–11 Eredivisie|2010–11, 2011–12 Eredivisie|2011–12, and in 2012–13 Eredivisie|2012–13 before signing with Tottenham Hotspur in August 2013. He won the Tottenham Hotspur Player of the Year award for the 2013–14 season and in 2015, he won the Danish Football Player of the Year award for the second year in a row.

Club career

=Early career in Middelfart and Odense=
Eriksen started playing football in his hometown Middelfart before his third birthday. In 2005 he switched to Odense Boldklub, who competed in the Danish youth championships. His team lost the semi-final against Brøndby IF, but afterwards he was named "Best Technical Player" of the tournament.

=AFC Ajax=

Youth & entrance in the first squad
On 17 October 2008 it was announced that Eriksen had signed a two-and-a-half-year contract with Amsterdam-based AFC Ajax|Ajax.


On 25 March 2010 he scored his first goal for Ajax in a 6–0 win against opponent Go Ahead Eagles in the Dutch Cup. On 10 April 2010, Eriksen extended his contract with Ajax until the summer of 2014, stating ''"I can still learn a lot here, I'm not finished yet and I hope I can mean a lot for the club."''

Breakthrough season
Eriksen started the 2010–11 Eredivisie|2010–11 season well, scoring his first Ajax league goal on 29 August 2010 in an away victory over De Graafschap.


During the rest of the season, Eriksen became more and more important for Ajax, due to his growing capabilities as the team's playmaker, providing many assists. After a good season during which he developed himself into an undisputed starter in the side that won the 2010–11 Eredivisie (Ajax's first Eredivisie title in seven years), he was named Ajax's Talent of the Year.

Rising influence in the squad
On 18 October 2011, Eriksen scored his first goal in the UEFA Champions League when Ajax beat Dinamo Zagreb 2–0 in the group stage, making him the 2nd youngest player to score in the 2011–12 season. In the second match versus Dinamo Zagreb on 2 November he provided assists to Gregory van der Wiel and Siem de Jong as well as being involved with the third goal, as Ajax cruised to a 4–0 victory.

=Tottenham Hotspur=

2013–14 season


On 30 August 2013, Tottenham Hotspur F.C.|Tottenham Hotspur announced that they had completed the transfer of Eriksen from Ajax,

On 14 September 2013, he made his Premier League debut against Norwich City F.C.|Norwich City, providing an assist for Gylfi Sigurðsson.

On 19 September 2013, Eriksen "clipped a wonderful dipping shot" over the goalkeeper to score his first Tottenham goal and complete a 3–0 win over Tromsø IL in the 2013–14 UEFA Europa League|Europa League. On 23 March, after goals from Jay Rodriguez and Adam Lallana had given Southampton F.C.|Southampton a 2–0 lead against Tottenham at White Hart Lane, Eriksen scored twice to level the score and assisted Gylfi Sigurðsson to score the winning goal.
On 12 April 2014, he scored a stoppage-time equalizer as ''Spurs'' came from 3–0 down to draw 3–3 at West Bromwich Albion. By the end of the season, Eriksen had scored 7 goals in the Premier League.

2014–15 season

Throughout the 2014/15 campaign, Eriksen played an integral part in many Spurs victories. The Danish international had a habit of scoring late winners, most notably against Swansea City, Sunderland, Hull City and many others.

On 10 January 2015, Eriksen was voted Danish Football Player of the Year twice in a row, making him the first player to do so since William Kvist.
On 28 January 2015, Eriksen completed his greatest performance of the campaign in the Capital One Cup semi-final second leg against Sheffield United to send Tottenham into the 2015 Football League Cup Final|League Cup Final. Despite a late threat from the Blades, Eriksen then scored a second late-on to send Spurs to Wembley.

Eriksen completed the 2014/15 campaign having featured in every Premier League game for Mauricio Pochettino. The Dane became the third Spurs player to reach double figures in the league, alongside Nacer Chadli and Harry Kane, scoring 10 goals.

On 9 June 2015, Eriksen confirmed to Danish media whilst on international duty that he would stay at Tottenham for the foreseeable future. Eriksen was quoted as saying: "I feel right at home at Tottenham and I haven't thought about leaving yet."

International career


=Youth squads=
Eriksen was called up to the Denmark national under-17 football team in July 2007, which was won by Mathias Jørgensen. He played 27 games for the under-17 team until February 2009. He played a total of 8 games for the Denmark national under-18 football team|Denmark U-18 and Denmark national under-19 football team|Denmark U-19 teams during 2009. Eriksen was also called up for the Danish U-21 squad to the European championship in Denmark in 2011, the Danish team only participated in the group stage and Eriksen scored a goal against Belarus.

=Senior squad=
He received his first senior Denmark call-up in February 2010,

On 28 May 2010, Denmark coach Morten Olsen announced that Eriksen would be part of the final squad of 23 participating in the 2010 FIFA World Cup in South Africa.

On 9 February 2011, in a 1–2 friendly loss at home against England, Eriksen was named Man of the Match, and was praised for his performance by a number of prominent footballing figures, including Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea star Frank Lampard,

On 10 January 2015, Eriksen was voted Danish Football Player of the Year twice in a row, making him the first player to do so since William Kvist.

Sponsorship
In 2012, Eriksen signed a sponsorship kit deal with American sportswear and equipment supplier, Nike, Inc.|Nike. He appeared in an advert for the new Nike Green Speed II alongside Raheem Sterling, Stephan El Shaarawy, Mario Götze, Eden Hazard and Theo Walcott in November 2012.

Christian then transitioned from Nike's Mercurial range, to the more 'creative' orientated Magista.
