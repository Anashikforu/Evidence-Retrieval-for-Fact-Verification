
The '''Formula One World Drivers' Championship (WDC)''' is the most successful Formula One Auto racing|race car driver of the year. The award is given by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA) to the driver who scores the most List of Formula One World Championship pointscoring systems|points during a season.

The '''WDC''' was first awarded in 1950 Formula One season|1950, to Giuseppe Farina|Giuseppe "Nino" Farina. The first driver to win more than one Championship was Alberto Ascari, in 1952 Formula One season|1952 and 1953 Formula One season|1953. The latest driver awarded the championship is called the ''reigning'' or ''defending'' champion. As of the end of the 2021 Formula One season, the reigning champion is Max Verstappen of Red Bull Racing.

The FIA does not officially award the Championship until the end of the season. Sometimes one driver will be far ahead of the other drivers in points. Even if the leading driver scores no more points, none of the other drivers could pass his point total. When this happens, the driver is said to have ''clinched'' the Championship.
