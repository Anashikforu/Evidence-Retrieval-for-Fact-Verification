
The British Rail '''Class 12''' is a diesel locomotive built mainly for Switcher|shunting duties. 

This was the second batch of standard Southern Railway (Great Britain)|Southern Railway shunters based on the English Electric 6KT 350&nbsp;hp (260&nbsp;kW) diesel engine.  These locomotives (BR numbers 15201-15203) were built by the SR in 1937 and were later classified British Rail Class D3/12|D3/12. 

The Class 12 locomotives were built at the Ashford railway works|BR Ashford Works over the period 1949–1952. They were numbered 15211–15236 and became Class 12. No locomotives survived long enough to acquire Class 12 TOPS numbers though.
