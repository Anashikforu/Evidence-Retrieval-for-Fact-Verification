

'''Tarn''' () is a France|French Departments of France|department in the Occitanie Regions of France|region in the southwest of France named after the Tarn (river)|Tarn river.

Its Prefectures in France|prefecture and largest city is Albi.

History
Tarn is one of the original 83 departments created during the French Revolution on 4 March 1790. It was created from a part of the old province of Languedoc.

The new department had five districts: Albi, Castres, Lavaur, Gaillac, Lacaune. The Capital (city)|capitals (now Prefectures in France|prefectures) were, alternatively, Albi and Castres, Tarn|Castres but, from 1790 to 1797, the capital was only Albi; in 1797, the capital was moved to Castres.

In 1800, Albi became again the capital of the department and the ''arrondissements'' were created; the department had four ''arrondissements'': Albi, Castres, Gaillac and Lavaur. In 1926, the ''arrondissements'' of Gaillac and Lavaur were eliminated.

While the valley of the Tarn river, the main river of the department, forms a plain, the central part  of the department has many hills. The main mountain ranges are the Monts de Lacaune, in the southeast, and the Montagne Noire in the south.

Administration
The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Tarn'' in Albi. Tarn is part of the region of Occitanie.

Demographics
The inhabitants of Tarn are known, in French language|French, as ''Tarnais'' (women: ''Tarnaises'').

Tarn has a population, in 2014, of 384,474, for a population density of  inhabitants/km2. The ''arrondissement'' of Castres, with 194,373 inhabitants, is the ''arrondissement'' with more inhabitants. The ''arrondissement'' of Albi has 190,101 inhabitants.

The city with more people living in it is the Capital (city)|capital, Albi (49,531). The subprefecture of Castres, Tarn|Castres has 41,382 inhabitants.
