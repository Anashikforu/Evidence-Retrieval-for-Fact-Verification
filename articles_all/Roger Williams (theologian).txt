He was widely known as a supporter of religious toleration and Freedom of religion|separation of church and state. He also supported fair dealings with Native Americans in the United States|Native Americans. In 1644, he received a royal charter creating the colony of Rhode Island, named for the main island in Narragansett Bay. He is believed to have started the first or second Baptist church in the United States. He is known to have left the church soon afterwards, saying, "God is too large to be housed under one roof."
