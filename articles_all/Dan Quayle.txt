Early life
Quayle was born at Indiana University Health Methodist Hospital (When he was born as Methodist Hospital in 1947) in Indianapolis, Indiana. been incorrectly referred to as ''James Danforth Quayle III''. In his memoirs, he points out that his birth name was simply James Danforth Quayle. The name Quayle originates from the Isle of Man, where his great-grandfather was born.

His maternal grandfather, Eugene C. Pulliam, was a rich and influential publishing magnate who founded Central Newspapers, Inc., owner of over a dozen major newspapers such as ''The Arizona Republic'' and ''The Indianapolis Star''. James C. Quayle moved his family to Arizona in 1955 to run part of the family's publishing business.
