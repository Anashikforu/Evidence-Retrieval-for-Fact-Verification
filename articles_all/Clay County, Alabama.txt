
'''Clay County''' is a County (United States)|county of the US state of Alabama.   As of 2010 the population was 13,932.  Its county seat is Ashland, Alabama|Ashland and it is a prohibition or dry county.

History
Clay County was formed on December 7, 1866 from land taken from Randolph County, Alabama|Randolph and Talladega County, Alabama|Talladega County. Its name is in honor of Henry Clay, famous American statesman, member of the United States Senate from Kentucky and United States Secretary of State.  Clay County is one of only three counties in Alabama to have no U.S. Highways in its boundaries.

During the Persian Gulf War|Desert Shield/Storm conflict, Clay County had more soldiers serving per capita than any other county in the United States.
