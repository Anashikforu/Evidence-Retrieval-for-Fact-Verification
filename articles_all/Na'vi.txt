The '''Na'vi''' are a Fictional character|fictional Indigenous peoples|indigenous species that live on the Fiction|fictional world of Pandora in the movie Avatar. They look like humans. They have two eyes, ears, arms, and legs. They also have a nose, a mouth, and expressions that are similar to humans. The Na'vi are different because they have blue skin, pointed ears, huge eyes, and Tail|tails.  They are also taller than humans. They have a smaller body frame. At the beginning, the Na'vi were meant to be more Alien life|alien in their looks. Movie director|Director James Cameron said that in the first drawing of the main character, "Neytiri", she had Fin|fins, Gill|gills, and cat-like features.

According to ''Entertainment Weekly'', "The Na'vi can wikt:commune|commune with animals on their planet by literally plugging their Ponytail|ponytails into the creatures' Nervous system|nerve systems. To become a wikt:warrior|warrior, a Na'vi must wikt:tame|tame and ride a creature known as Ikran."Josh Rottenberg, "James Cameron Talks ''Avatar'': Brave Blue World," ''Entertainment Weekly'' #1081 (December 18, 2009): 51. The Na'vi also use this system to mate with a "life partner". They make a bond that cannot be broken in the Na'vi's lifetime.

The Na'vi language is a constructed language. It was made for the movie to be spoken by the actors when they played Na'vi characters. The Na'vi language was made by linguistics professor Paul Frommer|Paul Former of the University of Southern California. Frommer made the language so it could be spoken by human Actor|actors. It uses syntax and grammar from real languages. The professor made up over 1,000 words for the Na'vi language. He taught the actors who played Na'vi characters to speak it for the movie.
