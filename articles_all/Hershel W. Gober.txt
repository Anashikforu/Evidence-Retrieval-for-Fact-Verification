
'''Hershel Wayne Gober''' (born December 21, 1936)  is a wikt:former|former government wikt:official|official and Vietnam veteran|Vietnam War veteran. He served as acting United States Secretary of Veterans Affairs (VA) two times during the Bill Clinton|Clinton wikt:administration|administration. The first time was from July 1, 1997 until January 2, 1998 between the resignation of Secretary Jesse Brown and the appointment of Togo D. West, Jr. as Acting Secretary.  The second time came on July 25, 2000 and lasted until January 20, 2001 after the resignation of Secretary West; this time Gober served in the post until the end of the Clinton presidency.

He started out in the United States Department of Veterans Affairs|VA as Deputy Secretary, serving from February 4, 1993 until August 10, 2000.  Gober was also Secretary-designate, when Clinton named him on July 31, 1997U.S. Senate. 
During his time as Secretary, Gober headed a delegation that traveled to Vietnam to try to find information about missing veterans there.Clinton, Bill. 
Before serving in the VA, Gober was Director of the Arkansas Department of Veterans Affairs from January 4, 1988 to February 4, 1993 during President Clinton's time as Governor.

In Vietnam
Gober served two tours in Vietnam. He also worked with an American/Vietnamese team that made Vietnamese songs. Later, in 1969, he was hurt while serving as a company commander.
