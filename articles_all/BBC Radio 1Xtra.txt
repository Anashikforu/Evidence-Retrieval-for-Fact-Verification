'''BBC Radio 1Xtra''' (also known as '''1Xtra''') is a digital radio in the United Kingdom|digital United Kingdom|British contemporary black music radio station|station from the BBC. The station was launched at 6pm on August 16, 2002. It was called ''Network X'' during the planning period. It is the complement station to BBC Radio 1|Radio 1 and is broadcast from the same building as Radio 1 - Yalding House in central London. This is part of the BBC's massive Broadcasting House complex of radio studios and other offices.

Music policy
The playlist generally includes British, Caribbean, African and North American hip hop music|hip hop, grime, bassline, UK garage|garage, dubstep, Funk|funky, drum and bass, bashment, Soca music|soca, reggae, gospel music, bhangra, Soul music|soul and R&B. It is available on digital radio (Digital Audio Broadcasting|DAB), digital satellite television, digital terrestrial television (Freeview (United Kingdom)|Freeview), and the Internet. It has its own news team: 1Xtra News.

Audience profile
1Xtra's audience is usually aged between 15 and 30 years old. The upper age range is deliberately lower than sister station Radio 1 which is closer to 35.

According to the "Submission to the Secretary of State's review of digital channels" in March 2004, 1Xtra "provides music output 24 hours a day, punctuated by bespoke BBC 
news bulletins and other speech output designed specifically to be pertinent to the 
audience."
