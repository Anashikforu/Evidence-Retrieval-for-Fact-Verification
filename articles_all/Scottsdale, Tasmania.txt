
'''Scottsdale''' is a town in the north-east of Tasmania, Australia. It is 63 kilometre|km northeast of Launceston, Tasmania|Launceston and 22 km southeast of  town of Bridport, Tasmania|Bridport. It is part of the Dorset Council.

The area was first surveyed in 1855 and was described as, ''"the best soil on the island ... well watered, with a mild climate"'' by surveyor James Scott. The town was named after him. Scottsdale is now a major agriculture|agricultural centre.
