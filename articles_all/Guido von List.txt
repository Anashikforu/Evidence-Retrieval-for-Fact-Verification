'''Guido (von) List''' (born '''Karl Anton List'''; 5 October 1848 in Vienna &ndash; 17 May 1919 in Berlin) was an Austrian writer. His works were influenced very much by ideas of race (sociology)|race. He was influenced by Arthur de Gobineau, Helena Petrovna Blavatsky, and others. He believed there was a global jewish conspiracy. He thought that the conspiracy threatened those he believed to be the Aryan race (sociology)|race. He also believed that runes had magic power. His beliefs were not unpopular in Western Europe in the 19th century. He was an occultist. A contemporary of his was Houston Steward Chamberlain. Chambelain lived at the same time as he did.
