

'''Palak''' is one of the 51 Union Councils of Pakistan|Union Councils of Abbottabad District in the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan.

 Location 
Palak is in the southeastern edge of Abbottabad District and is next to Punjab (Pakistan)|Punjab province and Kashmir, it is also next to the following Union Councils of Abbottabad District, Seer Gharbi to the west, Nathiagali to the North and Malkot to the north west.

Palak is the gateway into Abbottabad District.

 Subdivisions
Image:Palak Sub Div.svg|thumb|200px|right|Administrative subdivisions of Palak.

Palak is the largest Union Council of the Circle Bakote area, in 2000 Malkot which used to be a Union Council was joined into Palak.

Palak UC is subdivided into the following areas:
Arwar, Darwaza, Kala Bon, Longal, Malkot, Palak, Riala (Riyala) and Soorjal (Surajaal).

People
The total population of the Palak is almost 5,000, the main tribes of Palak are the Dhund Abbasi, Gujars and Sattis.
