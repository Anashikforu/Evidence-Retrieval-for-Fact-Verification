
'''Levon Ter-Petrosyan''' (Armenian language|Armenian: Լևոն Տեր-Պետրոսյան; Russian language|Russian: Левон Тер-Петросян) (born January 9, 1945), also '''Levon Ter-Petrosyan''', was the President of Armenia from 1991 to 1998. Because of many economics|economic and politics|political problems, he resigned on February 3, 1998. He was replaced by Robert Kocharyan. Levon Ter Petrosian, announced he will run again for office in the presidential election which will start next year. In 1968, Ter-Petrossian graduated from the Oriental Studies Department of the Yerevan State University. In 1972, he completed his postgraduate studies at the Leningrad State University. In 1987, he received his doctoral degree from the same university. In 1972-1978, Ter-Petrossian worked as junior researcher at the Literature Institute of Armenia named after Manouk Abeghian. In 1978-1985, he held the post of science secretary at Matenadaran named after Saint Mesrop Mashtots. Since 1985, Ter-Petrossian has been working at Matenadaran as a senior researcher.
