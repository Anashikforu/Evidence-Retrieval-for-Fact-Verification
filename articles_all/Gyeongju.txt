
'''Gyeongju''' (경주) is a wikt:coast|coastal city in the southeastern corner of North Gyeongsang province in South Korea. They are part of the Taebaek Mountains|Taebaek range.

Gyeongju was the capital of the ancient kingdom of Silla (57 BC – 935 AD). Silla ruled most of the Korean Peninsula between the 7th and 9th centuries, and many archaeology|archaeological sites from that time are still in the city. This is why Gyeongju is often called "the museum without walls".Robinson et al. 2007. p.187
For example, Gyeongju Historic Areas and Yangdong Folk Village were made World Heritage Sites by UNESCO.

In 1995, the city of Gyeongju and Gyeongju County were put together.Tamásy & Taylor, (2008) p.129
