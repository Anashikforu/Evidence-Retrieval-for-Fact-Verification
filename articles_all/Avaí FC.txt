

'''Avaí Futebol Clube''', or '''Avaí''' as they are usually called, is a Brazilian Football League Teams|Brazilian football team from Florianópolis in Santa Catarina (state)|Santa Catarina, founded on September 1, 1923. It became nationally known after the tennis player Gustavo Kuerten declared to be a supporter of Avaí.
Home stadium is the Estádio da Ressacada|Ressacada stadium, capacity 19,000.
 History 

The club was founded after a businessman called Amadeu Horn gave football kits to a group of boys. These boys played a match against a team called Humaitá, and won. On September 1, 1923, at Amadeu Horn's house, the club was founded, being called at that time Avahy Football Club. The team was named Avahy after the Battle of Avahy, in the War of the Triple Alliance|Paraguayan War In the following year, it became the first Santa Catarina State Championship champion.

Avaí played the Campeonato Brasileiro Série A|Brazilian First Division ("Série A") four times: in 1974, 1976, 1977 and 1979. In 1998, it won its only national title, the Campeonato Brasileiro Série C|Brazilian Third Division ("Série C"). Since 1999, it plays the Campeonato Brasileiro Série B|Série B and the best campaigns happened in 2001 and 2004, when Avaí reached the Final Four, but could not be promoted to the First Division (only the two best placed teams were promoted). Avaí is the club with more titles won in the Santa Catarina State Championship in the 20th century (13).

 Rival 
Avaí's greatest rival is Figueirense F.C..

 Club colors and nickname 
 The club colours are blue and white, and it is known as "''The Lion of the Island''" (because 90% of the Florianópolis territory is established on an island).
