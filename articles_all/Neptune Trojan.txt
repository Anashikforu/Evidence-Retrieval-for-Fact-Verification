A '''Neptune Trojan''' is an asteroid which is in the same orbit as the planet Neptune (planet)|Neptune. 

Their name comes from the Trojan asteroids which have the same orbital period as a planet. As of March 2007, there are six.

The discovery of  in a high inclination (>25°) orbit is significant because it suggests a ‘thick’ cloud of Trojans.
The New Horizons spacecraft passed through the Lagrangian point|L5 Neptune region in 2014. It was preparing to observe Pluto and did not observe any asteroids there.
