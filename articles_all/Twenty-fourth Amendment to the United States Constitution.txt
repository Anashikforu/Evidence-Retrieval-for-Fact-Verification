 
The '''Twenty-fourth Amendment to the United States Constitution''' is the twenty-fourth time the United States Constitution has been amended (wikt:revised|revised). The twenty-fourth amendment prevents the United States Congress and US states from charging a poll tax on federal elections. The amendment was proposed by Congress to the states on August 27, 1962, and was ratified by the states on January 23, 1964.

Text
{{quote|'''Section 1. '''The right of citizens of the United States to vote in any primary or other election for President or Vice President, for electors for President or Vice President, or for Senator or Representative in Congress, shall not be denied or abridged by the United States or any State by reason of failure to pay poll tax or other tax.

'''Section 2. '''The Congress shall have power to enforce this article by appropriate legislation.}}
