'''Pork barrel''' is the wikt:appropriation|appropriation of government spending for localized projects that are secured primarily to bring money to a representative's district. The usage originated in American English.Tom Wakeford; Jasber Singh, "Towards Empowered Participation: Stories and Reflections" (London; International Institute for Environment and Development, 2008), p. 50, n. 2 It was a reference to the custom of Slavery|slave owners giving a gift of Salt pork in a barrel to their Slavery|slaves. In Election|election campaigns, the term is used in a wikt:negative|negative way to attack wikt:opponent|opponents. However, scholars use it as a Technical terminology|technical term regarding legislative control of local appropriations.
