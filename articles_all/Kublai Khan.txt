
'''Kublai Khan''' (23 Sept. 1215 - 18 Feb. 1294) was a Mongol Empire|Mongol Khan (title)|khan, or military ruler, who brought the Mongol Empire to its greatest height. He finished the invasion of China and was also the founder of the Yuan Dynasty. He lived during the 13th century AD. Kublai Khan was the grandson of the great Mongol king Genghis Khan.

Kublai Khan was born to Tolui Khan (title)|Khan, and his wife Sorghaghtani. While growing up, he participated in campaigns with his father, until he was 17 when his father died.Davis, Paul K., and Stanley Sandler. "Kublai Khan." World History: Ancient and Medieval Eras. Kublai led armies against the Song Dynasty and took over most of China and much of Asia.Davis, Richard L. "Kublai Khan." World Book Advanced. World Book, 2013. Web.  27 Feb. 2013. As the first foreigner to rule all of China, he established the Yuan dynasty|Yuan Dynasty, which had Chinese ideas but Mongolian roots.
