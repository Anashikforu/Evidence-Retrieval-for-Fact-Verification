The '''Battle of Corinth''' was a battle fought between the Roman Republic and the Ancient Greece|Ancient Greek City-state|state of Corinth and its allies in the Achaean League in 146 BC. This was a few months after the Romans destroyed Carthage in the Third Punic War. It resulted in total destruction of Corinth, which was previously famous for its wealth. The Romans burnt it to the ground and went on to conquer the rest of Greece.
