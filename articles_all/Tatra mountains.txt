
The '''Tatra Mountains''', '''Tatras''' or '''Tatra''' ('''Tatry '''in both Slovak language|Slovak and Polish language|Polish), constitute a mountain range which forms a natural border between Slovakia and Poland. They occupy an area of 750&nbsp;km², the major part (600&nbsp;km²) of which lies in Slovakia. The highest Tatra peak, at 2655&nbsp;m, is Gerlachovský štít, located in Slovakia. Rysy, at 2499&nbsp;m, is the highest Polish peak.

The Tatra Mountains are the highest mountain range in the Carpathian Mountains. Although considerably smaller than the Alps, they are classified as having an alpine landscape. Their high mountain character, combined with great accessibility, makes them popular with tourists and scientists.
