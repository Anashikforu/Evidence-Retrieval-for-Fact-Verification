

The term '''Renaissance man''' or '''polymath''' is used for a very clever man who is good at many different things. It is named after the Renaissance|Renaissance period of history (from the 14th century to the 16th century|16th or 17th century in Europe). Two of the best-known people from this time were Leonardo da Vinci and Michelangelo. Leonardo was a famous painting|painter but he was also a scientist, philosopher, engineer, and mathematics|mathematician.  Michelangelo was an extraordinary sculpture|sculptor, painter, architecture|architect and poetry|poet. They are known as two of the highest examples of what a Renaissance man is.
But the term "Renaissance man" is also often used for wikt:extraordinary|extraordinary people not from the Renaissance period. It can be used for anyone who is very intelligence|clever at many different things, no matter when that person lived. Albert Schweitzer was a 20th century "Renaissance man" who was a theology|theologian, musician, philosophy|philosopher and Medical doctor|doctor. Benjamin Franklin was a "Renaissance man" who lived in the 18th century and was an author and printing|printer, politics|politician, scientist, inventor and soldier.

 Other polymaths 
= Ancient history =
 '''Aristotle''' (Greek: Ἀριστοτέλης, Aristotle) (384&nbsp;BC – 322&nbsp;BC) was a Greek philosopher who studied and wrote about many subjects, including physics, metaphysics, poetry, theater, music, logic, rhetoric, politics, government, ethics, biology and zoology.
 '''Archimedes'''  (Greek: Ἀρχιμήδης; c. 287&nbsp;BC – c. 212&nbsp;BC) was a Greek mathematician, physicist, engineer, inventor, and astronomer.
