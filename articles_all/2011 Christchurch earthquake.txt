Prime Minister of New Zealand|Prime Minister John Key said that the 22nd of February "may well be New Zealand's darkest day".
At least 166 people have been confirmed dead with the final death toll expected to be over 200.

At the end of the first day 65 people were known to have died, At 5pm local time Radio New Zealand reported that 80% of the city was without electricity.  Water and wastewater services are not working in many parts of the city. People are being asked to use as little water as possible and to collect rainwater. It is expected that the State of Emergency Level 3, the highest possible in a regional disaster, will last at least five days. Medical staff from the New Zealand Army|army are being used.

The ''Sydney Morning Herald'' reported that two buses and a number of cars were crushed by falling buildings.

Buildings damaged

Buildings have collapsed around Cathedral Square, Christchurch|Cathedral Square in downtown Christchurch. The spire of ChristChurch Cathedral has completely collapsed. Christchurch Hospital was evacuated due to damage in some areas,Radio New Zealand, monitored 8:55&nbsp;pm EST but soon reopened to treat the injured. It has treated over 220 serious injuries so far, even though sometimes they were working by torchlight. The New Zealand defence forces have been called in to help move people out of the central business district. Fires were reported at the Canterbury Television (CTV) building.

The historic Canterbury Provincial Chambers building has also suffered major damage from the earthquake.

The Statistics New Zealand buildings in Christchurch were also damaged. They were due to process much of the March 2011 census of New Zealand. The census has been cancelled because people living in Christchurch have had their lives seriously disrupted.

=Christchurch Cathedral=

ChristChurch Cathedral, Christchurch|ChristChurch Cathedral has lost the top of its main spire and its roof has fallen in. However, after the building was made safe to enter over a week after the quake, and searched thoroughly, ''no'' bodies were located.

=Cathedral of the Blessed Sacrament=
The Catholic cathedral of Christchurch, the Cathedral of the Blessed Sacrament, Christchurch|Cathedral of the Blessed Sacrament, has half collapsed.

=Lyttelton=

Buildings in Lyttelton, New Zealand|Lyttelton have been damaged; a fire officer said that 60% of the buildings in the main street have collapsed. The town's historic Timeball Station was also badly damaged. The New Zealand Historic Places Trust who own the building have decided that it is unsafe to try to repair it. It will be dismantled, if possible, and perhaps re-constructed.

Beyond Christchurch and Lyttelton
Around 30 million tonnes of ice fell off Tasman Glacier into Tasman Lake, about  from the epicentre, hitting tour boats with waves 3.5 high.

Emergency response and relief efforts
New Zealand Prime Minister, John Key, said "All Civil Defence procedures have now been activated; the Civil Defence bunker at Parliament is in operation here in Wellington, New Zealand|Wellington."

Australia has offered help, and immediately sent a search and rescue team of 70 from New South Wales on a RAAF C-130 Hercules. Minister for Foreign Affairs (Australia)|Foreign Affairs Minister Kevin Rudd also said a second team from South Australia will also be sent with more equipment. Australian Prime Minister Julia Gillard has offered John Key any other help he may need.The United States and Japan have also offered help.

Christchurch International Airport was only open for emergency air traffic, but has now been opened to all local and domestic flights. The earthquake has affected all air traffic in New Zealand. Air traffic control services by Airways New Zealand, the national Air Traffic Control organisation, have been disrupted.
