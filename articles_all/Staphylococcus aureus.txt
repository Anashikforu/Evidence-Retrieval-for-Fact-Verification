'''''Staphylococcus aureus''''' is the most common cause of staph infections. It is a round Bacteria|bacterium, often living on the skin or in the nose of a person. About one third of the U.S. population are carriers of Staph. Aureus Heyman D. 2004. ''Control of communicable diseases manual''. 19th ed, Washington DC: American Public Health Association. ''Staphylococcus aureus'' can cause a range of illnesses from minor skin infections to life-threatening diseases.

''S. aureus'' can be treated with antibiotics, but they easily get resistance to antibiotics. When they are resistant, they are called MRSA (methicillin-resistant S. aureus).Methicillin is a type of synthetic penicillin, found in many antibiotics.
