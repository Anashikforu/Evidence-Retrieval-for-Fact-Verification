'''Belinda''' is a closer Satellite (natural)|moon to Uranus (planet)|Uranus, a planet in the solar system. Belinda was found from the images taken by ''Voyager 2'' on 1986, It is named after the heroine of Alexander Pope's ''The Rape of the Lock''. It is also designated '''Uranus&nbsp;XIV'''.

Belinda belongs to Portia Group of moons, which also includes Bianca (moon)|Bianca, Cressida (moon)|Cressida, Desdemona (moon)|Desdemona, portia (moon)|Portia, Juliet (moon)|Juliet, Cupid (moon)|Cupid, Rosalind (moon)|Rosalind and Perdita (moon)|Perdita. These moons have similar orbits and photometric properties. Other than its orbit, radius of  45&nbsp;km and geometric albedo of 0.08 almost nothing is known about it.
