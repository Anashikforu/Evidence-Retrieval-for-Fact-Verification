A '''portmanteau word''' () is made of other words, or parts of words. It comes from putting  two (or more) other words together,

 Origin 
''Portmanteau'' originally meant a type of suitcase with two separate parts for storage.Oxford dictionaries. The first use as a word combination appeared in Lewis Carroll's book ''Through the Looking-Glass''. There Humpty Dumpty explains that some of the unusual words in ''Jabberwocky'' are other words packed together into one word, as two parts of a portmanteau suitcase are packed together.

 Examples 
''Portmanteau'' itself is an example, combining the French words for carry (porter) and coat (manteau) into a coat carrier. ''Wikipedia'' is a portmanteau of wiki (Hawaiian language|Hawaiian for fast) and encyclopedia.
