
'''Martin Luther King, Jr. Day''' is a United States|US Federal Holiday. It commemorates civil rights activist Martin Luther King, Jr.'s birthday. It is celebrated on the third Monday in January and is one of only four United States Federal holidays to honor an individual person.The other three federal holidays honoring individuals are George Washington|Washington's Birthday (which is usually called called ''President's Day''), Columbus Day, and Christmas.  Most people working in banks and other government owned places get the day off.  Many students get a day off from school on this day. See 
Ronald Reagan|President Ronald Reagan signed a bill, proposed by Katie Hall (politician)|Representative Katie Hall of Indiana, to create a federal holiday honoring King.
