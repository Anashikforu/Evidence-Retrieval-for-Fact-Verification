
'''Villeneuve-sur-Lot''' (Gascon language|Gascon: ''Vilanuèva d'Olt'') is a town and Communes of France|commune in southwestern France, in the Regions of France|region of Nouvelle-Aquitaine. It is a Prefectures in France|subprefecture of the Lot-et-Garonne Departments of France|department. The commune was formerly named ''Villeneuve-d'Agen''.

It is also the Capital city|capital of the ''Arrondissements of France|arrondissement'' of Arrondissement of Villeneuve-sur-Lot|Villeneuve-sur-Lot and of 2 Cantons in France|''cantons'' in the department: Canton of Villeneuve-sur-Lot-1|Villeneuve-sur-Lot-1 and Canton of Villeneuve-sur-Lot-2|Villeneuve-sur-Lot-2.

History
The region where the city is was first populated by the Ancient Rome|Romans. In the 11th century, a Benedictine abbey was constructed near where is the city now. There was a small village of pottery|potters and farmers around the abbey. Between 1254 and 1263, Alphonse of Poitiers had a bastide built. He also gave the bastide city rights. The bastide has a Rectangle|rectangular look, with a central Town square|square.

Because of its privileged geographical situation on the river lot, the city played an important role in wars of the region:
 In the Hundred Years' War
 In the Huguenot wars
 In the Fronde, a civil war in France.

Geography

Villeneuve-sur-Lot is surrounded by the ''communes'' Lédat, Castelnaud-de-Gratecambe, La Sauvetat-sur-Lède, Monflanquin, Savignac-sur-Leyze, Saint-Aubin, Lot-et-Garonne|Saint-Aubin, Trentels, Saint-Sylvestre-sur-Lot, Penne-d'Agenais, Hautefage-la-Tour, Pujols, Lot-et-Garonne|Pujols and Bias, Lot-et-Garonne|Bias.




The Lot (river)|Lot river flows through the city of Villeneuve-sur-Lot. The Lède river, a tributary of the Lot, flows through the northern part of the ''commune''.

=Climate=
The climate of Villeneuve-sur-Lot is an oceanic climate (Köppen climate classification '''Cfb'''), with mild winters and warm summers.

Population
The inhabitants of Villeneuve-sur-Lot are known, in French language|French, as ''Villeneuvois'' (women: ''Villeneuvoises'').
