The '''Wildlife Protection Act of 1972''' is a set of laws created in 1972 by the India#Government|Government of India. Before 1972, India had only five national parks.

The Act protects wild animals, birds and plants in all of India, except the State of Jammu and Kashmir, which has its own wildlife act. It has six sections that give different degrees of protection.
