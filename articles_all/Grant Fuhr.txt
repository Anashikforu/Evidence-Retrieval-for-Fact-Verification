
'''Grant Scott Fuhr''' (born September 28, 1962) is a Canadians|Canadian retired professional ice hockey goaltender. He played in the National Hockey League. In 2003 in sports|2003, he was added into the Hockey Hall of Fame. Born of biracial parents, Fuhr was adopted as a baby and raised in Spruce Grove, Alberta|Spruce Grove, Alberta.
Awards
Named to NHL Second All-Star Team in 1981–82 NHL season|1982
Named to NHL First All-Star Team  in 1987–88 NHL season|1988
Won Vezina Trophy in 1988
Won William M. Jennings Trophy in 1993–94 NHL season|1994 (shared with Dominik Hašek)
Participated in NHL All-Star Game in 1982, 1984, 1985, 1986 (MVP), 1988, 1989
In 1997, he was ranked number 70 on List of 100 greatest hockey players by The Hockey News|''The Hockey News''' list of the 100 Greatest Hockey Players.
