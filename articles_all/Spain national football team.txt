
'''Spain national football team '''(Spanish language|Spanish: ''Selección de fútbol de España)'' is the national football (soccer)|football team of Spain. The current head coach is Luis Enrique Martínez García|Luis Enrique. The team is often called ''La Roja ''("The Red One"), ''La Furia Roja'' ("The Red Fury"), ''La Furia Española'' ("The Spanish Fury") or just ''La Furia'' ("The Fury").

 Team history 
The Spanish team became a member of FIFA in 1904, even though the team was made in 1909. Spain had their first match on the 8th of August of 1920 against Denmark. Since the team's creation in 1909, they have been in 13 FIFA World Cups, and 9 UEFA European Football Championships.

Spain are three-time UEFA European Football Championship|European champions, having won the UEFA Euro 2012 qualifying Group A|UEFA Euro 2012, UEFA Euro 2008, and 1964 European Championship. From November 2006 through June 2009 Spain went undefeated for 35 matches, a record shared with Brazil national football team|Brazil. Spain are one of the eight national teams to have won the FIFA World Cup; they won their first at the 2010 World Cup in South Africa when they defeated the Netherlands national football team|Netherlands 1–0 after extra time. Andrés Iniesta scored the winning goal in the 116th minute. 

The team's achievements have led to many commentators and football experts naming them one of the best international sides in football history.
