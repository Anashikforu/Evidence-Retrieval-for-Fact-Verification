'''Anaximander''' ( ''Anaximandros''; c. 610 – c. 546 BC) was a Pre-Socratic Ancient Greece|Greek philosopher who lived in Miletus,"Anaximander" in ''Chambers's Encyclopædia''. London: George Newnes, 1961, Vol. 1, p. 403. a city of Ionia. He belonged to the Milesian school and learned the teachings of his master Thales. He succeeded him and became the second master of that school where he counted Anaximenes of Miletus|Anaximenes and Pythagoras amongst his pupils.Guthrie W.K.C. 1962. ''A history of Greek philosophy''. Vol 1: the earlier Presocratics and the Pythagoreans. Cambridge.

Little of his life and work is known today. According to available historical documents, he is the first philosopher known to have written down his studies,Themistius, ''Oratio'' 36, §&nbsp;317 although only one fragment of his work remains. Fragmentary testimonies found in documents after his death provide a portrait of the man.
