


'''Boris Franz Becker''' (born 22 November 1967) is a  Germans|German retirement|retired tennis player. He was born in Leimen (Baden)|Leimen, Germany. At the age of 17, he became the youngest ever winner at The Championships, Wimbledon|Wimbledon in 1985. He won the contest while being seeding|unseeded. That means that he had not achieved enough to be assigned within an order of the better players at the start of the tournament.

He became a very wikt:successful|successful player, winning the Wimbledon title again in 1986. Becker also helped West Germany win its first Davis Cup in 1988. In 1989, he won the Wimbledon, the U.S. Open (tennis)|US Open and another Davis Cup.

In 1991, he reached the World number 1 ranking by winning the Australian Open. Becker defeated Jim Courier in straight sets to win the 1992 Association of Tennis Professionals Tour World title. His final Grand Slam title was in 1996, where he beat Michael Chang for another Australian Open title in four sets.

Becker completed at the 1992 Summer Olympics where he won a gold medal in the men's doubles event.

Throughout his career, Becker won 49 singles titles and 15 doubles titles. He is known for his rivalry with certain players, including Ivan Lendl, Stefan Edberg and Michael Stich.
