History
''Nihon Shoki'' is the second oldest book of classical Japanese Japanese mythology|mythology and history of Japan|history.  It includes details that are not in the ''Kojiki'', which is the oldest historical record of ancient Japan.

This book is important to people who study the history of Japan because it has information about the early rulers not found in other books.William George Aston|Aston, William George. (1896). ''Nihongi: Chronicles of Japan from the Earliest Times to AD 697,'' pp. xiii-xvi.

Dates in the ''Nihon Shoki'' before the late 7th century were likely recorded using the Genka calendar system.
