Traditional history
Kōan is almost certainly a wikt:legend|legend. The ''Kojiki'' records only his name and genealogy. The  ''Nihonshoki'' includes Kōan as the fifth of .Aston, pp. 145-146.

Emperor Kōan was the second son of Emperor Kōshō.  ''Jinnō Shōtōki'' records that his mother was Yosotarashi-no-hime, who was the daughter of Okitsuyoso, and ancestress of the Owari.Varley, p. 90.
