


The '''Yupik''' (in the Central Alaskan Yup'ik language, '''Yup'ik''', plural '''Yupiit''') people are a group of Indigenous peoples|native people who mainly live in certain parts of Alaska, somewhere near Fairbanks, Alaska|Fairbanks.  The Spanish flu|1918 influenza pandemic killed many of the indigenous people in Alaska. Some of the Yupi'k people's traditions are celebrating the dead. They celebrate by having ceremonies with a shaman, where they connect with the dead. They also name the next child born after the most recent person who has died.
