
'''Golpar''' (''Heracleum persicum''), also known as '''Persian hogweed''', is a flowering plant. It grows wild in mountains.

 Uses 
Golpar seeds are used as a spice in Persia|Persian cooking. These seedpods are slightly wikt:bitter|bitter. They are usually sold in powder and are often sold as "Angelica Seeds." The powder is sprinkled over beans and potatoes. Golpar is also used in soups and stews. It is often sprinkled over pomegranate seeds. Golpar is also mixed with vinegar into which lettuce leaves are dipped before eating.
