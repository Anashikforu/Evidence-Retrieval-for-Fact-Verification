The ''Gukanshō'' records that Kōshō ruled from the palace of ''Ikekokoro-no-miya'' at Waki-no-kami in what will come to be known as Yamato province.Brown, p. 251; Varley, p. 90.

 Traditional history 
Kōshō is almost certainly a wikt:legend|legend. The ''Kojiki'' records only his name and genealogy. The  ''Nihonshoki'' includes Kōshō as the fourth of .Aston, pp. 142-143.

Emperor Kōshō was the oldest son of Emperor Itoku.Ponsonby-Fane, p. 30; Varley, p. 90.  ''Jinnō Shōtōki'' records that his mother was Amanotoyototsu-hime, and his grandmother by his mother's side was Okishimimi-no-kami.Varley, p. 90.

 Events of Kōshō's life 
The absence of information about Kōshō does not show that he was not real. Very little information is wikt:available|available for study before the rule of the 29th monarch, Emperor Kimmei (509?-571).Titsingh, pp. 34-36; Brown, pp. 261-262; Varley, pp. 123-124.
