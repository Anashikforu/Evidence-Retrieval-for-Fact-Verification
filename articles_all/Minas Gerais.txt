

'''Minas Gerais''' () is one of the 26 states of Brazil|states of Brazil. It has the second highest number of people. It has the fourth largest area. The capital (city)|capital is the city of Belo Horizonte, near the center of the state. The name means "General mines". Minas Gerais was formed mainly by colony|colonists searching for gold, gemstones and diamonds.

Location
Minas Gerais is in the west of the southeastern part of Brazil. That area also has the states of São Paulo (state)|São Paulo, Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro and Espírito Santo. Minas Gerais shares borders with Bahia and Goiás (north), Mato Grosso do Sul (far west), the states of São Paulo (state)|São Paulo and Rio de Janeiro (state)|Rio de Janeiro (south) and the state of Espírito Santo (east). It also shares a short border with the Brazilian Federal District.

Cities
Even though it is not the largest state in Brazil, Minas Gerais is the state with the most cities. Of the more than 5,500 municipality|municipalities in Brazil, Minas has more than 850 of them. The most important ones are the capital Belo Horizonte, Contagem, Betim, Ribeirão das Neves, Juiz de Fora, Montes Claros, Uberlândia and Ouro Preto.
