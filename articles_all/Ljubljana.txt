
'''Ljubljana''' is the capital (city)|capital city of Slovenia. It is the largest city in that country. The city became the most important settlement in the area when Ancient Rome|Romans ruled Slovenia. There are many museums with things from that time in Ljubljana.

As of 2002, there were 265,881 people living in Ljubljana. The University of Ljubljana is in Ljubljana.

 Geography 
The city has an area of 163.8&nbsp;square&nbsp;kilometers (63.2&nbsp;sq&nbsp;mi). It is located about 320&nbsp;kilometers (200&nbsp;mi) south of Munich, 477&nbsp;kilometers (296&nbsp;mi) east of Zürich, 250&nbsp;kilometers (160&nbsp;mi) east of Venice, 350&nbsp;kilometers (220&nbsp;mi) southwest of Vienna, 224&nbsp;kilometers (139&nbsp;mi) south of Salzburg and 400&nbsp;kilometers (250&nbsp;mi) southwest of Budapest.

Ljubljana is 295&nbsp;meters (968&nbsp;ft) above sea level.

The main bodies of water in Ljubljana are the Ljubljanica, the Sava, the Gradaščica, the Mali Graben, the Iška and the Iščica Rivers. 

July and August are the warmest months.  The daily temperatures at these times are generally between 25 and 30&nbsp;°C (77 and 86&nbsp;°F).  January is the coldest month with temperatures closer to 0&nbsp;°C (32&nbsp;°F).  Ljubljana is also one of the wettest European capitals. Yearly precipitation is about 1,400&nbsp;mm (55&nbsp;in).  It can also snow anytime from December to February.

 Cityscape 
Ljubljana features many kinds of architectural styles.  Prominent buildings include Prešeren Square, Ljubljana Castle, Town Hall, Ljubljana Cathedral, and Nebotičnik.

There are also several parks around the city.  Tivoli City Park (''Mestni park Tivoli'') is the largest park in Ljubljana.

The most notable bridges of Ljubljana are the Triple Bridge (''Tromostovje''), the Trnovo Bridge (''Trnovski most''), the Dragon Bridge (''Zmajski most''), the Hradecky Bridge (Slovene: ''Hradeckega most''), and the Butchers' Bridge (''Mesarski most''). The Trnovo Bridge crosses the Gradaščica, whereas the others cross the Ljubljanica.
