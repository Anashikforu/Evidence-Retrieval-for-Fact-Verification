
The '''sign of the cross''' is a Human body|physical gesture made by Roman Catholic Church|Catholic and Eastern Orthodox Church|Orthodox Christianity|Christians, and sometimes also Protestantism|Protestants. It is usually done at the start and end of prayer|prayers. 

In the Catholic tradition, the right hand goes from the forehead, to the chest, and then to the left and right shoulder|shoulders (the order of shoulders does not matter). This is sometimes done while saying "God|Father, Jesus|Son, Holy Spirit."

In the Orthodox tradition, the tips of the thumb and the middle and index fingers are put together, and the ring finger and little finger are put against the palm. The first three fingers are for faith in the Trinity, and the other two fingers are for the two natures of Jesus, divine and human.
