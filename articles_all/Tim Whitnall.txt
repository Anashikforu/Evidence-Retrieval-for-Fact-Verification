



'''Timothy Charles "Tim" Whitnall''' (born 27 June 1961 in Canvey Island, Essex) is an English playwright, screenwriter, and actor known for playing Angelo in the long-running CITV series ''Mike and Angelo'' and narrating the BBC children's TV programme ''Teletubbies'' (UK and Canada). In the USA, the USA narrator was Rolf Saxon.  He currently co-provides voices in ''Thomas and Friends|Thomas & Friends''.

Career
He has starred in many West End musicals including ''Elvis (musical)|Elvis'', ''Grease (musical)|Grease'', ''The Rocky Horror Show'', and ''Good Rockin' Tonight''.

He worked extensively in film, TV, and radio and presented (and wrote for) the BBC School's series, "The Music Arcade" (with Lucie Skeaping), "Into Music", "Time and Tune", "Music Workshop", and "Let's Sing". He has provided many voice-overs and vocals for TV commercials, animations, and jingles.

Whitnall's theatre play ''The Sociable Plover'', first performed at Old Red Lion Theatre in 2005,

Whitnall's stage-play ''Morecambe (play)|Morecambe'' – a tribute to the late comedian Eric Morecambe – won a Fringe First Award

In 2012, BBC Four screened Whitnall's 90-minute drama ''Best Possible Taste: The Kenny Everett Story'', which examined and celebrated the life of Kenny Everett. It was directed by James Strong, produced by Paul Frift and starred Oliver Lansley and Katherine Kelly. For it, Whitnall won the Breakthrough Talent Award in the 2013 BAFTA Television Craft Awards.

During 2014, Whitnall joined the cast of the Computer animation|CGI version of the British TV series ''Thomas & Friends'', and has provided the voices of the characters Railway engines (Thomas & Friends)#Timothy|Timothy and Non-rail vehicles (Thomas & Friends)#Reg|Reg (in the UK/US), and since 2015-6, Arlesdale Railway#Mike|Mike, Rolling stock (Thomas and Friends)#Jerome and Judy|Jerome and Non-rail vehicles (Thomas & Friends)#Oliver|Oliver the Excavator in the UK/US.

Personal life
He lives in Richmond, London|Richmond, Surrey with his partner, Anna Murphy, with whom he has a production company, Feather Productions Ltd.
