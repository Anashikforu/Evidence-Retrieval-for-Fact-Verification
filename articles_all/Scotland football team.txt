
The '''Scotland football team''' is the national football (soccer)|football team of Scotland. It is currently Coach (football)|managed by former Dundee United F.C.|Dundee United manager Craig Levein who took over from George Burley on 23rd December 2009.

Scotland has the joint oldest national football team in the world as they played England football team|England in the first ever international football match in 1872.

Stadium
Hampden Park in Glasgow is the home of the Scotland national team and is described by the Scottish Football Association as the ''National Stadium''.

Since the last redevelopment to Hampden was completed in 1999, Scotland have played all but one of their competitive matches there. Only when Celtic Park hosted the first 2008 European Football Championship|Euro 2008 qualification match against the Faroe Islands national football team|Faroe Islands. Celtic Park was used because the fixtures were decided by a random draw and Hampden had already been booked for a Robbie Williams concert on the same date.
