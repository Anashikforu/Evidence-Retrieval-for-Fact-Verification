Biography
Suzuki was born in Saitama, Saitama|Saitama on April 29, 1947. After graduating from Rikkyo University, he played for Yokohama F. Marinos|Nissan Motors from 1972 to 1974.

In 1989, he became manager for Japan women's national football team|Japan women's national team.
In August 1996, he became manager for Nikko Securities Dream Ladies and won the Nadeshiko League champions for 3 years in a row to 1998. However the club was disbanded due to financial strain end of 1998 season.

In June 1999, Japan under manager Miyauchi was defeated in group stage at 1999 FIFA Women's World Cup|1999 World Cup and failure to qualify for 2000 Summer Olympics. After 1999 World Cup, Suzuki became a manager for Japan national team again and managed Japan at 1999 AFC Women's Championship in November. Japan finished at the 4th place and he resigned end of the championship.
