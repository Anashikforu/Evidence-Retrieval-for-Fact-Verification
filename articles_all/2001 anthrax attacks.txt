The '''2001 Anthrax attacks''' were a series of incidents in the United States. The attacks began on September 18, 2001 and lasted for several weeks.
22 people were infected. Five people died. It was believed that the person who was responsible for this was Dr. Bruce Edward  Ivins. He was a senior biodefense researcher who was employed by the United States government|U.S. government. Ivins suicide|killed himself in 2008. He was not prosecuted for the crime but the evidence suggests that he acted alone in mailing the envelopes.
