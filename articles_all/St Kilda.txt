


'''St Kilda''' is a small archipelago of Western Isles|Hebridean islands. It is northwest of the coast of Scotland. The islands are a UNESCO World Heritage Site.UNESCO, 
St Kilda has large seabird colony|colonies.  The island has the largest colony of North Atlantic gannets in the world.National Trust for Scotland (National Trust), 
History
It is known that people have lived on St Kilda for over 2000 years.  The entire population was Evacuation|evacuated from the island in 1930. St Kilda was given to the National Trust for Scotland in 1957.

In the 21st century, the only year-round residents are military|defence personnel. Many conservation workers, volunteers and scientists spend time on the islands in the summer months.
National Nature Reserve
St Kilda became a National Nature Reserves (NNR) in 1957. It is one of 71 NNRs in Scotland. In 2004, the NNR was expanded.  The nearby sea|marine features were added. The islands were also added.

In July 2005, it became one of the few World Heritage Sites to have a "mixed" status for its Nature|natural and culture|cultural qualities.National Trust,
