'''''The Daily Telegraph''''' is a United Kingdom|British daily morning broadsheet newspaper, which began in 1855. The newspaper was started by Arthur B. Sleigh in June 1855 as the '''''Daily Telegraph and Courier''''', and since 2004 has been owned by David and Frederick Barclay.

In January 2008, the ''Telegraph'' was the highest selling newspaper among British broadsheets and former broadsheets, with selling an average of 842,912 copies each day. This compared to 617,483 for ''The Times'', 358,844 for ''The Guardian'', and 215,504 for ''The Independent''.
