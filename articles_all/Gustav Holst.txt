
'''Gustav Holst''' (21 September 1874 - 25 May 1934) was a famous England|English composer.
His most famous work is ''The Planets'' (1918). This is a suite (music)|suite of seven movements for orchestra, each about a different planet (the Earth is not included, and Pluto had not yet been discovered). The music does not really describe the planets, it describes the Roman mythology|Roman Deity|gods after which the planets are named.  Mars (mythology)|Mars, for example, is the “Bringer of War”. It has a very exciting rhythm with five beats in a Bar (music)|bar. Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter has a tune which has become famous as the hymn “I Vow to Thee, My Country”. He died of heart failure in London.
