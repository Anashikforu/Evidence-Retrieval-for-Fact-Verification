
'''Limoges''' ( or ''Limòtges'') is a city and Communes of France|commune, the Capital city|capital (Prefectures in France|prefecture) of the Haute-Vienne Departments of France|department and the historical Capital city|capital of the old Limousin region in west-central France. It is also the Capital city|capital of the ''Arrondissements of France|arrondissement'' of the same name.

Limoges is known for its medieval and Renaissance Vitreous enamel|enamels on copper, for its 19th-century porcelain and for its oak barrels which are used for Cognac (drink)|Cognac and Bordeaux production.

Geography
The city of Limoges is near the western limits of the Massif Central, in the centre of the Haute-Vienne department. The Vienne (river)|Vienne river flows through the city.
