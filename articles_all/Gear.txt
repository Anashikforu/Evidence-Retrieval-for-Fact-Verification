


'''Gears''' are machine|mechanical parts with cut teeth designed to fit with teeth on another part so as to transmit or receive Force (physics)|force and motion (physics)|motion. Gears are also sometimes called toothed wheels or cogged wheels or cogs.  The cut teeth are also sometimes called cogs.

Numerous nonferrous alloys, cast irons, powder-metallurgy and even plastics are used in the manufacture of gears. However steels are most commonly used because of their high strength to weight ratio and high cost.
