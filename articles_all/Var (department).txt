
The '''Var''' is a Departments of France|department in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur Regions of France|region in Provence in southeastern France. It takes its name from the river Var (river)|Var, which used to flow along its eastern border, but the border was moved in 1860.

Its Prefectures in France|prefecture is the city of Toulon.

History
Var is one of the original 83 departments created during the French Revolution on 4 March 1790 with Toulon as is Capital city|capital. It was formed with part of the former province of Provence.

It was divided in nine districts: Toulon, Grasse, Hyères, Draguignan, Brignoles, Saint-Maximin, Fréjus, Saint-Paul-lès-Vence and Barjols.

The capital was moved, in 1793, from Toulon to Grasse and, in 1795, from Grasse to Brignoles. In 1797, the capital was moved again, to Draguignan.

In 1800, with the creation of the Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' in France, the nine districts were changed into four ''arrondissements'': Brignoles, Draguignan, Grasse and Toulon.

After the Battle of Waterloo (18 June 1815), the department was occupied by the Austrian army from June 1815 to November 1818.

On 15 June 1860, the ''arrondissement'' of Grasse passed to the new department of Alpes-Maritimes. With this change, the Var (river)|Var river does not flow any longer through the department named after it.

On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Brignoles was eliminated but in 1974 Brignoles was made again an ''arrondissement'' and the capital was moved from Draguignan to Toulon. The Var is the only French department that has had four different capitals: Toulon, Grasse, Brignoles and Draguignan; now Toulon is the Prefectures in France|prefecture (capital) and Brignoles and Draguignan are Prefectures in France|subprefectures. Grasse is now a subprefecture of the Alpes-Maritimes department.

During World War II, the department was occupied by the Fascism|fascist Italy, from November 1942 to December 1943. In 1944, the Germany|German army occupied the territory of the Var department.

Geography
Var is part of the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur Regions of France|region. It has an area of .

The department borders with 3 other departments, all in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur Regions of France|region:

 Alpes-de-Haute-Provence (north)
 Bouches-du-Rhône (west)

It also borders the Vaucluse department, to the nortwest, but only for a very short distance.



Administration
Var is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Ardennes'' in Toulon. The department is part of the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur region.

=Administrative divisions=
There are 3 Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' (districts), 23 Cantons in France|''cantons'' and 153 Communes in France|''communes'' (Municipality|municipalities) in Var.
