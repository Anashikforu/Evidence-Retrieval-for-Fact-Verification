
'''Franklin County''' is a county in the U.S. state of North Carolina.  As of 2000, 47,260 people lived there.  Its county seat is Louisburg, North Carolina|Louisburg.

 History 
The county was made in 1779 from the southern half of Bute County, North Carolina|Bute County.  It was named for Benjamin Franklin.

In 1881, parts of Franklin County, Granville County, North Carolina|Granville County, and Warren County, North Carolina|Warren County were combined to form Vance County, North Carolina|Vance County.

 Law and government 
Franklin County is a member of the Kerr-Tar Regional Council of Governments.

 Education 
The Methodist Louisburg College and a campus of Vance-Granville Community College are in Franklin County.
