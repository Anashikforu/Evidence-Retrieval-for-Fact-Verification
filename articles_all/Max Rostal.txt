'''Max Rostal''' (born Cieszyn, 7 August  1905; died Bern (city)|Bern, 6 August 1991) was a violinist who was a famous violin teacher.  He was Austrian-born, but later took United Kingdom|British citizenship.

He was born in Cieszyn and studied with Carl Flesch. From 1930-33 he taught at the Berlin Hochschule, from 1944 to 1958 at the Guildhall School of Music, London and then at the conservatory in Bern (city)|Bern, Switzerland.  Many of his pupils became famous players.  They include three members of the Amadeus Quartet.

Rostal played a wide variety of music, including many newly composed works such as Violin Concerto no 2 by Béla Bartók.

He died in Bern.
