
'''Sœur Emmanuelle''' (born '''Madeleine Cinquin''', November 16, 1908 – October 20, 2008) was a Belgium|Belgian-born France|French nun.

She was born in Brussels, Belgium, the daughter of a family that made underwear|lingerie. At the age of six she saw her father drown. She learned at the Sorbonne, earning a Degree (school)|degree in philosophy. In 1929 she made a religious promise and became a nun.

She worked in Notre-Dame de Sion high school in İstanbul in 1930s.

In 1971 she saw how poorly trash wikt:collect|collectors in Cairo, Egypt were doing, and decided to live with them. She stayed until 1993, when she went back to France. After she went back, she became very famous in France and was liked by audiences and talk-show hosts.

Besides her work with the poor, she is also known for having had views that are not like how religion normally is, such as being for the use of birth control and being for the idea of priests to marriage|marry. She has been voted one of the most popular people in France, and has been compared to Mother Teresa. (Although she does not agree with this wikt:comparison|comparison). In 2003, French television broadcasting|broadcast ''Soeur Emmanuelle: An exceptional woman''.

She died on 20 October 2008.

''Les Amis de Soeur Emmanuelle'' (The Friends of Sister Emmanuelle) is a charitable organization based in Brussels.
