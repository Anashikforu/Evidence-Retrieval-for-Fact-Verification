
'''Solofa F. Fatu Jr.''' (born October 11, 1965) is an Americans|American professional wrestling|pro wrestler, best known under the ring name '''Rikishi'''. He is known by his work in WWE|World Wrestling Federation/Entertainment. Fatu is a member of the huge Samoan Anoa'i family|Anoa'i wrestling family and the nephew of the Wild Samoans, Afa Anoa'i|Afa and Sika Anoa'i|Sika, who trained him in the 1980s. He is the brother of the late Eddie Fatu, better known as Eddie Fatu|Umaga, and Sam Fatu|The Tonga Kid, who was part of The Islanders (professional wrestling)|The Islanders, a tag team with Tonga Fifita|Haku in the WWF. Fatu teamed with his cousin Samula Anoa'i|Samu, the son of Afa, as The Headshrinkers|The Samoan Swat Team. The Swat Team wrestled mainly in Southern United States|the south, winning titles in the World Class Wrestling Association in Dallas. The team later worked for the World Wrestling Federation as the Headshrinkers before Fatu went on a singles career. His twin sons Jonathan and Joshua now wrestle in WWE as Jimmy and Jey Uso, together known as The Usos.

On March 28, 2015, he was inducted into the WWE Hall of Fame by his sons Jimmy and Jey Uso.
