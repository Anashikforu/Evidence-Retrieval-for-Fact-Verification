
'''Burke County''' is a county in the U.S. state of North Carolina.  In 2000, 89,148 people lived there.  Its county seat is Morganton, North Carolina|Morganton.

 History 
The county was made in 1777 from Rowan County, North Carolina|Rowan County.  In 1791 parts of Burke County and Rutherford County, North Carolina|Rutherford County were made into Buncombe County, North Carolina|Buncombe County.  In 1833 parts of Burke County and Buncombe County were made into Yancey County, North Carolina|Yancey County.  In 1841 parts of Burke County and Wilkes County, North Carolina|Wilkes County were made into Caldwell County, North Carolina|Caldwell County.  In 1842 more parts of Burke County and Rutherford County were made into McDowell County, North Carolina|McDowell County. And in 1861 parts of Burke County, Caldwell County, McDowell County, Watauga County, North Carolina|Watauga County, and Yancey County were made into Mitchell County, North Carolina|Mitchell County.

 Government 
Burke County is part of the local Western Piedmont Council of Governments.
