The '''somatic nervous system''' (SNS) is part of the peripheral nervous system.Dorlands Medical Dictionary: somatic nervous system It controls various muscles and the sense|sensory input from external Stimulus|stimuli 

The SNS controls wikt:voluntary|voluntary body movements by skeletal muscles. Its outgoing (efferent) nerves stimulate muscle contraction. Its incoming (afferent) nerves relay sensation from the skin, and Sensory system|sense organs to the central nervous system. This includes touch, hearing, and Visual perception|sight.

The SNS does not, however, control reflex arcs.
