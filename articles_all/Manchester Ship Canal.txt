The '''Manchester Ship Canal''' is a wide,  long, river navigation in North West England, opened on 21 May 1894. At the time of its completion, it was the largest navigation canal in the world.Owen (1983), p.&nbsp;120.

It consists of the rivers River Irwell|Irwell and River Mersey|Mersey made navigable for seagoing ships from the Mersey Estuary to Salford Docks in Greater Manchester. It transformed Manchester from a landlocked city into a major sea port.
