The Municipalities in Japan|municipality calls itself "Chiyoda City" in English.
History
During the Edo period, most of the people living in this area were samurai.Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). 
Chiyoda developed around the Tokyo Imperial Palace|Imperial Palace. The Imperial Palace area occupies about one sixth of the area of Chiyoda. There was previously another castle here during the Edo period, but it was destroyed.

On March 15, 1947, the ward of Chiyoda was formed by combining the wards of Kanda, Tokyo|Kanda and Kōjimachi.

The Diet of Japan, the Supreme court of Japan and home of the Prime Minister of Japan are all in Chiyoda.

 Geography 
Chiyoda is in the center of Tokyo. It borders Chūō, Tokyo|Chūō and Minato, Tokyo|Minato on the south.  Shinjuku, Tokyo|Shinjuku is on the ward's western border. Bunkyō, Tokyo|Bunkyō and Taitō, Tokyō|Taitō is on the northern border.

The ward has an area of . The Imperial Palace covers about 12 percent of this area.

In Chiyoda is the hub of the Shinkansen high-speed train network, Tokyo Station.
