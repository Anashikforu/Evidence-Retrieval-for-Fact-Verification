
'''Hans-Dietrich Genscher''' (21 March 1927 &ndash; 31 March 2016) was a Germans|German politician. 

Early life
Genscher was born at Reideburg, near Halle. Genscher served in World War II from 1943 to 1945. He joined the Nazi Party at the age of 18. After the war, he Education|studied economics and law in Halle and Leipzig. He joined the East Germany|East German Liberal Democratic Party of Germany|Liberal Democratic Party (LDPD) in 1946.

In 1952, Genscher fled to West Germany. There he joined the Free Democratic Party (Germany)|Free Democratic Party (FDP). He passed his second state examination in law in Hamburg in 1954. After that, he became a lawyer|solicitor in Bremen. 

 Career 
In 1965, Genscher was election|elected to the Bundestag, the West German parliament for the first time. From 1969 to 1974, he was Ministry of the Interior of Germany|Federal Minister of the Interior under Chancellor of Germany|Chancellor Willy Brandt.
 
Genscher was Foreign Minister and Vice Chancellor of Germany from 1974 to 1982 under Helmut Schmidt.  Two weeks later, he began to serve in the same positions under Helmut Kohl. He served the second time from 1982 to 1992.  He was Germany's longest serving Foreign Minister and Vice Chancellor.

Death
Genscher died at his home in Wachtberg, Germany from heart failure on 31 March 2016, aged 89.
