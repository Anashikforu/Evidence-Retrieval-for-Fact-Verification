
'''''All I Ever Wanted''''' is the fourth studio album by United States|American pop rock singer-songwriter Kelly Clarkson. It was released on March 6, 2009 in Australia and Germany. On March 9, 2009 in the United Kingdom and New Zealand. On March 10, 2009 in Canada, Mexico and the United States|United States. And on March 13, 2009 in Italy. It was her second album to debut at #1 on the Billboard Hot 200. That made it Clarkson's second non-consecutive number-one album. ''All I Ever Wanted'' went Gold in Australia, Canada and the United Kingdom.

The album was originally going to be called ''Masquerade,''  but the title was changed to ''All I Ever Wanted.'' That was because because Clarkson felt that there were already albums with similar themes then on the charts, such as Pink (singer)|P!nk's ''Funhouse (Pink album)|Funhouse'' and Britney Spears' ''Circus (Britney Spears album)|Circus''.
