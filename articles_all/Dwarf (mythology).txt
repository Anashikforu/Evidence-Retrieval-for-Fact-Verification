
A '''dwarf''' is a short, humanlike creature from Norse mythology|Norse and Germanic paganism|Germanic mythologies. They have been used in many fairy tales, fantasy, fiction and role-playing games.

In some stories, dwarfs are wikt:mean|mean, living under bridges, and having a bad reputation for stealing treasure.  One example is the Norway|Norwegian Troll.  It is an ugly and foolish creature who tells lies.  It causes problems everywhere it goes.  That is how the Internet troll got its name.  In Robert Louis Stevenson's story, "Strange Case of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde," Edward Hyde is described as "a dwarf."
On the other hand, J. R. R. Tolkien's hobbits and the munchkins in The Wizard of Oz (1939 movie)|The Wizard of Oz are likeable, honest, good and smart.
