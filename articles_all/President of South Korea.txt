

The '''President of the Republic of Korea''' () is the head of state, leader of the executive of the South Korean government and the commander in chief of the South Korean Armed Forces

The president is directly elected by the people to a five-year term, with no possibility of re-election.Article 70 of the Constitution of the Republic of Korea. While in office, the chief executive lives in Blue House|Cheong Wa Dae (the "Blue House").

Moon Jae-in, former human rights lawyer and chief of staff to then-President Roh Moo-hyun,

If the National Assembly of South Korea|National Assembly votes against a presidential decision, it will be declared void immediately.

Powers and duties of the president
The president is required to:
 uphold the Constitution
 preserve the safety and homeland of South Korea
 work for the peaceful reunification of Korea, typically act as the Chairperson of the National Unification Advisory Council
