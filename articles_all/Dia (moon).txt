'''Dia''' (S/2000 J 11) ls the second-outermost Prograde and retrograde motion|prograde non-Sphere|spherical moon of Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter. It was found by a team of astronomers from the University of Hawaii led by Scott S. Sheppard in 2000.
Dia is about 4 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 12,555,000&nbsp;km in 287 days, at an inclination of 28° (to Jupiter's equator), and with an orbital eccentricity of 0.248.Sheppard, S. S.; Jewitt, D. C.; Carolyn Porco|Porco, C.; 
The moon is included in the Himalia group.Scott S. Sheppard|Sheppard, S. S.; David C. Jewitt|Jewitt, D. C.;
