

The '''Germanic''' languages are a group of Indo-European languages. They came from one language, Proto-Germanic language|Proto-Germanic, which was first spoken in Scandinavia in the Iron Age. Today, the Germanic languages are spoken by around 515 million people as a first language.

The Germanic languages are the East Germanic languages (all Extinct language|extinct), the North Germanic languages, and the West Germanic languages.
