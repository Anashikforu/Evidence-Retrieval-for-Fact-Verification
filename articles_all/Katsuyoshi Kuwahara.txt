
 is a former Japanese Association football|football player. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team. His brother Takashi Kuwahara is also a former footballer.

Biography
Kuwahara was born in Fujieda, Shizuoka|Fujieda on May 30, 1944. After graduating from Fujieda Higashi High School, he played for Hagoromo Club|Nippon Light Metal and Nagoya WEST FC|Nagoya Mutual Bank. In 1971, Nagoya Mutual Bank was disbanded and he retired.

On March 22, 1965, Kuwahara debuted for the Japan national football team|Japan national team against Myanmar national football team|Burma. On March 25, he also played against Singapore national football team|Singapore. He played 2 games for Japan in 1965.

After retirement, Kuwahara became a manager for Japanese Regional Leagues club Honda FC|Honda in 1973. He promoted the club to Japan Soccer League Division 2 in 1975 and Division 1 in 1981. He resigned in 1982. In 1987, he became a manager new club Sagan Tosu|PJM Futures and managed until 1992.
