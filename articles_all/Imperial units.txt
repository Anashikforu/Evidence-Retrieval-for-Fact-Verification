The '''Imperial units''' or the '''Imperial system''' is a system of units|collection of measurement units. An Act of Parliament in 1824 created it from traditional English units. Commonwealth of Nations|Commonwealth countries used this system from 1824 until they changed to the metric system. The imperial system is very similar to the U.S. customary units|American system but there are some Comparison of the Imperial and US customary systems|differences.

 Length 


The British nautical mile used to be 6080 feet. In 1970 the UK adopted the international definition of 1852 metres.
