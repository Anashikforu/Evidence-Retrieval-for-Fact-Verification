


'''Marlborough House''' is a mansion in Westminster, London, England. It was built for a British royal family|royal favourite, Sarah Churchill, Duchess of Marlborough|Sarah Churchill, Duke of Marlborough|Duchess of Marlborough, in 1711. Following her death, it passed to the hands of successive Dukes of Marlborough. 

In 1817, it became Crown property. It was used by members of the royal family, including Adelaide of Saxe-Meiningen|Queen Adelaide. It served as a base for the predecessor of the Royal College of Art, the "National Art Training School". In 1861, Marlborough House was made the Edward VII of the United Kingdom|Prince of Wales's London residence. It was used by him for the next 40 years, until he became king in 1901. It continued as a royal residence, and was last used by Mary of Teck|Queen Mary until 1953. After her death, her granddaughter, Elizabeth II of the United Kingdom|Queen Elizabeth II, gave the house to the Commonwealth Secretariat, which continues to use it today.
