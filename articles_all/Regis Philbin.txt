'''Regis Francis Xavier Philbin''' and for hosting the first season of ''America's Got Talent''.
Early life
Philbin was born in the Bronx, New York City. His father, Francis "Frank" Philbin, a U.S. Marine who served in the Pacific, was of Irish ancestry.

Philbin attended Our Lady of Solace grammar school in the Bronx, and graduated from Cardinal Hayes High School in the Bronx in 1949 before attending the University of Notre Dame, where he graduated in 1953 with a sociology degree. He later served in the United States Navy as a supply officer, then went through a few behind-the-scenes jobs in television and radio before moving into the broadcasting arena.

Television career
Philbin filled in for Dick Clark for the 2004 edition of ''Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve with Ryan Seacrest|Dick Clark's New Year's Rockin' Eve'' after Clark suffered a stroke.

On November 18, 2011, Philbin aired his final episode of ''Live! with Regis and Kelly.''

 Death 
On July 24, 2020, Philbin's family released a statement that he had died at the age of 88, one month before his 89th birthday.
