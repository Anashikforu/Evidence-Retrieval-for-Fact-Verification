

A '''bomb''' is an explosive object that makes and lets out its energy very quickly. This detonation makes a big shock wave. Bombs have been used for century|centuries. Some bombs also throw out dangerous metal fragments, and some are firebombs. 

Most bombs do not contain more energy than ordinary fuel, except in the case of a nuclear weapon. However, they release their energy more quickly, thus are more Power (physics)|powerful.

A bomb is usually some kind of container filled with explosive material that is designed to destroy things.  The word bomb comes from the Greek language|Greek word ''βόμβος'' (bombas), an wikt:onomatopoeia|onomatopoeic term with almost the same meaning as "boom" in English.

Air forces send bombers to destroy enemy wikt:target|targets. Terrorists use bombs in suicide attacks. Explosive materials used in mining work the same way, but are usually not called bombs.
