

'''Ilya Ilyich Mechnikov'''Ilya Ilyich Mechnikov (, also known as Élie Metchnikoff)  (16 May 1845 – 16 July 1916) was a Ukraine|Ukrainian-Russian zoologist of (partly) Jewish descent (his mother was Jewish).
Mechnikov was a protozoologist who became better known for the discovery of macrophages in 1882. He discovered how they dealt with germs by phagocytosis. Macrophages are found in virtually all Tissue (biology)|tissues,Ovchinnikov, Dmitry A. 2008. Macrophages in the embryo and beyond: much more than just giant phagocytes. ''Genesis'' '''46''' (9): 447–62. 
He is also credited with coining the term gerontology in 1903, for the emerging study of ageing and longevity.

He was born in Kharkov (now Kharkiv), Russian Empire (now Ukraine)
