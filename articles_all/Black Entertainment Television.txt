

'''Black Entertainment Television''' ('''BET''') is an United States|American cable television television network. It is based in Washington, D.C.. The network is targeted toward African-American audiences in the United States. Most of it used to show hip-hop and R&B music videos. Now it shows religion|religious shows, movies, award shows and original television television programme|series for African-Americans.

History
BET was :wikt:found|founded by Robert L. Johnson on January 25, 1980. The network started out as a two-hour Friday night show on the USA Network each week. It was on from 11 p.m. to 1 a.m. (Eastern Time Zone (North America)|EST). They mostly showed older movies and music videos. In May of that year, BET started showing Black college|collegiate sporting events. In November 1980, BET added two half-hour shows, ''Black Showcase'' and ''The Bobby Jones Gospel Show''. On June 26, 1983, BET started showing ''Video Soul'', a music video series.  On October 1, 1983, BET started broadcasting 24 hours daily with help from Home Box Office.  In the latter years, BET has moved from having a variety of Black entertainment to mostly showing music videos around the clock.
