Biography
Takahashi was born in Fukushima, Fukushima|Fukushima on April 11, 1916. He played for Waseda University. He won 1938 Emperor's Cup with Sei Fuwa, Sekiji Sasano, Kunitaka Sueoka, Shogo Kamo and so on. After graduation from university, he played for Kashiwa Reysol|Hitachi from 1941 to 1958.

From 1955, Takahashi started managering career too. In 1957, he became manager for Japan national football team|Japan national team as Shigemaru Takenokoshi successor for tour for China. In 1959, he became manager for Japan national under-20 football team|Japan U-20 national team for 1959 AFC Youth Championship and won the 3rd place. In 1960, he managed for Japan national team as Takenokoshi successor again. He managed at 1962 FIFA World Cup|1962 World Cup qualification and 1962 Asian Games until 1962. After that, he managed for Kashiwa Reysol|Hitachi.

On February 5, 2000, Takahashi died of pneumonia in Meguro, Tokyo Metropolis|Tokyo at the age of 83. In 2009, he was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame.
