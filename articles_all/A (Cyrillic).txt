'''Ah''' ('''A, a''') is the first letter of the Cyrillic alphabet.

It came from the Greek alpha (letter)|alpha. A long time ago, it was called ''az«î'' and stood for the number 1 (number)|1. It looks exactly the same as the Roman alphabet|Roman A.

All languages that use the Cyrillic alphabet say it as ah.
