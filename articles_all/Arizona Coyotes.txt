
The '''Arizona Coyotes''' are an ice hockey team that plays in the National Hockey League|NHL. They play in Glendale, Arizona. 
History
In 1996, the team moved to Phoenix, Arizona|Phoenix from Winnipeg, Canada, where they were known as the Jets. The team moved to the suburb of Glendale in December 2003. They have never won a Stanley Cup. Their head coach is Rick Tocchet and their general manager is Bill Armstrong. Wayne Gretzky resigned as head coach on September 14, 2009. In May of 2009, the Coyotes filed for bankruptcy protection.

They officially changed their name from the Pheonix Coyotes to the Arizona Coyotes on June 27, 2014.
