
'''Barbour County, Alabama''' is a county of the U.S. state of Alabama. Its name is in honor of James Barbour, who was Governor of Virginia.  As of 2000 the population was 29,038.  Its county seat is Clayton, Alabama|Clayton.

 History 
Barbour County was created on December 18, 1832 from former Creek Indian territory and a portion of Pike County, Alabama|Pike County. Its borders were altered in 1866 and 1868.
