
 was a Japanese Association football|football player. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team. His brother Shogo Kamo also played for Japan national team.

Biography
Kamo was born in Hamamatsu, Shizuoka|Hamamatsu on February 8, 1915. In 1936, when he was a Waseda University student, he was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for Football at the 1936 Summer Olympics|1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin. At this competition, on August 4, he debuted against Sweden national football team|Sweden. Japan completed a come-from-behind victory against Sweden. The first victory in Olympics for the Japan and the historic victory over one of the powerhouses became later known as "Miracle of Berlin" (:ja:ベルリンの奇跡|ベルリンの奇跡) in Japan. In 2016, this team was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame. On August 7, he also played against Italy national football team|Italy. He played 2 games for Japan in 1936. His younger brother Shogo Kamo was also an Olympic footballer for Japan.

On March 26, 2004, Kamo died of heart failure in Kawasaki, Kanagawa|Kawasaki at the age of 89. He was the last surviving member of his football team from the 1936 Olympics.
