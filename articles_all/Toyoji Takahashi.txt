
 was a Japanese Association football|football player. His grandfather Korekiyo Takahashi was a Prime Minister of Japan.

Biography
In 1936, when Takahashi was a University of Tokyo|Tokyo Imperial University student, he was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for Football at the 1936 Summer Olympics|1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin, but he did not play in the match. At this competition, Japan completed a come-from-behind victory first game against Sweden national football team|Sweden. The first victory in Olympics for the Japan and the historic victory over one of the powerhouses became later known as "Miracle of Berlin" (:ja:ベルリンの奇跡|ベルリンの奇跡) in Japan. In 2016, this team was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame.

After graduating from University of Tokyo|Tokyo Imperial University, Takahashi served in Imperial Japanese Navy. However on March 5, 1940, he died in an accident during training in Tateyama, Chiba|Tateyama at the age of 26. He was the first dead member of his football team from the 1936 Olympics.
