The '''Baluchistan States Union''' existed between 3 October 1952 and 14 October 1955 in modern southwest Pakistan. It was formed by the states of Kalat (princely state)|Kalat, Kharan (princely state)|Kharan, Las Bela (princely state)|Las Bela and State of Makran|Makran with the capital at the town of Kalat (Pakistan)|Kalat. The area of the Union was roughly the western half of the modern province of Balochistan (Pakistan)|Balochistan. The Union was separate from the Chief Commissioners Province of Baluchistan (Chief commissioners province)|Baluchistan which comprised areas to the northeast of the Union. The Union did not include the enclave of Gwadar City which was part of the Sultanate of Oman. The four state rulers continued in office but some matters became the responsibility of the Council of Rulers.
