 History 
In the Meiji period, Hyōgo Prefecture was formed by merging the provinces of Harima Province|Harima, Tajima Province|Tajima, Awaji Province|Awaji, and parts of Tamba Province|Tamba and Settsu Province|Settsu.Nussbaum, 
In 1993, Himeji Castle was named a United Nations Educational, Scientific and Cultural Organization|UNESCO World Heritage Site.  It is in the city of Himeji, Hyōgo|Himeji.Japan-i.jp, 
In 1995, the Great Hanshin earthquake damaged Kobe and neighboring Osaka Prefecture. It caused the deaths of 5,000+ people.Fukushima, Glen S. 
 Geography 
Hyōgo has coastlines on two seas. The Sea of Japan is north of the prefecture and the Seto Inland Sea is on the south.  Awaji Province|Awaji Island in the Inland Sea is part of Hyōgo.

Hyōgo shares land borders with Osaka Prefecture, Kyoto Prefecture, Tottori Prefecture and Okayama Prefecture.
