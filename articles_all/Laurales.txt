

The '''Laurales''' are an order (biology)|order of flowering plants. They are a basal group of dicots. They are related to, and sometimes included in, the Magnoliales.

The order includes 85-90 genera from seven family (biology)|families of trees and shrubs. Most of the species are tropical and subtropical, though a few genera reach the temperate zone. The best known species in this order are those of the Lauraceae (for example bay laurel or ''Laurus'', cinnamon or ''Cinnamomum'', avocado or ''Persea'', and ''Sassafras''), and the spice bush or ''Calycanthus'' of the Calycanthaceae.

The earliest lauraceous fossils are from the Cretaceous.
