
'''Caldwell County''' is a county in the U.S. state|state of North Carolina.  In 2000, 77,415 people lived there.  Its county seat is Lenoir, North Carolina|Lenoir.

History
The county was made in 1841 from parts of Burke County, North Carolina|Burke County and Wilkes County, North Carolina|Wilkes County.

In 1847 parts of Caldwell County, Iredell County, North Carolina|Iredell County, and Wilkes County were put together to make Alexander County, North Carolina|Alexander County.  In 1849 parts of Caldwell County, Ashe County, North Carolina|Ashe County, Wilkes County, and Yancey County, North Carolina|Yancey County were put together to make Watauga County, North Carolina|Watauga County.  In 1861, parts of Caldwell County, Burke County, McDowell County, North Carolina|McDowell County, Watauga County, and Yancey County were put together to make Mitchell County, North Carolina|Mitchell County.  And in 1911 parts of Caldwell County, Mitchell County, and Watauga County were put together to make Avery County, North Carolina|Avery County.

 Government 
Caldwell County is part of the local Western Piedmont Council of Governments.
