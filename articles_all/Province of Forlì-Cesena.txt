
The '''province of Forlì-Cesena''' () is a Provinces of Italy|province in the Emilia-Romagna region in northeastern Italy, on the Adriatic Sea. Its capital is the city of Forlì.

History
The province was created as ''Province of Forlì'' with the Vehicle registration plate|car plates ''FO''.

In 1992, the southern part of the province was separated to form the new Province of Rimini and the name of the province was changed to the present of '''Province of Forlì-Cesena''' and the car plates were changed in 1999 to ''FC''.

The ISO 3166-2 of the province was also changed to ''FC'' in 2010.
Geography

The province of Forlì-Cesena has an area of . It is, together  with the Province of Rimini|Rimini province, the most southerly of the provinces in the Emilia-Romagna region; it has a short coast along the Adriatic Sea.

The provincial Capital city|capital, the city of Forlì, is along the Montone (river)|Montone river and at about  southeast of Bologna, the capital of the region.

The province is bordered to the north by the province of Ravenna, to the northwest by the province of Mantua in Lombardy, to the west by the province of Florence province in Tuscany, to the south by the province of Arezzo, also in Tuscany, to the southeast by the province of Rimini|Rimini and to the east by the Adriatic Sea.



In the province there are two main protected areas:
 ''Parco naturale del fiume Savio'' (Natural park of the Savio river), along the Savio (river)|Savio river; and
 ''Parco nazionale delle Foreste Casentinesi, Monte Falterona e Campigna'', a national park in the northern Apennines.
