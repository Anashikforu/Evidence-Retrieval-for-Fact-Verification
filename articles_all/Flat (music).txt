In music, '''flat''', or '''''Bemolle''''', means "lower in pitch." In music notation, '''flat''' means "lower in pitch (music)|pitch by a semitone (half step)," and has the symbol (' (U+266D) is the flat sign. Its HTML entity is &amp;#9837;.

Under twelve tone equal temperament, ''C flat'' is the same as, or enharmonically equivalent to, ''B natural sign|natural'', and ''G flat'' is the same as ''F Sharp (music)|sharp''.

'''Double flats''' also exist, which look like  and lower a note by two semitones, or a whole step. Sometimes you will encounter half or three-quarter flats. The Unicode character '&#x1D12B;' &#x1D12B; (U+1D12B) represents the double flat sign.

A '''triple flat''' can be found, but it is very rare.  It lowers a note three semitones. 

The Note (music)|note ''A flat'' is shown in musical notation in Figure 1, together with ''A double flat''. 

In Musical tuning#Tuning practice|tuning, flat can also mean "slightly lower in pitch". If the same note is played on two instruments at the same time, the lower-pitched one is flat compared to the higher-pitched one.

A '''half flat''', showing quarter tones, is sometimes shown with a flat with a slash () or a reversed flat sign. A '''three-quarter flat''' is represented by a half flat and a regular flat.
