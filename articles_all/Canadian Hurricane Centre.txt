The '''Canadian Hurricane Centre''' (CHC) is a Canada|Canadian agency that is responsible for monitoring and tracking tropical cyclones that come near or affects Canadian territory. The agency warns people about on-coming tropical cyclones and makes hurricane advisories when needed. Its headquarters are in Dartmouth, Nova Scotia|Dartmouth, Nova Scotia.

CHC usually works together with its United States counterpart, the National Hurricane Center in Miami, to coordinate the tracks and positions of all storms that show a possible threat to Canada.

While Atlantic hurricanes are normally uncommon in Canada, they do approach or strike from time to time. Among recent events are 1991 Atlantic hurricane season|Hurricane Bob, which struck New Brunswick, in 1991, 1995 Atlantic hurricane season|Hurricane Luis which struck Newfoundland in September 1995, 1996 Atlantic hurricane season|Hurricane Hortense which struck Nova Scotia in September 1996, 2000 Atlantic hurricane season|Hurricane Michael which struck Newfoundland in October 2000, and 2003 Atlantic hurricane season|Hurricane Juan, which struck Nova Scotia in September 2003.
