The '''Treaty of Saint-Germain-en-Laye''' was signed on 10 September 1919 by the winning Allies of World War I|Allies and the new First Austrian Republic|Republic of Austria on the other. Austria lost large amounts of territory to the Allies after it lost World War I.

Like the Treaty of Versailles with German Empire|Germany, the treaty contained the Covenant of the League of Nations and so the United States did not ratify either of them.
