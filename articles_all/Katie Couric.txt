'''Katherine "Katie" Anne Couric''' (born January 7, 1957) was a co-host of the top-rated The Today Show|''Today'' show, broadcast live on NBC affiliates, from 1991 until late 2006, earning nearly $14 million each year for the last 4 years of her contract. 

Couric was born in Arlington, Virginia. She was hired by the CBS News|news division at CBS in late 2006 to anchor and be the main editor of the CBS Evening News,Meltzer, Kimberley TV News Anchors and Journalistic Tradition page 26 
Couric is active in Charitable organization|charities, especially related to colon cancer, after the death of her husband (to colon cancer) in 1998. Couric received a colonoscopy on live television in March 2000. She also released pictures of her mammogram on television. She voiced a fish version of herself named Katie Current from the 2004 animated comedy movie Shark Tale.
