
'''Manoj Nelliyattu Shyamalan''' (,'''Latin English''': ''Maṉōj Nelliyāṯṯu Ṣiyāmaḷaṉ'', born 6 August 1970), known professionally as '''M. Night Shyamalan''', is an United States|American filmmaker and screenwriter who resides and works primarily in the United States. He is known for making movies with contemporary supernatural plots that climax with a twist ending. Some of his notable movies are ''The Sixth Sense'' (1999), ''Unbreakable (movie)|Unbreakable'' (2000), ''Signs (movie)|Signs'' (2002), ''The Village (2004 film)|The Village'' (2004), ''Lady in the Water'' (2006), ''The Happening (2008 film)|The Happening'' (2008) and ''The Last Airbender'' (2010). He is also known for filming his movies (and staging his plots) in and around Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, where he was raised.
