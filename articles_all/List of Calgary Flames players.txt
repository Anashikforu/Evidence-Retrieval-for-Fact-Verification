The Calgary Flames are a professional ice hockey team based in Calgary, Alberta, Canada.  They are members of the Northwest Division (NHL)|Northwest Division of the Western Conference (NHL)|Western Conference in the National Hockey League (NHL).  Since their arrival in 1980, nearly 400 players have worn the Flames jersey for at least one regular season or playoff game. The Flames have won the Stanley Cup once, in 1988–89 NHL season|1989, and four players have been elected into the Hockey Hall of Fame; the most recent electee is Al MacInnis, who was enshrined in 2007.

Fourteen players have served as the Captain (ice hockey)|captain  of the Calgary Flames, beginning with Brad Marsh in 1980–81 Calgary Flames season|1980–81.
