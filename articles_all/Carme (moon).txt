'''Carme''' is a Prograde and retrograde motion|retrograde non-Sphere|spherical moon of Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter. It was found by Seth Barnes Nicholson at Mount Wilson Observatory in  California in July 1938. It is named after the mythological Carme (mythology)|Carme, mother by Zeus of Britomartis, a Crete|Cretan goddess.

Carme did not get its present name until 1975;
It gives its name to the Carme group, made up of non-spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23,000,000 and 24,000,000&nbsp;km and at an inclination of about 165Â°.
