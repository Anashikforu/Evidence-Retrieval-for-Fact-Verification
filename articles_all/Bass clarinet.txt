
The '''bass clarinet''' is a type of clarinet, and is a woodwind instrument|woodwind single-reed instrument. The earliest record of a bass clarinet is called a 'bass tube' invented in Paris in 1772 by G. Lott. It usually has a B-flat pitch. It plays an octave lower than the normal clarinet. This means that when a Bass Clarinet makes a note, the note that actually sounds is an octave and a whole tone lower."Clarinet", ''The New Grove Dictionary of Musical Instruments'' 3 volumes, ed. Stanley Sadie. London: Macmillan, 1984. There are some bass clarinets that have an A or C pitch as well as E flat, but there are not very many of these.
