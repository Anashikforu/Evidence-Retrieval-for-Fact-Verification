The '''batting order''', or '''batting lineup''', in baseball is an when nine members of the offense take their turns in batting (baseball)|batting against the Pitcher (baseball)|pitcher in an order.  The batting order is decided by the manager (baseball)|manager before the game begins, although sometimes during games, a player substitutes with another player which makes the take the original player's spoy. If a team batting out of turn|bats out of order, it is a violation of baseball's rules and subject to penalty.  When the whole batting order makes plate appearances (bats against the pitcher) in a single inning, it is called "batting around."

In modern United States|American baseball, some batting positions have nicknames:  "leadoff" for first, "cleanup" for fourth, and "last" for ninth. Others are known only by the ordinal numbers.
