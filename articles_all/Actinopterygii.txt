
The '''Actinopterygii''' is the Class (biology)|class of '''ray-finned fishes'''.

The ray-finned fishes get their name from the fact that they have lepidotrichia or "fin rays". Their fins are Tissue (biological)|webs of skin held by bone|bony or horn (anatomy)|horny spines ("rays"). This is different from the fleshy fins of the fish in the Sarcopterygii.

Actinopterygians are the largest class of vertebrates. There are nearly 25,000 species. They can be found in both fresh water and ocean|marine environments from the deep sea to the highest mountain river|streams. Most of the living species are teleosts.
