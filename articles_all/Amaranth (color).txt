



'''Amaranth''' is a Rose (color)|rose-red color that is the color of the flower of the amaranth plant.

The first written use of ''amaranth'' as a color name in English language|English was in 1690.Maerz and Paul ''A Dictionary of Color'' New York:1930--McGraw Hill Page 189; Color Sample of Amaranth: Page 127 Plate 53 Color Sample L3 (Note: The color sample called Amaranth in ''A Dictionary of Color'' is not the Amaranth Red color shown above that is normally considered Amaranth today, but is the color shown in the color comparison chart above as Amaranth Deep Purple.)


 Meaning 
 The color amaranth represents immortality in Western civilization because the name is derived from the name in Greek mythology of a flower that was believed to never die that grew in the abode of the Greek mythology|Greek gods on Mount Olympus.  Something that is perceived as ''everlasting'' may be described by the adjective ''amaranthine''.
 Where the name comes from 
The name ''amaranth'' comes from the Greek language|Greek ''a'' (not) + ''marainean'' (to waste away). A flower that never died that was believed to grow on Mount Olympus.
