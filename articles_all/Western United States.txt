The '''Western United States'''—commonly referred to as the '''American&nbsp;West''' or simply '''The&nbsp;West'''—traditionally refers to the westernmost U.S. state|states of the United States.  As the United States has Manifest Destiny|expanded westward since its founding, the definition of the West has changed over time.  In the 18th and early 19th centuries the Appalachian Mountains were considered to be the boundary.  Nowadays the Mississippi River is often referenced as the easternmost possible boundary of the West.  The United States Census Bureau|Census Bureau defines the western United States as these states:

 Alaska
 Arizona
 California
 Colorado
 Hawaii
 Idaho
 Montana
 Nevada
 New Mexico
 Oregon
 Utah
 Washington
 Wyoming.

The "West" had played a large role in American history; the American Old West is an important part of America's folklore.
