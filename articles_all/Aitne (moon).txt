Aitne is about 3&nbsp;kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 22,285,000&nbsp;km in 679.641 days, at an inclination of 166° to the ecliptic (164° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.393.

It was named in August 2003
Aitne belongs to the Carme group, made up of non-spherical irregular Retrograde and direct motion|retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23,000,000 and 24,000,000&nbsp;km and at an inclination of about 165°.
