
 is a former Japanese Association football|football player and manager. He managed Japan national football team|Japan national team.

Biography
Kamo was born in Ashiya, Hyogo|Ashiya on October 29, 1939. After graduation from Kwansei Gakuin University, he played for Japan Soccer League club Cerezo Osaka|Yanmar Diesel from 1965 to 1967.

In 1974, Kamo became a manager for Yokohama F. Marinos|Nissan Motors. In 1991, he became a manager for Yokohama Flügels|All Nippon Airways (later ''Yokohama Flügels'') and won the 1993 Emperor's Cup. In December 1994, he was named Japan national football team|Japan national team manager, replacing Paulo Roberto Falcão. In October 1997, at the 1998 FIFA World Cup|1998 World Cup qualification final round, after 4 games, he was dismissed and assistant coach Takeshi Okada was promoted to be the new manager.



In 1999, Kamo became a manager for Kyoto Sanga FC|Kyoto Purple Sanga until June 2000. From 2001, he managed a number of universities such as Shobi University, Osaka Gakuin University and his alma mater Kwansei Gakuin University. In 2017, he was selected to the Japan Football Hall of Fame.
