 '''Omar Khayyām''' () or '''Omar Khayyam''' (b. May 18 1048 Nishapur, (Iran) – d. December 4 1131) was a Persian people|Persian, mathematician, astronomer and poet.

In mathematics he contributed to the theory of equations, to the understanding of the Parallel postulate|parallel axiom, and possibly to the generalization of the Binomial expansion|binomial theorem. As an astronomer he contributed to a calendar reform when Sultan Malik-Shah decided that the calendar needed some adjusting. He is best known for his agnosticism|agnostic and scepticism|sceptic poetry, largely because of the publication in 1859 of ''Rubaiyat of Omar Khayyam'' by the translator Edward Fitzgerald.
