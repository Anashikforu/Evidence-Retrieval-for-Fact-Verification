
The '''Jagiellons''' were a royal dynasty that came from Lithuania and reigned in some Central European countries (present day Lithuania, Belarus, Poland, Ukraine, Latvia, Estonia, Kaliningrad, parts of Russia, Hungary, Czech Republic, Slovakia) between the 14th century|14th and 16th century. 

Members of the dynasty were grand dukes of Grand Duchy of Lithuania|Lithuania 1377–1392 and 1440–1572, Kingdom of Poland (1385–1569)|kings of History of Poland (1385-1569)|Poland 1386–1572, kings of Kingdom of Hungary|Hungary 1440–1444 and 1490–1526, and List of rulers of Bohemia|kings of Bohemia 1471–1526. The family was a branch of the Lithuanian Gediminaičiai|Gediminaičių dynasty.

The name (other variations used in English language|English include: '''Jagiellonians''', '''Jagiellos''', '''Jogailos''', '''Jagiellas''') comes from Władysław II Jagiełło|Jogaila (''pol.:'' Jagiełło), the first Polish king of that dynasty.In Polish, the dynasty is known as '''''Jagiellonowie''''' (singular: '''''Jagiellon''''', adjective, used of dynasty members, also patronimical form: ''Jagiellończyk''); in Lithuanian language|Lithuanian it is called '''''Jogailaičiai''''' (sing.: '''''Jogailaitis'''''), in Belarusian language|Belarusian '''Яґайлавічы''' (''Jagajłavičy'', sing.: '''Яґайлавіч''', ''Jagajłavič''), in Hungarian language|Hungarian '''''Jagellók''''' (sing.: '''''Jagelló'''''), and in Czech language|Czech '''''Jagellonci''''' (sing.: '''''Jagellonec'''''; adjective: '''''Jagellonský'''''), as well as '''Jagello''' or '''Jagellon''' (fem. ''Jagellonica'') in Latin. In all variations of that name, the letter J should be pronounced as in "Hallelu'''j'''ah" (or as Y in "'''y'''es"), and G – as in "'''g'''et".

 Jagiellon rulers 
Jagiellons were hereditary rulers of Poland and Lithuania.

The Jagiellon rulers of Polish-Lithuanian Union|Poland–Lithuania (with dates of ruling in brackets) were:

 Władysław III of Poland|Ladislaus III (1434–1444|44)
 Casimir IV Jagiellon|Casimir (1447–1492|92)
 John I of Poland|John Albert (1492–1501)
 Alexander of Poland|Alexander (1501–1505|05)
 Sigismund I the Old|Sigismund (1506–1548|48)
 Sigismund II Augustus|Sigismund Augustus (1548–1572|72) (also known as Sigismund II)
