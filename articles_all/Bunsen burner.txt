A '''Bunsen burner''' is a common piece of laboratory :wikt:equipment|equipment. It is commonly used for heating chemistry|chemical substances, sterilization, and combustion. It works by burning wikt:flammable|flammable gas. It is named after Robert Wilhelm Bunsen, a Germany|German chemist. He made important improvements on an earlier burner invented by Michael Faraday. 

The Bunsen burner can be adjusted to make a flame that is very hot without making much light. This allows seeing the different colors that various chemicals make when they are sprinkled into the flame.
