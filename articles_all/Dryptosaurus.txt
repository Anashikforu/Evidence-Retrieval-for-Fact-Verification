'''''Dryptosaurus''''' ("tearing lizard") was a theropod dinosaur of the Tyrannosauroidea type.Paul, G.S. 2010. ''The Princeton field guide to dinosaurs''. Princeton University Press, p. 100 It was found in Upper Cretaceous strata of Eastern North America. It was formerly known as ''Laelaps''.

It was given its name because it had 8-inch (21-cm) long finger claws. Fossils have been found in New Jersey. It is known only from a partial skeleton.
