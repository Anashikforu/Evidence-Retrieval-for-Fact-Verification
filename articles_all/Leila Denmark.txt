
'''Leila Alice Daughtry Denmark, M.D.''' (February 1, 1898&ndash; April 1, 2012) is an Americans|American pediatrics|pediatrician who still worked until she was 103. She worked on the vaccination|vaccine for pertussis (also known as whooping cough) in the 1920s and 1930s.

As a doctor, she was one of the first to be against tobacco|smoking near children and drug use in pregnancy|pregnant women. She also thinks that it is bad to drink cattle|cow's milk, people should eat fruit but not drink juice|fruit juices, and drink only water.

When she centenarian|turned 100, she did not want cake because it had too much sugar in it. She turned 110 in 2008, becoming a rare case of a supercentenarian who had been known for reasons other than her age.
