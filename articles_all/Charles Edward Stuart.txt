

'''Charles Edward Stuart''' (Prince Charles Edward Louis Philip Casimir Stuart, 31 December 1720 - 31 January 1788; better known as '''Bonnie Prince Charlie''' or as 'The Young Pretender') was the Jacobitism|Jacobite pretender to the thrones of Kingdom of Great Britain|England, Kingdom of Scotland|Scotland, and Kingdom of Ireland|Ireland. This claim was as the eldest son of James Francis Edward Stuart, and grandson of James II of England|James II and VII.

Charles is perhaps best known as the leading the unsuccessful Jacobite Rising of 1745|Jacobite uprising of 1745. He led a rebellion which ended in a heavy defeat at the Battle of Culloden. That ended the Jacobite cause. Charles's escape from Scotland after the uprising made him a romantic figure of heroic failure. The ''Skye Boat Song'' remembers him.

He lived, and died, in Rome, where his father had been given a home by Pope Clement XI|Clement XI.
