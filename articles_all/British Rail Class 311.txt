The British Rail '''Class 311''' alternating current (AC) electric multiple units (EMU) were built by Cravens at Sheffield in 1967. They were intended for use on the line from Glasgow Central railway station|Glasgow Central to Gourock railway station|Gourock and Wemyss Bay railway station|Wemyss Bay, which was electrified in 1967.
