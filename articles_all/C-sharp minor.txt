
'''C-sharp minor''' or '''C. Its key signature has four Sharp (music)|sharps.

Its relative key|relative major is E major, and its parallel key|parallel major is C-sharp major.

 Classical music in this key 
There are very few symphonies written in this key. One of them is by Joseph Martin Kraus, but he later rewrote it in C minor. Another two are Gustav Mahler|Mahler's Symphony No. 5 (Mahler)|Symphony No. 5 (though only the first Movement (music)|movement is in C-sharp minor, and the finale is actually in D majorConstantin Floros, translated by Vernon Wicker: ''Gustav Mahler: The Symphonies'' (Portland, Oregon: Amadeus Press, 1985) p. 141 "the choice of key of the movements (C-sharp minor—A minor—D major—F major—D major);") and Sergei Prokofiev|Prokofiev's Symphony No. 7 (Prokofiev)|Symphony No. 7. Dmitri Shostakovich|Shostakovich's Violin Concerto No. 2 (Shostakovich)|Violin Concerto No. 2 is also in C-sharp minor.

This key occurs more often in piano music. Domenico Scarlatti wrotetwo keyboard sonatas in C-sharp minor, K. 246 and K. 247. But after Ludwig van Beethoven|Beethoven's famous ''Piano Sonata No. 14 (Beethoven)|Moonlight Sonata'', the key became more popular for piano. Beethoven used this key again in the outer movements of his String Quartet No. 14 (Beethoven)|String Quartet No. 14 (Op.&nbsp;131, 1826). However, Johannes Brahms rewrote his C-sharp minor Piano Quartet to be published as Piano Quartet No. 3 in C minor, Op. 60. One of the most famous pieces in this key is Frédéric Chopin|Chopin's ''Fantaisie-Impromptu''. Other works include Chopin's Nocturnes  Nocturnes Op. 27 (Chopin)|No. 7 (Op. 27, No. 1) and Nocturne Op. Posth. in C-sharp minor (Chopin)|No. 20 (Op. posth.), and his Preludes (Chopin)|"Raindrop" Prelude in D-flat major (Op. 28, No. 15). Waltz in C sharp minor (op.64 No.2) is another well known musical piece from Chopin that uses this particular key (along with D flat minor key). Another very famous example of a work in C sharp minor is Sergei Rachmaninoff|Rachmaninoff's Prelude in C sharp minor (Rachmaninoff)|Prelude in C-sharp minor (Op. 3, No. 2).
