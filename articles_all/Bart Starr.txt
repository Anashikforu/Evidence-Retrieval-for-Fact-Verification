
'''Bryan Bartlett Starr''' (January 9, 1934 in Montgomery, Alabama &ndash; May 26, 2019) was a former American football player and coach for the National Football League. He was the quarterback for the Green Bay Packers (1956-1971) and the Super Bowl MVP|MVP of the first two Super Bowls. He went to the Pro Bowl four times. He was added to the Pro Football Hall of Fame in 1977.

Starr died May 26, 2019 in Birmingham, Alabama after failing health because of a stroke.
