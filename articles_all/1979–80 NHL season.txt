The '''1979–80 NHL season''' was the 63rd Season (sports)|season of the National Hockey League. Twenty-one teams each played 80 games. This season saw the addition of four teams from the collapse of the World Hockey Association (WHA) the previous 1978–79 WHA season|season as expansion teams. The Edmonton Oilers, Winnipeg Jets (1972–96)|Winnipeg Jets, New England Whalers (later renamed "Hartford Whalers" at the insistence of the Boston Bruins), and Quebec Nordiques joined the NHL bringing a new total of 21 teams. The other two WHA teams (Birmingham Bulls and Cincinnati Stingers) were paid to disband.
