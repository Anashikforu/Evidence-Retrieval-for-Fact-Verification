'''Pan''' is a Satellite (natural)|moon of Saturn (planet)|Saturn. It is the closest moon to the planet, orbiting 133,600 kilometre|km above Saturn's cloud tops. It was discovered in 1990 by Mark Showalter. He discovered Pan when he was analysing the photos taken by Voyager 2.
Pan was named after the Greek god Pan (mythology)|Pan on 16 September 1991.
Its mass is in the range 4.95 \pm 0.75 \times 10^{15} kg.
