


'''Alexander III''' (Scottish Gaelic language|Mediaeval Gaelic: ''Alaxandair mac Alaxandair''; Modern Gaelic: ''Alasdair mac Alasdair''), King of Scots, was born at Roxburgh on 4 September 1241 and died on 19 March, 1286. 

He was the only son of Alexander II of Scotland|Alexander II with his second wife, Marie de Coucy. Alexander's father died on 6 July 1249 and he officially became king at the age of eight. This happened at Scone, Perth and Kinross|Scone on 13 July, 1249.
