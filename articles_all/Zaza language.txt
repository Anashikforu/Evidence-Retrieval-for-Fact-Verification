'''Zaza language''', also called '''Zazaki''', '''Kirmanjki''' and '''Dimli''', is an Indo-European language spoken in eastern Anatolia above the rivers Euphrates and Tigris. It belongs to the northwest-Iranian languages|Iranian group of Iranian language. The Zaza language is related to Sangsarī, Māzandarānī, Tātī (Herzendī), Semnānī, Tāleshī, Kurdish language|Kurdish, Persian language|Persian and Balochi language|Balōchi. The number of Zaza speakers is not known exactly.
