
'''Clarence E. "Bud" Anderson''' (b. January 13, 1922 in Oakland, California|Oakland, California) was a fighter aircraft|fighter pilot in the United States Air Force. He joined the Air Force in 1942. During World War II, he flew a P-51 Mustang named "Old Crow". He had 16 kills, and was a Flying ace|triple ace. At the end of World War II, he was a captain. He flew an F-105 Thunderchief in the Vietnam War. He retired from the Air Force in 1972. He was also a pilot|test pilot.
