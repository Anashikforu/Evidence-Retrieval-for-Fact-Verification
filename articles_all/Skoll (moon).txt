'''Skoll''' or '''Saturn XLVII''' (provisional designation '''S/2006 S 8''') is non-Sphere|spherical moon of Saturn (planet)|Saturn. Its discovery was announced by Scott S. Sheppard, David C. Jewitt and Jan Kleyna on June 26, 2006, from observations taken between January 5 and April 30, 2006.

Skoll is about 6 kilometres in diameter (assuming an albedo of 0.04), and orbits Saturn at an average distance of 17,600,000 km in 869 days, following a highly eccentricity|eccentric and inclination|inclined orbit.
