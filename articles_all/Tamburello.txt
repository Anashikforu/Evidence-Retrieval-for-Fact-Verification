
'''Tamburello''' or '''pallatamburello''' is a traditional sport of Italy since 16th century. It has this name because they play with a roundish and oval tool named ''tamburello'' or ''tambourine'' in English language. Various forms of tamburello are popular in several nations of the world currently.

Forms
Image:Tambourin02.png|thumb|left|205px|roundish tambourin and Ball

= Tamburello open =
This form is also named ''classic'' and each team regularly has 5 players in open playing field 80 metres long and 20 large. Players use roundish tambourines during actions and they serve with oval tambourines. A rubber ball of 88 grams is used and the team which wins 13 games is final winner of the match: each game consists of 4 points. Professional Italian tamburello league is top level of competitions: in 2002 14 teams competed.
= Tamburello indoor =
Three players regularly form a side in indoor shorted courts.

=Tambutennis =
Two players regularly form a side in courts divided by a net like tennis.

= Tambeach =
One or two players regularly form a side on beach courts divided by a net like beach tennis.
