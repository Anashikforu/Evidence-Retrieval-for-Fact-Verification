'''''In vitro'' fertilisation''' ('''IVF''') is a way to let spermatozoon|sperm Fertilisation|fertilise ovum|egg cells outside of the womb. IVF is used so that infertility|infertile women may still have children. IVF is done by:
# Injecting a chemical drug to stop a woman's menstrual cycle
# Injecting FSH so that an ovum|ova is released from the woman's ovary|ovaries
# Human chorionic gonadotropin|HCG is injected to loosen the ova
# The ova is removed from the vagina
# A sample of spermatozoon|sperm is taken
# The sperm and egg are added together and a sperm will fertilise an egg
#The fertilised egg (zygote) is then injection|injected into the woman's uterus

Louise Brown, a female born in 1978 in Manchester, England, was the first ever person to be born as a result of in vitro fertilisation.

Some religious organisations believe that adoption is a better course of action for infertile couples. They think IVF is wrong because it is unnatural.

 The success rate of IVF 
IVF success rate among young women worldwide is around 35-40%. In India this figure is around 30-35%. Overall, the success rate of having a baby in the first IVF cycle is around 33% which increases to 50-70% by the 8th cycle. This success rate is measured in terms of the number of live births.
