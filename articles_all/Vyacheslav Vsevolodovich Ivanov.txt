

'''Vyacheslav Vsevolodovich Ivanov''' (21 August 1929 &ndash; 7 October 2017) was a Soviet Union|Soviet/Russian history|historical Linguistics|linguist. His main topic of study is Indo-European languages.  

 Works 
He is best known for his glottalic theory of how consonants are spoken in Indo-European languages and for placing point of origin for these languages in the area of the Armenian Highlands and Lake Urmia. He was born in Moscow.

Vyacheslav Ivanov's father was Vsevolod Ivanov, one of the most famous Soviet writers. His mother was an actor|actress. She worked in the theatre of Vsevolod Meyerhold. His childhood was filled by disease and war, which he spent in Tashkent.

Ivanov died in Moscow on 7 October 2017 at the age of 88.
