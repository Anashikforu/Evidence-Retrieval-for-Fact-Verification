'''Erich Alfred "Bubi" Hartmann''' (19 April 1922 in Weissach – 20 September 1993 in Weil im Schönbuch) Toliver, Raymond F. and Trevor J. Constable (1986). ''The Blond Knight of Germany''. New York: McGraw-Hill. . was a Germans|German flying ace in World War II. He was the flying ace with the most victories ever: 352 planes. After the war he spent ten years in Soviet prison camps. Later, he joined the West Germany Air Force.  He had a conflict with his superiors in the West German Air Force.  So he was forced to resign.  After that, he worked as a flight instructor.

His highest rank was that of colonel.
