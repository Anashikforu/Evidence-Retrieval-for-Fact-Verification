

'''Yonge Street''' (pronounced "young") is a main street in Toronto, Ontario, Canada, and its northern suburbs. It was once listed in the Guinness Book of Records as the longest street in the world and is listed as a national historic site. It was the site of Canada's Yonge-University-Spadina (TTC)|first subway line.

Yonge Street is the home of or close to many places of interest in Toronto, including street and theatre acts, the Toronto Eaton Centre|Eaton Centre, the Hockey Hall of Fame and–at the very start of the road–'One Yonge Street', the offices of the ''Toronto Star'' newspaper. The Yonge-University-Spadina Line|Yonge Line of the Toronto subway and RT|Toronto subway under or along it from south of King Street (Toronto)|King Street to Finch Avenue. The Viva Blue bus rapid transit|BRT line goes along Yonge from Finch Bus Terminal|Finch to Newmarket Bus Terminal.
