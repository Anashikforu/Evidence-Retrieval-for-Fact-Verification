

'''Vengeance (2006)''' was a professional wrestling pay-per-view List of WWE pay-per-view events|event by World Wrestling Entertainment (WWE), which happened on June 25, 2006 at the Time Warner Cable Arena|Charlotte Bobcats Arena in Charlotte, North Carolina. It was the sixth yearly WWE Night of Champions|Vengeance event. Eight professional wrestling matches were featured on the event's Card (sports)|card. The buildup to the matches and the situations that took place before, during, and after the event were planned by WWE's script writers. The event starred List of World Wrestling Entertainment employees|wrestlers from the ''WWE Raw|Raw'' brand: a WWE Brand Extension|storyline expansion of the promotion where employees are assigned to a wrestling brand under the WWE banner.

The Card (sports)#Main event|main event was a 5-on-2 Professional wrestling match types#Handicap match|Handicap match, a match consisting of one team of wrestlers facing off against a team of wrestlers with more people such as three against two, or five against two, between D-Generation X (DX) (Triple H and Shawn Michaels) versus the Spirit Squad (Kenny Dykstra|Kenny, Johnny Jeter|Johnny, Nick Mitchell|Mitch, Nick Nemeth|Nicky, and Michael Brendli|Mikey). Triple H and Michaels won the match after Pin (professional wrestling)|pinning Kenny and Mikey, respectively. Two other matches were on the card. In Professional wrestling match types#Variations of singles matches|singles matches, John Cena beat Sabu (wrestler)|Sabu in an Professional wrestling match types#No Disqualification match|Extreme Professional wrestling match types#Lumberjack match|lumberjack match, which featured List of World Wrestling Entertainment employees|wrestlers from the ''Raw'' and ''ECW on Sci Fi|ECW'' brands at ringside. The other featured match was for the WWE Championship, in which Rob Van Dam beat Edge (wrestler)|Edge to keep his title.

Vengeance made over United States dollar|$400,000 in ticket sales from an attendance of 6,800 and received 320,000 pay-per-view buys, which helped WWE increase its pay-per-view revenue by $21.6&nbsp;million compared to the year before. When the event was released on DVD, it reached the number one position on ''Billboard (magazine)|Billboard's'' DVD Sales Chart.
