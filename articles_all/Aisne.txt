

'''Aisne''' is a Departments of France|department in the north of France, in the Hauts-de-France Regions of France|region. It is named after the Aisne (river)|Aisne river, a tributary of the Oise (river)|Oise. Its Prefectures in France|prefecture is Laon.

History
Aisne is one of the original 83 departments created during the French Revolution on 4 March 1790 with Laon as is Capital city|capital. It was formed from parts of the former provinces of Île-de-France (Laon, Soissons, Noyon,and Valois), Picardy (Thiérache Vermandois), and Champagne, (province)|Champagne (Brie and Omois).

It was divided in six districts: Château-Thierry, Chauny, Laon, Saint-Quentin, Soissons and Vervins.

In 1800, with the creation of the Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' in France, the six districts were changed into five ''arrondissements'': Château-Thierry, Laon, Saint-Quentin, Soissons and Vervins.

On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Château-Thierry was eliminated but in 1942 it was made again an ''arrondissement''.

Administration
Aisne is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Aisne'' in Laon. The department is part of the Hauts-de-France region.

=Administrative divisions=
There are 5 Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' (districts), 21 Cantons in France|''cantons'' and 804 Communes in France|''communes'' (Municipality|municipalities) in Aisne.

Demographics
The inhabitants of Aisne are known, in French language|French, as ''Axonais'' (women: ''Axonaises''), from the Celts|Celtic name of the Aisne river, ''Axona''.

Aisne had a population, in 2014, of 539,783, for a population density of  inhabitants/km2. The ''arrondissement'' of Laon, with 165,489 inhabitants, is the ''arrondissement'' with more inhabitants.
