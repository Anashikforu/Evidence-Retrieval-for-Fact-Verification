

'''Richard Paul Astley''' (born 6 February 1966) is a United Kingdom|British singer, songwriter and musician. He was born in Great Sankey, Warrington, Lancashire, England and grew up in Newton-le-Willows, Merseyside. Astley has also lived in London Borough of Richmond upon Thames|Richmond, London with his Danish people|Danish wife, Lene Bausager, and their daughter, Emilie.

Career
In 1985, Astley was a drummer with a soul music|soul band named FBI when Pete Waterman noticed him and persuaded him to come to London to work at the PWL recording studio.

His first single (music)|single was the little-known "When You Gonna," released as a collaboration with Lisa Carter. His first solo offering was "Never Gonna Give You Up," released in 1987 which spent five weeks at the top of the The Official UK Charts Company|British charts and becoming the year's highest-selling single. On 12 March 1988, "Never Gonna Give You Up" also topped the Billboard charts|U.S. singles chart and was followed by a second U.S. #1 on 18 June with his second U.S. single release, "Together Forever (Rick Astley song)|Together Forever." In 1989 he was nominated for a Grammy Award for Best New Artist of the Year.  He lost to Tracy Chapman.

Much later, thanks to the rick rolling phenomenon and the persistent voting of the website 4chan, he was also nominated for the Best Act Ever Award 2008 at the Liverpool Music Awards.
