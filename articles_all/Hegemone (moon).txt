Hegemone is about 3 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 23,703,000&nbsp;km in 745.500 days, at an inclination of 153° to the ecliptic (151° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.4077.

It was named in March 2005 after Hegemone, one of the Charites|Graces, and a daughter of Zeus (Jupiter).
Hegemone belongs to the Pasiphaë group, non-Sphere|spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at distances ranging between 22,800,000 and 24,100,000&nbsp;km, and with inclinations ranging between 144.5° and 158.3°.
