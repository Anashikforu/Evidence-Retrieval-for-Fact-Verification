


'''Eta''' (uppercase/lowercase '''Η η''') is a letter of the Greek alphabet. In very early Greek writing it stood for the consonant sound "h", but in Ancient Greek language|Classical Greek it stood for a long vowel "e". In Modern Greek language|Modern Greek, it is pronounced as "i". In the system of Greek numerals, it has a value of 8. Letters that came from it include the Roman H and Cyrillic И.

In mathematics, the lowercase η is used to represent Dirichlet eta function.
