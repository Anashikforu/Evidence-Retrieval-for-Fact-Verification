The '''Avalon Peninsula''' is a large peninsula (9,270&nbsp;km²) that makes up the southeast portion of the island of Newfoundland.

The peninsula is home to forty percent of Newfoundland's population, and is the location of the provincial capital, St. John's, Newfoundland and Labrador|St. John's. It is connected to the main section of the island by the 5&nbsp;km/3 mile wide Isthmus of Avalon. The peninsula sticks out into the rich fishing zones near the Grand Banks. Its four major bays–Trinity Bay, Newfoundland and Labrador|Trinity Bay, Conception Bay, St. Mary's Bay, Newfoundland and Labrador|St. Mary's Bay, and Placentia Bay–have long been the center of Newfoundland's fishing industry.
