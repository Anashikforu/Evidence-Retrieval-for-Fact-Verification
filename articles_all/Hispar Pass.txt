

'''Hispar Pass''' (or Hispar La) (el. 5,128 m./16,824&nbsp;ft.) is a high-altitude, non-technical mountain pass in the Karakoram Range in Pakistan.

At the pass, the Biafo Glacier (63&nbsp;km. long) and Hispar Glacier (49&nbsp;km long) meet to form the world's longest glacial traverse outside of the polar regions, 100 kilometers in length.
