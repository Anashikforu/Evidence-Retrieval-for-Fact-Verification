
'''Tropical Storm Alberto''' (also known as Hurricane Alberto) became a Tropical cyclone|tropical storm farther north than any other Atlantic hurricane|Atlantic tropical cyclone. It was the first tropical storm of the 1988 Atlantic hurricane season. It started from a region of low pressure area|low pressure off of the coast of South Carolina on August 5. 

It quickly moved northeast. It became a tropical storm on August 7. It was about 60&nbsp;miles (95&nbsp;km) east of the Massachusetts island of Nantucket, Massachusetts|Nantucket. 

Tropical Storm Alberto continued to move quickly, and struck the western area of Nova Scotia late on August 7. It lost its status as a tropical storm the next day when it reached north of Newfoundland. Alberto produced light rain and wind across its path. No damage came from it.
