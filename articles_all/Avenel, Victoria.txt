
'''Avenel''' is a small town in Victoria (Australia)|Victoria, Australia.  It is in the Shire of Strathbogie local government area. At the 2006 census, 728 people lived in Avenel.{{Census 2006 AUS

|quick=on}} The town was built on the old Hume Highway. The new Hume Freeway now goes around the town. The town has sporting clubs for Australian Rules Football|football, netball, tennis, cricket and lawn bowls. The town is about 15 minutes north-east of Seymour, Victoria|Seymour along the Hume Highway.

Bushranger Ned Kelly lived in Avenel when he was young. He saved a boy from drowning in Hughes Creek and was given a green silk sash as a reward. He was wearing this sash under his armour when he was captured by the police after the Siege of Glenrowan, Victoria|Glenrowan. His brother and father are buried in the Avenel cemetery.

There is a railway station in Avenel which is on the main line between Melbourne and Albury, New South Wales|Albury.
