
'''Huseyn Shaheed Suhrawardy''' (September 8, 1892 - December 5, 1963) was a politician from Bengal in undivided British India|India, and later in Bangladesh#History|East Pakistan. He served as Prime Minister of Pakistan from 1956 until 1957. He is also known for his controversial role in the Direct Action Day of August 16, 1946. Following riots in Bengal during the last days of India's fight for freedom, he was considered a favourite of Muhammad Ali Jinnah. He is also considered to be the first Populist movement|populist leader in Pakistan's history. He is the founder of the Awami League, one of the two major Political party|political parties of present-day Bangladesh.
