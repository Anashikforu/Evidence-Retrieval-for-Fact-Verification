
'''Tammany Hall''', (also called the '''Society of St. Tammany''', the '''Sons of St. Tammany''', or the '''Columbian Order'''), named for '''Tamanend''' (meaning "''wikt:affable|affable''"), a Native American leader of the Lenni Lenape, was started in 1786 and was declared on May 12, 1789 as the Tammany Society. It was the Democratic Party|Democratic political machine that played a major role in controlling New York City politics. It also helped many immigrants, especially the Ireland|Irish, rise in American politics. In 1858 Boss Tweed became its leader. In the middle 20th century it became less powerful and dissolved in the 1960s.
