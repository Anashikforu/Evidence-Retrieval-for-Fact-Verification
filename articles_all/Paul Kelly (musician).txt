
'''Paul Maurice Kelly'''
Works
Kelly writes and plays in many music styles, from Bluegrass music|bluegrass to reggae.  His main styles are folk music|folk, Rock and roll|rock, and country music|country. Kelly's Top 40 singles on the National charts were his 1980s songs "Billy Baxter", "Before Too Long", "Darling it Hurts", "To Her Door", "Dumb Things", and his 2000 single "Roll on Summer". He has also had success with his albums with Top 20 hits on the National charts for ''Gossip (album)|Gossip'', ''Under the Sun (Paul Kelly album)|Under The Sun'' and ''So Much Water So Close To Home'' from the 1980s; ''Comedy (album)|Comedy'', ''Wanted Man (Paul Kelly album)|Wanted Man'', ''Songs from the South|Songs from the South: Paul Kelly's Greatest Hits'' and ''Words and Music (Paul Kelly album)|Words and Music'' from the 1990s; and ''Nothing But A Dream'', ''Ways & Means (album)|Ways & Means'' and ''Stolen Apples (album)|Stolen Apples'' from the 2000s. The compilation album ''Songs from the South'' got to Number 2 in 1997. His highest charting studio album was ''Nothing But A Dream'' which got to Number 7 in 2001.
