

'''Sylhet Division''' is the Northeastern Divisions of Bangladesh|division of Bangladesh. It is named after its main city, Sylhet. The division is bordered by the Meghalaya State of India on the north, Tripura State on the south, Assam State of India on the east and Dhaka Division|Dhaka and Chittagong Division|Chittagong divisions on the west. Bangladeshis also claims the Karimganj Thana as part of its country.

Demographics
The population of Sylhet Division is about 10 million. Followers of different religions are: Islam|Muslim 81.16%, Hinduism|Hindu 17.80%, Christianity|Christian 0.06%, Buddhism|Buddhist 0.02% and others 0.96%.

History of Sylhet

In the late 18th century, the British East India Company became interested in Sylhet. They saw it as an area of strategy|strategic importance in the war against Burma. Sylhet gradually fell under British India|British control and administration. It was governed as a part of Bengal. After the British administrative reorganization of India, Sylhet became a part of Assam. It remained a part of Assam for the rest of the era of British India|British rule.

In 1947, after a referendum, almost all of Sylhet as a district became a part of Bangladesh#History|East Bengal at the time and remained so after Bangladeshi independence. The Karimganj district|Karimganj subdivision became part of the new Indian state of Assam.
