
'''Arshak II''' (or '''Arsaces II''') (350-367) was the son of Diran of Armenia|Diran. During his reign, Shapur II of Persia tried to take over Armenia once and for all. Arshak II was taken prisoner during talks about peace with Shapur and later killed himself in prison.

Despite having many troubles during his reign, Arshak II was able to change many parts of his kingdom for the better. They included:
The creation of many monestary|monasteries, to keep monks away from the problems of everyday life. They also helped spread the gospel
The building of hospitals
The creation of many schools to teach Assyrian language|Assyrian and Greek language|Greek, since the Bible|Holy Bible was read in those languages at that time
The Wikt:ban|ban of inbred marriages, polygamy, divorce, pagan rituals, alcohol|drunkenness and killings for wikt:revenge|revenge
The better treatment of slaves
