
'''Neuss''' (Low Franconian and Ripuarian language|Ripuarian: ''Nüüs'', Latin: ''Novaesium''; officially written in Neuß until 1968) is a city in the west of Germany, in the States of Germany|state of North Rhine-Westphalia. In the local dialect it is called ''Nüss'' or Neuß. Neuss has about 150,000 inhabitants. It is on the Rhine, opposite the big city of Düsseldorf.

Neuss was founded by the Ancient Rome|Romans about 2,000 years ago. The Romans called the town ''Castra Novesia''.
