'''Nun''' is a woman who made a special vow (or promise) dedication|dedicating herself to a religion|religious life. Catholic nuns are said to "take the veil" as a symbol of their new life.''Crown and Veil: Female Monasticism from the Fifth to the Fifteenth Centuries'', eds. Jeffrey F Hamburger; Susan Marti; et al. (New York: Columbia University Press, 2008), p. 13 Roman Catholic nuns are the most commonly thought of nuns in the West, but nuns are found in different forms of Christianity as well as non-Christian religions such as Buddhism.
