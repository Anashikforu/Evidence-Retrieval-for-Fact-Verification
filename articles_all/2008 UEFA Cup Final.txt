
The '''2008 UEFA Cup Final''' was the 37th final of the UEFA Cup. It is UEFA's second-tier club association football|football tournament. The match was played at the City of Manchester Stadium. It is the home stadium of Manchester City F.C., in Manchester, England, at 20:45 Central European Summer Time|CEST (19:45 local time) on 14 May 2008.

The match was played by FC Zenit St. Petersburg|Zenit St. Petersburg of Russia and Rangers F.C.|Rangers of Scotland. It was called a battle of Rangers coaching staff, with ex-boss Dick Advocaat, currently the manager of Zenit against current boss Walter Smith (footballer)|Walter Smith. Smith has had two times as manager of Rangers. Both coaches led the Rangers to the Scottish Treble (association football)|domestic treble; Smith in 1993 and Advocaat in 1999.

Zenit won the match 2–0. They had goals from Igor Denisov and Konstantin Zyryanov. It was their first UEFA Cup title. It made them only the second Russian side to win the competition, after PFC CSKA Moscow|CSKA Moscow in 2004–05 UEFA Cup|2004–05.

The logo of the final shows a picture of the City of Manchester Stadium, created by English artist Liam Spencer. It was first shown at a ceremony in the stadium on December 6.

Fan violence


The event was marred by fans rioting in Manchester city centre; these riots started after a big screen that was due to show the match had failed. BBC News 24 interrupted normal programming to broadcast the riots live on television.

A Zenit fan was also attacked and stabbed.
