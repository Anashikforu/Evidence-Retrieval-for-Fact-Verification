

'''Nuneaton''' is the largest town in the England|English county of Warwickshire. Nuneaton is famous for the novelist, George Eliot, who was born on a farm outside Nuneaton in 1819 and lived in Nuneaton during her early life.

Geography
The town is 14.5&nbsp;km (9 miles) north of Coventry, 32&nbsp;km (20 miles) east of Birmingham and 166&nbsp;km (103 miles) northwest of London. The ''River Anker'' runs through the town.
Nuneaton had a population of 70,721 as of 20012001 census though the 2008 estimate is 73,000.

Nuneaton also has a football team called Nuneaton Town.

Notable inhabitants
 George Eliot, Victorian novelist
