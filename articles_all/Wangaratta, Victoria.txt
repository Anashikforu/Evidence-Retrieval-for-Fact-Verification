

'''Wangaratta''' is a city in the northeast of Victoria, Australia. About 19,000 people live there. It is about  to the north east. The Ovens River, Victoria|Ovens and King River (Victoria)|King Rivers which flow from the Victorian Alps meet at Wangaratta. It is in the local government area of the Rural City of Wangaratta.

History
Gold was found in the area during the 1850s.  A Post office|Post Office opened on 1 February 1843, as Ovens, and moved to the town and was renamed Wangaratta in 1854. The Ovens office, and the Kilmore, Victoria|Kilmore office which opened the same day, were the fifth and sixth to open in the Port Phillip District (Victoria).
