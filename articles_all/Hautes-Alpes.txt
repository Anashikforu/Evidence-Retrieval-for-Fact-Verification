
'''Hautes-Alpes''' () is a departments of France|department in southeastern France, in the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur Regions of France|region. It is named after the Alps mountain range and its Prefectures in France|prefecture (Capital city|capital) is Gap, Hautes-Alpes|Gap.

History

The department of the Hautes-Alpes was one of the 83 original departments created during the French Revolution on 4 March 1790 with the southeastern part of the old province of the Dauphiné.

It was divided in four districts: Briançon, Embrun, Gap and Serres, with Chorges as its Capital (city)|capital but during that same year the capital was moved to Gap.

In 1800, with the creation of the Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' in France, the four districts were changed into three ''arrondissements'': Gap, Briançon and Embrun.

On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Embrun was eliminated.

After the Battle of Waterloo in 1815, the department was occupied by troops from Austria and of the Kingdom of Sardinia.

During World War II, the department was occupied by the Fascism|fascist Italy.

Administration
Hautes-Alpes is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Hautes-Alpes'' in Gap, Hautes-Alpes|Gap. The department is part of the Provence-Alpes-Côte d'Azur region.

=Administrative divisions=
There are 2 Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' (districts), 15 Cantons in France|''cantons'' and 167 Communes in France|''communes'' (Municipality|municipalities) in Hautes-Alpes.
