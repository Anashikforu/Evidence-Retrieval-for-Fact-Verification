
'''Führer''' is the German language|German word for leader. The word ''Führer'' is most often used to name Adolf Hitler. Hitler was the Chancellor of Germany|German Chancellor during the Third Reich. After the death of Reich President Paul von Hindenburg in 1934, Hitler declared the office of President vacant and made himself ''Führer'' (both Head of State and Head of Government). He used the word ''Führer'' for his title ''Führer und Reichskanzler'' (''Leader and Reich Chancellor'').
