
'''Lager Norderney''' was a Nazi concentration camps|Nazi concentration camp on Alderney, in the Channel Islands, named after the East Frisian island of Norderney.

The Germans built four Nazi concentration camps|concentration camps on the island, subcamps of the Neuengamme concentration camp (in Hamburg, Nazi Germany|Germany). Each subcamp was named after one of the Frisian Islands: ''Lager Norderney'' at Saye, Lager Borkum at Platte Saline, Lager Sylt near the old telegraph tower at La Foulère and Lager Helgoland, in the northwest corner of the island. 700 people died in the Alderney concentration camps (out of about 6,000 inmates). These were the only Nazi concentration camps on United Kingdom|British soil.

It was organised by the Schutzstaffel|Schutzstaffel - SS-Baubrigade I–which was first under supervision of the Sachsenhausen concentration camp; and since mid-February 1943 ran under the Neuengamme camp in northern Germany. It was used by the Nazi Organisation Todt, a forced labour programme, to build bunkers, gun emplacements, air-raid shelters, and concrete fortifications.

''Norderney camp'' housed European (usually Eastern but including Spaniard) and Russian enforced labourers. The prisoners in ''Lager Norderney'' and ''Lager Sylt'' were slave labourers forced to build the many military fortifications and installations throughout Alderney. ''Sylt camp'' held Jewish enforced labourers and was a Extermination camp|death camp.

In 1942, ''Lager Norderney'', containing Russian and Polish POWs, and ''Lager Sylt'', holding Jews, were placed under the control of the SS Hauptsturmführer Max List. Over 700 of the inmates lost their lives before the camps were closed and the remaining inmates transferred to Nazi Germany|Germany in 1944.
