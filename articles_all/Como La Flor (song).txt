

"'''Como La Flor'''" ("''Like The Flower''") is a song by American singer Selena. The song was written by Selena's brother, A.B. Quintanilla III. A former Selena y Los Dinos member, Pete Astudillo, co-wrote the song. It was on her third album, ''Entre A Mi Mundo'' (1992). The song talks about a sad woman who is hurting because she has lost her boyfriend and she is explaining how it hurts to lose his love but she is saying that his love was like a flower that has dried up and died. As he says goodbye he takes her heart with him she hopes someday some other woman  or his new woman will give him what he is looking for.

The song was released in the United States and Mexico in October 1992. It was the third single off of the album, following "La Carcacha" ("The Jalopy"). It was the lead single in both countries. The single reached number-one on the Mexican Singles Chart and the ''MonitorLatino'' charts. This gave Selena her first number-one song as a solo artist. Despite the success, a music video was not produced. "Como La Flor" helped Selena to be accepted into Mexico. She toured there for the first time after the single peaked at number-one. "Como La Flor" also became one of her signature songs.

Various recording artists worldwide have since made their own versions of "Como La Flor", some being non-Hispanic. The song was nominated for several awards at the Tejano Music Awards and the Premio Lo Nuestro awards. It has been certified Diamond in AMPROFON|Mexico and Gold in Argentine Chamber of Phonograms and Videograms Producers|Argentina. It remains one of Selena's most popular songs.

 Production and background 
"Como La Flor" was recorded in Q-Productions in Corpus Christi, Texas. Selena's brother, A.B. Quintanilla III had written the song during Selena's Ven Conmigo#Tour|Ven Conmigo Tour (1990-92). Pete Astudillo, a former back-up dancer for Selena y Los Dinos, had helped writing the song. A family friend, Brian "Red" Moore, remixed the song after it was recorded.

Quintanilla had wanted to write a song about a sad woman whose ex-boyfriend is in love with another women and wants him back. The message of the song was to show people that you should love your partner if they truly love you back. The song compares love to a flower, which needs love and care.

The band had wanted to write a song that can help Selena crossover (music)|crossover into Mexico.Patoski, p. 102 After the song was played in radio stations, Selena was in constant demand in venues across Mexico. She had performed to several record-breaking audience records in Mexico. She was also the biggest act in the Tejano market during her tour. Selena was declared "Una artista del pueblo" ("An Artist of The People") when she had arrived in Mexico.Patoski, p. 124Richmond, p. 66 The song was also covered among young children in Mexico, who competed in several "Selena look-alike" competitions.Patoski, p. 105

 Release 
"Como La Flor" was released in the United States and Mexico as the lead single for the album ''Entre A Mi Mundo'' (1992) on October 4, 1992.Richmond, p. 65 It was the second single to be released from ''Entre A Mi Mundo'' (1992). "Como La Flor" was positioned at number ten on the AMPROFON|Mexican Singles Chart, when it was released.Patoski, p. 125Richmond, p. 97Wheeler, p. 34 It was also at number ten on the US Billboard charts|Latin Regional Mexican Airplay chart. The song peaked at number-one on the Mexican Singles Chart on November 22. It knocked off Luis Miguel's song "Inólvidable" from the number-one spot. The song remained at number-one for eight weeks, breaking Tejano artist Emilio Navaira's record. The song also peaked at number-one on ''MonitorLatino''s Top 20 Latin Hits the following year.

"Como La Flor" peaked at number nine on the Latin Regional Mexican Airplay chart on April 15, 1995. It also peaked at number four on the Latin Songs Airplay chart on December 27.Wheeler, p. 94 The remix version found on ''Dreaming of You (album)|Dreaming of You'' (1995), peaked at number seventeen on the List of music recording sales certifications|Italian Singles Chart.Richmond, p. 99 The song peaked at number eleven on the List of music recording sales certifications|Spanish Singles Chart.Richmond, p. 101 In Mexico, the single was certified Diamond for selling over 80,000 copies in 1994. In 2005, the single was certified Diamond in digital sales. In Argentina, the single was certified Gold for selling over 50,000 copies. In 2005, the single was certified Gold in digital sales.

"Como La Flor" was nominated for "Song of the Year" and "Single of the Year" at the Tejano Music Awards|1993 Tejano Music Awards. The song was also nominated for "Pop Ballad of the Year" at the 1994 Premio Lo Nuestro awards. The single was also nominated for the same two categories at the following Tejano Music Awards. Quintanilla, was nominated for "Songwriter of the Year" at the 1994 Tejano Music Awards.

 Critical reception 
Raúl Manuel Rodríguez of ''El Dictamen'' said that "Selena displayed an wikt:instinctive|instinctive voice on catchy Melody|melodies". He also said that "Como La Flor" helped build wikt:awareness|awareness for Selena and her Selena y Los Dinos|band. He also stated that the song "best fits with Mexico" because of its Tejano sounds.

Antonio Morales of ''Gringo Gazette'', liked the song's message and meaning. Morales also reported that "Como La Flor" is a "Dance music|dance-Cumbia music|cumbia song that is wikt:contagious|contagious when you hear it".
