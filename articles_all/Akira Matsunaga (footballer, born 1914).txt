
 was a Japanese Association football|football player. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team. His brother Nobuo Matsunaga and Seki Matsunaga also played for Japan national team.

Biography
Matsunaga was born in Yaizu, Shizuoka|Yaizu on September 21, 1914. In 1936, when he was a University of Tsukuba|Tokyo Liberal Arts and Science University student, he was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for Football at the 1936 Summer Olympics|1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin. At this competition, on August 4, he debuted and scored a goal against Sweden national football team|Sweden. Japan completed a come-from-behind victory against Sweden. The first victory in Olympics for the Japan and the historic victory over one of the powerhouses became later known as "Miracle of Berlin" (:ja:ベルリンの奇跡|ベルリンの奇跡) in Japan. In 2016, this team was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame. On August 7, he also played against Italy national football team|Italy. He played 2 games and scored 1 goal for Japan in 1936.

In 1937, Matsunaga entered the Imperial Japanese Army and served in World War II with the 230th Infantry Regiment. On January 20, 1943 he was killed in action during the Guadalcanal Campaign at the age of 28.
