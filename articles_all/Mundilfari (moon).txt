'''Mundilfari''' or '''Saturn&nbsp;XXV''', is a Satellite (natural)|moon of Saturn (planet)|Saturn. It was found by Brett J. Gladman, et al. in 2000, and given the designation '''S/2000&nbsp;S&nbsp;9'''.

Mundilfari is about 5.6 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Saturn at an average distance of 18,360,000&nbsp;km in 928.806 days, at an inclination of 170° to the ecliptic (150° to Saturn's equator), with an eccentricity of 0.198.

Mundilfari may have formed from debris knocked off of Phoebe (moon)|Phoebe by big impacts at some point in the solar system's history.

It was named in August 2003 from Norse mythology, where Mundilfari is a Jotun or giant who is the father of the goddess Sol (goddess)|Sol and the god Mani (god)|Mani.
