The '''historical method''' are the :wikt:technique|techniques and guidelines by which historians use primary sources and other evidence to research and then to historiography|write history.

 Guidelines 

Following Garraghan (in: ''A Guide to Historical Method'', 168) puts six questions that should be answered as minimal standard for a sound historical method:

# ''When'' was the source, written or unwritten, produced (date)?
# ''Where'' was it produced (localization)?
# ''By whom'' was it produced (authorship)?
# ''From what pre-existing material'' was it produced (analysis)?
#''In what original form'' was it produced (integrity)?
# ''What is the evidential value'' of its contents (credibility)?
