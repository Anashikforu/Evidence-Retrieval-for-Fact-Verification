
The '''Tower of London''' is an Ancient history|ancient Norman architecture|Norman stone fortress in London, England. It stands on the bank of the River Thames, in the oldest part of the city.

It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.UNESCO, 
History
The fortress was built by William the Conqueror, William I of England|King William I, starting in 1078. The moat was built by Richard I of England|Richard I, using water diverted from the River Thames.

The Tower had many uses. Its main function was to protect Norman rule in the years after the conquest. It was a prison, and a place of execution. Today, the Crown Jewels are kept there. This is the collection of jewels owned by the British state, and sometimes worn by the monarch. There is also a museum of armour.

Only the most important people were executed (by axe) inside the Tower of London. Among the most famous were:

George, Duke of Clarence (1478)
Anne Boleyn, Queen of England (1536) wife of Henry VIII of England|King Henry VIII
Thomas Cromwell, 1st Earl of Essex (1540)
Margaret Pole, Countess of Salisbury (1541)
Jane Boleyn, Viscountess Rochford (1542)
Catherine Howard, Queen of England (1542), wife of King Henry VIII
Lady Jane Grey (1554)
Robert Devereux, 2nd Earl of Essex (1601)

The Tower of London has a collection of ravens, large black birds of the Crow family. They are taken care of by the Employee|staff who work there. The ravens' wing feathers are kept short so they cannot fly away. This is because a legend (story) says that if the ravens leave the Tower, the Tower and the Kingdom will fall.

The closest London Underground|Underground station to the Tower of London is Tower Hill.

 Escape attempts 
Ranulf Flambard: 1100, successfully escaped.  Gruffudd ap Llywelyn ap Iorwerth: 1244, fell from the tower and died during escape attempt.
