

'''Victoria Louise "Pixie" Lott''' (born 12 January 1991) Her second single, "Boys and Girls (Pixie Lott song)|Boys and Girls", also went to the top of the UK charts in September 2009. Her first album, ''Turn It Up (Pixie Lott album)|Turn It Up'' was released in September 2009. It reached number six on the UK Albums Chart. The album had Top 20 singles. Over a million copies of the album were sold. Her second album, ''Young Foolish Happy'', was released on 14 November 2011. "All About Tonight (Pixie Lott song)|All About Tonight", the first single from that album, entered the charts at number one on 17 September 2011. It later became her third UK number one Hit (music)|hit.

Many good things are said about her live performances and Art|style.
Lott released "Boys and Girls (Pixie Lott song)|Boys and Girls" as her first United States|US single on 24 August 2010. The release happened at the same time as her first movie, ''Fred: The Movie'' was shown on television. In January 2010, Lott was named one of 2010's "Faces to Watch" by the United States|American magazine, ''Billboard (magazine)|Billboard''.
Early and personal life
Pixie was born in Bromley. She lived in Petts Wood and then Bickley. All three are suburban areas of south-east London.
She was voted No. 45 in 2010 and No. 31 in 2011 in ''FHM'''s 100 Sexiest Women list.

Music career
=2008–09: Early career=
In 2006, at the age of 15, she sang for L. A. Reid. He then signed her to the Island Def Jam Music Group. After a change of managers, many different record labels wanted her to sing for them. Lott left the Island Def Jam Music Group. She began working with Mercury Records in the UK and Interscope Records in the U.S. She formed a publishing contract|deal, as a songwriter, with Sony/ATV Music Publishing in December 2007. This was as the same time that she was writing and recording track (music)|tracks for ''Turn It Up''.

Lott played her first music festival|festival concert in the Big Top at the Isle of Wight Festival 2009. This took place during her first full United Kingdom|British Concert tour|tour. She was on tour to support The Saturdays on The Work Tour.

=2009–10: ''Turn It Up''=

Lottt released her first single, "Mama Do (Uh Oh, Uh Oh)|Mama Do", on 8 June 2009. The music video|video was movie director|directed by Trudy Bellinger. It was released to MySpace. The video had over three million views on YouTube. The song released was also released as her first single in the United States.

Lott's third single from the album was "Cry Me Out". It was released on 23 November 2009., ''Variety Club'' the children's charity, 15 November 2009. Retrieved 2 April 2010.

"The Way The World Works", from Lott's first record was used in the U.S. medical drama ''Private Practice (TV series)|Private Practice''., BRIT Awards 2010. Retrieved 2 April 2010. She was also the opening act of Rihanna's Last Girl on Earth Tour in the UK 7–26 May 2010.
The fifth single from Lott's album, "Turn It Up (Pixie Lott song)|Turn It Up", was released on 7 June 2010.

Lott has written a song for Alexandra Burke's first album, ''Overcome (Alexandra Burke album)|Overcome''. The song is "You Broke My Heart". She also wrote a song for Girls Can't Catch named "Happy Alone". This song was a free Christmas download on their website. She also has written two tracks on Lisa Lois's album. Lois was the The Netherlands|Dutch X Factor's winner. These two songs were "No Good For Me" and "Promises, Promises". They where the first and second singles from that album.

Lott was Cast member|cast as Judy, Fred Figglehorn's love interest, in the movie ''Fred: The Movie''. The comedy was released on America's Nickelodeon (TV channel)|Nickelodeon television network|network on the 18 September 2010.

=2011–present: ''Young Foolish Happy''=
Lott's second album, ''Young Foolish Happy'' was to be released on 7 November 2011.

Lott was a contestant in the 2014 series of ''Strictly Come Dancing''.
