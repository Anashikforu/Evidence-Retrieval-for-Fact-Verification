

'''San Gimignano''' is a small Town|hill town in the province of Siena, Tuscany, Italy. San Gimignano is famous for the Middle Ages|medieval towers, and for the wine, called "Vernaccia di San Gimignano".

 History 
San Gimignano was founded as a small village in about 1300 BC by the Etruscan civilization|Etruscans. The city is named after Saint Geminianus, who defended it from Attila the Hun in the mid 5th century.

In medieval and Renaissance times, it was a stopping point for pilgrims on their way to Rome and the Vatican City|Vatican as it sits on the medieval via Francigena.

In 1199, during the period of its highest splendour, the city made itself independent from Volterra.

 Important places 
There are many churches in the town: the two main ones are the ''Collegiata di San Gimignano|Collegiata'', a cathedral, and ''Sant'Agostino di San Gimignano|Sant'Agostino''.

The Communal Palace is now the home of San Gimignano Gallery. Hhere there are works by Pinturicchio, Benozzo Gozzoli, Filippino Lippi, Domenico di Michelino, Pier Francesco Fiorentino, and others. 

The heart of the town contains the four squares, "Piazza della Cisterna", "Piazza Duomo", "Piazza Pecori", and "Piazza delle Erbe". 

 Culture 
San Gimignano is the birthplace of the poet Folgore da San Gimignano (1270-1332).

''Tea with Mussolini'', a 1999 drama about England|English and United States|American women in Italy during World War II, was filmed in part at San Gimignano.
