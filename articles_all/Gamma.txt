


'''Gamma''' (uppercase/lowercase '''Γ γ'''), is the third letter of the Greek alphabet, used to represent the "g" sound in Ancient Greek|Ancient and Modern Greek language|Modern Greek. In the system of Greek numerals, it has a value of 3. Letters that came from it include the Roman C and Cyrillic Г.

In mathematics, the lowercase γ is used to represent Euler–Mascheroni constant, and the uppercase Γ is used to represent gamma function and gamma distribution. 

In physics, gamma rays (γ-rays) are a kind of radiation that result from radioactive decay|nuclear decay. In the physics and engineering, gamma is used to represent many different things, depending on the subject.
