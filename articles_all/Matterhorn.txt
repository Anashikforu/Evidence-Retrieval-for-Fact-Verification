The '''Matterhorn''' (in German language|German;  or ''Le Cervin'') is ranked, by height, 12th  mountain in the European Alps.Even though the Matterhorn is very famous, both because of its beauty, and because it is hard to climb, it is not one of the 100 tallest mountains in the Alps. Several mountains near it, including Monte Rosa, the Dom (mountain)|Dom, Lyskamm|Liskamm and the Weisshorn, are taller. Its height is 4,478 metres.NHK, 
The mountain is on the border between Switzerland and Italy, it towers over the Swiss village of Zermatt and the Italian village Cervinia|Breuil-Cervinia in the Val Tournanche. The name of the mountain comes from the German language|German words ''Matte'', meaning ''valley'' or ''meadow'', and ''Horn'', which means ''peak''.
