'''Richard Bache''' (1737–1811) worked for the United States Postal Service|Postal Service under Benjamin Franklin  from 1775–1776, and was Franklin's successor as United States Postmaster General|Postmaster General, serving from 1776–1782 during the American Revolution|Revolutionary War. He also served on the Board of War.

He was born in Yorkshire, England and married the only daughter of Franklin in 1767, after immigrating to New York City in 1765. His wife, Sarah Franklin Bache (1743-1808), was widely known for patriotism and benevolent activities.

Their son, Richard Bache Jr. (Texas politician)|Richard Bache Jr., served in the Republic of Texas Navy and was elected as a Representative to the Second Texas Legislature in 1847. Another son was Benjamin Franklin Bache (Journalist)|Benjamin Franklin Bache (1769-1798).
