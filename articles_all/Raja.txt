


A '''Raja''' ( (rājā), also spelled Rajah) is a monarch, or princely ruler of the Kshatriya varna.

The title has a long history in the History of India|Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia, as it is found in the Rigveda.where it is more accurately translated as "tribal chief"; see for example the ''दाशराज्ञ'' (dasharajna|), the "battle of ten ''rajas''" It can also be used as a name for non-royal Indians.
In Pakistan, Raja is still used by many Muslim Rajput clans as  titles. ''Raja'' is also used as a name by Hindus and Sikhs.

 Rajas in the Malay world 
 The ruler of the state of Perlis, Malaysia is titled the Raja of Perlis. Most of the other state rulers are titled Sultans. But the Raja has equal status with the other rulers and is one of the electors who designate one of their number as the Yang di-Pertuan Agong every five years.
