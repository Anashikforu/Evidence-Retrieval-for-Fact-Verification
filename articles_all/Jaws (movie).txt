'''''Jaws''''' is a 1975 United States|American thriller movie movie director|directed by Steven Spielberg. It is based on the novel Jaws (novel)|''Jaws'' by Peter Benchley.

The events of the movie are set in the small, quiet, rural town of Amity, Massachusetts. The movie follows three men as they try to capture and kill a great white shark that is threatening the town on US Independence Day|Independence Day. The movie follows Martin Brody, the slightly unheroic local Police officer|sheriff who must decide whether to follow his instinct to shut the beach for wikt:safe|safety reasons or keep the beach open to help the local tourism|tourist economy. Matt Hooper, a marine biology|marine biologist, and Quint, the local shark hunter, help Brody to overcome his fears of the water and stop the great white shark attacks. It has three sequels: ''Jaws 2'', ''Jaws 3'', and ''Jaws 4: The Revenge|Jaws: The Revenge''.

Awards
''Jaws'' won three Academy Awards for Academy Award for Best Film Editing|Best Film Editing, Academy Award for Best Original Score|Best Original Dramatic Score, and Academy Award for Best Sound|Best Sound. Along with the Oscar, John Williams's score won the Grammy Award for Best Score Soundtrack for Visual Media|Grammy Award,

''Jaws'' was chosen Favorite Movie at the People's Choice Awards.

In 2001, the United States Library of Congress selected it for the National Film Registry.
