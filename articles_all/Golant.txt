



'''Golant''' is a small village in Cornwall, United Kingdom. It is in the parish of St Sampson on the shore|banks of the Fowey estuary and between St. Austell and Polperro. The church in the villiage is dedicated to Saint Sampson|St. Sampson.

Golant railway station was at one time by the river to the south of the village.  It was served by Railway|trains on the Great Western Railway's Fowey branch line from 1 July 1896 until 4 January 1965. The station has since been removed. The space is now used for a car park.

Annie Vernon, the Olympic games|Olympic Rowing|rower, first learned her sport at Golant's Castle Dore Rowing Club.

 History 
Until very recently, Golant has been almost unknown except to the people who live there and pilgrims and other walkers of the Saints' Way. Nearby, there is a little used ford across the Fowey estuary. In late summer 1644, during the first English Civil War, war came to Golant and the area around the village. A large group of about 10,000 Parliament of England|Parliamentary soldiers  fought against the forces of List of English monarchs|King Charles I of England|Charles on the peninsula between Lostwithiel, St Blazey and Fowey. King Charles was in the area at the time.
