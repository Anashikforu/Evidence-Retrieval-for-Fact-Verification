
The '''Iowa Hawkeyes''' are the Sport|athletics teams of the University of Iowa in Iowa City, Iowa|Iowa City, Iowa, United States. The Hawkeyes have varsity teams in 24 sports. They have 11 teams for men and 13 for women. The teams are a part of Division I (NCAA)|Division I of the National Collegiate Athletic Association (NCAA) and are members of the Big Ten Conference. 

The term "Hawkeye" first appeared in the book ''The Last of the Mohicans''. It was later used in its plural form to describe the people of Iowa.  

Historically, Iowa has done well in collegiate wrestling|wrestling. They have 34 team Big Ten championships and 23 team national championships. The Hawkeyes have also won national championships in three other sports. These are men's gymnastics, American football|football and field hockey.  In college basketball|basketball, Iowa has reached the Final Four four times. The men's team has done this three times and the women's team has done it once. The college baseball|baseball team has reached the College World Series once, in 1972. Iowa's softball team has played in the Women's College World Series four times.
