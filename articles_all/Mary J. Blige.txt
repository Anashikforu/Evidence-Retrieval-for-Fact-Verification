'''Mary Jane Blige''' (or simply '''Mary J Blige''', born January 11, 1971) is an Americans|American R&B, hip hop and soul music|soul singer. She released 11 studio albums, 82 single (music)|singles, and 8 other albums.

 Music career 
In 1988, just for fun, Blige recorded an cover version|cover of Anita Baker's "Caught Up in the Rapture" at a recording booth in a local mall. Her mother's boyfriend at the time later played the cassette for Jeff Redd, a recording artist and A&R runner for Uptown Records. Redd then sent it to the president and chief executive officer|CEO of the label, Andre Harrell.  Harrell met with Blige and, in 1989, she joined the label, becoming the company's youngest and first female artist. 

Other works

Production for Blige's first album, ''Whats the 411?'', began in 1991. Sean Combs|Sean "Puffy" Combs (at the time a beginning A&R executive at Uptown) organised the project.  Also enlisted were some of the top R&B and hip hop producers of the time, among them Tony Dofat, Mark Morales and Cory Rooney, Dave Hall (record producer)|Dave "Jam" Hall, and DeVante Swing.

In 2014 Blige will tour with R. Kelly called  "The King and Queen Tour".

 Personal life 
Blige is a Democrat.
