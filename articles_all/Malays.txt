 Overview 
Malay people were once a tribal group who inhabited the island of Sumatra. According to the Malay Annals, the origin of Malay people are from the area known as Palembang in modern-day South Sumatra.

Sang Sapurba also mentioned in the Malay Annals as the King of Malays who originated from the hill of Siguntang Mahameru area as well.

Malay people transformed from the tribe|tribal group into an ethnic group during the glorious era of Srivijaya—the empire that originated from Palembang— when the trading activities grew. Nowadays, Malay people are inhabiting areas that was under the Srivijayan empire rule, with the big population mainly in Sumatra and its surrounding regions such as Bangka Belitung Islands, Riau Islands, Malacca Peninsula ( (including Brunei), Thailand|southern Thailand, Vietnam|southern Vietnam, and Philippines|southern Philippines.

Language

Most ethnic Malays speak one or more of the many dialects (versions) of the Malay language, a language of the Austronesian languages|Austronesian family of languages. In Indonesia, the standardized form of Malay language|Malay is Indonesian language|Indonesian (. About 80% of their words Cognate|mean the same thing in either dialect. These naming policies were created to form national unities in the two countries instead of making the Malays a ruling influence or the ruling class, something that it is in Brunei. In Brunei, Thailand and Singapore it is known as ''Bahasa Melayu''. 

Today, the language is usually written a version of the Roman alphabet, called Malay alphabet|Rumi. Malay written using the Arabic alphabet is called Jawi, which is mostly used in official and religious contexts. Jawi is more common than Rumi in very conservative Muslim areas like Kelantan in Malaysia and Pattani in Thailand.
