
'''Jeremy John Durham''' "'''Paddy'''" '''Ashdown, Baron Ashdown of Norton-sub-Hamdon'''  (27 February 1941 &ndash; 22 December 2018) was a British people|British politician and international diplomat. He served as leader of the Liberal Democrats from July 1988 to August 1999.

 Biography 
Paddy Ashdown was born in New Delhi, British India, and his Irish accent brought him the nickname "Paddy". He served in the Royal Marines from 1959 to 1972 and saw fighting in Borneo, Hong Kong and The Troubles|Northern Ireland. At the 1983 United Kingdom general election|1983 general election, Ashdown became the Liberal Party (UK)|Liberal Party's Member of Parliament (MP) for the constituency of Yeovil.

In 1988, when the Liberal Party wikt:merge|merged with the Social Democratic Party (UK)|Social Democratic Party to form the Liberal Democrats, Ashdown was elected the leader of the new party and led the party through the 1992 and 1997 general elections. He resigned in 1999, and was given both a knighthood and a seat in the House of Lords. In the House of Lords, he was given the title Baron Ashdown of Norton-sub-Hamdon. From 2002 to 2006 he served as High wikt:representative|Representative for Bosnia and Herzegovina.
