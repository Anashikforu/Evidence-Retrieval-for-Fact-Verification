
'''Cleo Virginia Andrews''' ('''V. C. Andrews''') (June 6, 1923 â€“ December 19, 1986) was an Americans|American writer from Portsmouth, Virginia. Her best-selling stories included "My Sweet Audrina" and two series of books about the "Dollanganger" and "Casteel" families.  

Her stories were written in English language|English and translated into French language|French, Italian language|Italian, German language|German, Spanish language|Spanish, Dutch language|Dutch, Japanese language|Japanese, Korean language|Korean, Turkish language|Turkish, Greek language|Greek, Finnish language|Finnish, Hungarian language|Hungarian, Swedish language|Swedish, Portuguese language|Portuguese and Hebrew language|Hebrew.

Her first novel, "Flowers in the Attic," was published in 1979.   

V. C. Andrews died from breast cancer in 1986, leaving behind some unpublished stories. The estate of V. C. Andrews hired a ghostwriter, Andrew Neiderman, to continue the stories, which are still published under the name V. C. Andrews.Estate of Andrews v. United States, 850 F.Supp.1279 (E.D. Va. 1994)
