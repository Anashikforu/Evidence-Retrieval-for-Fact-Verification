

'''Patriot Day''' is a day of remembrance in the United States. It happens on September 11 of each year. It remembers the Casualties of the September 11 attacks|2,977 killed in the September 11 attacks|September 11, 2001 attacks. Most Americans call this day "Nine-Eleven (9/11)," "September Eleventh," or something similar.

The day was officially created on October 25, 2001. The United States House of Representatives|U.S. House of Representatives Joint resolution|Joint Resolution 71 was approved by a vote of 407–0. The Resolution asked that President of the United States|the President would designate September 11 of each year as "Patriot Day."  President George W. Bush signed the resolution into law on December 18, 2001 (as 
On this day, the Flag of the United States|American flag is to be flown at half-staff at individual American homes, at the White House, and on all Federal government of the United States|U.S. government buildings, home and abroad. The President also asks Americans to observe a moment of silence beginning at 8:46 A.M. (Eastern Time Zone (North America)|Eastern Daylight Time). This is the time the first plane struck the North Tower of the World Trade Center (1973–2001)|World Trade Center on September 11, 2001.
