




In geology, a '''trough''' refers to a linear Depression (geology)|depression that extends in one direction over a distance. It is less steep than a oceanic trench|trench. A trough can be a narrow Basin (geology)|basin or a geologic Rift (geology)|rift. There are various '''oceanic troughs''', troughs found under oceans; examples include the rifts along the mid-ocean ridges.
