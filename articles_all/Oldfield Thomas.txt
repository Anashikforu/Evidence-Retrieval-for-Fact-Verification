
'''Michael Rogers Oldfield Thomas''' (usually known as ''Oldfield Thomas'') Fellow of the Royal Society|FRS (February 21, 1858 â€“ June 16, 1929) was a United Kingdom|British zoologist.

Thomas worked at the Natural History Museum in their mammals department. He described about 2,000 new species and sub-species for the first time. He was given a job in the Museum Secretary's office in 1876. In 1878 he moved to the Zoological Department. In 1891 Thomas married a woman who was an Heir apparent|heiress to a large amount of money. Having this money allowed him to hire mammal collectors and to give their specimens to the museum.
