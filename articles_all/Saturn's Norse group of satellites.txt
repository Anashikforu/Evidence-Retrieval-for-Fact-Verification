The '''Norse group''' is a collection of Natural satellite|moons of the planet Saturn (planet)|Saturn that go around the planet in a different direction to the spin of Saturn. They also go in an odd ellipsis direction instead of a circle.

The names of this group come from Norse mythology|gods from Norway. However, Phoebe is a Greek mythology|Greek god, because it was discovered first.

Saturn has at least 61 moons, which can be put into ten groups. The Norse group is one of these groups, as is the Saturn's Inuit group of satellites|Inuit group, and the Saturn's Gallic group of satellites|Gallic group.
