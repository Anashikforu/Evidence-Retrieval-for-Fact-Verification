'''Neoprene''' is the '''DuPont Performance Elastomers''' trade name for a family of synthetic rubbers based on '''polychloroprene''' (polymer form of Chloroprene).  Neoprene was invented by DuPont scientists after Dr. Elmer Keiser Bolton|Elmer K. Bolton of DuPont attended a lecture by Priesthood (Catholic Church)|Fr Julius Arthur Nieuwland, a professor of chemistry at the University of Notre Dame.

Applications
It is used in a wide variety of environments, such as in wetsuits, laptop sleeves, cars, bombs, electricity|electrical Electrical insulation|insulation, and automobile|car fan belt (mechanical)|belts.  Its chemical inertness makes it well suited for industrial applications such as gaskets, hose (tubing)|hoses, and corrosion-resistant coatings. It can also be used as a base for adhesives.
