

 Events 
 June 4 – Canton of Glarus|Glarus joins the Switzerland|Swiss Confederation.
 June 27 – Canton of Zug|Zug joins the Switzerland|Swiss Confederation.
 December 18 – Pope Innocent VI succeeds Pope Clement VI as the 199th pope.
 Moroccan traveller Ibn Battuta reports the existence of the Xalam|ngoni and balafon instruments at the court of Mansa Musa.
 Dragoş becomes voivode of Moldova.
 Corpus Christi College, Cambridge|Corpus Christi College founded as a College of the University of Cambridge by the Guilds of Corpus Christi and the Blessed Virgin Mary.
 The Ottoman Turk army crosses the Bosporus, entering the Balkans.
 Lionel of Antwerp marries Elizabeth, daughter of William de Burgh, 3rd Earl of Ulster.
 William de Ashlee becomes Rector of Maids Moreton, England.
 The town of Biel/Bienne, Switzerland finalizes its alliance with the city of Bern (city)|Bern.
 Reginald de Cobham, 1st Baron Cobham becomes a Companion of the Order of the Garter|Most Noble Order of the Garter of England.
 The Earl of Kent|Earldom of Kent becomes extinct (see '''1352#Deaths|Deaths''', below).
 The metropolitan bishop|Metropolitan of the Ukrainian Greek Catholic Church, the Metropolitan of Halych, began to relocate back to Kiev, after having moved to Halych in 1299. Thereafter, the Metropolitan would hold the title of Metropolitan of Kiev-Halych and All Rus.
