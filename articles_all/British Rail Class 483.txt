
The '''British Rail Class 483''' electrical multiple units were originally built by Metro-Cammell as London Underground 1938 Stock|1938 tube stock units for London Underground. They were extensively refurbished between 1989 and 1992 by Eastleigh Works, for use on services on the Isle of Wight's Island Line, Isle of Wight|Island Line. This was despite having already worked for nearly fifty years on the London Underground. The units replaced the even older  and life-expired British Rail Class 485|Class 485 and British Rail Class 486|Class 486 units, which were introduced in 1967, but were originally built as London Underground Standard Stock|'Standard' stock units for the London Electric Railway in 1923.
