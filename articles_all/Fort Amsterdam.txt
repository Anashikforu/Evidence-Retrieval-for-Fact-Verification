
'''Fort Amsterdam''' (later called '''Fort James''', '''Fort Willem Hendrick''', '''Fort James''' (again), '''Fort William''', '''Fort Anne''' and '''Fort George''' between United Kingdom|British and Netherlands|Dutch rule) was a fort on the southern tip of modern-day Manhattan that was the administrative headquarters for the Dutch and then British rule of New York from 1625 to 1790, when it was torn down after the American Revolution.
