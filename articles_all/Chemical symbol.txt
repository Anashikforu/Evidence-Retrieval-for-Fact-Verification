A '''chemical symbol''' is the shortform of the name of an chemical element|element. Usually, it is made up of one or two letters, but sometimes it is made up of three letters. For example, the chemical symbol for oxygen is '''O'''. The chemical symbol for calcium is '''Ca'''. Until it is discovered and scientists decide what it will be called, the chemical symbol for ununennium will be '''Uue'''.

The chemical symbols are listed with the chemical element|elements in the periodic table. Chemical symbols are also used when writing chemical formulas. For example, the chemical formula for water is H2O, meaning that it contains two hydrogen atoms and one oxygen atom.
