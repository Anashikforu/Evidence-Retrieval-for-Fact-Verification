Nord (department)|department., and is not far from the border with Belgium, where in Dutch language|Dutch it is called . About two million people live in the urban area (''conurbation'') shared with Kortrijk and Tournai, which is the fifth largest in France behind Paris, Lyon, Marseille, and Toulouse.

 Transport 
There are two big railway stations in Lille with many trains to and from different places around France and Belgium. Most of these serve , has Eurostar trains to and from Brussels, Paris, and St Pancras station in London via the Channel Tunnel to the United Kingdom.

Public transport in Lille is made up of buses, two rapid transit|metro lines, and two tram lines to the nearby towns of Tourcoing and Roubaix. The metro system is one of the first in the world that does not have drivers.
