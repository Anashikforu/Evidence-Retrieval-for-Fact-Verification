

'''Mary-Louise Parker''' (born August 2, 1964) is an Americans|American television and movie actress.
Early life
Parker was born in 1964 in Fort Jackson (South Carolina)|Fort Jackson, South Carolina. Her mother was Sweden|Swedish and her father was a judge and served in the United States Army. They lived in Thailand where she attended the International School Bangkok.
That same year, Parker was noticed by critics when she appeared in a movie called ''Longtime Companion''. It was one of the first movies about AIDS. After that, she acted in 1991's ''Grand Canyon (1991 film)|Grand Canyon'', which also starred Mary McDonnell, Danny Glover, Alfre Woodard and Kevin Kline. Parker's next film was ''Fried Green Tomatoes (film)|Fried Green Tomatoes'', with Jessica Tandy, Mary Stuart Masterson, Kathy Bates and Cicely Tyson.
