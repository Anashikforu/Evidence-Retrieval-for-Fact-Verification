'''Gerhard Hendrik Armauer Hansen''' (July 29, 1841 - February 12, 1912) was a Norway|Norwegian Medical doctor|doctor. He is famous for discovering the bacteria that causes leprosy in 1873. That disease is also called ''Hansen's disease'' in his honour.
