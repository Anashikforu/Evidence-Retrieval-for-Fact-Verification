



'''Satsuma'''It is commonly called '''mikan''' in Japan, '''satsuma''' in the UK, '''naartjie''' in Africa and '''mandarin''' or '''tangerine''' in Canada. In the United States, satsumas are most frequently marketed as tangerines. It is less commonly called '''satsuma tangerine''', '''satsuma orange''', or '''satsuma mandarin'''. / sat-soo-muh/ '''.n'''. is a variety of tangerine with a loose skin. 
It is named after the former Satsuma Province of Japan.  In the United Kingdom it is often associated with Christmas.
