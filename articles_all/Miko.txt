 are women in the service of Shinto Jinja|shrines. The translation of ''Miko'' is "Woman of God" (chosen by, or totally devoted to, the gods), but the closest meaning is ''"shrine maiden"''. In Shinto, there are priestesses; but Miko are not priestesses, because they have less authority. However, they can act as priestesses if needed. They usually help Shintoist priests.
