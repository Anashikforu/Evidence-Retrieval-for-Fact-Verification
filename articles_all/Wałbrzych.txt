'''Wałbrzych''' (pronounce:  'vawbʒix, German language|German: ''Waldenburg'') is a town in southwestern Poland in the Lower Silesian Voivodeship. About 126 000 people live there and the area is 84&nbsp;km². The city belonged to Germany until 1945. The original German population was expelled after WW II.
