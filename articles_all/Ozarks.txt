The '''Ozarks''',  also called the '''Ozark Mountains''' and '''Ozark Plateau''', are a Physiography|physiographic, Geology|geologic, and culture|cultural highland region of the central United States. It covers much of the south half of Missouri and an large portion of northwest and North central Arkansas. The region also goes westward into northeast Oklahoma and extreme southeast Kansas. The name Ozarks comes from the French language|French ''Aux Arcs''. The name means "with bows" and was given by an early French people|French explorer to the Bow Indians (Quapaw). They were native to the area that later became Arkansas.
