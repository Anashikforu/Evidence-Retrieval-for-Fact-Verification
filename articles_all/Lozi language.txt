
'''Lozi''', also called '''Silozi''' and '''Rozi''', is a Bantu languages|Bantu language of the Niger-Congo languages|Niger-Congo language family.

It is spoken by the Lozi people in southwestern Zambia. It is also spoken in Zimbabwe, Botswana, and the Zambezi Region of Namibia.

 History 

The Lozi language comes from a mix of two languages: Luyana language|Luyana and Kololo.
