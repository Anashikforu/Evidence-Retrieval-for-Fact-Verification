'''Apologetic proverbs''' (also known as '''wellerisms'''), make fun of proverbs by showing that they are wrong, in some situations.

They have : 
a proverb  (or saying ), and
an  explain|explanation (often humorous ).

They often have Sardonism|sardonic humor.

Examples

 A body can get used to anything, even to being hanged, as the Irish people|Irishman said. (Lucy Maud Montgomery--''Anne of Green Gables'')
