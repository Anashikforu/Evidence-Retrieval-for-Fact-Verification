
'''Antwerp''' () is the northernmost province both of the Flemish Region, also called Flanders, and of Belgium.

Antwerp (city)|Antwerp is largest city of the province and its Capital (city)|capital; the Port of Antwerp is part of the city.

History
The modern province of Antwerp was the old Deux-NÃ¨thes Departments of France|department, created in 1795 during the First French Empire in the northern part of the Duchy of Brabant.

In 1815, during the creation of the Kingdom of the Netherlands, the territory became part of the United Kingdom of the Netherlands as the province of '''Central Brabant''', distinguishing it from North Brabant and South Brabant. In 1830, after Belgian Revolution|Belgium's independence the province was renamed Antwerp (after its major city and capital).
