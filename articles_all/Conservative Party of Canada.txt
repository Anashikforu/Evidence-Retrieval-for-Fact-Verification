
The '''Conservative Party of Canada''' (French language|French: Parti Conservateur du Canada) is a politics|political political party|party in Canada. The party is considered to be on the center-right to right-wing|right of the Political spectrum, unlike the Liberal Party of Canada, Bloc Québécois or the New Democratic Party. The party is the successor to the numerous right-wing parties, mainly the Progressive Conservative Party of Canada. Right now, the interim leader of the party is Candice Bergen (politician)|Candice Bergen, who is also the Leader of the Official Opposition (Canada)|Leader of the Official Opposition.

The party won a minority government in the 2006 and 2008 elections and a majority government in 2011 election under the leadership of Stephen Harper, who served as the 22nd Prime Minister of Canada. Following the loss of the 2015 election falling to second place, Harper resigned as leader and was filled in by interim leader Rona Ambrose until the next leadership election, which Andrew Scheer won.
