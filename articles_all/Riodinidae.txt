

'''Riodinidae''' is a family (biology)|family of butterfly|butterflies.  They are commonly known as '''riodinids''' or '''metalmarks'''. Metalmarks get their name from the bright, metallic (looking like metal) markings found on the wings of most species.Jim P. Brock and Kenn Kaufman (2003). ''Butterflies of North America.'' Houghton Mifflin, New York, NY. 

Riodinidae was formerly known as the family Nemeobiidae and as the family Erycinidae. It was also once thought to be a subfamily of Lycaenidae.
