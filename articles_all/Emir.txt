
'''Emir''' (''') "commander" or "general", also "prince"; also transliterated as '''amir''', '''aamir''' or '''ameer''') is a high Nobility|title of nobility or office. It is used throughout the Arab world and historically in some Turkic peoples|Turkic states and Afghanistan. Emirs are usually considered high-ranking sheikh|sheiks, but in monarchical states the term is also used for princes; then "emirate" means more or less the same as principality in this context.
Emir is used also as a name in Turkey like Emir Niego and Emir Sevinc.  
''Emir'' is the most common spelling in English and many other languages (for example, United Arab Emirates). The spelling ''amir'', that is closer to the original Arabic, is more common for its compounds (e.g., admiral) and in individual names.
