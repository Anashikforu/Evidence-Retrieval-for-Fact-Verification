The '''Assembly of Kosovo''' (, ''Skup≈°tina Kosova'') is an institution linked to governing the territory of Kosovo. The Assembly was created by the United Nations to provide for a democratic form of government.

 Structure 
The assembly has 120 seats. 100 seats are filled with people directly elected into the assembly. The remaining 20 are always assigned as follows:
 10 seats for the representatives of the Serbs.
 4 seats for the representatives of the Roma people|Roma, Ashkali and Egyptians (Balkans)|Egyptians.
 3 seats for the Bosniaks.
 2 seats for the Turkish people|Turks.
 1 seat for the Gorani (Kosovo)|Gorans.
