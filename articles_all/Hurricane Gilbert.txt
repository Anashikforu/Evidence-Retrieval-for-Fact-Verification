
'''Hurricane Gilbert''' was a very powerful tropical cyclone which caused much wikt:damage|damage to the Caribbean, Mexico and parts of South Texas in the 1988 Atlantic hurricane season. Before Hurricane Wilma in 2005, Hurricane Gilbert was the strongest hurricane ever recorded in the Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic in terms of Barometric pressure.

This hurricane was similar to Hurricane Beulah and Hurricane Allen, but Hurricane Gilbert was unique in many ways. Gilbert killed 341 people and left $5.5 billion in damage.

Similar storms to Hurricane Gilbert
In addition to Beulah and Allen, Hurricanes Charlie (1951), Hurricane Emily (2005)|Emily during 2005 and Hurricane Dean|Dean in 2007 had tracks similar to Hurricane Gilbert. Emily and Dean were Category 5 hurricanes.
 Retirement 

Because Gilbert left a powerful impact, the name Gilbert was retired. The name "Gordon" was used in Hurricane Gordon (1994)|1994, 2000, and Hurricane Gordon (2006)|2006.
