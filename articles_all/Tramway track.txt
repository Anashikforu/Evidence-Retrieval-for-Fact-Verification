'''Tramway track'''s are used on Tram system|tramways or light railways. Groove (engineering)|Grooved rails (or girder rails) are normally used to let the trams run on streets. This is called "street-running". Like normal rail tracks, tram tracks consist of two Parallel (geometry)|parallel steel Rail tracks#Railway Rail|rails.

Tram rails can be placed in several surfaces. Rail tracks#Railway rail|Standard rails can be put on railroad tie|sleepers like railway tracks, or with grooved rails on concrete sleepers into street surfaces (Pavement (material)|pavement) for street running.  Another Environmentalism|environmentally-friendly or Ecology|ecologically-friendly alternative is to lay tracks into grass Sod|turf surfaces. This is known as ''grassed track'' (or ''track in a lawn''). It was first used in Liverpool in 1924.
