

'''Santiago de los Caballeros de Mérida''', Venezuela, is the capital of the Municipalities of Venezuela|municipality of Libertador Municipality, Mérida|Libertador and the state of Mérida (state)|Mérida, and is one of the principal cities of the Venezuelan Andes. It was founded in 1558, forming part of Viceroyalty of New Granada|Nueva Granada, but later became part of the Captaincy General of Venezuela, and played an active role in the Venezuelan War of Independence|War of Independence.

Mérida has more than 200,000 inhabitants and a metropolitan area containing some 350,000 people. It is the main center for education and tourism in western Venezuela, the home of the prestigious University of the Andes, Venezuela|University of the Andes, and the location of the highest and second longest aerial tramway in the world.

Mérida is at an altitude of approximately 1,600&nbsp;meters (5,249&nbsp;ft). The city sits on a plain in the valley of the Chama river, which runs from one end to the other.  The backdrop of  Mérida's skyline is the country's highest summit, Pico Bolívar.
