Geography

The Monte Cristi province is in the northwestern part of the Cibao Valley; this part is also called the ''Yaque del Norte Valley'' or ''Línea Noroeste'' and is very dry because it does not rain much here.

The most important river in the province is the River Yaque del Norte|Yaque del Norte, the longest river of the Dominican Republic. Other rivers are Guayubín and Maguaca, Tributary|tributaries of the Yaque del Norte.

The ''Cordillera Septentrional'' ("Northern mountain range") runs along the northern coast of the province; there are not high mountains, only low hills. At the western end of this mountain range is the "El Morro" mountain, forming a Headlands and bays|headland or a small peninsula.

=Climate=
The climate of the province is a tropical climate, hot most of the year.

Municipalities
There are six Municipality|municipalities and four Municipality|municipal districts (M.D.) in the province.




 Economy 
The main economic activity in the province is farming and banana and rice are grown here. Fishing and salt production is also important in the capital city of San Fernando de Monte Cristi.
