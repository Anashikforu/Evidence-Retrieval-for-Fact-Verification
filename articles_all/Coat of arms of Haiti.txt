The first '''coat of arms of Haiti''' was created in 1807, and has appeared in its current form since 1986.

It has a Palm tree|palm, with several flags at its sides, and two Artillery|cannons on a green lawn. On the lawn there are several objects, such as musical instruments (a Drum|snare drum and Bugle (instrument)|bugles), guns, and ship anchors. Above the palm tree, there is a Phrygian cap placed as a symbol of freedom.  

The ribbon on the bottom displays the national motto: ''L'Union Fait La Force'' (French language|French for "Union Makes Strength").
