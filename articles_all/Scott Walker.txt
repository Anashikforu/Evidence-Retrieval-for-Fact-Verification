

'''Scott Kevin Walker''' (born November 2, 1967) is an Americans|American Republican Party (United States)|Republican politician. He was the List of Governors of Wisconsin|45th Governor of Wisconsin from 2011 to 2019. He was a two-term Governor, elected in 2010 and 2014, but losing in 2018 to Tony Evers.

Early life
Walker was born in Colorado Springs, Colorado. His family moved to Plainfield, Iowa and later to Delavan, Wisconsin. He studied at Marquette University, but did not graduate.

Early career
Walker began his political career in the Wisconsin State Assembly, serving from 1993â€“2002.  After the 2002 resignation of Tom Ament as Executive of Milwaukee County, Wisconsin|Milwaukee County, Walker won the special election to fill the seat, which he held from 2002 to 2010.

Governor of Wisconsin (2011-2019)
Walker was elected governor of Wisconsin in Wisconsin gubernatorial election, 2010|2010, defeating the Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic nominee, Mayor of Milwaukee|Milwaukee Mayor Tom Barrett (politician)|Tom Barrett.

After being sworn into office in 2011, Walker introduced a Wisconsin Budget Repair Bill|budget repair plan which limited many collective bargaining powers for most public employees. The legislation led to significant 2011 Wisconsin protests|protests at the Wisconsin State Capitol and an effort to recall Walker.

In June 2012, Walker faced Barrett in Wisconsin's only Wisconsin gubernatorial recall election|gubernatorial recall election. After emerging as the winner, Walker became the only governor in the U.S. to date to win a gubernatorial recall election.

He lost his third re-election bid to Tony Evers on November 7, 2018 by one percent.

Personal life
Walker married Tonette Tarantino in 1993. Together, they have 2 children.

2016 presidential campaign

Walker has been seen by many as a potential candidate for the GOP's nomination in the United States Presidential Election, 2016|2016 presidential election. He formed a 527 organization in January of 2015.

Walker announced his Scott Walker presidential campaign, 2016|presidential campaign on July 13, 2015 on his Twitter account.
