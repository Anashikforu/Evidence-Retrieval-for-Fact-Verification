The '''edible dormouse''' or '''fat dormouse''' (''Glis glis'') is a small dormouse and the only species in the genus '''''Glis'''''.

Fat dormice are the largest dormice in Europe. The Ancient Rome|ancient Romans used to breed them to be eaten (usually as a snack). This is the reason for the word ''edible'' in its name. The dormice were kept in large :wikt:pit|pits or in Pottery|terra cotta containers (which were called ''glirarium|gliraria''),E. Saglio, "
Wild edible dormice are still eaten in Slovenia, and trapping dormice is a Slovenian tradition. Using dormice for food and fur is mentioned in documents which were written in the 13th century. They also mention the use of dormouse fat as a medicine. Poor and hungry peasants would eat dormice during winter because it is a good protein source.Haberl, Werner. 
The edible dormouse lives in Europe.  It was accidentally Introduced species|introduced to the town of Tring in England after some dormice escaped from the :wikt:private|private wikt:collection|collection of Lionel Walter Rothschild in 1902.. ''Amersham - News, Views and Information.'' 3 October 2007 
This animal is seen as a pest (organism)|pest by some people. In the United Kingdom the Wildlife and Countryside Act 1981 does not allow some ways of killing them, and to remove the dormice from the area may require a license.
