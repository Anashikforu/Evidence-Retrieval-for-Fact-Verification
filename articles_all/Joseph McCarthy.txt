
'''Joseph Raymond McCarthy''' (November 14, 1908&nbsp;â€“ May 2, 1957) was an Americans|American politician. He was a Republican Party (United States)|Republican United States Senate|U.S. Senator from the state of Wisconsin from 1947 until his death in 1957. Beginning in 1950, McCarthy became the most visible public face of a period of intense anti-communism|anti-communist suspicion inspired by the tensions of the Cold War.

He made claims that there were large numbers of Communism|Communists and Soviet Union|Soviet spies and sympathizers inside the federal government and elsewhere. The term "McCarthyism," coined in 1950 in reference to McCarthy's practices, was soon applied to similar anti-communist pursuits.

During World War II he was a captain in the United States Marine Corps.

McCarthy died of hepatitis at the Walter Reed National Military Medical Center.
