'''Sir''' '''Philip Anthony Hopkins''' Order of the British Empire|CBE (born 31 December 1937) is a Welsh people|Welsh actor of movies, theater and television. He is very famous for many roles in movies, especially as Hannibal Lecter in ''The Silence of the Lambs''. He has won an Academy Awards|Academy Award, three BAFTA Awards, two Emmy Award|Emmys and the Cecil B. DeMille Golden Globe Award.

In 2011, Hopkins played Odin (character)|Odin in the MCU movie ''Thor (movie)|Thor''. In 2019, he played Pope Benedict XVI in the Netflix movie ''The Two Popes''.

Hopkins was Knight Bachelor|knighted by Queen regnant|Queen Elizabeth II in 1993 for services to the arts.
Hopkins won a fifth BAFTA Award for Best Actor in a Leading Role|BAFTA Award and a second Academy Award for Best Actor|Academy Award for Best Actor in a Leading Role for ''The Father (2020 movie)|The Father'' (2020), becoming the oldest Best Actor winner to date.
