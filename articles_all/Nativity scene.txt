

A '''nativity scene''' is a three-dimensional depiction of the Virgin birth of Jesus|Nativity of Jesus. It is also called a crib, manger or cr√®che. Typically, a large scale scene includes: the Holy Family, the Angel|angels, the Magi, the Cattle|ox and the donkey, and an assortment of Shepherd|shepherds, villagers, servants and others.

There are two types of scenes: static representations of figures made of clay or other material and living scenes. Static scenes are often displayed on a table top in the home. In living scenes, humans and animals portray the characters in the scene.  

St. Francis of Assisi is said to have created the first nativity scene in 1223 (a "living" one) near Greccio, Italy.Thomas, George F.. ''Vitality of the Christian Tradition''. Ayer Co. Publishing, 1944.Johnson, Kevin Orlin. ''Why Do Catholics Do That?'' Random House, Inc., 1994. It was a "hit". The scene's popularity inspired communities throughout Catholic countries to stage similar scenes.

In the modern world, nativity scenes have provoked controversy. Animals are said to be abused in living scenes and objections have been placed against static scenes displayed on public lands. In addition, outdoor scenes are often the target of vandalism|vandals. Theft or destruction of outdoor figures is not uncommon.
