
'''Asia Minor''', a peninsula also called '''Anatolia''' (from the Ancient Greek: 'Άνατολή),  (Turkish language|Turkish: '''Anadolu'''), comprises the Asian part of modern Turkey.

Asia Minor is 750,000 square kilometers (290,000 square miles). Most of Asia Minor is a plateau averaging 1,000 meters (3,000 feet) above sea level. The climate is continental climate|continental and semi-arid in the interior, temperate along the north coast, and subtropics|subtropical along the south and west coasts. Most people there today speak Turkish language|Turkish. The seas surrounding Asia Minor are the Black Sea, the Aegean Sea and the Mediterranean Sea. Because Asia Minor is between Christian Europe and Asia, many different cultures have lived there. Remnants (small parts) of these cultures are there today.

Many great history|historical peoples, like the Hittites, Lydia|Lydians, Greece|Greeks, Persians, Armenians, Ancient Rome|Romans, Goths, Minoans, Byzantines and Turkish people|Turks, have lived in or occupied Asia Minor. The Roman Empire had a province called Asia, also called Phrygia. Later people started to call the entire continent Asia, so the peninsula Asia was called Asia Minor (little Asia). 

Troy is one of many famous cities that were in Asia Minor long ago.
