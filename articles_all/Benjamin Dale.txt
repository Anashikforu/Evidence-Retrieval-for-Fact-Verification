'''Benjamin Dale''' (born London 17 July 1885; died London 30 July 1943) was a Britain|British composer.

Dale won many prizes at the Royal Academy of Music for Composition (music)|composition when he was still very young.  He wrote a '' Piano sonata in D minor'' which shows the influence of Romantic music|Romantic composers such as Liszt and Robert Schumann|Schumann.  It was played by pianists such as York Bowen and  Myra Hess|Dame Myra Hess who were becoming famous.  He wrote music for viola for Lionel Tertis to play.

He was in Germany when World War I broke out and was a prisoner in the camp at Ruhleben P.O.W. Camp|Ruhleben.  There were other musicians there, including the  Canada|Canadian Ernest MacMillan who later became a famous Conductor (music)|conductor. They organized a lot of musical events at the camp.   Ernest MacMillan gave lectures about each of the nine symphony|symphonies by Beethoven, after which he and Dale would play the whole symphony in a piano duet arrangement.

After the war he worked as an Associated Board examiner.  He became professor of harmony at the Royal Academy of Music.  He continued to compose music.  His ballade (music)|ballade for violin was especially popular.
