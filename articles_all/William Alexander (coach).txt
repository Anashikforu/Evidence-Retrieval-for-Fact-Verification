Alexander graduated from Georgia Tech in 1912 as wiktionary:valedictorian|valedictorian of his class. Alexander became head coach after John Heisman retired in April 1920. 

As coach, he led Georgia Tech to three Southern Intercollegiate Athletic Association|SIAA titles (1920, 1921, 1922) and its second national championship in 1928. In 1944 he retired from coaching.  He was replaced by Bobby Dodd who was one of his assistant coaches. The Alexander Memorial Coliseum at Georgia Tech is named after him.
