'''Beh''' ('''Б, б''') is the second letter in the Cyrillic alphabet.

It came from the Greek alphabet|Greek Beta (letter)|beta, along with the Cyrillic Ve (Cyrillic)|veh. Its old name was ''buky''. It stands for the Roman alphabet|Roman B.

The Capital letters|uppercase Б looks like a B with the top curve missing and the wikt:lowercase|lowercase one looks like the number six|6.

People say it as b.
