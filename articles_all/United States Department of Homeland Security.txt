
The '''United States Department of Homeland Security''' ('''DHS'''), commonly known in the United States as "Homeland Security", is a United States Cabinet|Cabinet United States federal executive departments|department of the federal government of the United States|U.S. federal government with the duty of protecting the U.S. from Terrorism|terrorist attacks and helping when there is a natural disaster.

In 2002, because of the September 11 attacks in 2001, Congress passed a bill called the Homeland Security Act, which created the Department of Homeland Security. Tom Ridge was the first leader of the Department of Homeland Security. This was the biggest change to the federal government in 50 years.
