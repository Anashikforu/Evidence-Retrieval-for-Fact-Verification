

'''Sigma''' (uppercase/lowercase '''Σ σ ς''') is the 18th letter of the Greek alphabet. It is used to represent the "s" sound in Ancient Greek|Ancient and Modern Greek language|Greek. In the system of Greek numerals, it has a value of 200. Letters that came from it include the Roman alphabet|Roman S and Cyrillic alphabet|Cyrillic С.

 Mathematics 
The uppercase Σ is the shorthand symbol for summation (the symbol is also known as the summation operator). It is often used to condense Taylor series and to write out trigonometry|trigonometric functions such as sine, cosine, and tangent as series.

The lowercase σ is used to represent population standard deviation in statistics, and permutation function in abstract algebra.
