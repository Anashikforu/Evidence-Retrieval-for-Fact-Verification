Formula One, abbreviated to '''F1''', is a form of Open wheel car|open-wheeled auto racing. It is the highest class defined by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA), motorsport's world governing body. The top finisher of each race are awarded championship points. The points are added up at the end of they year. There are two yearly Championships. The one for List of Formula One World Drivers' Champions|drivers and one for List of Formula One World Constructors' Champions|constructors.

Michael Schumacher holds the record for the most Grand Prix victories. He has won 91&nbsp;times.

As of the  Hungarian Grand Prix.  746 drivers have started a Grand Prix race.
