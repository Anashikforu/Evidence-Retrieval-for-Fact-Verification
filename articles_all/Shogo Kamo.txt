
 was a Japanese Association football|football player. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team. His brother Takeshi Kamo also played for Japan national team.

Biography
Kamo was born in Hamamatsu, Shizuoka|Hamamatsu on December 12, 1915.
In 1936, when Kamo was a Waseda University student, he was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for Football at the 1936 Summer Olympics|1936 Summer Olympics in Berlin. At this competition, on August 4, he debuted against Sweden national football team|Sweden. He assisted Taizo Kawamoto and Tokutaro Ukon goals, and Japan completed a come-from-behind victory. The first victory in Olympics for the Japan and the historic victory over one of the powerhouses became later known as "Miracle of Berlin" (:ja:ベルリンの奇跡|ベルリンの奇跡) in Japan. In 2016, this team was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame. On August 7, he also played against Italy national football team|Italy. He played 2 games for Japan in 1936. His older brother Takeshi Kamo was also an Olympic footballer for Japan.

On September 14, 1977, Kamo collapsed during training for exhibition match at National Stadium (Tokyo, 1958)|National Stadium in Shinjuku, Tokyo Metropolis|Tokyo. He died of myocardial infarction on the day at hospital at the age of 61.
