Early life
Hildebrand was born in Sovana in Tuscany.  As a youth, he became a Benedictine monk.
In Rome, Hildebrand became the chaplain of Pope Gregory VI.  When Gregory VI abdicated and went into in exile in Germany, Hildebrand went with him.

Cardinal
Pope Leo IX raised Hildebrand to the rank of Cardinal (Catholicism)|Cardinal in 1049.
  
 Pope 
Cardinal Hildebrand was elected pope on April 22, 1073; and he chose to be called Gregory.Society for the Diffusion of Useful Knowledge. (1838). 
Pope Gregory was involved in Italian and European political disputes.

=After his death=
Pope Gregory died an exile in Salerno. His last words were: "I have loved justice and hated iniquity; therefore, I now die in exile." ("''Dilexi iustitiam et odivi iniquitatem propterea morior in exilio''")..Douglas, J. James Dixon. (1992).
