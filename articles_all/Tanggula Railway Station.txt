'''Tanggula railway station''' is a railway station on the Qingzang railway in Tibet. It is in Amdo County. Tibet is currently an Tibet Autonomous Region|Autonomous Region inside People's Republic of China|China. At an wikt:altitude|altitude of 5,068 meters above sea level it is said to be the highest train station in the world.
In 2009, the station was closed, but there are plans to make special trains for tourists stop there.



 Introduction 
Tanggula railway station is less than one kilometre from Tanggula Pass which is the highest point of the Qingzang railway. That point is 5,072 meters above sea level. 

There are no Employee|staff at the station, which opened for service on July 1, 2006. Some say that the railway was built to help China control the people of Tibet.

There are three railway tracks at the station. Two of them can be accessed by a platform. The platforms are 1.25km long. The place where the station was built was specially selected for the view it offers.
