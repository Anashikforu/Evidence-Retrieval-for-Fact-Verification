The '''Yucatán Peninsula''' is a peninsula in North America. It separates the Caribbean Sea from the Gulf of Mexico. The peninsula is east of the Isthmus of Tehuantepec, a northwestern geography|geographic divider, separating Central America from the rest of North America.

The peninsula includes the Mexico|Mexican states of Yucatán (state)|Yucatán, Campeche, and Quintana Roo; the northern part of Belize; and Guatemala's northern Departments of Guatemala|department of El Petén.  Mexican states on the isthmus to the south and west of the peninsula include Chiapas and Tabasco (state)|Tabasco. 

The Chicxulub crater is on the north coast. It is believed to be made by the impact that led to the K/T extinction event|extinction of the dinosaurs.
