
'''Hurricane Paka''' (international designation: ''9728'', JTWC designation: ''05C'', Philippine Atmospheric, Geophysical and Astronomical Services Administration|PAGASA designation: '''Rubing''', also known as '' Typhoon Paka'') was the last tropical cyclone in the 1997 Pacific 1997 Pacific hurricane season|hurricane and 1997 Pacific typhoon season|typhoon season, and was among the strongest Pacific typhoons in the month of December. developed on November 28 from a trough (meteorology)|trough well to the southwest of Hawaii. The storm tracked generally westward for much of its duration, and on December 7 it crossed into the western Pacific Ocean. Much of its track was characterized by fluctuations in intensity, and on December 10 the cyclone attained typhoon status as it crossed the Marshall Islands. On December 16, Paka struck Guam and Rota (island)|Rota with winds of 230&nbsp;km/h (145&nbsp;mph), and it strengthened further to reach peak winds on December 18 over open waters. Subsequently, it underwent a steady weakening trend, and on December 23 Paka dissipated.

Typhoon Paka first impacted the Marshall Islands, where it dropped heavy rainfall and left $80&nbsp;million in damage (1997&nbsp;USD, $100&nbsp;million 2007&nbsp;USD). Later, it passed just north of Guam, where strong winds destroyed about 1,500&nbsp;buildings and damaged 10,000&nbsp;more; 5,000&nbsp;people were left homeless, and the island experienced a complete power outage following the typhoon. Damage on the island totaled $500&nbsp;million (1997&nbsp;USD, $645&nbsp;million 2007&nbsp;USD), which warranted the List of retired Pacific hurricane names|retirement of its name. Paka also caused light damage in the Northern Marianas Islands, and overall the storm caused no reported deaths.
