
'''Kirkwood gaps''' are areas of the asteroid belt where asteroids are unusually scarce, as seen in the :Image:Kirkwood Gaps.png|graph on the right. They are caused by orbital resonances with Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter.

The gaps were first noticed in 1857 by Daniel Kirkwood, who also correctly explained their origin in the orbital resonances with Jupiter while a professor at Washington & Jefferson College|Jefferson College in Canonsburg, Pennsylvania.
