A '''count''' is a nobleman in most of European countries, equivalent in rank to a United Kingdom|British earl, whose wife is also still a "countess" (for lack of an Anglo-Saxon term). The word ''count'' comes from French language|French ''comte'' which in turn comes from the Latin ''comitas'', "companion" (of the emperor). In feudalism the territory ruled by a count was a county.

A woman having this title or the wife of a count is called '''countess'''.
