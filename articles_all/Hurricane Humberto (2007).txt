
'''Hurricane Humberto''' formed and strengthened faster than any other tropical cyclone on List of Atlantic hurricane seasons|record within 18 hours of Landfall (meteorology)|landfall; developing on September 12, 2007 in the northwestern Gulf of Mexico, the cyclone quickly strengthened and struck High Island, Texas|High Island, Texas, with winds of about 90&nbsp;mph (150&nbsp;km/h) early on September 13. It steadily weakened after moving ashore, and on September 14 it began dissipating over northwestern Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia as it interacted with an approaching cold front. Humberto was the eighth named storm and third hurricane of the 2007 Atlantic hurricane season, and became the first hurricane to strike the United States since Hurricane Wilma of 2005 Atlantic hurricane season|2005. Damage was fairly light, estimated at about $50 million.
