The '''Lycaenidae'''  are the second-largest family (biology)|family of butterfly|butterflies, with about 6000 species worldwide.Fiedler, K. 1996. Host-plant relationships of lycaenid butterflies: large-scale patterns, interactions with plant chemistry, and mutualism with ants. Entomologia Experimentalis et Applicata 80(1):259-267 doi:10.1007/BF00194770 
The family includes many beautiful species. Some, like the Palos Verdes blue, are critically endangered. The Lycaenids were favourites of the Russians|Russian novelist and lepidopterist Vladimir Nabokov.
