

'''Émile Zola''' () (2 April 1840 – 29 September 1902) was a major France|French writer and the most important Naturalism (literature)|naturalist writer.  He worked toward political liberalization of France.

Zola was nominated for the first and second Nobel Prize in Literature in 1901 and 1902. His death from carbon monoxide poisoning is suspected to have been suicide.
