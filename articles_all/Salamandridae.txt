The family '''Salamandridae''' is a family (biology)|family of amphibians. It consists of true salamanders and newts. 

The species are spread all over the Northern Hemisphere|northern hemisphere - Europe, Asia, the northern tip of Africa and North America. 

As of 2007, there were 74 known species, in 20 genus|genera.
