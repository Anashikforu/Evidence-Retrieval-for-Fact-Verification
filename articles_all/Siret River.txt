
 

The '''Siret River''' (Ukrainian language|Ukrainian: ''Серет'', Russian language|Russian: ''Сирет '', Hungarian language|Hungarian: ''Szeret'') is a river that comes up from the Carpathian Mountains|Carpathians in the northern Bukovina region of Ukraine. It flows southward into Romania for about 470 kilometers before it joins the Danube.  It was named ''Hierasus'' in times of old.

Towns and villages
The following towns and villages can be found along the Siret River, from source to mouth: Berehomet, Storozhynets, Siret, Grămeşti, Zvoriştea, Liteni, Paşcani, Stolniceni-Prăjescu, Iaşi|Stolniceni-Prăjescu, Roman, Romania|Roman, Bacău, Adjud, Mărăşeşti, Galaţi.
