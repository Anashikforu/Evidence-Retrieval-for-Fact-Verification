


The '''''Venus de Milo''''' is an Ancient Greece|ancient Greek statue. It is in the Louvre, a museum in Paris, France. The ''Venus de Milo'' represents Aphrodite, the deity|goddess of love in Greek mythology. The statue is made of marble and is 203centimetre|cm high. It is believed to be the work of Alexandros of Antioch. The ''Venus de Milo'' was found on 8 April 1820 on the Aegean Sea|Aegean island of Melos. King of France|King Louis XVIII of France presented the ''Venus de Milo'' to the Louvre in 1821.
