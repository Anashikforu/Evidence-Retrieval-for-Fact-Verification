'''Samuel Timothy''' '''"Tim"''' '''McGraw''' (born May 1, 1966) is an American actor, singer and songwriter. Between 1994 and 2016, he had number one songs on Billboard (magazine)|''Billboard'''s Country Airplay music chart.

His father is of Bohemian Czech people|Czech, Dutch people|Dutch, English people|English, French people|French Huguenots|Huguenot, Germans|German, Irish people|Irish, People of Northern Ireland|Northern Irish (Ulster Scots people|Scotch-Irish) and Scottish people|Scottish descent and His mother is of Italians|Italian and Irish people|Irish descent. He is well known for his Single (music)|singles "I Like It, I Love It", "Please Don't Take the Girl", "Grown Men Don't Cry", and "Live Like You We're Dying".

McGraw had 11 consecutive albums debut at Number One on the ''Billboard'' albums charts. Twenty-one singles hit No.&nbsp;1 on the Billboard Hot Country Songs chart. He has won 3 Grammy Awards|Grammys, 14 Academy of Country Music awards, 11 Country Music Association (CMA) awards, 10 American Music Awards, and 3 People's Choice Awards.

Marriage and family
He has been Marriage|married to Faith Hill since 1996. They have three daughters: Gracie Katherine (born May 1997), Maggie Elizabeth (born August 1998), and Audrey Caroline (born December 2001).
