
The '''Super Nintendo Entertainment System''' (often shortened to ''SNES'' or ''Super NES'') is a 16-bit video game console created by Nintendo, released first in 1990. It is the sucessor to the Nintendo Entertainment System|NES. It was called the '''Super Famicom''' in Japan, and the '''Super Comboy''' in South Korea.  The console can display 2D|2D graphics, with some limited support for 3D graphics with the Super FX chip.  Super NES games are loaded from a Cartridge (electronics)|cartridge. The major competitor for the Super Nintendo was the Sega Genesis/Mega Drive, but it also competed with the 3D0 Interactive Multiplayer|Panasonic 3D0.
