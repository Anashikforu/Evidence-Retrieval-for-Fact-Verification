'''Aleph''' is the first letter of the Hebrew language|Hebrew alphabet.

 Modern uses 
 In mathematics, Georg Cantor decided to use alephs to determine the cardinality of infinite set theory|sets. These are called aleph numbers, with some of the most notable ones being aleph null (\aleph_0) and aleph one (\aleph_1).

 Hebrew alphabet 
Aleph ("a" pronounced as "father") has a mute sound and is represented by the symbol "◊ê".
