

The '''National Geographic Channel''', also known as '''Nat Geo''', is an Asian subscription television channel. It has non-fiction, programming involving nature, science, culture, and history. These programs are made by the National Geographic Society, just like History (South East Asian TV channel)|History and the Discovery Channel.

The channel was launched on August 1, 1994 in partnership with STAR TV (Asia)|STAR TV. Star TV is a Hong Kong-based Satellite television|satelite television service for Asia and the Pacific Ocean|Pacific. It is owned by Rupert Murdoch's News Corporation. Nat Geo replaced the NBC Asia|NBC Asia channel on July 1, 1998. , the Asian version of National Geographic Channel was available in over 56 million homes. NGC Asia has eight different channels feeds.

Operating Channels
 National Geographic Channel Asia - This channel has a bilingual feed with English and Cantonese in selected programs only.
 National Geographic Channel India - This channel has a bilingual feed with English and Hindi in selected programs only.
 National Geographic Channel Korea - With Korean subtitles.
 National Geographic Channel Japan - This channel has a bilingual feed with English and Japanese in selected programs only.
 National Geographic Channel MENA - Simulcast from NGC Asia from 11:00 P.M. to 5:00 A.M. HK Time.
 National Geographic Channel Philippines - Same with Asia feed with local advertisement.
 National Geographic Channel Taiwan - With Traditional Chinese subtitles.
 National Geographic Channel Israel - With Hebrew subtitles.
 National Geographic Channel HD Asia
