
'''Laon Cathedral''' () is a cathedral in Laon, Hauts-de-France, France. The current gothic cathedral was built between 1135 and 1270, on the site of an romanesque architecture|romanesque church which had been destroyed by a fire. It is one of the most important examples of the Gothic architecture of the 12th and 13th centuries. It is also among the first gothic cathedrals built in France.  The cathedral has been compared to those of Cathédrale Saint-Étienne de Sens|Saint-Étienne of Sens and Notre-Dame de Paris|Notre Dame of Paris.
