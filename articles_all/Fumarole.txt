

A '''fumarole''' (Latin ''fumus'', smoke) is an opening in the crust of the Earth, often in the neighborhood of dormant volcano|dormant volcanoes, where steam and gases come out, for instance carbon dioxide, sulfur dioxide, hydrochloric acid, and hydrogen sulfide.

Etymology
The name solfatara (from the Italian language|Italian ''solfo'', sulfur), is given to fumaroles with sulfurous gases.
