
'''Bonifacio''' () is a town and commune in France|commune near the southern tip of the island of Corsica, in the ''département in France|département'' of Corse-du-Sud, France. Its inhabitants are called ''Bonifaciens'' and the city was founded as a fortress and named after Boniface II of Tuscany in 828. Bonifacio is split into two sections. The ''vieille ville'' (old town), or ''la Haute Ville'' (the Upper city).

Geography
Bonifacio is located directly on the Mediterranean Sea, separated from Sardinia by the Strait of Bonifacio. The commune includes the Lavezzi Islands.

Famous people born in Bonifacio
 Tommaso Maria Zigliara (1833-1893), Catholicism|Roman Catholic cardinal (Catholicism)|cardinal, theologian, and philosopher.
