'''Hermippe''' or ''''''.
Hermippe is about 4 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 21,182,000&nbsp;km in 629.809 days, at an orbital inclination of 151° to the ecliptic (149° to Jupiter's equator), with an eccentricity of 0.2290.

It was named in August 2003 by the International Astronomical Union|IAU, after Hermippe, a lover of Zeus (Jupiter).
Hermippe belongs to the Ananke group, retrograde non-Sphere|spherical moons which orbit Jupiter between 19,300,000 and 22,700,000&nbsp;km, at inclinations of about 150°.
