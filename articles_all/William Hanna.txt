
'''William Denby "Bill" Hanna''' (July 14, 1910 in Mexico, New Mexico &ndash; March 22, 2001 in Los Angeles, California) was an United States|American Animation|animator, film director|director, Film producer|producer, cartoon artist, and co-wikt:founder|founder with Joseph Barbera of Hanna-Barbera. The studio made well-known cartoons, such as ''The Huckleberry Hound Show'', ''The Flintstones'', ''The Jetsons'', and ''Scooby-Doo'', as well as the musical movie, ''Charlotte's Web (1973 movie)|Charlotte's Web.
