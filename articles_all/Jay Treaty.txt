The '''Jay Treaty''' was a treaty between the United States and United Kingdom of Great Britain|Britain in the years following the American Revolutionary War|American Revolution. The main American involved in the treaty was John Jay, who was also Chief Justice of the United States. It was Ratification|passed by the United States Congress in 1795. It allowed for increased trade with Britain in exchange for the British giving up their forts on the Great Lakes.  The Democratic-Republican Party thought that the Jay Treaty was good for the British and bad for the Americans, which led to them burning effigy|effigies of Jay.
