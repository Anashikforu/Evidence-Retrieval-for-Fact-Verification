


'''Simony''' means that one pays for an office or a position in the hierarchy of Christianity|Christian One Holy Catholic and Apostolic Church|church. It is an offense against canon law. Simony became widespread in the Catholic Church in the 9th and 10th centuries.Merriam-Webster's Encyclopedia of World Religions, edited by Wendy Doniger, 1999 The name comes from Simon Magus, who appears in the Acts of the Apostles 8:18-24. Simon Magus offers the disciples of Jesus, Saint Peter|Peter and John the Apostle|John, payment so that anyone he would place his hands on would receive the power of the Holy Spirit. This is the origin of the term ''simony''.''The Reader's Encyclopedia'' (1965), New York: Thomas Y. Crowell Company, vol.2, ''p.''932, "Simon." but it also extends to other forms of trafficking for money in "spiritual things".Smith, W. (1880). ''A dictionary of Christian antiquities: being a continuation of the 'Dictionary of the Bible'''. J.B. Burr. "Simony".

 Catholic Church 
In the Middle Ages there were problems with simony and accusations of simony, especially during the Investiture Controversy. Rulers wanted to employ the educated and centrally organized clergy in their administrations.
