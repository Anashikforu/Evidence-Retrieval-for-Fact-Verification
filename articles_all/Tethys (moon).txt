'''Tethys''' (pronounced ''TEE-this'') is a large Satellite (natural)|moon of the planet Saturn (planet)|Saturn.

 Discovery 
It was discovered by Giovanni Domenico Cassini in 1684. It was one of the first Saturnian moons discovered.

 Physical characteristics 
= Mass, density and volume =
Tethys' mass is 617,551,805,221,061,000,000 kg, its density is 0.973 g/cm3 and its volume is 634,264,255&nbsp;km3.

= Craters and valleys =
It has a very large crater on its surface, called Odysseus. It is named after a Greece|Greek warrior king in Homer's two great works, ''The Iliad'' and ''The Odyssey''. There is also a very long valley called Ithaca Chasma. It is 100 kilometre|km wide and 2000&nbsp;km long.

= Orbit and rotation =
Tethys takes 45.3 hours to orbit (go around) Saturn and orbits Saturn 294,660&nbsp;km away. Tethys is tidal locking|tidally locked in phase with its parent planet - one side always faces toward Saturn.
