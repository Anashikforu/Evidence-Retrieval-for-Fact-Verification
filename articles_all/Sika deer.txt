The '''sika deer''' (''Cervus nippon'') is a member of the deer family '''Cervidae'''. It lives in East Asia. It is found in mixed deciduous forests to the north, and mixed subtropical deciduous and evergreen forests to the south.

 Distribution 
Sika deer are native to Taiwan to Japan, but they have been introduced to many parks in Europe. In the United Kingdom|UK, there are large populations in Ireland and Scotland and there are patchy populations scattered across England.
