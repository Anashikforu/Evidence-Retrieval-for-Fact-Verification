'''Breastfeeding''' is when a mother milk|feeds her baby|babies milk from her breasts. The baby puts its mouth on to their mother's nipple and sucks the milk out of the breast's mammary gland. Breast milk is healthier for the baby because it has living cells including enzymes, probiotics, stem cells and antibodies such as lysosomes.  Other forms of  breastmilk substitutes most usually cow's milk
