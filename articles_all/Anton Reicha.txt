'''Anton Reicha''' (born 26 February 1770; died 28 May 1836) was a Czech Republic|Czech-born  composer who became Naturalization|naturalized France|French. He was a lifelong friend of Ludwig van Beethoven|Beethoven.
Reicha is now best remembered for the large number of quintets that he wrote for wind instruments, and for his work as a music teacher.  Franz Liszt and Hector Berlioz were both pupils of Reicha.  Reicha wrote several books about music theory and Composition (music)|composition.
