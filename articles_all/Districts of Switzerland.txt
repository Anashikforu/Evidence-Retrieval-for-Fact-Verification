In '''Switzerland''' each Cantons of Switzerland|Canton is free to decide its own internal organisation. Because of this there many types and names for smaller administration|administrative divisions in each canton. The term '''District''' is used for these smaller divisions. 

Amt, Amtsbezirk, District and Distretto
Most Cantons are divided into ''Bezirke'' (German language|German for districts). Some are called ''Ämter'' (Canton of Lucerne|Lucerne), ''Amtsbezirke'' (Canton of Bern|Bern), ''district'' (in French) or ''distretto'' (Tessin and part of Canton of Graubünden|Graubünden).

The Bezirke generally only deals with administration and court organization. The cantons Graubünden and Canton of Schwyz|Schwyz are their own Juristic person|legal entities. They have the power to control taxes as well. 

No district level in ten cantons
Ten of the 26 Cantons do not have a district level of government:

Canton of Uri|Uri, Canton of Obwalden|Obwalden, Canton of Nidwalden|Nidwalden, Appenzell Ausserrhoden, Canton of Glarus|Glarus, Canton of Zug|Zug, Basel-City and Canton of Geneva|Geneva. Canton of Schaffhausen|Schaffhausen stopped using Bezirke in the middle of 1999. Since the start of 2003 Canton of St. Gallen does not use the Bezirk level any more.
