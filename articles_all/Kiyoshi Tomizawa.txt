
 is a former Japanese Association football|football player. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team.

Biography
Tomizawa was born in Fujieda, Shizuoka|Fujieda on December 3, 1943. After graduating from Fujieda Higashi High School, he joined Nippon Steel Yawata SC|Yawata Steel (later ''Nippon Steel''). In 1965, Yawata Steel joined new league Japan Soccer League. He retired in 1976, having played 164 games and scored 22 goals in the league.

In October 1964, Tomizawa was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for Football at the 1964 Summer Olympics|1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo. However, he did not play in the match. On March 14, 1965, he debuted for Japan against Hong Kong national football team|Hong Kong. In 1968, he was selected Japan for Football at the 1968 Summer Olympics|1968 Summer Olympics in Mexico City. He played against Hungary national football team|Hungary in semifinal and Japan won Bronze Medal. In 2018, this team was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame. He also played at Football at the 1970 Asian Games|1970 Asian Games. At Football at the 1972 Summer Olympics|1972 Summer Olympics qualification in 1971, Japan's failure to qualify for 1972 Summer Olympics. This qualification was his last game for Japan. He played 9 games and scored 2 goals for Japan until 1965.
