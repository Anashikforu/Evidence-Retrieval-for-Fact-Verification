
'''Robert Graeme Pollock''', known as '''Graeme''', (born 27 February 1944 in Durban, KwaZulu-Natal|Natal, South Africa) was a national cricketer for South Africa. He played in 23 Test cricket|test matches for his country.

He comes from a famous cricketing family, and as one of the best left-handed Cricket#Batsman|batsmen to have played Test cricket. 

He won many awards, including:
 South Africa's ''Cricketer of the 20th Century''  (chosen in 1999)
 One of Wisden's Wisden Cricketer of the Year|Cricketers of the Year  (chosen in 1966)
 Wisden Leading Cricketer in the World for 1967 and 1969 (chosen in 2007).

Donald Bradman said that Pollock and Garry Sobers|Sir Garfield Sobers were the best left-handers Bradman had ever seen play cricket.
