'''Public Ivy''' is a term started by Richard Moll in his 1985 book ''Public Ivys: A Guide to America's best public undergraduate colleges and universities''. The book is about university|universities which "provide an Ivy League college|collegiate experience at a public school price."Richard Moll (Author)|Richard Moll in his book ''Public Ivys: A Guide to America's best public undergraduate colleges and universities'' (1985) Public Ivies are chosen, according to the ''Journal of Blacks in Higher Education'', to be able to be "successfully competing with the Ivy League schools in academic rigor... attracting superstar faculty and in competing for the best and brightest students of all races."
