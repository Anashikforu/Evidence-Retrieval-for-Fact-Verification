
 is a former Japanese Association football|football player and manager. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team.

Biography
Yamamoto was born in Numazu, Shizuoka|Numazu on April 4, 1958. After graduating from Kokushikan University, he joined Japan Soccer League club Júbilo Iwata|Yamaha Motors in 1981. First season, the club finished at the bottom place and was relegated to Division 2. In 1982, the club won the champions and was promoted Division 1. The club also won 1982 Emperor's Cup. He retired in 1987. He played 109 games and scored 3 goals in the league.

In December 1980, when Yamamoto was a Kokushikan University student, he was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for 1982 FIFA World Cup|1982 World Cup qualification. At this qualification, on December 26, he debuted against China national football team|China. He played 4 games for Japan in 1981.

After retirement, Yamamoto started coaching career at Júbilo Iwata|Yamaha Motors (later ''Júbilo Iwata'') in 1987. In 1995, he became a manager for Japan national under-20 football team|Japan U-20 national team and managed at 1997 FIFA World Youth Championship|1997 World Youth Championship. In 1997, he returned to Júbilo Iwata. In October 1998, he became an assistant coach for Japan national football team|Japan national team under manager Philippe Troussier. After 2002 FIFA World Cup|2002 World Cup, in August, Yamamoto became a manager for Japan national under-23 football team|Japan U-23 national team and managed at Football at the 2004 Summer Olympics – Men's tournament|2004 Summer Olympics. In November 2004, he returned to Júbilo Iwata and became a manager. He resigned in June 2006.
