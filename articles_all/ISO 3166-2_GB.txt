'''ISO 3166-2:GB''' is an International Organization for Standardization (ISO) code|coding system that gives geocodes to Subdivisions of the United Kingdom|administrative divisions of the United Kingdom:
England
 34 Counties of England|counties with county councils (all Shire county|shire counties except Berkshire)
 32 Boroughs of London|London boroughs
 36 Metropolitan borough|City Sections 
 47 Unitary authority|Large Areas (including the Isles of Scilly)
 1 city corporation (the City of London)
Northern Ireland
 26 Subdivisions of Northern Ireland
Scotland
 32 Council Areas of Scotland
Wales
 22 Unitary Authorities of Wales
The Channel Islands and the Isle of Man are not part of the UK, but ISO offers codes for them under "GB-".

The first part of each code is the ISO 3166-1 code GB for the United Kingdom. The second part is a three-letter code which is the same as in the British Standard BS 6879.

Codes 


=Alternate Welsh codes=
The ISO has also issued codes for some areas with Welsh names and codes.


Other codes
These codes are used by the BS. They were added to ISO 3166-2 in Newsletter 9, except for the Isle of Man and Channel Islands:

GB-UKM United Kingdom
GB-GBN Great Britain
GB-EAW England and Wales
GB-ENG England
GB-WLS Wales (ISO also includes the Welsh version, GB-CYM for Cymru)
GB-SCT Scotland
GB-NIR Northern Ireland
GB-IOM Isle of Man
GB-CHA Channel Islands

BS also has Welsh language code for Welsh names of Wales and its subdivisions, where they differ from the English language names. These are not listed here.
