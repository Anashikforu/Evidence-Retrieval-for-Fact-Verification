
'''Friedrich Rittelmeyer''' (5 October 1872 – 23 March 1938) was a Germany|German Protestantism|Protestant Theology|theologian. He was born in Dillingen an der Donau, Bavaria. From 1895 to 1922 he worked as a Lutheranism|Lutheran pastor in Würzburg, Nuremberg and Berlin. In 1922 he became the founder (and leader up to his death) of The Christian Community, a christian denomination, inspired by anthroposophy. He died in Hamburg.
