
'''James William Ward''' (September 1, 1906 – November 15, 1990) was a Winger (ice hockey)|right winger in the National Hockey League|NHL from 1927–28 NHL season|1928 - 1938–39 NHL season|1939. Jimmy Ward's son, Pete Ward, played nine seasons of professional baseball, winning The Sporting News Rookie of the Year Award|TSN Rookie Of The Year honors in 1963 while playing for the Chicago White Sox.

Playing career
He played his entire National Hockey League|NHL career in Montreal. It began in the 1927–28 NHL season|1928 season with the Montreal Maroons. He would stay with the English Montreal team until they folded following the 1937–38 NHL season|1938 season. Along the way he scored 10 or more goals in 10 of 11 seasons during a time when the regular season was only 48 games long. He won the Stanley Cup in 1934–35 NHL season|1935. He played in the Ace Bailey Benefit Game in 1934 and the Howie Morenz Memorial Game in 1937. When the Maroons folded he joined the rival Montreal Canadiens for the 1938–39 NHL season|1939 season.

Coaching career
In 1939–40 AHL season|1940 he served as a player/coach of the New Haven Eagles of the IAHL. He would later serve as head coach of the Pacific Coast Hockey League|PCHL's Portland Eagles and Portland Penguins.
