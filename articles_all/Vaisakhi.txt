
'''Vaisakhi''' ('' (also known as '''Vishu''' or '''Baisaki''') is an important festival celebrated by Sikhs and Hindus. It is a very big Festival|celebration in South Asia and is celebrated around April 13.

 Observance 

Vaisakhi is the ancient :en:wikt:harvest festival|harvest festival in the Punjab region.  It is also the start of a new :en:wikt:solar year|solar year, and new harvest season. Baisakhi is a Sikh religious festival.

This day is also observed as the beginning of the Hindu solar new year celebrated by the people of Nepal and India in Assam|Assam Valley, Kerala, Orissa, West Bengal and some other regions of India. There are regional variation outside of Punjab (India)|Punjab too. In Himachal Pradesh, the Hindu Goddess Jwalamukhi is worshipped on Vaisakhi, while in Bihar, the Sun-god Surya is honoured.

 Other observances 

The festival is celebrated as 
''Bohag Bihu|Rongali Bihu'' in Assam, 
''Naba Barsha'' or ''Pohela Boishakh'' in Bengal and Tripura, ''Puthandu'' (Tamil New Year) in Tamil Nadu, 
''Vishu'' (or Vaishakhi) in Kerala, 
''Bikhu'' or ''Bikhauti'' in the Kumaon division|Kumaon region of Uttarakhand,
 ''Maha Vishuba Sankranti'' (or Pana Sankranti) in Orissa, and the 
Sinhalese New Year festival in Sri Lanka.

Calendars

Hinduism and Sikhism have several religious calendars. Some places of worship accept one version and others use a different version. So the festival will always fall on the same day in the western Gregorian calendar.
