


The '''Atlas Mountains''' (Moroccan Amazigh|Amazigh: '''Idurar n Watlas''', ) are a mountain range in northwest Africa. They extend about 2,400&nbsp;km (1,500 miles) through Morocco, Algeria, and Tunisia. The highest peak is Tougkal, with an elevation of 4,167&nbsp;metres&nbsp;(13,671&nbsp;ft) in southwestern Morocco. The second highest mountain is the M'Goun of 4071 meters. The southwestern part is called "Anti-Atlas".

The Atlas ranges separate the Mediterranean Sea|Mediterranean and Atlantic Ocean|Atlantic coastlines from the Sahara Desert. Most of the people living in the Atlas Mountains are part of Berber people|Berber tribes in Morocco and in Algeria. 

The mountains are named after the ancient Greek Titan (mythology)|Titan, Atlas (Mythology)|Atlas.
