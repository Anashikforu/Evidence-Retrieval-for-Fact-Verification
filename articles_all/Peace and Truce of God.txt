The '''Peace of God''' was a movement begun by bishops in southern France around 990 CE to limit the violence done to property and to the unarmed.Thomas Head, ''The Peace of God: Social Violence and Religious Response in France Around the Year 1000'' (Ithaca, NY: Cornell University Press, 1992), p. 4 The Carolingian Empire had collapsed and the nobles were making war almost all the time.

The '''Truce of God''' :wikt:extended|extended the Peace by setting aside certain days of the week when violence was not allowed.Adriaan H. Bredero, ''Christendom and Christianity in the Middle Ages'' (Grand Rapids, MI: W.B. Eerdmans, 1994), p. 110 Where the Peace of God limited violence against the church and the poor, the Truce of God was about preventing fighting between Christians, :wikt:especially|especially knights.Thomas Head, ''The Peace of God: Social Violence and Religious Response in France Around the Year 1000'' (Ithaca, NY: Cornell University Press, 1992), p. 7
