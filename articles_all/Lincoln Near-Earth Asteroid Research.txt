The '''Lincoln Near-Earth Asteroid Research''' ('''LINEAR''') wikt:project|project is a project that is used for the finding and wikt:track|tracking of near-Earth asteroids. The project is done together by the United States Air Force, NASA, and Massachusetts Institute of Technology|MIT's Lincoln Laboratory. LINEAR has found most of the asteroids since 1998. LINEAR has found 226,193 new wikt:object|objects since it started. Out of those, 2,019 were near-Earth asteroids and 236 were comets. LINEAR uses robotic telescopes to help them find asteroids. LINEAR is wikt:credit|credited with the finding of 67,208 asteroids as of June 13, 2006. LINEAR has also found these comets: 11P/Tempel-Swift-LINEAR, 146P/Shoemaker-LINEAR, 148P/Anderson-LINEAR, 156P/Russell-LINEAR, 158P/Kowal-LINEAR, 160P/LINEAR (LINEAR 43), 165P/LINEAR (LINEAR 10), and 176P/LINEAR.
