
'''Trois-Rivières''' () is a city in Quebec, Canada.
It is where the Saint-Maurice River|Saint-Maurice and Saint Lawrence Rivers meet. 
It was first created on July 4, 1634.
It was the second permanent settlement in New France, after Quebec City in 1608.Roy-Sole, Monique. "A Tale of Tenacity", ''Canadian Geographic Magazine'', April 2009, Vol. 129, No. 2, p. 31

The city's name is French for ''three rivers''.
It is called because the Saint-Maurice River, which is divided by two small islands at the river 's opening, has three mouths at the Saint Lawrence River.
