
The '''Elizabeth Cross''' is an award within the United Kingdom honours system|British honours system that is issued to the next of kin of a service man or woman who died due to enemy action or terrorism since 1949. It is not an award for wikt:valour|valour or wikt:bravery|bravery. It is in the shape of a cross with four equal sides that contains flowers at the end of each arm that represents the four countries within the union of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland. The medal also contains a laurel wreath behind the cross and bears the dead person's name on the back. The medal comes with a miniature (small version) medal for day wear. The medal can only be worn by the next of kin and for anyone else to wear it (even if related to the person who died) is a criminal offence.
