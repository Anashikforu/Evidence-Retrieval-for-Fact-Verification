San Jose State University sports teams have won in the NCAA in track and field, cross country running, golf, and boxing. By 2008, SJSU had won 10 NCAA team wikt:championship|championships and 50 NCAA Division 1 champions. SJSU also has an international wikt:reputation|reputation for judo, winning 43 out of 46 wikt:collegiate|collegiate national championships in the sport (by 2008).Ryan Sholin; "The judo that you don't know"; ''The Spartan Daily'', San José State University; April 11, 2006;  

People who once went to SJSU have won 18 Olympic games|Olympic medals (including seven gold medals) since the gold medal won by Willie Steel in track and field in the 1948 Olympics. These people have won medals in swimming (sport)|swimming, judo and boxing. 

The track team Coaching|coached by Lloyd (Bud) Winter|"Bud" Winter earned San Jose the nickname "Speed City," and has included Olympic medalists and social Activism|activists Lee Evans (athlete)|Lee Evans, John Carlos and Tommie Smith. Smith and Carlos are remembered for giving the raised fist salute from the medalist's podium during the 1968 Summer Olympic Games in Mexico City.

In 2008 head coaches from the men’s soccer, baseball, women’s gymnastics, and women’s golf programs had all been named the conference “Coach of the Year.”
