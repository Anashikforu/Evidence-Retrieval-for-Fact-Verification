

The '''American Museum of Natural History''' is a museum about science. It is one of the biggest and Tourism|most-visited museums in the world.

The museum is made of 25 connected buildings in Theodore Roosevelt Park on the Upper West Side, Manhattan, New York (state)|New York, United States|USA. It has 46 exhibition halls, a lot of Laboratory|laboratories, a research library, and more than 34 million specimens and Artifact (archaeology)|artifacts.

More than 200 scientists work at the museum, researching Biological species concept|biology, paleontology, earth science, astrophysics, and anthropology. Its graduate school awards Doctorate|doctorate degrees in biology and Master's degree|masters degrees in education. The museum also provides education programs for children and adults, including for teachers.




Famous people who have worked at the museum include Theodore Roosevelt|Teddy Roosevelt, Franz Boas, Margaret Mead, and Neil deGrasse Tyson|Neil DeGrasse Tyson.
