The '''discus throw''' is an event in Athletics (track and field)|athletics, where a heavy disc is thrown for distance. It was invented in 708 BC in Ancient Greece.
The discus throw is the subject of a number of well-known ancient Ancient Greece|Greek statues and Ancient Rome|Roman copies of them.US thrower Al Oerter is one of only two athletes to win the same individual event at four consecutive Olympic Games.
