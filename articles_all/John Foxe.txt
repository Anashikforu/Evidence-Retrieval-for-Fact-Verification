 Education and Resignation from Oxford 
Foxe was born in Boston, Lincolnshire|Boston, Lincolnshire, England into a prominent family. In 1535 Foxe was admitted to Magdalen College School, Oxford|Magdalen College School. He became  a fellow in July 1539. 
Foxe resigned from his college in 1545, after referring to it as "a prison." During his time at University of Oxford|Oxford he became an evangelicalism|evangelical, meaning that he converted to Protestant beliefs not accepted by the Church of England under Henry VIII of England|Henry VIII. As he wanted to leave Oxford, Foxe looked to other evangelicals for help but received only advice and a little money. Foxe married Agnes Randall on February 3, 1547.

 Life in London under Edward VI 
With the death of Henry VIII of England|Henry VIII in January of 1547 Foxe's prospects (and those of the evangelical cause generally) changed for the better. Foxe was :wikt:ordain|ordained deacon by Nicholas Ridley (martyr)|Nicholas Ridley on June 24, 1550.

 Marian Exile 
In the fall of 1554 Foxe moved to Frankfurt, where he lived with Anthony Gilby in the English colony of Protestant refugees. 
Foxe then removed to Basel where he lived and worked with John Bale and Lawrence Humphrey.

 Return to England 
In 1559, when Mary I of England|Mary I had died Foxe returned to England. He lived for some time at Aldgate, London, in the house of his former pupil, Thomas Howard, 4th Duke of Norfolk|Thomas Howard. Foxe started publishing works of religious wikt:controversy|controversy and worked on a new martyrology, which would become the ''Foxe's Book of Martyrs''. 
Foxe was ordained priest by Edmund Grindal, now Bishop of London, on January 25, 1560, and he moved to Norwich to live with its bishop, John Parkhurst. On March 23 of the following year the first edition of Foxe's Book of Martyrs was published.

Foxe was one of the earliest students of Old English language|Anglo-Saxon, and he and Day published an edition of the ''Saxon gospels'' with the help of Matthew Parker|Archbishop Parker. 

Foxe died on 8 April 1587 and was buried at St Giles-without-Cripplegate|St. Giles's, Cripplegate.
