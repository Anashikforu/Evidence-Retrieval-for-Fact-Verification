
'''Mark Edward McGhee''' (born 25 May, 1957 in Glasgow, Scotland) is a former professional association football|footballer and current manager of England|English club Bristol Rovers F.C.|Bristol Rovers.

Playing career
McGhee played for many Scotland|Scottish and England|English clubs, before he had to retire because of injury. He started his playing career at Greenock Morton F.C.|Greenock Morton before moving on to Newcastle United F.C.|Newcastle United, Aberdeen F.C.|Aberdeen, Hamburger SV|Hamburg, Celtic F.C.|Celtic, IK Brage and Reading F.C.|Reading and won the '''Scottish PFA Players' Player of the Year''' in 1982 as well as four caps and two goals for the Scotland national football team.  His greatest career moment came in 1983 when he was in the Aberdeen side that defeated Real Madrid 2-1 to lift the European Cup Winner's Cup.
