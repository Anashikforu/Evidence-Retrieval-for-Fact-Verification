Traditional history
Chūai is almost certainly a wikt:legend|legend; but the ''Kojiki'' and ''Nihonshoki'' record his name.Aston, William. (1998). ''Nihongi.'' Vol. 1, pp. 188-214.  He was a grandson of Emperor Keiko|Emperor Keikō.

Chūai's wife is known as Empress Jingū.  Her son would become known as Emperor Ōjin.Aston (1998), pp. 217-223.

Events of Chūai's life
The limited information about Chūai does not imply that no such person ever existed. Very little information is wikt:available|available for study prior to the reign of the 29th monarch, Emperor Kimmei.Titsingh, pp. 34-36; Brown, pp. 261-262; Varley, pp. 123-124.

= After his death =
This emperor's official name after his death (his posthumous name) was wikt:regularize|regularized many centuries after the lifetime which was wikt:ascribe|ascribed to Chūai.
