
The '''Halifax explosion''' took place on December 6, 1917 when the SS Mont-Blanc|SS ''Mont-Blanc'', a France|French Freighter|cargo ship loaded with explosives collided with the SS Imo|SS ''Imo'', a Norway|Norwegian ship in the harbour in Halifax (former city)|Halifax, Nova Scotia, Canada. 1,782 people were killed and over 9,000 people were injured. It is still the largest accidental explosion in history.
