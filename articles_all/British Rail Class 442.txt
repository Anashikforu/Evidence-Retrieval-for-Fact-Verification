The British Rail '''Class 442 ''Wessex Electric''''' (or SR Multiple Unit Numbering and Classification#DC Third Rail EMUs|5WES) electrical multiple units, were introduced in 1988 on the South Western Main Line from London Waterloo station|London Waterloo to Southampton Central railway station|Southampton Central, Bournemouth railway station|Bournemouth, Poole railway station|Poole and Weymouth railway station|Weymouth. Twenty-four of these 5-car units were built between 1988 and 1989 by BREL at their Derby Carriage and Wagon Works|Derby works. Their introduction coincided with the completion of electrification from Bournemouth to Weymouth. The entire fleet was operated by South West Trains up until February 2007, when the Class 442 services were replaced.
