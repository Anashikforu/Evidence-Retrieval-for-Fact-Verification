
 was a Japanese Association football|football player and manager. He played for the Japan national football team|Japan national team. He also managed Japan national team. He was the president of the Japan Football Association from 1994 to 1998.

Biography
Naganuma was born in Hiroshima on September 5, 1930. After graduating from Kwansei Gakuin University and Chuo University, he joined JEF United Chiba|Furukawa Electric in 1955. Furukawa Electric won 1960, 1961 and 1964 Emperor's Cup. In 1961, he was selected first "Japanese Footballer of the Year award". In 1965, Furukawa Electric joined new league Japan Soccer League. He played 19 games and scored 8 goals in the league. He retired in 1967.

In March 1954, Naganuma was selected the Japan national football team|Japan national team for 1954 FIFA World Cup|1954 World Cup qualification. At this qualification, on March 7, he debuted and scored a goal against South Korea national football team|South Korea. He also played at Football at the 1954 Asian Games|1954 Asian Games. In 1956, He was selected Japan for Football at the 1956 Summer Olympics|1956 Summer Olympics. However, he could not play in the match for physical condition.
In 1959, when Naganuma was player, he became a manager for JEF United Chiba|Furukawa Electric. In 1960, he led the club won to 1960 Emperor's Cup champions. This was the first Emperor's Cup champions as a works team. In 1962, when he was 32 years old, he named a manager for the Japan national football team|Japan national team as Hidetoki Takahashi successor. He managed Japan with assistant coach Shunichiro Okano at Football at the 1964 Summer Olympics|1964 Summer Olympics in Tokyo and Football at the 1968 Summer Olympics|1968 Summer Olympics in Mexico City. At 1968 Summer Olympics, Japan won Bronze Medal. This is the first time an Asian team won a medal at Olympics. In 2018, this team was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame. He also managed at Football at the 1966 Asian Games|1966 Asian Games. In 1969, he resigned after 1970 FIFA World Cup|1970 World Cup qualification and Okano was promoted to new manager.

At Football at the 1972 Summer Olympics|1972 Summer Olympics qualification, following Japan's failure to qualify for 1972 Summer Olympics, Okano resigned a manager and Naganuma became a manager again in 1972. He managed 1974 FIFA World Cup|1974 World Cup qualification and Football at the 1974 Asian Games|1974 Asian Games. At Football at the 1976 Summer Olympics|1976 Summer Olympics qualification in April 1976, following Japan's failure to qualify for 1976 Summer Olympics, Naganuma resigned.

Naganuma became a vice-president of Japan Football Association (JFA) in 1987 and a president of JFA in 1994. He also served as vice-president World Cup bidding committee and Japan World Cup Organizing Committee for 2002 FIFA World Cup|2002 World Cup. In 1998, he resigned a president of JFA. In 2005, he was selected Japan Football Hall of Fame.

On June 2, 2008, Naganuma died of pneumonia at the age of 77.
