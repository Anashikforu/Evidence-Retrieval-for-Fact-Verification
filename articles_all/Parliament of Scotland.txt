The '''Parliament of Scotland''', officially the '''Estates of Parliament''', was the legislature of the Independence|independent Kingdom of Scotland.

The parliament of Scotland had only one house (cf. House of Commons, House of Lords) and was mentioned for the first time during the early thirteenth century, and the first meeting one knows of was in 1235, during the reign of Alexander II of Scotland.K. Brown and R. Tanner, ''History of the Scottish Parliament'', i, 'introduction'.

The parliament, which is also referred to as the '''Estates of Scotland''', the '''Three Estates'''  the '''Scots Parliament''' or the '''auld Scots Parliament''' (), met until the Acts of Union 1707|Acts of Union in 1707.
