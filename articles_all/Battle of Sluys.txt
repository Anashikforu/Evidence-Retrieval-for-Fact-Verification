
The '''Battle of Sluys''' (; 1340) was an early and important Naval warfare|naval battle of the Hundred Years' War. The England|English attacked near the port of Sluis and destroyed most of the France|French Fleet (ships)|fleet. This made it impossible for France to invade England. Nearly all the fighting afterward took place on land in France.
