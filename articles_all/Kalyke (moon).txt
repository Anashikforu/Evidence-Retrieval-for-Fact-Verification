'''Kalyke''' or ''''''.
Kalyke is about 5.2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 23,181,000&nbsp;km in 721.021 days, at an inclination of 166° to the ecliptic (165° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.2140.

It was named in October 2002 after the Greek mythological figure Kalyke or Calyce (mythology)|Calyce.
It belongs to the Carme group, made up of non-spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23,000,000 and 24,000,000&nbsp;km and at an inclination of about 165°.
