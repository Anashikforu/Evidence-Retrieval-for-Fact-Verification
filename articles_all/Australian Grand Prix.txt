
The '''Australian Grand Prix''' is a motorsport race contested by cars in the Formula One section. The race was first held in Adelaide before moving to Melbourne in 1996.

 Alain Prost is the only driver, as of the 2019 Australian Grand Prix, to win the race in both Australian domestic (1982 Australian Grand Prix|1982) and World Championship (1986 Australian Grand Prix|1986 and 1988 Australian Grand Prix|1988) formats.
 The last Australian driver to win the AGP was  World Champion Alan Jones (racing driver)|Alan Jones who won 1980 Australian Grand Prix|1980's non-championship race.
 The last Australian driver to officially finish on the podium was John Smith in 1983 Australian Grand Prix|1983. Australia's only current () Grand Prix driver Daniel Ricciardo had finished 2nd in the 2014 Australian Grand Prix|2014 race, but was disqualified post-race.
 Since becoming a round of the Formula One World Championship in , Jones (1985 Australian Grand Prix|1985 and 1986 Australian Grand Prix|1986), David Brabham (1990 Australian Grand Prix|1990 and 1994 Australian Grand Prix|1994), Mark Webber (2002–2013) and Ricciardo (2012–present) are the only Australian drivers to race in the Australian Grand Prix.
