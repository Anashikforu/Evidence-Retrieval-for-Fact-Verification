'''Henri Troyat''', born '''Levon Aslan Torossian''' or '''Lev Aslanovich Tarasov''', (November 1, 1911 – March 2, 2007
He was born in Moscow. His family fled Russia, because they feared the coming Russian Revolution of 1917|revolution. After a long exile, the family  settled in Paris in 1920, where young Troyat was schooled and later earned a law degree.

He won his first literary award at the age of 24, "le prix du Roman populaire". At the age of 27 he was awarded the prestigious Prix Goncourt.

He  published more than 100 books, novels and biographies, among them those of Anton Chekhov, Catherine the Great, Rasputin, Ivan the Terrible and Leo Tolstoy. He was at his death the dean of the Académie française.

His best-known work is ''La neige en deuil'', which was adapted as an English language|English-language movie in 1956 under the title ''The Mountain''.
