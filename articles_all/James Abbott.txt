General '''Sir James Abbott''' (12 March 1807 – 6 October 1896) was a British army officer in colonial British India|India. He became famous in the northwest frontier region of India in the middle part of the 19th century (today this is the Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province of Pakistan). He was one of Henry Lawrence's 'advisers' to the Sikhs, after the First Anglo-Sikh War (1846),Isobel Shaw, ''Pakistan Handbook'', Hong Kong, Local Colour Limited, (1998) P.519 and then became the first deputy commissioner of Hazara, Pakistan|Hazara (1849 - 1853). 

As part of the Treaty of Lahore signed after the defeat of the Sikhs in the First Anglo-Sikh War, Hazara and Kashmir were going to be transferred to Gulab Singh, Hazara however stayed under the Lahore government and governed by James Abbott.:wikisource:1911 Encyclopædia Britannica/Hazara, India|Hazara India : Encyclopædia Britannica Eleventh Edition Article The Pakistani city of Abbottabad was founded by him in January 1853 and is named after him. 

James Abbott was the brother of Augustus Abbott and Frederick Abbott, who also served as officers in the forces of the East India Company. His picture was created by the artist B. Baldwin, this picture is located in the National Portrait Gallery (United Kingdom)|National Portrait Gallery in London, though it is not currently on display.
