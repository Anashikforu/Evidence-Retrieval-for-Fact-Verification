The '''inner moons of Jupiter'''  are four small moons that orbit close to Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter. These moons are closely interconnected with the rings of Jupiter and serve as both sources and sinks of the ring's material. Their semi-major axis|semi-major axes range between 128,000 and 222,000 kilometer|km.

The members of the group are (in order from closest to farthest):
 Metis (moon)|Metis
 Adrastea (moon)|Adrastea
 Amalthea (moon)|Amalthea, the biggest, after which the group is sometimes named
 Thebe (moon)|Thebe

Metis (moon)|Metis and Adrastea (moon)|Adrastea each orbit Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter in less than one Jovian day (the time it takes for Jupiter to rotate once).  Phobos (moon)|Phobos, a moon of Mars (planet)|Mars, is the only other moon known to orbit its planet in less than the length of that planet's day.
