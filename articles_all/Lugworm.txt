
The '''lugworm''' (''Arenicola marina''), also known as the '''sandworm''', is a large ocean|marine worm of the Phylum (biology)|phylum Annelida. Lugworms make the coiled tubes of sand that are a familiar sight on a beach at low tide. The lugworm itself is not seen except by people who dig them up from curiosity or to use as fishing bait.

The lugworm lives in a U-shaped tube in the sand, and filter feeder|filters out its food from the water.

Arenicola marina can grow to about 5 inches long (13 cm). Its body is wikt:segmented|segmented, like that of an earthworm. It has bristles in the middle of its body. It is a polychaeta|polychaete. It also has well-developed blood-vessels, and gills to breathe in the water. 

Although the textbook example is usually ''Arenicola marina'', there is a second species, ''Arenicola defodiens'', which lives a similar life. It grows to about 9 inches long (23 cm)
