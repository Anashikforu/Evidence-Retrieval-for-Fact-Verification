

This is a '''Formula One World Championship scoring system''' used by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA). The FIA uses this system to decide the FIA World List of Formula One World Drivers' Champions|Drivers' and List of Formula One World Constructors' Champions|Constructors' Championships since the  season. The Championships are awarded to the driver and constructor (car builder) who score the most points over the course of the season.

In some seasons, not all race results would be counted towards the World Championship. Only a certain number of a driver's best results would count. In . One additional point was also awarded to the race winner.

The current system was introduced for the  season. It was designed to allow more teams and drivers to score points. 

The most dominant Drivers' Champion in terms of points scored is Jim Clark. Clark scored the maximum of 54 points (6 wins) in both 1963 Formula One season|1963 and 1965 Formula One season|1965. In recent years, Michael Schumacher finished on the podium (one of the top three finishers) in every race of the 2002 Formula One season|2002 season. Schumacher scored 144 of a maximum 170 points. The most dominant Constructors' Champion in recent times was Team McLaren|McLaren in 1988 Formula One season|1988. McLaren scored 199 of a maximum 240 points available. McLaren finished 134 points ahead of the second place team. In , Scuderia Ferrari|Ferrari scored 221 points of a maximum 340 points available. Ferrari finished 129 points ahead of the second place team.
