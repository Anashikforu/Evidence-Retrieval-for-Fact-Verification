
'''Unstrut-Hainich''' is a ''Landkreis'' (Rural Districts of Germany|district) in the north of Thuringia, Germany.

The district was set up on July 1, 1994 by joining the two previous districts of MÃ¼hlhausen and Bad Langensalza.

Partnerships
The district has a twin town|partnership with the Wetteraukreis in Hesse dating back to the German reunification of 1990. The district also has a partnership with the Lithuanian districts of Ukmerge and Raseiniai.

 Geography 
The district is named after the river Unstrut and a hill chain, the Hainich. The Hainich became the 13th national park of Germany in 1997.
