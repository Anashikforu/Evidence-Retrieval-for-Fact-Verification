The '''Parma wallaby''' ('''''Macropus parma''''') is the smallest of the macropus genus. It can grow up to 52 centimetre|cm in length, with a tail about the same length. They weigh up to 5 kilograms|kg. The Parma wallaby's fur is a grey-brown colour on the back with a dark stripe. It has a white stripe on its face, and a white belly.


In the late 1800s it was thought to be extinct. Parma wallabies had been introduced and established on Kauwau Island, New Zealand, where they were rediscovered in the 1960s.
