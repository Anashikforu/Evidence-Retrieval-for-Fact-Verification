'''Herse''' is a Satellite (natural)|moon of Jupiter (planet)|Jupiter. It was found by a team of astronomers led by Brett J. Gladman, ''et al.'' in 2003 
Herse is about 2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 22,134,000&nbsp;km in 672.752 days, at an inclination of 162° to the ecliptic (161° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.2379.

It is the closest member of the Carme group, made up of non-Sphere|spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23,000,000 and 24,000,000&nbsp;km and at an inclination of about 165°.
