
'''Crab spiders''' are the ''Thomisidae'' family of spiders. The group has 170 genera with over 2000 species.

They have two front pairs of legs angled outward and bodies that are flattened and often angular. Also, like crabs, Thomisidae spiders can move sideways or backwards. 

Crab spiders are hunters and ambushers. Some species sit on or among flowers, bark, fruit or leaves where they grab visiting insects. Crab spiders use their powerful front legs to grab and hold onto prey while Paralysis|paralyzing it with a Venom (poison)|venomous bite.

They are camouflaged: several different types are known.Platnick, Norman I. 2010.
