

'''30 Rockefeller Plaza''' is a skyscraper at Rockefeller Center in Manhattan, New York City, United States. It was known in the past as the '''RCA Building''', the '''GE Building''', and the '''Comcast Building'''. 30 Rockefeller Plaza is  tall and has 69 floors.  It was built in 1933 and was one of the tallest buildings in the world when it opened.

History
Raymond Hood, the architect, made the plans for Rockefeller Center. He talked with the RCA|Radio Corporation of America (RCA) to make a large place for mass media. RCA owned the NBC|National Broadcasting Company (NBC) and RKO Pictures|Radio-Keith-Orpheum (RKO) companies. The first plan for 30 Rockefeller Plaza was made in March 1930.

Workers started making the building in March 1932. It was the first building at Rockefeller Center to be opened.  It was one of the tallest buildings in the world.

Companies
NBC was one of the first companies in 30 Rockefeller Plaza. It had 35 television studios in the building.

The Standard Oil Company moved into 30 Rockefeller Plaza in 1934.

People wanted to use the roof of the building for wikt:entertainment|entertainment.
