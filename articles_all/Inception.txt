

'''''Inception''''' is a 2010 British-American science fiction action movie|action heist movie written and directed by Christopher Nolan, who also produced the movie with his wife, Emma Thomas. The movie stars Leonardo DiCaprio as a professional thief who steals information by infiltrating the subconscious of his targets. He is offered a chance to have his criminal history erased as payment for the implantation of another person's idea into a target's subconscious. The ensemble cast includes Ken Watanabe, Joseph Gordon-Levitt, Marion Cotillard, Elliot Page, Tom Hardy, Dileep Rao, Cillian Murphy, Tom Berenger, and Michael Caine.

The idea behind ''Inception'' was first developed over nine years before it was released. But, there is also another anime film just like it called Paprika (2006 film). After the 2002 completion of ''Insomnia (movie)|Insomnia'', Nolan wrote an 80-page movie treatment|treatment for a horror movie envisioning "dream-stealers", presenting the idea to Warner Bros. based on ideas of lucid dreaming and dream incubation.  
Deciding he needed more experience before tackling a production of this magnitude and complexity, Nolan shelved the project Nolan's success with ''The Dark Knight'' helped gain the movie $100 million to spend on advertising.

''Inception'' was first screened in London on July 8, 2010 and was shown in standard and IMAX theatres on July 16, 2010. It won four Academy Awards (Academy Award for Best Cinematography|Best Cinematography, Academy Award for Best Sound Editing|Best Sound Editing, Academy Award for Best Sound Mixing|Best Sound Mixing, and Academy Award for Best Visual Effects|Best Visual Effects) and was nominated for four more: Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture, Academy Award for Best Original Screenplay|Best Original Screenplay, Academy Award for Best Art Direction|Best Art Direction, and Academy Award for Best Original Score|Best Original Score.
