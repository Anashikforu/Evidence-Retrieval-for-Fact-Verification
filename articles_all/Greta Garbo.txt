
'''Greta Garbo''' (born '''Greta Lovisa Gustafsson'''; 18 September 1905 – 15 April 1990) was a Swedes|Swedish-Americans|American actress.

Life and career
'''Greta Gustafson''' was born in Stockholm, she studied at the Royal School of Dramatic Art in her native city, while working as a fashion model. She became a star in Sweden when Mauritz Stiller cast her in the 1924 silent movie ''The Atonement of Gösta Berling''. 

Soon after, she came to the attention of Louis B. Mayer and went to Hollywood in 1925. Soon, she established herself as one of the world's most popular movie stars. 

Some of her most notable movies include; ''Mata Hari (1931 movie)|Mata Hari'' (1931), ''Queen Christina (movie)|Queen Christina'' (1933), ''Camille (1936 movie)|Camille'' (1936), ''Conquest (1937 movie)|Conquest'' (1937) and ''Ninotchka'' (1939).

She retirement|retired from movies in 1941 and  became an American citizen on 9 February 1951. In 1984 she became breast Cancer . So, she lived as a semi-recluse in New York City until her death (April 15; 1990) there from renal failure and pneumonia.
