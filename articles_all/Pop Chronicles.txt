

The '''Pop Chronicles''' were two radio documentaries.  They told the story of music that was popular in America in the 1940s, 1950s, and 1960s.  John Gilliland made both documentaries.

The Pop Chronicles of the 1950s and 1960s
Los Angeles Radio station KRLA 1110 broadcast the Pop Chronicles documentary in 1969.  The engineer and associate producer was Chester Coleman.{{Citation

Other radio stations also broadcast the Pop Chronicles.

Pop Chronicles the 1940s

San Francisco radio station KSFO broadcast the "Pop Chronicles of the 1940s" in 1972 and 1976.

After his death, Gilliand's sister donated the "Pop Chronicles" tapes to the University of North Texas Music Library.   They form The John Gilliland Collection.Earlier version.
