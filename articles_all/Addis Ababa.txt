
'''Addis Ababa''' (spelled '''Addis Abeba''' by the Ethiopian Mapping Institute, also called Finfinne) is the capital (city)|capital city of Ethiopia. 

History

Addis Ababa is both a city and a state. The city has people living in it from as many as 80 different county|countries speaking 80 different languages. There are many different community|communities in the city including Christianity|Christian, Islam|Muslim, and Judaism|Jewish communities. Addis Ababa is about 2,500 metre|m above sea level. It has about four million people.

The site was chosen by Empress Taytu Betul. The city was :wikt:found|founded in 1886 by her husband, Emperor of Ethiopia|Emperor Menelik II.  The city is at the foot of Mount Entoto. In 1935 the city (renamed Addis Abeba) was conquered by the Italians and was made the capital of their empire in eastern Africa until 1941. Italians made huge improvements and modern projects to the city.

The city has grown since World War II and is now a huge metropolis that has nearly 4 people living there as of 2020.

Addis Ababa University is in the city. Addis Ababa University was formerly known as Haile Selassie I University. It was named after the former Emperor of Ethiopia, who gave his palace to be used the main campus of the university in 1961.
