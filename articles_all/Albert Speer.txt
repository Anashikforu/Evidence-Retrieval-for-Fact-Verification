

'''Berthold Konrad Hermann Albert Speer''' (19 March 1905 in Mannheim, Germany&nbsp;– 1 September 1981 in London, England) was a leading Germany|German architect in the Third Reich. Speer was born in Mannheim, Baden. Adolf Hitler's chief architect before he became the minister for munitions and armaments in 1942. He was also a member of Hitler's Cabinet. After World War II he was convicted of slavery and spent 20 years in prison.  

Speer died of a stroke. His son, Albert Speer Jr. was also an architect.
