
The '''1964 Winter Olympics''', officially known as the '''IX Olympic Winter Games''', were held in Innsbruck, Austria, from January 29 to February 9 1964. The games included 1091 athletes from 36 nations, and the Olympic Torch was carried by Joseph Rieder,

The games were affected by the deaths of Australian alpine skier Ross Milne and a Great Britain|British luge slider, during training, and by the deaths, 3 years earlier, of the entire US figure skating team and family members.
Participating nations
36 nations sent athletes to compete in Innsbruck.  India, Mongolia, and North Korea participated in the Winter Games for the first time.  Athletes from West Germany (FRG) and German Democratic Republic|East Germany (GDR) competed together as the United Team of Germany from 1956 to 1964.
