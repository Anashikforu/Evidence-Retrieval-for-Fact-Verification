
'''Lucerne''' (, Swiss German: ''Lozärn'', French language|French: ''Lucerne'', Italian language|Italian: ''Lucerna'', Romansh language|Romansh: ''Lucerna'') is a city in Switzerland. It is the Capital (city)|capital of the Cantons of Switzerland|Canton of Lucerne (canton)|Lucerne and county seat|seat of the districts of Switzerland|district with the same name. The city has a population of about 60,000. Lucerne is the biggest and most important city in Central Switzerland. The city's metropolitan area consists of 17 municipality|municipalities in three cantons. It has an overall population of nearly 200,000 people.

Since 2010, Littau part of the city.

 Geography 
Lucerne is located in central Switzerland on the northern end of Lake Lucerne. The Reuss (river)|river Reuss flows from the lake through the city. The city is surrounded by hills and Mountain|mountains, for example the mountains Pilatus (mountain)|Pilatus and Rigi.

 History 
Lucerne joined Switzerland in 1332.

 Sights and attractions 
Due to its location on the shore of Lake Lucerne ('''') within sight of the mountains Pilatus (mountain)|Pilatus and Rigi, Lucerne is mainly a tourism|tourist destination. One of the city's famous landmarks is Kapellbrücke|Chapel Bridge (Kapellbrücke), a wooden bridge from the 14th century. Many tourists choose Lucerne as their place to stay because the city is close to the alps in in the center of the country.

Lucerne is also famous for it's traditional ''Fasnacht ''(Carnival|carneval) celebration which takes place every spring.
