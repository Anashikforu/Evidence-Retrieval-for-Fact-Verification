'''Salisbury Plain''' is in the central southern England covering ."Salisbury Plain", ''Encyclopaedia Britannica''. It largely lies within the county of Wiltshire, with a little in Hampshire. 

The Plain is famous for its rich archaeology, including Avebury and Stonehenge, which are a joint World Heritage Site. The Plain is sparsely populated and is the largest remaining area of calcareous grassland in north-west Europe. The Plain also has wikt:arable|arable land, and a few small areas of beech| beech trees and coniferous woodland.

On the Plain is the British Army's largest training ground in England. It is suitable terrain for tank wikt:manoeuvre|manoeuvres; buildings such as Imber are used for urban warfare training.

 Boundaries 
The boundaries of Salisbury Plain have never been defined, and there is some difference of opinion as to its exact area.James N.D.G. 1987. ''Plain Soldiering''. Hobnob Press. The valley|river valleys surrounding it, and other downland|downs and plains beyond them loosely define its boundaries.  To the north the escarpment|scarp of the downs overlooks the Vale of Pewsey, and to the north west the  River Avon|Avon. The River Wylye runs along the south west, and the River Bourne runs to the east."
