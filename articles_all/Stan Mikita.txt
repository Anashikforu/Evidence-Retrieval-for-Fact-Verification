

'''Stan Mikita''' (born '''Stanislav Guoth'''; May 20, 1940 &ndash; August 7, 2018) was a Slovak-born Canadians|Canadian retired professional ice hockey Centre (ice hockey)|centre. He played a career total of 22 years in the National Hockey League (NHL). He played his entire NHL career with the Chicago Blackhawks|Chicago Black Hawks. He was often regarded as the best centre of the 1960s.

He won the Stanley Cup with the Chicago Blackhawks|Chicago Black Hawks in 1961 Stanley Cup Finals|1961. He was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1983.

He worked as an good will ambassador for the Chicago Blackhawks.

Mikita died at the age of 78 on August 7, 2018 from complications of Lewy body dementia in Chicago.
