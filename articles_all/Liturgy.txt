A '''liturgy''' is a ceremony, according to the traditions of a particular group or event. In religion, it may mean an wikt:elaborate|elaborate formal ritual (such as the Mass (liturgy)|Catholic Mass), or a daily activity such as the Muslim Salats.''Oxford Dictionary of World Religions'', p.582-3) Anglican, Eastern Orthodox Church|Orthodox, Lutheran, or other groups, when using the words "the liturgy", normally mean a standardized order of events observed during a religious service, often including the Eucharist |Eucharist or Communion. The ''open'' or ''waiting'' worship of Religious Society of Friends|Quakers is an example of a non-liturgical service because there is no structured order of events.
