A '''member of parliament''' (usually shortened to '''MP''') is a person in the United Kingdom or in Canada who represents the people of an area (called a "constituency") in the House of Commons of the United Kingdom|House of Commons. They are voted for by the people in a general election. The Prime minister|Prime Minister chooses when to hold a general election, but must have an election at least once every five years. MPs are usually members of a group called a political party. 

In the UK, the main political parties are:
 Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative party
 Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party
 Liberal Democrats
 Scottish National Party
 Green Party of England and Wales|Green Party

In Canada, the main political parties are:
 Liberal Party of Canada|Liberal Party
 Conservative Party of Canada|Conservative Party
 New Democratic Party (NDP)
 Bloc Québécois (BQ)
 Green Party of Canada|Green Party

Members of provincial legislatures in Canada are sometimes referred to as '''member of provincial parliament''' (MPP) and other times '''member of legislative assembly''' (MLA).
