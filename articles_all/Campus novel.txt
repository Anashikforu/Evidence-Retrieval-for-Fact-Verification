A '''campus novel''', also called the '''academic novel''', is a novel that's story is around or about a campus of a university. The genre is said to have started in the 1950s. ''The Groves of Academe'' by Mary McCarthy (author)|Mary McCarthy, written in 1952, is often called the first campus novel, but some have also said that novels such as C.P. Snow's ''Strangers and Brothers|The Masters'', of the year before, were campus novels.
