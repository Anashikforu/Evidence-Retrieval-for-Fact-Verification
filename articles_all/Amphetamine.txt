

'''Amphetamine''' is a wikt:synthetic|synthetic drug. The drug is a stimulant: it can help people keep the focus and stay awake. It also decreases appetite and fatigue. Chemically, it is similar to methamphetamine. These drugs increase the level of dopamine and norepinephrine in the brain, which can create euphoria.Rang and Dale, Pharmacology The class of drugs containing amphetamine includes prescription drugs commonly used to treat attention-deficit hyperactivity disorder (ADHD). It is also used to treat symptoms of traumatic brain injury (TBI) and the daytime drowsiness symptoms of narcolepsy, amongst others. Initially, amphetamine was more popularly used to anorectic|reduce appetite and to control weight.

Sometimes, the drug is used illegal drugs|for fun or to boost performance.  Many different names are used for amphetamines.  They include Benzedrine and '''speed'''.  The European Monitoring Centre for Drugs and Drug Addiction reports the typical retail price of amphetamine in Europe varied between €3 and €15 ($4 to $21.55 United States Dollar|USD) a gram in half of the reporting countries.
