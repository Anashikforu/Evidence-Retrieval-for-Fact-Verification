''' Landsberg Prison''' is a prison in the town of Landsberg am Lech in the southwest of the Germany|German state of Bavaria. It is about  west of Munich.

History
The Landsberg Prison was built around 1910 on the town's western edge. Its most noted prisoner was Adolf Hitler. Hitler was there in 1924 after being found guilty of treason for his part in the Beer Hall Putsch. It was during his time at Landsberg that Hitler wrote his book ''Mein Kampf'' (''My Struggle'') with help from Rudolf Hess. During the Military occupation|occupation of Germany by the Allies of World War II|Allies after World War II, the United States Army|US Army named the prison as "War Criminal Prison No. 1".
