
The British Rail '''Class 37''' is a diesel locomotive|diesel-electric locomotive.  It is also known as the '''English Electric Type 3'''. The Class was ordered as part of the British Rail#1955 Modernisation Plan|British Rail modernisation plan.

The Class 37 became a familiar sight on many parts of the British Rail network. They were on InterCity (British Rail)|Inter-City services in East Anglia and within Scotland. They also performed well on secondary and inter-regional services for many years. The Class 37 is known by railway enthusiasts as a "Tractor". This nickname came from the similarity of the sound of the locomotive.
