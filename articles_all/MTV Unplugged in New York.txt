'''''MTV Unplugged in New York''''' is a live album by the United States|American rock music|rock band Nirvana (band)|Nirvana. It was recorded on November 18, 1993 as a part of the ''MTV Unplugged'' television series, which many artists and bands perform on with acoustic music|acoustic instruments instead of electric guitar|electric ones. It was released on November 1, 1994, six months after the band had broken up because of the death of Kurt Cobain, who was the lead Singing|singer and guitarist.

It was unusual compared to other artists on the ''MTV Unplugged'' series, because Nirvana played mostly their less-famous songs and cover versions of other artists' songs. Most artists on the program played mainly their hits.

Four Single (music)|singles were released from the album. This was unusual because it was a live album. The singles were "About a Girl (song)|About a Girl", "The Man Who Sold the World", "Where Did You Sleep Last Night", and "Lake of Fire". All of these except for "About a Girl" were covers.
