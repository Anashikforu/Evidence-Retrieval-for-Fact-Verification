
'''Haute-Marne''' is a departments of France|department in the northeast of France named after the Marne (river)|Marner river. It is in the Grand Est Regions of France|region and its Prefectures in France|prefecture (Capital city|capital) is the city of Chaumont, Haute-Marne|Chaumont.

Charles de Gaulle lived until his death in Colombey-les-Deux-Églises.

History
Haute-Marne is one of the original 83 departments created during the French Revolution on 4 March 1790 with Chaumont as is Capital city|capital. It was formed with parts of the former provinces of Champagne (province)|Champagne,  Burgundy, Duchy of Lorraine|Lorraine and Franche-Comté.

It was divided in six districts: Bourbonne, Bourmont, Chaumont, Joinville, Langres and Saint-Dizier.

In 1800, with the creation of the Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' in France, the six districts were changed into three ''arrondissements'': Chaumont, Langres and Wassy.

After the Battle of Waterloo (18 June 1815), the department was occupied by the Austrian army from June 1815 to November 1818.

On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Wassy was eliminated but in 1940 Wassy was made again an ''arrondissement'' but the Prefectures in France|subprefecture was moved to Saint-Dizier.

Geography
The Haute-Marne department is in the Grand Est region. It is bordered by 6 departments in two Regions of France|regions:

 Vosges (east)
 Aube (west)

 Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region
 Haute-Saône (southeast)
 Côte-d'Or (southwest)



Administration
Haute-Marne is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Haute-Marne'' in Chaumont, Haute-Marne|Chaumont. The department is part of the Grand Est region.

=Administrative divisions=
There are 5 Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' (districts), 23 Cantons in France|''cantons'' and 427 Communes in France|''communes'' (Municipality|municipalities) in Haute-Marne.
