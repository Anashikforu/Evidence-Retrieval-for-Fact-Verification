'''Parliament Hill''' (in ) refers to a set of building|buildings in Ottawa, Ontario, the Capital city|capital of Canada Dry|Canada, where the Government of Canada meets and Member of Parliament|Members of Parliament make law|laws.  It is called Parliament Hill because it is on a hill above the Ottawa River.

There are three main buildings: the West Block, the East Block and the Centre Block.

A fire in 1916 burned the Centre Block and only the library was saved.  The building was rebuilt and the Peace Tower was finished in 1927.

The roof of each building is made of copper which turns green over time.

There are many statue|statues around Parliament Hill including many former Prime Minister of Canada|Prime Ministers and 5 famous Woman|women who helped women Women's rights|gain equal rights in Canada.  There is also a centennial flame that was lit when Canada turned centenary|100 years old.
