

In chess notation, the white rooks start on the ''a1'' and ''h1'' squares, and the black rooks start on the ''a8'' and ''h8'' squares. 

The rook moves forward or back on the List of chess terms#File|files through any number of squares without other pieces on them, and sideways on the List of chess terms#Rank|ranks. This is shown in the diagram below. Like other pieces, it captures by going into the square on which an enemy piece stands. 

The rook and king (chess)|king also take part in a special move called castling.
