
'''Despina''' or '''Neptune&nbsp;V''' is the third closest moon to Neptune (planet)|Neptune. It is named after Despina (mythology)|Despina, a nymph who was a daughter of Poseidon. 

Despina was found in late July 1989 from the images taken by the ''Voyager 2'' space probe|probe. It was given the designation '''S/1989&nbsp;N&nbsp;3'''.

Despina is not a sphere and shows no sign of any geological changes.
