'''Erinome''' or '''.
Erinome is about 3.2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 22,986,000&nbsp;km in 711.965 days, at an inclination of 164° to the ecliptic (162° to Jupiter's equator), with an orbital eccentricity of 0.2552.

It was named in October, 2002 after the Roman mythological Erinome (mythology)|Erinome, a lover of Jupiter (mythology)|Jupiter.
Erinome belongs to the Carme group, made up of non-spherical retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23,000,000 and 24,000,000&nbsp;km and at an inclination of about 165°.
