

'''Transport for London''' ('''TfL''') is an organisation that runs the public transport network in London. It is held with direct responsibility to the mayor of London.

Numbering of bus routes
=Historic numbering=
Historically, bus routes were grouped by the type of service that they provided.

The London Traffic Act 1924|1924 London Traffic Act used the Bassom Scheme. It was named after A.E. Bassom of the Metropolitan Police who made it. Changing and short lines used letter suffixes. Also, the numbers were grouped by the company that operated the route.

The numbering was changed in 1934 after London Passenger Transport Board|London Transport was formed.
