
'''Robert Marvin "Bobby" Hull''', Order of Canada|OC (born January 3, 1939 in Belleville, Ontario|Pointe Anne, Ontario) is a Canadians|Canadian retired ice hockey left winger.

Hull is highly regarded as one of the greatest ice hockey players of all time and perhaps the greatest left winger to ever play the game of hockey. Hull was known for his blonde hair, blinding skating speed, and having the fastest shot in the game which also earned him the nickname, "the Golden Jet".

Hull played 17 seasons in the National Hockey League|NHL with 3 teams, the Chicago Blackhawks|Chicago Black Hawks for 15 seasons, the Winnipeg Jets (1972–96)|Winnipeg Jets for 1 season and the Hartford Whalers for 1 season.
 
Hull was inducted in the Hockey Hall of Fame in 1983.
