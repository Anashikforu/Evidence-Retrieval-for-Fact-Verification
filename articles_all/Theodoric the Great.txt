'''Theodoric the Great''' (454–August 30, 526), known to the Ancient Rome|Romans as '''Flavius Theodoricus''', was king of the Ostrogoths (488–526), ruler of Italy (493–526), and regent of the Visigoths (511–526). He united several German tribes and encouraged Arianism.
