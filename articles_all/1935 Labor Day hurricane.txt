

The '''Labor Day Hurricane of 1935''' was the strongest tropical cyclone during the 1935 Atlantic hurricane season. The second tropical cyclone, second hurricane, and second Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Scale|intense hurricane of the season was the most intense List of Category 5 Atlantic hurricanes|Atlantic hurricane that affected the United States, and it remains the first of three Category 5 hurricanes that struck the country. After forming as a weak tropical storm east of the The Bahamas|Bahamas on August 29, it slowly proceeded westward, became a hurricane on September 1, and underwent rapid deepening|rapid intensification prior to striking the upper Florida Keys on September 2. After landfall at its peak intensity, it continued northwest along the Florida west coast, and it weakened prior to landfall near Cedar Key, Florida|Cedar Key on September 4.

The large and strong hurricane caused severe damage in the upper Florida Keys, and a storm surge about 18 to 20 feet affected the region. The hurricane's strong winds destroyed most of the buildings in the Islamorada, Florida|Islamorada area, and many World War I veteran workers were killed by the storm surge. Portions of the Overseas Railroad|Key West Extension of the Florida East Coast Railroad were severely damaged or destroyed. The hurricane also caused additional damage in northwest Florida, Georgia (U.S. state)|Georgia, and the The Carolinas|Carolinas. In total, more than 400 people were killed.
