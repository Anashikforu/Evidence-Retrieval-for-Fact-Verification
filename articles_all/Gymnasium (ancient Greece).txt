In Ancient Greece the '''Gymnasion''' (or '''Gymnasium''', romanized name) was a place where Sportsperson|athletes could training|train for the competitions in public games, such as the Olympic Games. ''Gymnos'' means nudity|naked. Only men were allowed to enter, and train; they did so fully naked (as the name implies). Athletes also competed in the nude. This was said to encourage aesthetic appreciation of the male body. It was believed to be a tribute to the Gods. Gymnasia and palaestra|palestrae were under the protection and patronage of Heracles, Hermes and, in Athens, Theseus.Pausanias (geographer), ''Guide to Greece,'' 4.32.1
