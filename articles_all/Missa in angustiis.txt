The '''Missa in Angustiis''' (''Mass for troubled times'') is one of fourteen Mass (liturgy)|masses written by Joseph Haydn. It is also called '''Nelson mass''', because it was first performed after Lord Horatio Nelson won a battle against Napoleon Bonaparte. It was written in 1798. It is one of the six masses Haydn wrote towards the end of his life.
