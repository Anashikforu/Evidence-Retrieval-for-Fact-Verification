Image:HMS Victory 1884.jpg|thumb|240px|The HMS Victory flagshipA '''flagship''' is the ship used by the officer who commanded a group of navy|naval ships. The term comes from the custom of the commanding officer to fly a special flag to show he was present.

Used in this way, "flagship" is normally a temporary name; the flagship is wherever the officer is flying his flag. However, admirals have always needed a special place; a meeting room large enough to hold all the captains of the fleet, and a place for the admiral's staff to make plans and draw up orders.

Over the years, the term "flagship" has been wikt:borrow|borrowed by other industry|industries. These include broadcasting, automobiles, cell phones, and others to mean their wikt:core|core Product (business)|products or Service (economics)|services.
