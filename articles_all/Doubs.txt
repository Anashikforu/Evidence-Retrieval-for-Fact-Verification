

'''Doubs''' is a Departments of France|department in eastern France. It is in the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté Regions of France|region. It is named after the Doubs (river)|Doubs river.

The largest city in the department, and its Prefectures in France|prefecture (Capital (city)|capital), is the city of Besançon.

History
Doubs is one of the original 83 departments created during the French Revolution on 4 March 1790 with Besançon as its capital. It was formed from part of the old province of ''Franche-Comté''.

It was divided in six districts: Besançon, Baume-les-Dames, Saint-Hippolyte, Pontarlier, Quingey and Ornans. In 1800, with the creation of the Arrondissements of France|''arrondissements'' in France, the six districts were changed into four ''arrondissements'': Besançon, Baume-les-Dames, Pontarlier and Saint-Hippolyte. In 1816, the Prefectures in France|subprefecture of Saint-Hippolyte is moved to Montbéliard.

After the Battle of Waterloo (18 June 1815), the department was occupied by the Austrian army from June 1815 to November 1818.

On 10 September 1926, the ''arrondissement'' of Baume-les-Dames was eliminated.

Geography
Doubs is part of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté Regions of France|region. It has an area of .

The department borders with 3 departments of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region (Jura (department)|Jura, Haute-Saône and Territoire de Belfort) and 3 Cantons of Switzerland|cantons of Switzerland (Vaud, Neuchâtel (canton)|Neuchâtel and Jura (canton)|Jura).

The Doubs department is partially on the Jura Mountains, mainly the eastern part of the department on the border with Switzerland. The highest point in the department is Mont d'Or (Jura Mountains)|Mont d'Or ( high; high.

The main rivers in the department are the Doubs, that gives its name to the department, the Ognon and the Loue (tributary of the Doubs).

Administration
Doubs is managed by the ''Departmental Council of Doubs'' in Besançon. The department is part of the Bourgogne-Franche-Comté region.
