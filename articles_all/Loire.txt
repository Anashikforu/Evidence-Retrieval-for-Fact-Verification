
'''Loire''' () is a France|French Departments of France|department in the east-central part of France in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes Regions of France|region and named after the Loire River|Loire river. Its Prefectures in France|prefecture is the city of Saint-Étienne.

History
Rhône-et-Loire was one French departments made during the French revolution, on 4 March 1790. The department was divided in two departments in 1793: Rhône and Loire. The department of Loire had three districts: Montbrison, Roanne and Saint-Étienne. The Capital city|capital was Feurs but in 1795 the capital was moved to Montbrison, Loire|Montbrison.

With the creation of the ''arrondissements'', the three districts became three ''arrondissements'': Montbrison, Roanne and Saint-Étienne.

After the Battle of Waterloo in 1815, the department was occupied by troops from Austria from June 1815 to November 1818.

The capital was moved from Montbrison to Saint-Étienne in 1855.

Administration
Loire is part of the region of Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes. The department is managed by the ''Departamental Council of the Loire'' in Saint-Étienne.

Demographics
The inhabitants of Loire are known, in French language|French, as ''Ligériens'' (women: ''Ligériennes''). ''Ligérien'' comes from ''Liger'', the Latin name of the Loire river.

Loire has a population, in 2014, of 757,305, for a population density of  inhabitants/km2. The ''arrondissement'' of Saint-Étienne, with 411,402 inhabitants, is by far the largest. The other two, Montbrison and Roanne, have respectively 188,635 and 157,268 inhabitants.

The city with more people living in it is the Capital city|capital, Saint-Étienne (170,761 inhabitants). The subprefectures of Roanne and Montbrison, Loire|Montbrison have, respectively, 35,200 and 15,689 inhabitants.
