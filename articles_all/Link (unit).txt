A '''link''', also called a '''Gunterâ€™s link''', was a unit of length in the Imperial unit|imperial and U.S. customary units|US customary systems. Links were commonly used in measure|measuring land in the English-speaking world before the twentieth century, but are never used. ''Link'' is Abbreviation|abbreviated as ''ln''. It was invented by Edmund Gunter, a professor of astronomy at Gresham College, London, the same man who made the chain. He made the link as a low-technology piece of equipment that could survey small areas of land very accurately, whereas the chain could measure towns and large areas of land.
