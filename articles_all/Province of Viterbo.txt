
The '''Province of Viterbo''' () is a Provinces of Italy|province in the Lazio region of Central Italy. Its Capital city|capital is the city of Viterbo.

The province is also called ''Tuscia''.

History
The province of Viterbo was created in January of 1927 with part of the old ''Province of Rome''. In March of the same, the comune of Monte Romano.

In 1928, five ''comuni'' were added:  Montalto di Castro, Monterosi, Nepi, Oriolo Romano and Tarquinia.

Geography
The province of Viterbo has an area of , making it the second largest province of the Lazio region.

The province is bordered to the northwest with Tuscany (Province of Grosseto|Grosseto and Province of Siena|Siena provinces), to the northeast with Umbria (Province of Terni|Terni province), to the east with the Province of Rieti|Rieti province and to the south with Metropolitan City of Rome Capital|Rome. The Tyrrhenian Sea is to the west.



The province is in the northwest of the Lazio region. Most of the territory of the province is flat or with small hills. The highest mountain in the province is Monte Cimino (.

The main river in the province is the Tiber River|Tiber that forms the border between the Umbria (province of Terni) and Lazio (province of Viterbo) regions.
