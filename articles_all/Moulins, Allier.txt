
'''Moulins''' is a Communes of France|''commune'' in the Allier Departments of France|department in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes Regions of France|region in central France.

It is the ''Prefectures in France|préfecture'' (Capital city|capital) of the department and the History|historical capital of the old province of ''Bourbonnais'' (the Duchy of Bourbonnais).

History
Moulins experienced its golden age in the 15th century, when the Dukes of House of Bourbon|Bourbon lived in the city on the Allier (river)|Allier river. The region of the northern Massif Central where is Moulins is called the ''Bourbonnais'' after the House of Bourbon.

Moulins became the capital of Allier on 4 March 1790 with the creation of the departments in France.

During the Germany|German Occupied territory|occupation in the World War II|Second World War, the border between Occupied territory|occupied France and the pro-German France (Vichy France) went through the midst of Moulins.

Administration
Moulins is the Prefectures in France|prefecture of the Allier department since 1800. It is also the capital of the ''arrondissement'' of Arrondissement of Moulins|Moulins and the administrative centre () of two cantons:
 Canton of Moulins-1|Moulins-1, formed by 6 ''communes'' and part of Moulins, with 18,030 inhabitants (2014).
 Canton of Moulins-2|Moulins-2, formed by 22 ''communes'' and part of Moulins, with 19,368 inhabitants (2014).

It is part of the Communes of France|intercommunality ''Moulins Communauté'' ().
