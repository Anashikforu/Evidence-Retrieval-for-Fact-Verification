

'''Ars-sur-Formans''' is a Communes of France|commune. It is found in the Auvergne-Rhône-Alpes Regions of France|region in the Ain Departments of France|department in the east of France. In 2009, 1,326 people lived in the commune. The inhabitants are the ''Arsois''. The most important monuments in the commune are the Ars basilica|Ars basilica, built in 1862, and the ''Presbytère du curé d'Ars'', located at the side of the basilica, and which contains a relic (his heart) of Jean-Marie Vianney, ''curé'' of Ars in the 19th century. The commune is twinned with Freihalden in Germany since 1977.
