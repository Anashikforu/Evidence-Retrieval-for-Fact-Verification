'''Margaret Spellings''' (born '''Margaret Dudar'''; November 30, 1957) was the second  United States Secretary of Education|Secretary of Education under the administration of President of the United States|U.S. President George W. Bush. She was previously Assistant to the President for Domestic Policy to President Bush. 

She was one of the principal authors of the 2001 No Child Left Behind Act.
