'''Warship''' was a popular United Kingdom|British television drama series produced by the BBC between 1973 and 1977.

The series was about life on board a Royal Navy warship, the fictional '''HMS Hero'''. It was mainly filmed aboard the Leander class frigate|Leander-class frigate '''HMS Phoebe (F42)|HMS Phoebe'''.

Four Leander class frigates played the role of HMS Hero. They were HMS Phoebe (F42)|HMS Phoebe, '''HMS Dido (F104)|HMS Dido''', '''HMS Hermione (F58)|HMS Hermione''' and '''HMS Jupiter (F60)|HMS Jupiter'''. All the crews of these warships were given HMS Hero cap tallies for filming purposes.

The creator of the series was a serving Royal Navy Naval officer ranks|officer, Ian Mackintosh. He wrote three books based on episodes of the series. They were simultaneously published in hardback and paperback. The books were:
 ''Warship'' (published in 1973);
 ''HMS Hero'' (published in 1976);
 and ''Holt RN'' (published in 1977).

The opening music of the series was a march played by the Royal Marines, called ''Warship'', and written by Anthony Isaac. It is still played by Royal Marine bands.
