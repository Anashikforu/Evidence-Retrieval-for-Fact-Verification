 Traditional history 
According to ''Nihonshoki'' and ''Kojiki'', he was a son of Emperor Nintoku.  The eldest son would become known as Emperor Richū.

Hanzei was the brother of Richū.  Hanzei's enthronement by-passed Richū's two sons.  Few other details have survived.Ponsonby-Fane, Richard. (1959). ''The Imperial House of Japan,'' p. 39.

 Events of Hanzei's life 
Very little is known about the events of Hanzei's life and reign.  Only limited information is wikt:available|available for study prior to the reign of the 29th monarch, Emperor Kimmei.Titsingh, pp. 34-36; Brown, pp. 261-262; Varley, pp. 123-124.


Hanzei's reign is described as a time of peace. He died quietly in his palace.Aston, William. (1998). Nihongi, Vol. 1, pp. 310-311.
